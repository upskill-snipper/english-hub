# LinkedIn follow-up — connection requests + DMs

LinkedIn is where Qatar school leaders actually keep their work relationships. Send connection requests **the same evening** as the conversation, not three days later.

---

## Connection request — Tier 1/2 (with a personalised note)

Stay under LinkedIn's 200-character limit for the connection note. The note is the difference between accept (high) and ignore (high).

> Great meeting you at the Qatar Education Leadership Expo today — would love to stay in touch. I'll send a follow-up email separately on the [Y11 IGCSE pilot / PDPPL diligence] we discussed. — Calum

If 200 chars is tight: drop "today" and "would love to stay in touch" — keep the specific topic and the named-action commitment.

---

## Connection request — Tier 3 (decision-maker, Leadership Lounge)

> Thank you for the time at the Leadership Lounge today — your point on [the strategic thing they said] stayed with me. Sending a follow-up by email; would value staying in touch here too. — Calum

---

## Connection request — Tier 3 (group COO / education group)

## Connection request — cold card (Tier 3, light conversation)

> We met briefly at the Qatar Education Leadership Expo at the weekend — The English Hub booth (UK GCSE/IGCSE English, PDPPL-compliant). Connecting here so you have me on file. — Calum

---

## After the connection is accepted — the follow-up DM

Wait **24–48 hours** after the connection is accepted before sending the DM. Sending it immediately after acceptance looks automated.

> Hi [First name],
>
> Thanks for connecting. As mentioned at the expo, I sent the follow-up email to [their email] earlier this week with the [pilot terms / compliance one-pager / proposal]. If it's easier to talk here than over email, happy to do that. Otherwise — happy to wait until you've had a chance to read the email.
>
> Calum

Short. No second pitch. The DM is a polite "I'm here when you're ready" — not a re-sell.

---

## What to NOT do on LinkedIn

- ❌ Don't connect with everyone you exchanged a card with on the same day — LinkedIn's algorithm flags this as spam-like behaviour and starts hiding your requests. Space them across 24–48 hours, 20-30 per day max.
- ❌ Don't send a long DM as your first message. The whole point of LinkedIn is the relationship; long pitches go to email.
- ❌ Don't auto-post a generic "great to meet everyone at #qatareducation" post and tag 40 people. Looks performative and dilutes the personal-touch value of the individual connections.
- ❌ Don't connect with their executive assistant unless the assistant was the contact at the booth. EAs flag promiscuous connection-grabbing fast.
- ❌ Don't pitch in the connection note. The note is for context, not the ask. The ask comes in the email or the post-connection DM.

## After 30 days — the relationship maintenance cadence

For everyone you connected with but didn't close:

- **Month 1:** one personalised LinkedIn message — "saw [thing happening at their school / in their network], thought of you". Not a pitch.
- **Month 3:** one product update — "we shipped X you might find interesting". Single sentence; link to a blog post if there is one.
- **Month 6:** one annual-cycle check-in — "your software budget is probably being set around now; happy to send the pilot terms again if it's worth a fresh look."

That's it. Three touches over six months keeps you warm without becoming the founder who keeps emailing.
