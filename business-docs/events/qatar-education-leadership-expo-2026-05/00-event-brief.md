# Event brief — Qatar Education Leadership Expo 2026

## The event in one paragraph

Two-day exhibition + conference at the Radisson Blu Doha (Sat 23 – Sun 24 May 2026, 08:00–19:00 daily). 100+ exhibitor booths (schools, universities, EdTech, international education consultants), 120+ speakers across panels and workshops (principals, deans, ministry representatives), 4,000+ attendees from Qatar, the GCC, and beyond. Hosted by Crossway Event Management. An **invite-only "Leadership Lounge"** runs alongside the main floor for CEO/decision-maker networking. Programmed themes include **AI in education**, leadership, curriculum design, school growth. Year 11 & Year 12 students are explicitly invited; this matches our IGCSE/A-level reach.

## Who you actually want to meet

Ranked by closing potential within 90 days:

| Tier | Role | Why they matter | Where to find them |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Head of English / Head of Department (HoD)** at British / American / Cambridge-curriculum schools serving Y10–Y13 | They feel the pain (workload, marking turnaround, EAL writing quality) and can champion a pilot to the principal without needing the principal to "discover" us | Curriculum-design + AI-in-education panels; ed-tech booth row |
| 1 | **Deputy Head (Academic)** | Approves academic-pilots <£10k without board sign-off in most British international schools | Leadership Lounge if you can get in; principal/deputy panels |

| 3 | **Education group COO / CEO** (Qatar Foundation network, Doha British School chain, Maple Leaf, GEMS regional reps, ISP, Cognita, Nord Anglia) | Multi-school deal potential | Leadership Lounge exclusively |
| 3 | **MoEHE / Ministry of Education and Higher Education official** | Long-cycle but defines what Qatari national schools can buy | Ministry panel sessions; opening-day keynote |

**Do NOT** spend the two days primarily on parents and students — they convert at £29.99/year and won't repay the airfare. Spend the days on Tiers 1–3. Use the parent/student conversations as warm-ups between tier 1-3 conversations.

## Working the room — daily structure

**Day 1 (Sat 23 May):**
- 08:00–09:00 — booth setup, fact-check display copy, mount the QR code, test the live demo on iPad
- 09:00–11:00 — high-energy booth time (peak morning floor traffic)
- 11:00–11:30 — first walk of competitor booths (10 minutes for each of the 3 nearest competitors — listen to their pitch, note what works, note what doesn't)
- 11:30–13:00 — back to booth
- 13:00–14:00 — lunch + **work the food queue** (every principal has to eat; the queue is the cheapest networking in the building)
- 14:00–16:00 — booth time, target principals coming out of keynotes
- 16:00–17:00 — pick ONE workshop or panel to attend (preferably "AI in education" or "curriculum design") — ask one good question from the audience, your name + The English Hub goes on the record
- 17:00–19:00 — booth time + Leadership Lounge if you got the invite
- Evening — debrief: enter every business card into the spreadsheet (template in `02-discovery-questions.md`), send same-night LinkedIn connections to anyone with a "Tier 1/2" rating

**Day 2 (Sun 24 May):** same structure. By Day 2 you should have a Day-1 conversation list of "people to find again" — close the loop on these first. Day 2 afternoon is when serious follow-up scheduling happens because everyone is heading home Monday and wants to clear their plate.

## What to bring

- **Booth signage** — pull-up banner with: logo, "AI-marked GCSE & IGCSE English revision", QR code to /for-schools, "Founding cohort — 10 schools only — £4,000/year". One pull-up banner is plenty; two is overkill at a single-table booth.
- **Demo device** — iPad with a pre-seeded demo account showing a Year 11 student dashboard, an essay submission, AI marking output, and a parent progress report. Pre-loaded, offline-capable. Test the airport Wi-Fi cannot break it.
- **Phone with the live site bookmarked** — for "let me show you the actual signup flow" moments
- **Leave-behind one-pager** (`03-leave-behind-one-pager.md`) — print **200 copies**, A4, double-sided, on decent matte stock. Do not waste good paper on someone walking past at speed.
- **Pilot offer sheet** (`04-pilot-offer.md`) — print **30 copies**, A4 single-sided. Hand to anyone Tier 1–3 who shows genuine engagement.
- **Compliance one-pager** (`05-compliance-one-pager.md`) — print **30 copies**. Hand to anyone who asks any data/GDPR/PDPPL question, and to every Director of Learning Technology.
- **Business cards** — 300 minimum. Have something distinctive on them. Your full name + DPO email (`dpo@theenglishhub.app`) so school DPOs can write to the right address.
- **A notebook** — for handwritten notes you genuinely want to remember. The cards plus a quick scribble on the back beats any CRM app while you're standing.
- **Spreadsheet on phone** — for end-of-day lead entry. Template in `02-discovery-questions.md`.
- **Charger + power bank** — non-negotiable.
- **Refillable water bottle** — Doha in May is 38°C+; the AC inside Radisson is fine but you will dehydrate from talking all day.
- **Backup polo with logo** — Day 2 you will be glad you packed one.
- **Two-line printed elevator pitch** taped inside your business-card holder so when your brain goes blank at 16:30 on Day 2 you have anchor words to start from. Use the 15-second version from `01-elevator-pitches.md`.
