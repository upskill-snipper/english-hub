## Discovery questions — what to actually ask

Don't fire all of these. Pick the ones that fit the conversation. You're trying to **qualify**, not interrogate. Listen for 70% of the conversation; talk 30%.

### Qualifying (use first — never more than two of these)

1. **"Tell me about your school — which curriculum, what size, which year groups?"**
   — establishes whether they're in our addressable market at all.

2. **"What's your role there?"**
   — establishes their authority. Surprising how many "principals" are actually deputy heads or HoDs in disguise. Doesn't change how you treat them, but helps with the follow-up wording.

3. **"What brought you to this expo specifically?"**
   — tells you what they're actually shopping for. "AI in education" panel → AI angle. "Curriculum design" → curriculum-fit angle. Procurement → compliance angle.

### Pain discovery (use once you know it's a real school in our market)

4. **"Right now, what's the marking turnaround look like in the English department — fortnightly essay practice and how long until it lands back with the student?"**
   — this almost always elicits a real number (7–14 days) and a sigh. The sigh tells you they care.

5. **"For your Year 11 IGCSE cohort, what's the single biggest thing standing between current grade-7 students and a grade 9?"**
   — surfaces whether they think the gap is volume of practice (we solve), quality of feedback (we solve), or content knowledge (we contribute to). All three are good for us.

6. **"How much of your English-department time goes to EAL support specifically? What's the rough proportion of L1 Arabic speakers?"**
   — opens the Khaleeji bilingual + EAL track angle without you having to push it.

7. **"How is AI currently used in your school for English — if at all?"**
   — find out who they're already paying. Common answers: Magic School AI (US, free tier), Curipod, Quillbot, sometimes Century Tech.

### Procurement reality (use only with Tier 1–3)

8. **"How does your school typically decide on software at this price point? Is it a department-level call or does it need to go to the board?"**
   — find out the real decision path.

9. **"When is your software budget actually set — before the academic year, or rolling?"**
   — tells you when you need to be in front of them. Most international schools set budgets in May/June for September; you've missed that for this academic year, so the pilot angle (off-cycle) is the live one.

10. **"If a pilot proposal landed on your desk next week, who else would need to be in the room — IT, data protection, board?"**
    — surfaces the procurement stakeholders so the pilot proposal can be addressed to all of them, not just one.

### The closing question (use to end every Tier 1–3 conversation)

11. **"Would it be useful if I sent you our pilot terms after the expo, and we set up a 30-minute call to walk through whether your [specific year group] would be a good fit?"**

If they say yes → get the email written into the notebook AND on the back of their business card. Belt and braces.

## Red flags — when to politely move on

- They asked nothing back and are clearly just collecting swag — give the leave-behind, smile, move on, do NOT spend more than 90 seconds.
- They claim to be the principal but don't know their own student count — they're not. Lower-tier them.
- They start telling you why their school is the most important — listen for 30 seconds, hand the leave-behind, ask them a question that requires them to talk about your product not theirs.
- They are visibly representing a competitor — be cordial, exchange cards, you don't need to brief them on your roadmap.

## What to do with each tier at end-of-day

- **Tier 1/2**: same-day LinkedIn connection with a personalised note (template in `08-linkedin-followup.md`). Personalised follow-up email by Tuesday (`07-followup-emails.md`).
- **Tier 3**: same-day LinkedIn connection. Templated follow-up email by Wednesday.
- **Tier 4**: bulk welcome email with founder code by Friday.

Targets:
- **Tier 1/2 conversations**: 20–30 over two days
- **Tier 3 conversations**: 5–10 over two days
- **Pilot conversations escalated to a scheduled call within 14 days**: 5–8
- **Signed pilot terms within 30 days**: 2–3
