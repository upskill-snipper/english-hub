## 3. Pilot duration

**One term — approximately 8 to 12 weeks.** We scope the pilot to fit a natural half-term boundary at your school so the close-out review lands before your holiday calendar interferes.

**Indicative timeline:**
- Week 1: contract countersignature + 30-minute kickoff call + class setup + agreed success criteria signed off in writing
- Week 2: pilot live, weekly platform updates begin
- Week 4–6: mid-pilot review (30 minutes) — go/no-go check
- Week 8–12: close-out review (30 minutes) — pilot success-criteria assessment + annual deployment decision

## 4. Success criteria — to be agreed in writing in the kickoff call

We do not run open-ended evaluations. The criteria below are our defaults; we adapt them in the kickoff call to fit your school's specific situation. **If we miss them, you don't renew and you owe nothing beyond the pilot fee.**

| Metric | Default target | How we measure | What it tells you |
|---|---|---|---|
| Department adoption | ≥ 70% of teachers active by week 4 | Teacher login + active class data | The tool isn't sitting on the shelf |
| Marking turnaround for the practice cohort | from ≥ 7 days → under 1 hour median | Platform analytics + your existing data | The headline operational outcome |
| Student time-on-platform per week | ≥ 25 minutes per active student | Platform analytics | Students using it, not just signed up |
| Parent engagement with weekly emails | ≥ 40% open rate | Email analytics | The parent channel is working |
| Teacher NPS at close-out | ≥ +30 | Anonymous survey | The department wants to renew |

## 5. What [School name] commits to during the pilot

- Name **one school-side project owner** (typically Head of English or Deputy Head Academic) — approximately 60 minutes per week of attention
- 30-minute kickoff call in Week 1
- 30-minute mid-pilot review in Week 4–6
- 30-minute close-out review in the final week

That's everything. No teacher-training sessions to schedule. No "implementation consultant" fee. No integration project. The platform is designed to be self-service for any teacher who can use a web browser.

- **Bi-weekly platform updates** with at least one visibly improved feature per cycle, prioritising requests from Founding-cohort schools

## 7. What's NOT in the pilot fee

- **Sworn Modern Standard Arabic translation** of policy documents — we provide an informational MSA translation; sworn translation can be commissioned separately (~£500–£1,500)
- **Bespoke MIS integrations** (iSAMS, Engage, etc.) — scoped separately if required
- **On-site training visits** — the platform is self-service; if you want a half-day on-site, travel + £750

## 8. Annual deployment — if the pilot meets the agreed criteria

If the pilot meets the success criteria and you choose to continue, you convert to annual deployment at the **Founding Schools rate for the life of your licence**:

- **£4,000 per school per year** for whole-school access (Years 7–13 English)
- Standard price from August 2026: **£8,000/year**; you are grandfathered at £4,000
- **Custom pricing** for schools >1,000 students or multi-campus — typically from £6,000–£12,000/year mid-size, from £25,000/year large

## 9. Legal framework

- **One-page pilot agreement** — drafted and signed within 48 hours of your "yes". Not a 40-page MSA.
- **Data Processing Agreement** — our standard, fits UK GDPR + PDPPL. Available to your DPO on request before signature.
- **Termination at will** by either side with 7 days' notice during the pilot. Refund pro-rated to weeks delivered.
- **Governing law:** England and Wales for the pilot. We can switch to Qatar law for the annual deployment if your procurement requires it.
- **Liability:** capped at the pilot fee. Standard for our stage and the contract value.
