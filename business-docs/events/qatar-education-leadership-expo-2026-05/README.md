## Hard guardrails (read before the booth opens)

- ❌ **No invented testimonials.** No "trusted by [name]" quotes. No "we work with [school]".
- ❌ **No exam-board endorsement claims.** AQA / Edexcel / OCR / WJEC / Cambridge have not endorsed us. We *cover* their specifications.
- ❌ **No grade guarantees.** No "get a 9 in three weeks."
- ❌ **No fabricated user counts.** If asked "how many schools?", answer truthfully: "We're in the founding cohort phase, with capacity for 10 founding schools; this is one of the conversations that fills it."
- ❌ **No NIA certification claim.** We do not hold one (deferred).
- ❌ **No "Article 16 permit granted".** The dossier is shelf-ready; submission is held pending Qatari legal opinion. Honest framing only.
- ✅ **Compliance is the moat** — UK GDPR, ICO Children's Code, PDPPL Article 17 cross-border consent in signup, three v1.0 FINAL DPIAs, EU-region data residency, separately appointed DPO + DSL. These are facts. Use them.
- ✅ **Founder-led, launch-stage, transparent.** Schools respect honesty about stage more than they trust fake scale.

## Top 3 things to bring back from Doha

1. **A signed Founding-Schools pilot agreement** with at least one Qatar British/American school for Year 11/12 IGCSE English Language + Literature. £3,000–£4,000 pilot, one term, defined success criteria.
2. **5–10 qualified follow-up meetings** within 14 days post-event with named decision-makers (Principal / Deputy Head / Head of English).
3. **The procurement calendar** for each engaged school — when is their software budget set, who signs, what's the typical contract length, do they buy in-cycle or off-cycle.
