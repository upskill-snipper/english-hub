# Assignment of IP Agreement — Template

**Purpose:** Transfer all intellectual property rights from Upskill Energy Ltd to The English Hub Ltd. Also includes a back-fill template for freelancers who contributed IP without a written assignment.

**Disclaimer:** Starting-point template. Must be reviewed by a solicitor before signing. IP law interacts with tax, VAT and consideration — do not free-solo.

---

## Template A — Intercompany IP Assignment Deed (Upskill Energy → The English Hub)

> **DEED OF ASSIGNMENT OF INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY**
>
> **Dated:** [DATE]
>
> **Parties:**
>
> (1) **Upskill Energy Limited**, company number [CRN], registered office at [ADDRESS] (the "**Assignor**"); and
>
> (2) **The English Hub Limited**, company number [NEW CRN], registered office at [ADDRESS] (the "**Assignee**").
>
> **Background:**
>
> A. The Assignor has developed, commissioned or otherwise acquired certain intellectual property rights in connection with the operation of an English-language learning business under the brand "The English Hub" (the "**Business**").
>
> B. In connection with the transfer of the Business from the Assignor to the Assignee pursuant to an asset transfer agreement dated [DATE] (the "**ATA**"), the Assignor has agreed to assign to the Assignee all of its right, title and interest in and to the Intellectual Property Rights on the terms set out in this Deed.
>
> **It is agreed as follows:**
>
> **1. Definitions**
>
> 1.1 "**Intellectual Property Rights**" means, collectively:
>
> (a) all copyright and related rights in the software source code, object code, documentation, databases and data listed in Schedule 1;
>
> (b) all copyright and related rights in the content (including written materials, course content, video recordings, audio recordings, illustrations, photographs and music) listed in Schedule 1;
>
> (c) all rights in databases under the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 and the Copyright and Rights in Databases Regulations 1997;
>
> (d) the goodwill of the Business and the benefit of all rights associated with it;
>
> (e) the registered trademarks and pending applications listed in Schedule 2, and any unregistered trademark and passing-off rights associated with the brand "The English Hub";
>
> (f) the domain names listed in Schedule 3;
>
> (g) all know-how, trade secrets and confidential information relating to the Business;
>
> (h) any and all other intellectual property rights of whatever nature, whether registered or unregistered, arising out of or in connection with the Business, anywhere in the world; and
>
> (i) all rights of action in respect of past, present or future infringement of any of the rights listed in paragraphs (a) to (h).
>
> **2. Assignment**
>
> 2.1 The Assignor assigns to the Assignee absolutely, with full title guarantee and free from any encumbrance, all of its right, title and interest in and to the Intellectual Property Rights, together with all rights of action, remedies, and benefits arising from them, to hold to the Assignee absolutely for the full term of each right and any extension or renewal.
>
> 2.2 The assignment under clause 2.1 includes the right to claim damages and other remedies for any past, present or future infringement of the Intellectual Property Rights.
>
> **3. Consideration**
>
> 3.1 This Deed is entered into in consideration of the sum of £[AMOUNT] and the other promises set out in the ATA, receipt of which the Assignor acknowledges.
>
> **4. Further assurance**
>
> 4.1 The Assignor will, at the request and cost of the Assignee, execute and deliver such further documents and do such further acts as the Assignee may reasonably require to give effect to this Deed, including assisting with the recordal of the assignment at the UK Intellectual Property Office and any equivalent foreign registry.
>
> **5. Waiver of moral rights**
>
> 5.1 The Assignor unconditionally and irrevocably waives all moral rights in relation to the Intellectual Property Rights under Chapter IV of the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 and any similar rights in any jurisdiction.
>
> **6. Governing law**
>
> 6.1 This Deed and any dispute arising out of or in connection with it shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales.

## Template B — Freelancer IP back-fill assignment

Use this template when the IP audit identifies a freelancer who contributed code, content, design or other copyrighted work without a written IP assignment on file.

> **DEED OF ASSIGNMENT OF INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY**
>
> **Dated:** [DATE]
>
> **Parties:**
>
> (1) **[FREELANCER FULL LEGAL NAME]** of [ADDRESS] (the "**Assignor**"); and
>
> (2) **The English Hub Limited**, company number [CRN], registered office at [ADDRESS] (the "**Assignee**").
>
> **Background:**
>
> A. During the period from [START DATE] to [END DATE] the Assignor performed services for the Assignee (or its predecessor-in-title Upskill Energy Limited) in the capacity of [e.g. "contract software engineer", "course content writer", "illustrator"] and produced certain works described in Schedule 1 (the "**Works**").
>
> B. The parties wish to confirm in writing that all intellectual property rights in the Works are vested in the Assignee.
>
> **It is agreed as follows:**
>
> **1. Assignment**
>
> 1.1 The Assignor assigns to the Assignee absolutely, with full title guarantee, all existing and future copyright, database rights, design rights and any other intellectual property rights subsisting in the Works, together with all rights of action in respect of past, present or future infringement, to hold to the Assignee absolutely for the full term of those rights.
>
> **2. Waiver of moral rights**
>
> 2.1 The Assignor unconditionally and irrevocably waives all moral rights in the Works under Chapter IV of the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988.
>
> **3. Consideration**
>
> 3.1 The Assignee has paid the Assignor the sum of £[NOMINAL AMOUNT — £50 to £500 is typical] as consideration for this assignment, receipt of which the Assignor acknowledges.
>
> **4. Warranties**
>
> 4.1 The Assignor warrants that:
>
> (a) the Works are original to the Assignor and were created by the Assignor;
>
> (b) the Assignor has the full right and authority to assign the rights in the Works;
>
> (c) the Works do not infringe the intellectual property rights of any third party;
>
> (d) the Assignor has not previously assigned or licensed the rights in the Works to any third party.
>
> **5. Further assurance**
>
> 5.1 The Assignor will, at the reasonable request and cost of the Assignee, execute any further documents and take any further steps required to give effect to this Deed.
>
> **6. Governing law**
>
> 6.1 This Deed is governed by the laws of England and Wales.

### Practical notes on freelancer back-fills

- **Why pay nominal consideration?** Under English law a deed does not strictly require consideration, but paying a small amount (£50–£500) removes any doubt about whether the assignment is binding and makes the freelancer feel the exchange is fair. It also creates a paper trail (the bank transfer) that a buyer's solicitor can evidence.
- **Keep records.** Each assignment should have: the signed deed, a record of the payment, an email exchange confirming receipt, and a dated entry in the IP register.
- **What if the freelancer won't sign?** Get solicitor advice. Options include: paying more, rewriting the affected work from scratch, seeking a licence in lieu of assignment, or flagging the gap in DD disclosure and accepting a reduced price.

## What the IP Assignment is NOT

- It does **not** transfer software licences held by the Assignor (e.g. Figma, Adobe). Those are governed by the licence T&Cs and are usually handled through a change of billing entity on the provider's side — see `contracts-held-audit.md`.
- It does **not** transfer open-source licences — those continue to apply under their original terms.
- It does **not** transfer personal data (customer lists). Personal data sits under UK GDPR — the change of controller is handled via the privacy policy update and customer notification, not under IP assignment.
- It does **not** replace a properly-drafted Asset Transfer Agreement — the IP Assignment is a companion document that is executed alongside the ATA.
