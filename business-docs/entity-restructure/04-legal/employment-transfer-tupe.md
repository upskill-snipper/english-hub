# Employment Transfer and TUPE Considerations

**Purpose:** Identify whether TUPE applies, and if so, what must be done to transfer employees lawfully from Upskill Energy Ltd to The English Hub Ltd.

**If there are zero employees in the old entity: most of this document is informational only. Skip to "If there are no employees" at the bottom.**

**Disclaimer:** Employment law is a specialist area. If TUPE applies to this restructure, **the employment pack must be drafted and reviewed by an employment solicitor**, not a general corporate solicitor. TUPE claims can run to substantial awards.

---

## 1. Does TUPE apply?

The Transfer of Undertakings (Protection of Employment) Regulations 2006 ("TUPE") applies when an economic entity (a business or part of a business) transfers from one employer to another and retains its identity.

For an intercompany restructure like this one, TUPE almost certainly applies if:

- Employees of Upskill Energy Ltd are engaged in the trade being transferred (the English learning business)
- The trade continues substantially unchanged in the new entity
- The transfer is not a mere share transfer (TUPE does not apply to share sales, but this restructure is an asset transfer)

**In this restructure, if there are any employees in Upskill Energy Ltd who work on the English Hub business, assume TUPE applies and proceed accordingly. Getting it wrong is expensive.**

## 2. What TUPE does (in plain English)

If TUPE applies:

1. **Employees transfer automatically** from Upskill Energy Ltd (transferor) to The English Hub Ltd (transferee) on the transfer date. They don't sign new contracts — their existing contracts move with them, on the same terms.
2. **Continuity of service is preserved.** Length of service, accrued holiday, pension rights (with some exceptions), etc., all carry over.
3. **Terms and conditions cannot be worsened** for a reason related to the transfer. You can't use the transfer as an opportunity to cut salary, increase hours, remove benefits, etc.
4. **Dismissal because of the transfer is automatically unfair** unless there is an "Economic, Technical or Organisational" (ETO) reason entailing changes in the workforce — and ETO reasons are narrower than most people assume.
5. **The duty to inform and consult** kicks in, even if there is only one employee. You must inform, and in some cases consult with, employee representatives about the transfer.
6. **Liabilities transfer too.** Any pending claims (unfair dismissal, discrimination, unpaid wages) from the old employer transfer to the new employer. Due diligence matters.

## 3. The information and consultation obligation

Both the transferor and the transferee must inform affected employees (or their representatives) of:

- The fact that the transfer is to take place
- The proposed date of the transfer
- The reasons for the transfer
- The legal, economic and social implications of the transfer for the affected employees
- The measures the employer envisages taking in relation to the affected employees (or a statement that no measures are envisaged)

**Timing:** "Long enough before the transfer to enable consultation with representatives". There is no fixed statutory minimum for informing, but expect tribunal to look unkindly on anything less than 2–4 weeks for a planned restructure.

**Employee representatives:** if there is no recognised trade union and no existing representative body, you must arrange for employee representatives to be elected. For very small employers (fewer than 10 employees at the transferor), you can inform each employee directly instead of going via representatives. Verify the current threshold — it has been amended.

## 4. Documents to prepare (TUPE pack)

If TUPE applies:

- [ ] **Employee Liability Information (ELI)** — transferor must provide this to transferee at least 28 days before transfer. Includes: identity, ages, employment particulars, disciplinary/grievance history in the last 2 years, claims in the last 2 years, collective agreements.
- [ ] **Information letter to each affected employee** — covering the five required topics above.
- [ ] **Statement of Measures** — confirming what, if any, changes will be made. In this restructure the honest answer is usually "no measures — your role, location, salary and benefits all continue unchanged".
- [ ] **Transfer confirmation letter** — sent to each employee on the day of transfer, confirming that their employment has transferred and giving new employer contact details, bank details for payroll, emergency contacts, etc.
- [ ] **Updated payslip / payroll setup** — the new entity must be registered as an employer with HMRC and have a PAYE scheme live on or before the transfer date. See `05-hmrc/paye-registration.md`.
- [ ] **Pension arrangements continuity** — if there's an auto-enrolment pension scheme, the new employer must enrol employees at least as favourably. Check with the pension provider.

## 5. Special note — single-director companies with no other employees

In many early-stage restructures, the "employer" is the founder's own company and the founder is the sole director with no employment contract.

- If the founder is a **director only, not an employee**, TUPE does not apply — there's no employment relationship to transfer. Appoint the founder as a director of the new company and resign as director of the old one.
- If the founder has a **formal employment contract** with the old entity (e.g. paid a salary through PAYE), TUPE likely applies even to a one-person transfer. In that case: follow the TUPE pack, inform yourself as an employee (literally), and document the transfer.

**If unsure: ask the accountant whether the founder is currently on a PAYE scheme in the old entity. If yes, treat as a TUPE transfer of one employee.**

## 6. If there are no employees

Most common case for early-stage EdTech. Action list:

- [ ] Confirm in writing there are no employees (e.g. as a line in the board minutes approving the restructure)
- [ ] If founder is a director-only (not on PAYE), no TUPE steps are needed
- [ ] If freelancers are used: confirm they are genuinely self-employed (not disguised employees) — IR35 off-payroll rules can re-characterise a contractor as an employee for tax, though not for employment rights. Have each freelancer sign an IR35 confirmation as part of the restructure tidy-up.

## 7. Pensions

If there is an auto-enrolment scheme in the old entity:

- New employer must set up its own auto-enrolment scheme or inherit the existing one (some providers allow this with notice).
- Employee contributions and employer contributions cannot be reduced below statutory minimums.
- Notify the pension provider of the employer change at least 30 days before the transfer.

## 8. Common mistakes to avoid

1. **Failing to inform and consult.** This is the single most common TUPE breach. Penalty: up to 13 weeks' actual pay per affected employee. Easy to avoid — just do it.
2. **Treating the restructure as a chance to "modernise" contracts.** Changing terms for a transfer-related reason is automatically void. If new terms are genuinely needed, do it later and for a non-transfer reason.
3. **Assuming sole-director companies are exempt.** If there's a salaried founder, TUPE is in play.
4. **Missing the ELI deadline.** 28 days before transfer is a hard number. Start the packet in Week 2 if transfer is in Week 4.
5. **Not checking for collective agreements.** Unlikely in a small EdTech business, but if there is one, it transfers.
6. **Forgetting to transfer employment-related insurance (Employer's Liability).** New entity needs its own EL insurance before any employee starts work for it.
