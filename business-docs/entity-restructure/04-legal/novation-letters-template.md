# Novation Letters — Template

**Purpose:** Template for moving a contract from Upskill Energy Ltd (Outgoing Party) to The English Hub Ltd (Incoming Party) with the counterparty's consent.

**Important disclaimer:** This is a starting-point template to speed up conversations with a solicitor. **Do not use without a solicitor's review.** Novations of material contracts must be reviewed by a qualified lawyer who has read the underlying contract, because novation provisions can interact with indemnities, warranties and liability caps in ways that differ contract by contract.

---

## Key concept — novation vs. assignment

- **Assignment** transfers the benefit of a contract (the right to receive payment) but not the burden (the obligation to perform). Usually allowed unless the contract prohibits it. Does not require counterparty consent.
- **Novation** terminates the old contract between Old Party and Counterparty and replaces it with an identical new contract between New Party and Counterparty. Requires counterparty consent by definition. This is what we're doing — it's cleaner and gives the counterparty positive choice.

A novation agreement must be signed by **three** parties: Outgoing, Incoming, Counterparty.

## Template 1 — Short-form novation letter (B2B customer contract)

> **NOVATION LETTER**
>
> **Dated:** [DATE]
>
> **Parties:**
>
> (1) **Upskill Energy Limited**, a company incorporated in England and Wales with company number [CRN], whose registered office is at [ADDRESS] ("**Outgoing Party**");
>
> (2) **The English Hub Limited**, a company incorporated in England and Wales with company number [NEW CRN], whose registered office is at [ADDRESS] ("**Incoming Party**"); and
>
> (3) **[COUNTERPARTY LEGAL NAME]**, a company incorporated in [JURISDICTION] with company number [NUMBER], whose registered office is at [ADDRESS] ("**Counterparty**").
>
> **Background:**
>
> A. The Outgoing Party and the Counterparty entered into an agreement dated [DATE OF ORIGINAL CONTRACT] titled "[TITLE]" (the "**Contract**") under which the Outgoing Party provides [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF SERVICES].
>
> B. The Outgoing Party has restructured its operations and wishes to transfer all of its rights, benefits and obligations under the Contract to the Incoming Party, which is a wholly-owned operating company under the same ultimate ownership as the Outgoing Party.
>
> C. The Counterparty has agreed to accept the Incoming Party as a party to the Contract in place of the Outgoing Party.
>
> **It is agreed as follows:**
>
> **1. Novation**
>
> 1.1 With effect from [EFFECTIVE DATE] (the "**Effective Date**"), the Incoming Party is substituted for the Outgoing Party as a party to the Contract and the Contract shall be read and construed as if the Incoming Party had originally been named in place of the Outgoing Party.
>
> 1.2 With effect from the Effective Date, the Incoming Party assumes all rights, benefits, obligations and liabilities of the Outgoing Party under the Contract.
>
> 1.3 The Counterparty releases and discharges the Outgoing Party from any further obligations or liabilities under the Contract arising on or after the Effective Date, and accepts the Incoming Party in place of the Outgoing Party.
>
> **2. Prior liabilities**
>
> 2.1 Nothing in this deed affects any rights, obligations or liabilities of the Outgoing Party or the Counterparty which accrued before the Effective Date.
>
> **3. Continuing effect**
>
> 3.1 Except as set out in this deed, the Contract continues in full force and effect.
>
> **4. Governing law**
>
> 4.1 This deed and any dispute arising out of or in connection with it shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales. Each party submits to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.
>
> **Signed as a deed by the parties on the date stated at the start of this document.**
>

## Template 2 — Cover letter to the counterparty (the bit they read)

> **[DATE]**
>
> **[COUNTERPARTY CONTACT NAME]**
> **[COUNTERPARTY ORGANISATION]**
> **[ADDRESS]**
>
> Dear [NAME],
>
> **Re: Transfer of your contract with Upskill Energy Ltd to The English Hub Ltd**
>
> We're writing to let you know about an administrative restructure of our business, and to ask you to sign one document so your service continues seamlessly.
>
> **What's changing:** the company you've been contracting with, Upskill Energy Ltd, is being renamed and restructured to trade under the same brand you already know — **The English Hub**. As part of that restructure, we're moving your contract to a new, cleanly-named company: **The English Hub Ltd**.
>
> **What's NOT changing:**
>
> - The service itself, the team, the pricing and all your existing rights under the contract are unchanged.
> - The people you deal with are the same.
> - The bank details, invoicing and contact details will update at the same time and we'll send those to your finance team separately.
>
> **What we need from you:**
>
> Please sign the attached short novation deed and return a scanned copy to [EMAIL]. We've pre-signed on both sides, so it's just your signature we need.
>
> If your procurement or legal team would like to review the document, happy to answer any questions — our direct line is [PHONE] and [EMAIL].
>
> Thank you for your continued partnership — we're excited to continue working with you as The English Hub Ltd.
>
> Kind regards,
>
> [FOUNDER NAME]
> Director, The English Hub Ltd
> [EMAIL] | [PHONE]

## Template 3 — Minimal novation for low-value / volume supplier contracts

For small SaaS tools and low-value suppliers where a full tripartite deed is overkill, some counterparties will accept a simpler written confirmation. Check terms and conditions first — most SaaS T&Cs permit assignment by the customer to an affiliate or group company without consent.

> **Subject: Change of billing entity — [ACCOUNT NAME]**
>
> Hi [SUPPLIER SUPPORT TEAM],
>
> We've undergone an internal restructure. The billing entity on our account [ACCOUNT ID / EMAIL] needs to change from:
>
> - **Old entity:** Upskill Energy Ltd, company number [CRN]
> - **New entity:** The English Hub Ltd, company number [NEW CRN]
>
> The new billing address and VAT number are:
>
> - Address: [REGISTERED OFFICE]
> - VAT: [VRN]
>
> Please confirm the change has been applied. Service, plan, usage and login details remain unchanged.
>
> Many thanks,
> [FOUNDER]

## Tips for managing a batch of novations

1. **Prioritise by value.** Top 10 customers by ARR first. Enterprise contracts with change-of-control clauses get the most legal care.
2. **Send in waves, not all at once.** Wave 1: top 10 customers. Wave 2: next 40. Wave 3: long tail.
3. **Track counter-signatures** in a simple spreadsheet: sent / acknowledged / signed.
4. **Set a deadline** in the cover letter ("please return by [DATE] so we can keep your service uninterrupted").
5. **Plan for non-responders.** For any contract where you get silence, talk to the solicitor about whether passive continuation + sweeping payments counts as novation by conduct, or whether a forced termination and re-signing is needed. Different answers for different contracts.
6. **Keep originals.** Scan, then file the paper originals — novations may be needed during DD.

## Red flags to escalate to the solicitor

- Counterparty lawyer wants to renegotiate terms on the back of the novation
- Counterparty refuses to sign
- Contract has a "no assignment, no novation" clause
- Change-of-control clause gives counterparty a termination right
- Contract has a specific performance obligation that only the Outgoing Party can perform
- Contract has personal guarantees from the founder that need to be released and re-issued
