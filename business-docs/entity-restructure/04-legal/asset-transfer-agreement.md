# Asset Transfer Agreement (ATA) — Template

**Purpose:** Transfer the trade and assets of Upskill Energy Ltd (Seller) to The English Hub Ltd (Buyer). This is the backbone document of the restructure.

**Disclaimer:** Draft template only. **Must** be reviewed and finalised by a solicitor before signing. This is the document a buyer's due diligence lawyer will read first at exit — get it right.

---

## What an ATA does

An Asset Transfer Agreement (sometimes called an Asset Purchase Agreement or APA) moves specific assets and liabilities from one company to another. It is different from:

- A **share sale** — sale of shares in the company itself, which transfers everything
- An **IP assignment** — narrow to IP only
- A **novation** — transfer of a single contract with counterparty consent

In this restructure, the ATA moves the **bulk** of assets and goodwill; the **IP Assignment Deed** is usually a companion document for the IP specifically; and **novations** handle individual customer and supplier contracts that don't transfer automatically.

>
> 6.1 The Seller and the Buyer shall cooperate to obtain the consent of each third-party counterparty to the novation of each Contract to the Buyer as soon as reasonably practicable.
>

> **7. Warranties**
>
> 7.1 The Seller warrants to the Buyer that, as at the Transfer Date:
>
> (a) the Seller is the legal and beneficial owner of the Assets, free from encumbrances;
>
> (b) the Seller has the full power and authority to enter into this agreement and to transfer the Assets;
>
> (c) so far as the Seller is aware, the Assets are not subject to any pending or threatened claim or dispute;
>
> (d) so far as the Seller is aware, the Business has been operated in material compliance with applicable laws; and
>
> (e) the information provided by the Seller to the Buyer in the schedules is accurate in all material respects.
>
> 7.2 The Seller's liability under the warranties is capped at the Consideration.
>
> **8. Further assurance**
>
> 8.1 The Seller shall execute and deliver such further documents and do such further acts as the Buyer may reasonably require to perfect the transfer of the Assets.
>
> **9. Books and records**
>
> 9.1 The Seller shall deliver the Records to the Buyer on the Transfer Date. The Buyer shall make the Records available to the Seller on reasonable notice to the extent the Seller needs them to comply with its remaining tax, accounting or regulatory obligations.
>
> **10. Data protection**
>
> 10.1 The parties acknowledge that the transfer of the Customer Database involves a change of data controller for UK GDPR purposes. The Buyer shall notify affected data subjects of the change and update the privacy notice accordingly. The Seller shall continue to comply with UK GDPR in relation to any personal data it retains for legal or tax reasons.
>
> **11. Costs**
>
> 11.1 Each party shall bear its own costs in connection with this agreement.
>
> **12. Governing law and jurisdiction**
>
> 12.1 This agreement and any dispute arising out of or in connection with it shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales. The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.

> [NAME], Director

### Schedules

- **Schedule 1** — Equipment (tangible assets: laptops, monitors, hardware)
- **Schedule 2** — Contracts being transferred (cross-reference to `contracts-held-audit.md`)
- **Schedule 3** — Domain names
- **Schedule 4** — Assumed Liabilities
- **Schedule 5** — Excluded Assets (anything staying behind — e.g. pre-transfer debtors/cash, legacy liabilities)
- **Schedule 6** — Consideration allocation (goodwill / IP / customer list / stock / equipment)
- **Schedule 7** — Employees (or "None" if zero)

## Practical notes

### Consideration allocation matters

Allocating consideration across asset categories has tax consequences for both sides. Goodwill and customer lists are generally treated as "intangibles" in the Seller's books and disposal may generate taxable profit; IP depending on its nature may fall into different CT regimes. **Talk to the accountant about this BEFORE signing.**

### VAT TOGC

The ATA explicitly invokes TOGC treatment. For TOGC to apply, all of the following typically must be true:
- A business is being transferred as a going concern
- The business is continued by the transferee
- The transferee is VAT-registered (or liable to be)
- The assets transferred are used in carrying on the same kind of business
- There is no significant break in trading

See `05-hmrc/vat-togc-notes.md`.

### Cash movement

If the consideration is paid in cash, the Buyer needs that cash first — usually injected by the shareholders or borrowed. For a restructure where the Buyer has no cash yet, the intercompany loan route is usually cleaner. When the old entity is wound down, the loan is released. Releasing the loan may have CT consequences — check.

### Don't sign until...

- Accountant has confirmed TOGC treatment
- Solicitor has reviewed the draft
- Valuation memo is on file
- IP Assignment Deed is ready to sign at the same time
- Both boards have approved in minutes
- Schedule 2 (contracts) is accurate
