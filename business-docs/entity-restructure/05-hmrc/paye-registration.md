# PAYE Registration — The English Hub Ltd

**Purpose:** If the new entity will employ anyone — including paying the founder a salary — it must operate PAYE. This guide walks through when to register, how, and how to close the old scheme.

---

## When PAYE is required

Register for PAYE if any of the following are true for the new entity:

- You employ anyone (including yourself, as a director) at **£[LEL threshold]** per week or more (verify current Lower Earnings Limit, around £123/week as of recent years)
- Any employee has another job
- Any employee gets expenses and benefits
- You pay anyone at or above the National Insurance threshold
- You run a directors' salary through payroll

**Common case for small founder-led businesses:** the founder pays themselves a small salary (typically just above the NI threshold) for tax efficiency, which requires running PAYE even for one person.

**If the founder takes only dividends and no salary:** PAYE is not needed. But this has consequences for state pension contributions, maternity/paternity rights, and is a tax-planning choice rather than a legal one. Discuss with the accountant.

## If there are no employees and founder takes dividends only

Skip this document. Do not register for PAYE. Note the decision in the restructure plan with the accountant's written sign-off.

## Registering for PAYE

### How

- Online at `https://www.gov.uk/register-employer`
- Through the accountant as the business's PAYE agent
- Need to have Government Gateway access for the new entity already set up

### Information needed

- Company name, CRN
- Registered office
- Business type (limited company)
- Date of first payday
- Number of employees (including directors)
- Accountant's agent code (if using an accountant)
- Contact details

### What you receive

- PAYE reference (e.g. 123/AB45678)
- Accounts Office reference (used for payments)
- Welcome pack and activation code

### Timing

- Apply **no more than 2 months before** the first payday
- Activation takes a few days to arrive in the post
- Have the scheme live **before** the first payday

**Critical for restructure:** if transferring employees from the old entity under TUPE, the new PAYE scheme must be live on or before the Transfer Date so that the first payroll run in the new entity can happen on schedule.

## Running payroll

### Software

Must be HMRC-recognised RTI (Real Time Information) compatible software:
- Xero Payroll
- QuickBooks Payroll
- BrightPay
- Moneysoft
- KashFlow Payroll
- Free HMRC Basic PAYE Tools (only for under 10 employees)

### Cadence

- Run payroll on the normal pay date
- Submit Full Payment Submission (FPS) to HMRC **on or before** payday
- Submit Employer Payment Summary (EPS) if there are adjustments or you're claiming Employment Allowance

### Payment to HMRC

- Monthly by 22nd of the following month (online), 19th (post)
- Quarterly if PAYE + NI bill is under £1,500/month on average

### Employment Allowance

If eligible, claim Employment Allowance (currently **£[verify current amount]** — around £5,000/year as of recent years) which reduces the employer's NI bill. Exclusions: single-director companies where the director is the sole employee are **not** eligible. If the founder is the only person on PAYE, do not claim EA.

## Auto-enrolment pension

If you have employees (other than a sole-director founder), auto-enrolment pension obligations apply. In summary:

1. Choose a pension provider (NEST, Smart Pension, People's Pension are common low-cost options)
2. Assess each employee to see if they must be enrolled
3. Enrol eligible employees
4. Deduct and contribute the required percentages (employee min 5%, employer min 3%, verify current rates)
5. Declare compliance to The Pensions Regulator
6. Re-declare every 3 years

**Director-only companies** where the director has no employment contract are exempt. Director-only companies where the director IS on a PAYE employment contract are borderline and should be checked with the pension provider.

## Closing the old entity's PAYE scheme

When the old entity stops employing anyone:

### Step 1: Final Full Payment Submission

On the last payday for any remaining employees in the old entity, submit the FPS with the "Leaver" indicator and "Final submission because scheme ceased" ticked.

### Step 2: Notify HMRC

Use HMRC's online service to notify that the PAYE scheme has ceased, giving the cessation date.

### Step 3: Settle final PAYE / NI

Pay any final PAYE and NI for the last pay period.

### Step 4: Issue P45s

Each employee transferring to the new entity receives a P45 from the old employer and is taken on as a "new starter" by the new employer — even though under TUPE their continuous employment is preserved.

### Step 5: Confirm closure

Wait for HMRC to confirm the scheme is closed. Retain the confirmation for DD.

## Moving one person between schemes (founder-only case)

If the only person on PAYE is the founder, and the founder will continue to be the only person on PAYE in the new entity, the process is:

1. Register new PAYE scheme before Transfer Date.
2. Run final payroll in old scheme on or before Transfer Date — founder gets a P45.
3. Run first payroll in new scheme on or after Transfer Date — founder is a "new starter" with the P45.
4. Close old PAYE scheme after final RTI submission.

**Continuity-of-employment point:** even in a one-person transfer, if the founder is on an employment contract, this is legally a TUPE transfer. See `04-legal/employment-transfer-tupe.md`.
