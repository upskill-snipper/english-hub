# Customer Notification — Letter / Email Template

**Purpose:** Tell customers about the entity change without making them worry. The tone is **routine admin**, not **crisis management**. Customers should read the email, think "ah, neat", and move on.

---

## Positioning principles

1. **Do not apologise.** There is nothing to apologise for. Apologies imply something went wrong.
2. **Lead with continuity, not change.** "Your service, login, content and pricing are unchanged."
3. **Explain in one sentence.** "We're consolidating under a single company name that matches our brand."
4. **Tell them what to do.** For most customers: nothing. For finance-team contacts: update a vendor record.
5. **Give a clear point of contact.** One email address that goes to a human who can answer questions.
6. **Don't name the old entity more than once.** Mention it for completeness, then move on.
7. **Time the send correctly.** Before invoices / debits show the new name, not after.

## Template 1 — B2C customer email (mass-send)

**Subject:** A quick note about our company name

**Preview text:** Nothing is changing about your account or subscription.

---

Hi [FIRST NAME],

A quick, tidy-up note from The English Hub.

As of [DATE], the company behind The English Hub will be called **The English Hub Ltd**. Previously, the legal entity running things was called Upskill Energy Ltd — a legacy name from a previous venture that no longer reflects what we do.

**What changes for you:** nothing, except the company name on your invoices and on our Terms of Service / Privacy Policy.

**What stays exactly the same:**
- Your login, password and progress
- Your subscription and billing
- The price you pay
- The team, the content and the support you already get
- Our commitment to you as a learner

**What to do:** nothing is needed from you.

**Where to find the update:**
- Our Terms of Service: [URL]
- Our Privacy Policy: [URL]
- Our updated company details on the website footer

If you have any questions, just reply to this email or contact us at [SUPPORT EMAIL] — a real person will get back to you within [TIMEFRAME].

Thank you for being part of The English Hub. Back to learning English!

[FOUNDER NAME]
Founder, The English Hub Ltd

## Template 4 — Short FAQ page to link to

**Q: Why is the company name changing?**
Our previous legal entity had a legacy name ("Upskill Energy Ltd") from an earlier venture. We've moved the business to a new legal entity whose name matches our brand: **The English Hub Ltd**.

**Q: Is the business being sold or acquired?**
No. This is an internal restructure under the same ownership.

**Q: Does anything change about my subscription?**
No. Your login, your access, your content, your progress, and your pricing are all unchanged.

**Q: Does anything change about my invoices / billing?**
Invoices will be issued in the name of The English Hub Ltd with a new company registration number and VAT number. The amount, frequency and payment method are unchanged.

**Q: Do I need to do anything?**
Nothing, unless your finance team needs to update their vendor records — in which case, new details are here: [LINK].

**Q: Is my data safe?**
Yes. We've updated our Privacy Policy to reflect the new data controller (The English Hub Ltd). Your rights under UK GDPR are exactly the same.

**Q: Who can I contact if I have questions?**
Email [SUPPORT EMAIL] — a real person will reply.

## Send timing

1. **Internal freeze (1 week before send):** no edits to ToS, Privacy Policy, footers, invoices. Stage everything.
2. **Send (T-0):** one mass email to B2C, individual letters to B2B.
3. **Update live assets (T+0, same day):** ToS, Privacy Policy, website footer, invoice template.
4. **First invoice from new entity (T+1 to T+7):** customers get the new company name on their first invoice after seeing the email. No surprise.
5. **Social media post (T+0 or T+1):** short public version for transparency.

## Do NOT include in customer comms

- The words "restructure", "reorganisation" unless necessary — they sound corporate and stressful to B2C users
- Any mention of exit preparation, investors, or buyers
- Any apology
- Legal jargon ("novation", "assignment", "ATA") in the B2C email — save those for the B2B letter
- Any hint of cashflow, tax or strategy reasoning — customers don't care and shouldn't have to

## Data protection disclosure requirements

Under UK GDPR Articles 13/14, you must inform data subjects when the controller of their personal data changes. The B2C email above satisfies this obligation when paired with an updated Privacy Policy that names the new controller. Keep the sent date on file as evidence of compliance.
