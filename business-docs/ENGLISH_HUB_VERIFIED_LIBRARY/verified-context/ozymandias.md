# Ozymandias -- Verified Context
Author: Percy Bysshe Shelley (1792-1822). Romantic poet. PUBLIC DOMAIN.
Published: The Examiner, 11 January 1818.

Historical context: Inspired by a fragment of a statue of Ramesses II. Written as a sonnet competition with his friend Horace Smith.

Literary context: Petrarchan sonnet (14 lines, ABABACDCEDEFEF). Three narrators (I / traveller / Ozymandias himself), each framing the next. Central irony: power is temporary, art (the sculptor's work) outlasts it.

Themes: Power and its limitations; the transience of human achievement; the hubris of rulers; art vs. power.

Exam: Edexcel IGCSE Anthology / AQA P&C / Cambridge IGCSE Songs of Ourselves.
Compare (AQA): My Last Duchess, London, Checking Out Me History.
