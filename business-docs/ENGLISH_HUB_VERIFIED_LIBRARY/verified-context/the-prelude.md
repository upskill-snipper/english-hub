# The Prelude (Boat Stealing) -- Verified Context
Author: William Wordsworth (1770-1850). Romantic poet. Poet Laureate 1843. PUBLIC DOMAIN.
VERSION NOTE: AQA = 1850 Prelude. OCR = 1799 Prelude. DIFFERENT TEXTS.

Historical context: Autobiographical. Lake District childhood. Father died 1783 leaving him a ward of nature. Based on real incident at Ullswater, Lake District.

Literary context: Blank verse (unrhymed iambic pentameter). 'Led by her' = Nature as female guide (Romantic). Movement: confidence > terror > lasting psychological disturbance. Sublime: the 'huge peak' embodies the Romantic Sublime -- nature overwhelming the human.

Themes: Power of nature; human insignificance; guilt; self-realisation; the Romantic sublime.

Exam: AQA P&C. Compare: Storm on the Island (nature as threat), Exposure (nature overpowers).
