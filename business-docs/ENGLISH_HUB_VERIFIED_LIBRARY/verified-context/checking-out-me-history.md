# Checking Out Me History -- Verified Context
Author: John Agard (b.1949, Georgetown, Guyana). Came to UK 1977. Published Half-Caste and Other Poems (Hodder, 2007).

Historical context: Inspired by a school textbook claiming West Indian history began with Columbus. Agard's education centred on British colonial history while erasing Black historical figures.

Literary context: Two fonts: standard = colonial history imposed on the speaker; italic = Black history reclaimed. The structural contrast IS the argument. Caribbean Creole dialect 'Dem' is intentional, not an error.

Figures mentioned: Toussaint L'Ouverture (Haitian Revolution), Nanny of the Maroons (Jamaica), Mary Seacole (nurse).

CRITICAL: This is in the P&C anthology -- NOT Love & Relationships.

Themes: Identity; colonial history; power; resistance; cultural heritage.

Exam: AQA P&C. Compare: The Emigree (identity), Tissue (layers of identity).
