# Drummer Hodge -- Verified Context
Author: Thomas Hardy (1840-1928). Published Poems of the Past and the Present (1901). PUBLIC DOMAIN.

Historical context: Second Boer War, South Africa, 1899-1902. NOT World War One.
'Drummer Hodge' = a young farm boy from Wessex (Hardy's fictional Dorset) serving as a drummer boy.

Literary context: 3 stanzas. Regular ABABAB rhyme. Foreign words (kopje, veldt, Karoo) emphasise alien environment. Final stanza: transformation -- the foreign stars become 'his' eternally.

Themes: Anonymity in war; far-from-home death; nature; mortality; unexpected peace in death.
