# Exposure -- Verified Context
Author: Wilfred Owen (1893-1918). Served on Western Front 1916-18. Killed 4 November 1918. PUBLIC DOMAIN.
Published posthumously 1920.

Historical context: Western Front winter 1917-18. Owen was shell-shocked and recovering at Craiglockhart when he wrote this.

Literary context: 8 stanzas of 5 lines (quintains). Near-rhyme scheme (ABBAC). Refrain 'But nothing happens.' appears throughout. Nature (wind, snow, frost) is as deadly as the enemy. Personification of dawn as a military force.

CRITICAL: 'knive' (Line 1) IS correct -- it is used as a verb.

Themes: Futility of war; nature as the enemy; psychological suffering; loss of faith.

Exam: AQA P&C. Compare: Bayonet Charge, Charge of the Light Brigade.
