# The English Hub — Elevator Pitches

Five pitches, five audiences. Each one is designed to be spoken out loud, not read. Memorise the 10-second and 30-second versions word-for-word. The longer ones should feel like a conversation, not a recital.

Rule of thumb: **every pitch ends with an ask.** If the listener doesn't know what to do next, the pitch has failed.

---

## 1. The 10-second pitch — for a journalist (at an event, in a lift, on a panel)

> "The English Hub is an AI English tutor that marks GCSE essays in 30 seconds against the real mark scheme — for £9.99 a month."

**Ask:** "Would it be useful if I sent you a one-page fact sheet?"

## Notes for delivery

- **Pace:** Do not rush. Count to one after every full stop.
- **Numbers:** Only quote numbers that are in `04-boilerplate/key-statistics.md`. If it's not in the doc, don't say it out loud.
- **Jargon:** Never say "AI-powered personalised learning journey." Say "the app marks essays in under a minute."
- **Ending:** Every pitch ends with a single, concrete ask. Do not list three.
- **Rehearsal:** Read every pitch out loud once a week until it's unconscious.
