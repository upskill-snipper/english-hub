# EdTech Podcasts — Target List

**Purpose:** the podcast list the founder uses for long-form interviews, founder stories, and EdTech thought leadership.
**Compiled:** April 2026 via Feedspot, Apple Podcasts, publication websites, and direct research.
**Rule:** a podcast appearance is an 8-to-12 week process end-to-end. Start pitching well before you need the exposure.

---

## Why podcasts matter for The English Hub

- **Teachers and school leaders listen to podcasts on the commute.** One long-form interview on the right podcast is worth more than 20 trade-press mentions.
- **Podcasts favour the founder's voice.** If the founder is a good storyteller (our origin story is built to be told out loud — see `03-founder-story/origin-story.md`), this is our highest-leverage channel.
- **Evergreen reach.** A good episode keeps delivering listens, searches, and inbound leads for 12–18 months after airing.

## How to use this list

1. **Listen to at least one episode** before pitching. Do not pitch a podcast you have not heard.
2. **Pitch the angle, not the company.** "I'd like to come on and talk about the private-tutoring gap" beats "I'd like to promote my app."
3. **Lead with what the audience learns.** A podcast host needs to see a teaching-useful takeaway, not a pitch.
4. **Offer data.** Hosts love founder guests who bring one proprietary stat the audience has never heard.
5. **Track outreach in `09-tracking/press-outreach-log.csv`.**

## Pitch template

Use this as the starting point for every podcast pitch, and personalise:

>
> Hi [HOST FIRST NAME],
>
> I really enjoyed your episode with [SPECIFIC GUEST FROM RECENT EPISODE], especially the bit where [SPECIFIC TAKEAWAY]. It made me think you might find this interesting.
>
> I'm [FOUNDER NAME], founder of The English Hub. We mark students' GCSE English essays in under 30 seconds against the official AQA, Edexcel, OCR, WJEC and Cambridge IGCSE mark schemes — the work that typically takes a teacher a week. We've marked [X,XXX] essays to date and work with [XX] schools across the UK and the Gulf.

> I'd love to come on the show and talk about the private-tutoring gap — why the students who need fast essay feedback most are the ones least likely to get it, what we learned building the first AI that can mark to a real mark scheme, and what teachers tell us about which parts of essay feedback actually move grades.
>
> Happy to record any time that suits — I'll work around your schedule.
>
> [FOUNDER NAME]
> theenglishhub.app

## Anti-patterns

- Pitching every podcast on the list in one day. Do three per week, well.
- Going on a podcast without listening first.
- Talking only about the product. Good guests tell stories and share surprising stats.
- Reading from a script on air.
- Not sending the episode to your own list on the day it airs. That's a 2x multiplier wasted.
