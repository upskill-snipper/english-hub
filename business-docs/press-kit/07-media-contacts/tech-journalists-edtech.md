# EdTech and Startup Tech Journalists — Target List

**Purpose:** the target list for tech and EdTech-specialist press, covering UK, Europe and global EdTech news.
**Compiled:** April 2026 via desk research (Sifted, EdSurge, HolonIQ, Business Insider, TechCrunch, EdWeek Market Brief, UK Tech News, Tech.eu, Muck Rack, journalists' public profiles).
**Rule:** every name here is verified externally before outreach. Email addresses are **not stored in this file**.

---

## How to use this list

- Use tech-press contacts for **funding, partnerships, product launches, and market stories.** Not for pedagogy features — those belong on the education-journalists list.
- Keep the pitch short (under 150 words) and lead with the number.
- If your story is funding, always include the lead investor's firm name in the subject line — it is the single most reliable open-rate hack on tech desks.
- UK tech coverage has thinned since 2025 (see note below); broaden your net to include European publications where possible.

## Key publication profiles (for background)

### Sifted
Europe's leading startup publication, based in London and backed by the Financial Times. Covers funding, product launches, and founder stories. The default destination for a UK EdTech funding announcement.

### EdSurge
US-based EdTech industry publication, widely read by European founders. Tony Wan is the managing editor and has written extensively on EdTech startup funding, and Emily Tate Sullivan leads K–12 coverage. Less likely to cover a UK-only launch unless there is an international angle.

### HolonIQ
Not strictly press — HolonIQ is a global education intelligence company that produces the annual Global Education Outlook (the 2026 edition has 160+ pages on AI adoption, skills alignment, and capital conditions) and maintains the Global EdTech 1000 list. They are a credible source for third-party market stats and a useful endorsement target rather than a media target.

### Tech.eu
European tech business news. Covers edtech deals regularly; ran extensive coverage of Emerge Education's $73M Fund II. Good for continental exposure.

### EducationInvestor
Trade title aimed at the investor community within education. The right home for funding, M&A, and partnership stories.

## Important: TechCrunch Europe

TechCrunch Europe effectively shut down its European operations in mid-2025, with Mike Butcher (Editor-at-large) leaving after 18 years and Natasha Lomas (senior reporter) moving to Euractiv. **Do not pitch TechCrunch Europe as if it still operates as a standalone desk.** If you need TechCrunch coverage, pitch the central US desk and be prepared for a slower response.

## Pitching tips by publication

- **Sifted:** Data and traction sell. Include MRR/ARR, growth rate, and LTV/CAC. Europe angle mandatory.
- **EdSurge:** Pedagogy angle sells. Lead with the teaching outcome, not the tech.
- **Tech.eu:** Deal size matters. A sub-£500k round is unlikely to clear the bar.
- **UKTN:** UK-first stories. Good for a "first UK EdTech raise this quarter" angle.
- **EducationInvestor:** Deal structure and use-of-proceeds matter most. Be specific.
