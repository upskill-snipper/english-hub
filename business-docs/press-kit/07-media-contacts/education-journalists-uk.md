# UK Education Journalists — Target List

**Purpose:** the prioritised contact list for pitching The English Hub to UK education desks.
**Last compiled:** April 2026 via desk research (Muck Rack, publication websites, Roxhill Media, Prowly, CIPR Education Journalism Awards, and journalists' own public profiles).
**Rule:** every contact below is verified externally before outreach. Email addresses, phone numbers, and DMs are **not** stored in this file — store them privately in the CRM and add each contact here by name, publication and beat only.

## How to use this list

1. **Personalise every pitch.** Reference a specific article they wrote in the last 30 days.
2. **Pitch the right beat.** A general news reporter will not cover an EdTech launch — aim at education correspondents, not politics reporters.
3. **Short first emails.** 120 words or fewer. Lead with the number, not the company.
4. **Respect the embargo.** Never break a stated embargo, even by 5 minutes.
5. **Log every outreach in `09-tracking/press-outreach-log.csv`.** One row per pitch, updated when you get a reply.
6. **Re-verify annually.** UK education desks churn — always double-check a journalist is still in the same role before pitching.

## Notes on beats and timing

- **Best days to pitch:** Tuesday and Wednesday. Mondays are too busy with weekend-news follow-ups; Thursdays and Fridays are for long lead times.
- **Best months for back-to-school stories:** August (results day) and September (new term).
- **Best months for revision / tutoring stories:** January ("100 days to the exam"), February, March, April.
- **Worst months:** July (end of term), August (national papers skeleton-staffed, *except* on results day), December.
- **Results day** (late August) is the single biggest opportunity every year for a GCSE English story. Plan the pitch 6 weeks in advance.

## A note on accuracy

The names above are drawn from public sources. Role-holders change: the Telegraph's education editor has rotated since 2022, TechCrunch Europe has shed staff, and BBC education team personnel shift. Before sending any pitch, re-verify the current occupant of each role through:

- The publication's own "Meet the Team" or masthead page.
- Muck Rack or RoxHill for the most recent byline dates.
- The journalist's own Twitter/X or LinkedIn profile.

Contacts marked `[IN BRACKETS]` are roles we know exist but whose current occupant must be confirmed at the time of outreach. Do not pitch an unverified name.

## Anti-patterns

- Mass BCC to 20 journalists. They will see through it instantly.
- Pitching a Schools Week political reporter on a product launch. Wrong beat.
- Pitching the Guardian education desk on a Friday afternoon for a Monday piece.
- Quoting an internal number that is not in `04-boilerplate/key-statistics.md`.
