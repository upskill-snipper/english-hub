# The English Hub — Company Boilerplate

Three lengths. The paragraph that goes at the end of every press release is the 150-word version. Use the 50-word version for bylines and tight space, and the 300-word version for feature pieces where the reporter needs more colour.

All three versions are kept factually in sync with `../01-fact-sheet/fact-sheet.md` and `key-statistics.md`. If you change a number in one of those files, update this one on the same day.

---

## 50-word boilerplate
*For author bylines, ad bios, conference programme listings.*

> The English Hub is an AI-powered GCSE and IGCSE English revision platform that marks student essays in 30 seconds against the official mark schemes of every major UK exam board. Founded in 2025. More at theenglishhub.app.

*(36 words — pad only if required by a template.)*

Learn more at theenglishhub.app.

## 300-word boilerplate
*For feature pieces, investor reports, long-form coverage.*

> The English Hub is the AI-powered GCSE and IGCSE English revision platform built to close the private-tutoring gap. Founded in 2025 in the United Kingdom by [FOUNDER NAME], the company was started after [he / she / they] spent [X] years [teaching / tutoring] English and watching students wait a week to get essays marked — by which point the feedback was too late to influence the next draft. The students who got around that problem all had private tutors, at £40 to £80 per hour. The English Hub gives every student the same loop — rapid, specific, mark-scheme-aligned feedback on their own writing — for £9.99 a month.
>
> The platform combines five products in one subscription. Exam-board-specific courses cover every major set text and skill for AQA, Edexcel, OCR, WJEC and Cambridge IGCSE. An AI essay marker reads student responses in under 30 seconds and returns a grade, an assessment-objective breakdown, and a rewritten model sentence drawn from the student's own work. Interactive revision games — Grade Climber, Quote Detective, Theme Matcher, Speed Analysis, Comprehension Challenge and others — turn quote memorisation and exam technique into short daily sessions. Timed mock exams auto-mark and generate a "what to revise next" plan. And teacher and parent dashboards give schools and families a clear view of progress by set text, assessment objective and exam board.
>
> The English Hub is used by [X,XXX] students and [XX] schools across the UK and the Gulf region. It is [bootstrapped / seed-funded], generates revenue from both direct consumer subscriptions (£9.99 per month) and school licences (from £199 per year), and is free to students on free school meals verified via their school. More at theenglishhub.app. Press enquiries: press@theenglishhub.app.

## How to fill the placeholders

Before using any version externally, replace:

| Placeholder | Where to get it |
|---|---|
| `[X,XXX] students` | `key-statistics.md` — current monthly active students |
| `[XX] schools` | `key-statistics.md` — schools in trial or partnership |
| `[FOUNDER NAME]` | `03-founder-story/founder-bio.md` |
| `[X] years` | Founder bio |
| `[he / she / they]` | Founder bio |
| `[bootstrapped / seed-funded]` | `01-fact-sheet/fact-sheet.md` — Funding section |

Do not quote numbers that are not in the fact sheet or key-statistics file. If a journalist asks for a number that is not in those two files, refer them to the founder by email.

## Update rhythm

- The **50-word** boilerplate almost never changes.
- The **150-word** boilerplate is updated whenever student or school counts change by more than 20%, or whenever the funding status changes.
- The **300-word** boilerplate is updated whenever the feature set materially changes or the founder's background details change.

## A note on tone

All three boilerplates are written in the third person, in the present tense, with active verbs. They end with a URL and (for the 300-word version) a press email. They do not contain exclamation marks, emojis, or adjectives we can't substantiate. They are paragraphs of fact — not marketing copy.
