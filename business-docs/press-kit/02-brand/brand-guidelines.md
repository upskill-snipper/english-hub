# The English Hub — Brand Guidelines

*Version 1.0. Internal and partner reference.*

This document is the master rulebook for how The English Hub looks, sounds and feels. If something in this press kit contradicts another document, this file wins.

Four sub-documents go deeper on specific topics. Always read this first, then the specialist guide:

- `logo-usage-guide.md` — where and how to use the logo
- `colour-palette.md` — exact hex, HSL, Pantone, and accessibility pairs
- `typography-guide.md` — typefaces for web, print, social
- `voice-and-tone.md` — three voice attributes and tone-by-situation

---

## 1. Brand essence

**One-line brand statement:** The English Hub is the calm, confident English coach every student deserves.

**Brand personality:** Quietly confident. Academic but never dry. Warm but never patronising. We sound like the best English teacher you ever had — the one who told you your writing was good *and then* told you exactly how to make it better.

**What we are not:** We are not loud, laddish, "hack your way to a grade 9" EdTech. We are not corporate textbook publishing. We are not a cold algorithm. We are not a chatbot.

---

## 2. Logo — rules at a glance

Full rules live in `logo-usage-guide.md`. The short version:

- **Primary logo:** wordmark with icon, horizontal lockup.
- **Icon-only:** use only when space is tight (favicon, app icon, avatar circles under 64px).
- **Minimum clear space:** equal to the height of the "E" in the wordmark, on all four sides.
- **Minimum size:** 120px wide on screen, 24mm wide in print.
- **Never:** stretch, rotate, recolour, apply drop shadows, place over busy imagery without a scrim, or reproduce below minimum size.

## 3. Colours — rules at a glance

Full palette and accessibility pairs live in `colour-palette.md`. The short version:

| Role | Name | Hex | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | Hub Emerald | `#22C58A` | Our "go" colour. CTAs, highlights, progress bars. |
| Background (dark) | Ink | `#0B0F17` | Default product background. Premium feel. |
| Surface (dark) | Slate | `#12172050` | Cards and layered surfaces. |
| Text (on dark) | Paper | `#F1F4F9` | 95% white, never pure `#FFFFFF`. |
| Accent | Signal Blue | `#3B82F6` | Links, secondary CTAs, charts. |
| Warning | Warm Amber | `#F5B638` | Notices, non-blocking alerts. |
| Error | Deep Red | `#E23D3D` | Destructive actions only. Never decorative. |

Pantone equivalents are in `colour-palette.md` for print work.

**Rule:** Hub Emerald on Ink is the signature colour combination. Any piece of marketing that is definitively "on brand" uses that pair.

## 4. Typography — rules at a glance

Full spec lives in `typography-guide.md`. The short version:

- **Primary typeface:** Mona Sans (variable, open source by GitHub).
- **Fallback stack:** Inter, -apple-system, Segoe UI, Roboto, sans-serif.
- **Display headlines:** Mona Sans SemiBold or Bold, tight tracking, never ALL CAPS.
- **Body copy:** Mona Sans Regular, 16px minimum on web, 1.6 line-height.
- **Never use:** Comic Sans, Papyrus, any serif for UI, any decorative font.

## 5. Voice & tone — rules at a glance

Full spec lives in `voice-and-tone.md`. The short version:

Our voice has three attributes:

1. **Clear** — We write short sentences. We remove jargon. If a 15-year-old can't follow it, we rewrite it.
2. **Confident** — We do not hedge. We do not use words like "probably" or "might help". We know what the mark scheme says.
3. **Kind** — We never mock a student's work. We never imply that English is easy. We never talk down.

## 6. Do's and don'ts

### Do
- Use the hyphenated form: "exam-board-specific."
- Write "GCSE English" and "IGCSE English" — never "english GCSE."
- Capitalise proper nouns for exam boards: AQA, Edexcel, OCR, WJEC, Cambridge IGCSE.
- Refer to our mission in human terms: "every student should have a personal English tutor."
- Use real student work (with permission) in marketing.

### Don't
- Don't say "revolutionise," "disrupt," "game-changer," "unlock," "leverage," "best-in-class," or "empower" in copy.
- Don't claim we "guarantee" a grade. We can say students improve; we cannot promise a number.
- Don't use emojis in serious contexts (investor docs, press releases, school emails). Light use is fine for student-facing social.
- Don't use stock photography of students wearing lab coats or jumping in graduation gowns.
- Don't compare ourselves by name to teachers — we augment teachers, we do not replace them.
- Don't reproduce copyrighted set text passages beyond fair dealing (short quotations with attribution).

## 7. Trademark and naming

- The company is **The English Hub**. Always capitalise "The." In headlines and sentence case.
- Never abbreviate to "TEH" in external copy. Internally, "TEH" is acceptable in file names.
- The product has the same name as the company. We do not sub-brand (e.g. we don't call it "Hub Marker" — we say "The English Hub's AI marking").
- `.app` is the primary domain: **theenglishhub.app**.
