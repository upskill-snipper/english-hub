# Founder Headshot — Requirements

A single consistent set of founder headshots is non-negotiable for press. Journalists will use the first one they find; make sure the first one they find is on-brand and editorial-quality.

Shoot once, use for two years, refresh when the founder's appearance materially changes.

---

## 1. The set we need

We need a pack of six shots, delivered in the formats below:

1. **Hero editorial** — upper body, neutral dark background, three-quarter pose, looking just past the camera. Our default headshot.
2. **Smiling editorial** — upper body, same background, direct eye contact, a real (not forced) smile.
3. **Candid working** — founder at a laptop in a real working environment, looking at screen, half-silhouetted. Used for feature pieces.
4. **Environmental** — full-body or wide shot of founder in a classroom-adjacent setting (an empty classroom, a library, a school corridor). Used for profile pieces tying the founder to the teaching world.
5. **Square crop — social** — same as hero but cropped 1:1 for profile avatars.
6. **On-stage** — wide shot of founder mid-talk at a real event. (Acceptable to use a staged "as if" shot if no real stage photo exists yet.)

## 2. Technical specs

- **Camera:** Full-frame DSLR / mirrorless. No phone shots for the hero set.
- **Resolution:** Minimum 4000 × 6000 px native. Retouch-safe RAW masters archived.
- **Format delivered:**
  - **Hi-res:** TIFF or sRGB JPEG at 100% quality for print.
  - **Web:** 2000 px longest edge sRGB JPEG, optimised under 400 KB.
  - **Thumbnail:** 512 × 512 sRGB JPEG for avatars, under 100 KB.
- **Colour profile:** sRGB for digital. Adobe RGB 1998 for any print edition.
- **Background:** Clean and uncluttered. Our preferred option is dark neutral (like our brand's Ink), with soft key light from the left.
- **Lighting:** Flattering, not dramatic. Avoid hard shadows across the face.

## 3. Framing

- Headroom above the crown: 10–15% of the frame.
- Chin safe area: do not crop tight under the chin.
- Shoulders in frame for editorial hero shots.
- Leave negative space to the right of the subject where possible, so art directors can add pullquotes or captions.
- Always shoot landscape *and* portrait variants — magazines and web will ask for both.

## 4. Wardrobe

- **Default:** plain, solid colour — navy, charcoal, forest green, or off-white. No bright patterns, no logos (including competitors' and our own), no busy stripes or dots.
- **Shirt collar versus knit:** a quality knit or button-down. No t-shirts with slogans. No branded company merch — not even our own.
- **Jewellery:** minimal.
- **Glasses:** if the founder wears them day-to-day, keep them on. Consistent branding matters.
- **Hair/makeup:** natural and professionally prepared. Invest in a single day's hair and makeup for the shoot.

## 5. Expression

- **Hero:** thoughtful, composed, not stern. Half-smile.
- **Smiling editorial:** real laugh, not a stock-photo grin. Triggered by the photographer saying something funny, not on command.
- **Candid working:** absorbed in a real task, not performing absorption.
- **Environmental:** contextually appropriate — attentive if in a classroom, relaxed if in a library.
- **On-stage:** mid-gesture, mouth open mid-word. Never a posed "about to give a thumbs-up" shot.

## 6. Location and background

- **Studio or editorial backdrop** for 1, 2, 5.
- **Real working space** for 3 (the founder's actual desk is best, lightly dressed).
- **Real school or library** for 4 — get permission in writing before shooting. Never shoot on a school site without consent.
- **Real stage** for 6 — ideally at a conference or meetup the founder is already speaking at.

## 7. Usage rights

- The photographer grants a perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free licence for The English Hub to use the images in any context — editorial, commercial, advertising, press, investor — without further approval.
- The photographer is credited in the photo metadata (`IPTC Creator` field) and in the press kit index, but **not** on the image itself as a watermark.
- Right to crop, retouch, and composite is granted.
- Model release is signed by the founder (and by any other subjects in candid shots) at the end of the shoot, in writing.

## 8. Delivery and storage

- **Delivered within:** 7 working days of the shoot.
- **Stored at:** `[INTERNAL ASSET URL]`
- **Public versions at:** https://theenglishhub.app/press
- **Naming convention:** `theenglishhub_founder_[shot-name]_[YYYY-MM].jpg`
  - Example: `theenglishhub_founder_hero_2026-04.jpg`

## 9. What to send journalists

- Send **one** preferred hero image unless otherwise asked.
- Include the credit line: *"Photo: [PHOTOGRAPHER NAME]. Courtesy of The English Hub."*
- Include a one-line permission statement: *"Free for editorial use in stories about The English Hub. Do not modify beyond cropping."*

## 10. Do not

- Do not use a phone selfie.
- Do not use a LinkedIn profile crop. It is always too tight.
- Do not use a badly-lit Zoom capture, even "just for now."
- Do not use an old photo. A stale founder photo is worse than none.
- Do not let journalists pull a random photo from Google Images. Always supply.
