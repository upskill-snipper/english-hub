# Product Video Brief — 60-second explainer

This is the brief for the canonical 60-second product video. It is the single film we expect to use on the homepage, in investor decks, in press coverage, and in every pitch until the product meaningfully changes.

Commission once, use everywhere. Do not make this video again in six months unless the product has meaningfully shifted.

---

## 1. Purpose

- Convince a GCSE student, a parent, and a teacher — in that order — that The English Hub will change how they revise English.
- Show the AI essay marker in action. Nothing else matters as much as this.
- End on a concrete CTA that works across contexts: "Free 30-day trial at theenglishhub.app."

## 2. Audience

- **Primary:** UK Year 10 and Year 11 students (aged 14–16). They are choosing revision tools on TikTok and YouTube.
- **Secondary:** parents of those students.
- **Tertiary:** English teachers considering recommending the platform.

The same video must work for all three. Do not make three videos.

## 3. Length and format

- **Hero length:** 60 seconds exactly.
- **Required deliverables:**
  - 60-second hero cut — 16:9 — 1080p + 4K masters.
  - 30-second social cut — 9:16 — vertical, sub-titled hard.
  - 15-second teaser cut — 9:16 — hook + payoff only.
  - 6-second bumper — for YouTube pre-roll — one shot, one line.
  - All cuts delivered with and without burnt-in subtitles.

## 4. Structure — the 60-second hero cut

| Time | Beat | What we see | What we hear |

| 0:00–0:05 | **The universal pain** | A Year 11 student handing an essay to a teacher. Cut to a calendar page flipping: Tuesday → Sunday. | VO: "You hand in your essay on Tuesday. You get it back on Sunday." |

## 5. Tone and direction

- **Visual tone:** warm, naturalistic, dark-mode premium. Looks like a good consumer tech brand, not a classroom.
- **Actors:** A real Year 11-age student. Diverse casting is not optional. Non-actors preferred — authenticity over polish.
- **Colour grade:** Lean into our brand palette: Ink, Slate, Hub Emerald accents.
- **Music:** Single instrument melody, warm piano or soft acoustic guitar. Rising gently from 0:30. Never upbeat corporate royalty-free stock.
- **Voiceover:** A single voice, calm and confident, faint regional UK accent preferred (not RP, not American). Female or male — the founder can choose.
- **Subtitles:** Burned-in for social cuts. Positioned for 9:16 crops to avoid the bottom-nav safe zone.

## 6. What is off-limits

- No exclamation marks in VO or on-screen text.
- No "revolutionise", "unlock", "game-changer", "AI-powered" (we show the AI, we don't announce it).
- No students or actors in graduation gowns, lab coats, throwing hats.
- No stock footage of gears, globes, glowing brains, or chalkboards with equations.
- No comparison to teachers. Never "faster than your teacher."
- No real student faces on screen without written parental consent.
- No real student essays without written consent.

## 7. Approval workflow

1. Script → founder sign-off before the first call with the production company.
2. Storyboard → founder + design lead sign-off before shoot day.
3. Rough cut → founder sign-off only.
4. Final cut → founder + brand lead sign-off.

Three rounds of revisions maximum. Build that into the SOW with the production company.

## 8. Budget guidance

- **Target all-in:** £[X,XXX] to £[XX,XXX] depending on production tier.
- **Cheap and cheerful route:** founder on camera, screen recordings, single studio day, voiceover in post. ~£2–4k.
- **Premium route:** cast, single-day shoot on location, professional grade, VO artist. ~£8–15k.
- **Do not** spend more than £20k on the 60-second unit until the company has over £500k ARR. The economics do not work.

## 9. Delivery format

- 1080p .mp4 H.264 for web.
- 4K ProRes master for archive.
- .srt subtitle file.
- Raw footage, signed model releases, and music licence handed over with the final cut — non-negotiable.

## 10. Re-use

- The same film is the homepage hero video.
- It is cut down to form the landing page for every paid-ad campaign.
- It is the opening video of every investor deck page-to-page meeting.
- Every press kit link goes to the same file.
