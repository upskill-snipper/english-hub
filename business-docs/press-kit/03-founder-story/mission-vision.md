# The English Hub — Mission and Vision

Two short statements. A mission tells the world why we exist *today*. A vision tells the world where we are going *tomorrow*. We keep both short enough that any employee can recite them on demand.

---

## Mission
*Why we exist — right now, today.*

> **To give every student in the UK and internationally a personal English tutor — available any time, priced for any household, and as effective as the best human coaching.**

### What this mission commits us to

1. **Speed of feedback.** The gap between writing an essay and getting it marked must collapse to under a minute. That is the threshold at which feedback still influences the next draft.
2. **Accuracy.** Our feedback must be specific to the mark scheme, the exam board, the set text, and the assessment objective — not generic writing advice.
3. **Affordability.** The headline price must stay within reach of households that cannot pay for private tutoring. Students on free school meals pay nothing.
4. **Kindness.** No student using our product should feel judged. Criticism is specific. Encouragement is specific. Both are sincere.
5. **Teacher alignment.** We build with teachers, not around them. Every major product decision is pressure-tested with practising English teachers before release.

### What this mission does *not* commit us to

- We are not trying to replace teachers. We are trying to give them their Sundays back.
- We are not trying to grade every subject. We are the English specialists and we stay in our lane.
- We are not trying to be the cheapest. We are trying to be the fairest.

---

## Vision
*Where we are going — the world we are trying to build.*

> **A world where the essay a student writes at 11pm the night before the mock is marked before they go to bed — regardless of their postcode, their household income, or their exam board.**

### What this vision looks like when we get there

- Every GCSE and IGCSE English student, in any country running a British curriculum, has access to marking-quality feedback on demand.
- The private-tutoring premium — the hidden tax paid by families that can afford £40 an hour — has collapsed. English grades correlate less tightly with household income than they did in 2025.
- Teachers use The English Hub as a drafting layer beneath their classroom work: students arrive at lessons with AI-marked first drafts, and teachers spend their time on the higher-order coaching only a human can give.
- The word "tutor" in the context of GCSE English means an app, a teacher, or both — not a luxury.
- The English Hub is the default platform for GCSE and IGCSE English in the UK and the top three international markets (UAE, Qatar, Saudi Arabia).

### The 10-year measure of success

If, ten years from now, a student on free school meals has the same access to same-day essay feedback as a student at a fee-paying independent, we have done what we set out to do.

---

## How to use these statements

- **Mission** belongs at the top of the about page, at the end of investor decks, and inside the founder's mouth at every interview.
- **Vision** is quoted less often. It is the punchline — saved for keynote talks, closing slides, and the final paragraph of long-form features.
- Neither is an aspirational billboard. Both are commitments that we audit against quarterly.

## Auditing the mission

Every quarter, we ask five questions against the mission:

1. What is the median time from "student taps Mark my essay" to "feedback delivered"? (Target: under 30 seconds, 95th percentile under 60 seconds.)
2. What share of our students come from schools in the bottom income quartile? (Target: proportional to the UK student body.)
3. What share of students on free school meals are getting the platform free? (Target: 100% of those who apply.)
4. What does a random sample of 20 essay-marking outputs score when moderated by a practising English examiner? (Target: agreement within one grade band.)
5. Are practising teachers willing to recommend the product without being paid to? (Target: teacher NPS above +40.)

If we miss any of those five, the mission is being violated and we fix it before we ship anything else.
