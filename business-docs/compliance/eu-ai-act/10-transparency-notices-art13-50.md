# 10 — Transparency Notices (Art 13 + Art 50 EU AI Act)

**Regulation:** Regulation (EU) 2024/1689, Article 13 (information to deployers) and Article 50 (transparency to natural persons interacting with / exposed to AI). Cross-cuts GDPR Arts 13–14, Art 22, and UK Children's Code (already adopted — `business-docs/compliance/childrens-code/`).
**System:** "The English Hub" — high-risk, **Annex III(3)(b)**.
**Document status:** v0.1 DRAFT — pending legal sign-off.
**Cross-references:** doc 08 §7, doc 09 (Part A 26(7)), doc 15 (DPIA), doc 16 (roadmap).

> **Why this document is urgent.** The product currently renders a **factually false** transparency claim — "Made with AI · **Reviewed by humans**" — directly above AI marking results, where **no human reviews B2C marks**. Under Art 50(1)/(4) and Art 13, transparency information must be accurate and not misleading; a false human-review claim is also an unfair/ deceptive practice and undermines the Art 14 oversight story. §2 specifies the exact replacement copy. **This is a correctness defect, not a wording preference.**

## 2. REQUIRED accurate user-facing notice wording (per touchpoint)

> These are the Provider's mandated copy. They are accurate against the code as it exists today (no invented controls). Implement verbatim; do not soften.

### 2.1 Correcting the false "Reviewed by humans" label (THE KEY FIX)

**Replace** the `ai_label.*` strings used by `AiGeneratedNotice` (`src/lib/i18n/dictionary.ts:208-220`) **for marking / feedback / CEFR surfaces** with copy that is true. The current single shared label conflates AI *authoring* (where a human may review) with AI *marking* (where no human reviews). The fix is to (a) stop claiming human review where there is none, and (b) make the panel marking-specific.

**Recommended replacement copy — short label (replaces "Made with AI · Reviewed by humans"):**

> **"AI-generated · Not human-reviewed · Practice only"**

**Recommended replacement copy — panel title:**

> **"This result was generated by AI"**

**Recommended replacement copy — panel body (marking/feedback/CEFR surfaces — T1, T2, T3):**

> **"This mark, grade and feedback were produced automatically by an AI system (Anthropic's Claude model). It has not been checked by a human and may be wrong or inconsistent. The grade is a *predicted, indicative* grade for practice — it is NOT an official exam result and, for boards other than AQA, it is derived from AQA grade boundaries. If you have a teacher account with your school, your teacher can review and change this mark. To ask a human to review your result, [request a human review](/dashboard/human-review) or email info@upskillenergy.com — your essay will be re-marked by a person. Read more on our [AI Transparency page](/legal/ai-transparency)."**

*Notes for implementation:*
- The link target `/dashboard/human-review` is **aspirational** — it must point to a working B2C human-review entry that creates a `HumanReviewRequest` (model `prisma/schema.prisma:294-320`; `POST /api/review/route.ts`). **Until that UI ships, the copy must say email-only:** *"To ask a human to review your result, email info@upskillenergy.com and a person will re-mark it."* **GAP — owner: Provider eng; build the B2C human-review surface; target: see doc 16.** Do not ship the link wording before the route exists (that would repeat the original false-promise defect).
- For T4 (revision/study material) keep a *separate* label; only claim human editorial review where it is actually true per item (verify the blog editorial workflow; if unverified, use the neutral copy below).

**Neutral copy for AI-authored content where human review is NOT verified (T4 fallback):**

> **"AI-generated · This content was drafted by AI and may contain errors. [How we use AI](/legal/ai-transparency)."**

## 5. Art 50 applicability summary

- **Art 50(1)** (interaction with an AI system): applies — pre-submission notice §2.2.
- **Art 50(4)** (AI-generated/manipulated text published to inform the public / made available to a person): applies to AI marking output, feedback, CEFR and AI-authored revision content — labels §2.1/§2.5/§2.6.
- Disclosure must be **clear, distinguishable, at first interaction/exposure**, and **accessible to minors** (Children's Code already requires child-friendly framing — `business-docs/compliance/childrens-code/04-evidence/child-friendly-notices-examples.md`). The §2 copy is written at an age-appropriate reading level.
- High-risk Art 50 transparency does **not** displace Art 13 instructions (doc 08) or the deployer's GDPR Art 13–14/Art 22 duties.
