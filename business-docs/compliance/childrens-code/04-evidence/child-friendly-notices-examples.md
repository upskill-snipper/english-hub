# Child-Friendly Notices — Examples

**Service:** The English Hub
**Version:** 0.9 (draft)
**Owner:** Content lead
**Last updated:** 2026-04-10

**Purpose:** A library of the actual wording The English Hub uses (or, where marked **[draft]**, will use once shipped) to explain data handling to children aged 13–16. This file is the source of truth for the child-facing copy. Copy here should match the product — any drift between product and this file is a documentation bug.

Target reading grade: Flesch-Kincaid 6–8.

---

## 1. Onboarding — first-run welcome screen

> **Welcome to The English Hub**
>
> This is a safe place to practise for GCSE English. Before you start, three things you should know:
>
> 1. **We don't sell ads.** You'll never see a sponsored post in the app.
> 2. **We don't sell your data.** Ever.
> 3. **You're in control.** You can delete your account any time from the menu.
>
> [ Got it ] [ Tell me more ]
>
> *"Tell me more" opens the child-friendly privacy policy inside the app.*

---

## 2. Signup — email field

**Label:** Your email

**Inline helper text:** *We use this to log you in and send you account messages. That's it. No marketing.*

**Privacy icon next to field (on tap):**

> **Why we need your email**
> We use it so you can log in and so we can send important messages (like a password reset). We will never sell it and we will never send you marketing unless you ask us to.

## 3. Signup — first name

**Label:** Your first name

**Inline helper:** *Just your first name. Your teacher will see this; nobody outside school will.*

## 4. Age gate

**Prompt (neutrally worded, no "click here if you are 18+" shortcut):**

> **How old are you?**
>
> [ number input ]
>
> We ask so we can make the app right for you. It takes one try.

**If under 13:**

> **We need a parent or carer to set this up**
>
> Children under 13 need a parent or carer to create the account. That's a UK rule, not just our rule.
>
> Enter your parent's email and we'll send them a link:
>
> [ parent email input ]   [ Send link ]
>
> Nothing else will happen until your parent clicks the link.

## 5. Essay submission — just-in-time notice

> **Submit your essay?**
>
> Your essay will be sent to our AI marker for feedback. Only **you** and **your teacher** (if you're on a school account) can see your essays in our app.
>
> Our AI marker **does not keep a copy** of your essay and **is not trained on what you write**.
>
> [ Submit ] [ Not yet ]

## 6. AI marking — first-use explainer **[draft — GAP-12A pending]**

> **About your AI feedback**
>
> Below your essay you'll see feedback written by an **AI marker**. Three things to know:
>
> 1. **It's a suggestion, not a decision.** A real teacher is always the final word for anything that matters, like a school report.
> 2. **It can get things wrong.** If you think the feedback is unfair, tap "Ask my teacher" on the feedback block.
> 3. **You can turn it off.** Go to Settings → AI Marking if you'd rather mark your own work.
>
> [ Show me the feedback ] [ Turn it off ]

## 7. Notifications opt-in

> **Want a daily reminder?**
>
> If you turn this on, we'll send **one** quiet notification a day during your chosen study time. We won't send any between 9pm and 7am.
>
> If you don't, that's fine — you won't miss anything important.
>
> [ Yes, one reminder ] [ No thanks ]

> **Join your class leaderboard?**
>
> If you opt in, people in **your class** will see **your first name** and **your score**. Nobody outside your class will.
>
> You can turn this off again any time in Settings.

> [ Join the class board ] [ Keep it private ]

## 9. Profile visibility

**Settings → Profile → Visibility**

> **Who can see your profile?**
>
> - **Only me** (default)
> - **Only my teacher** (if you have one)
> - **My class**
>
> Private means only you can see it. That's what we recommend and it's what we set for everyone at the start.

## 10. Parent-linked badge (persistent, top of every screen on parent-linked accounts)

> 🔗 **A parent is linked to this account.** [ What can they see? ]

Tapping the link opens:

> **Your parent can see:**
> - Your progress summary (topics, scores, hours practised)
> - Your privacy settings
> - Whether you've logged in recently
>
> **Your parent cannot see:**
> - The text of your essays (unless you share them)
> - The messages of your feedback, word for word
> - Private notes you write to yourself
>
> Your parent can remove their link at any time. If you're 13 or over, you can also ask us to remove the link — email **privacy@englishhub.example**.

## 13. Delete account flow

**Settings → Account → Delete account**

> **Delete your account**
>
> This will permanently delete your account, your essays, your scores, and your progress. You can't undo this.
>
> Before you go, you can **download a copy of everything we have about you** — takes about a minute.
>
> [ Download my data ]
>
> [ Delete everything ]   [ Cancel ]

**Confirmation step:**

> **Are you sure?**
>
> Type **DELETE** below to confirm.
>
> [ text input ]
>
> [ Yes, delete my account ]   [ Cancel ]

*(No hostage-taking copy. No "you'll lose your streak!" guilt-trip.)*

## 14. Report a concern

**Menu → Your data → Report a problem**

> **Tell us if something doesn't feel right**
>
> You can tell us about:
> - A message that felt mean or scary
> - Something that looked like a trick
> - A bug or a mistake in the app
> - Anything else about your data or safety
>
> Tell us in your own words:
>
> [ text area ]
>
> **If you're in danger right now**, please call **999** (UK emergency services) or talk to Childline at **0800 1111** — they are free, private, and open 24/7.
>
> [ Send ]

## 15. SAR confirmation

**After "Download my data" is tapped:**

> **Making your download…**
>
> We're gathering everything we have about you. This usually takes less than a minute but can take up to 30 days for big accounts.
>
> We'll email you at **[their email]** when it's ready.

## 16. Content-warning / safeguarding response **[used when an essay contains self-harm / abuse indicators]**

> **We noticed something in your essay**
>
> Your essay mentions things that made us worried about you. We don't want to ignore that.
>
> **If you want to talk to someone right now:**
> - **Childline** — 0800 1111, free, private, open 24/7 — childline.org.uk
> - **Samaritans** — 116 123, free, open 24/7
> - **A trusted adult** — a parent, teacher, coach, older sibling
>
> Your essay has not been marked by the AI. A member of our team will look at it and decide what to do. We will never publish it.
>
> [ I'm okay, continue ]   [ I want to talk to someone ]
