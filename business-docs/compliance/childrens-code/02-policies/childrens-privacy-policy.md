# Your Privacy at The English Hub

**For students aged 13–16**
**Last updated:** 10 April 2026

> **Reading time:** about 5 minutes.
> **If you only read one thing:** we use your data to help you get better at GCSE English. We do not sell it. We do not use it for adverts. You can delete it whenever you want.

---

## What this page is

This is the **student version** of our privacy policy. It is written for GCSE students, mostly aged 13 to 16. A longer version — the one a parent, teacher, or lawyer would read — also exists.

We want you to actually understand what happens to your data. So this page uses short sentences, simple words, and no tricks. If anything here is confusing, email **privacy@englishhub.example** and we will explain.

## Who we are

The English Hub is a website and app that helps you practise for GCSE English Language and Literature. It is made by a small UK company. Our full legal name, company number, and address are at the bottom of this page.

## What data we collect

Here is everything we ask for or store about you, and why.

### When you sign up

- **Your email address** — so you can log in and so we can send you important account messages.
- **A password** — we never see this in plain text. We turn it into a scrambled code that even we can't reverse.
- **Your first name** — so we can say hello and so your teacher can see your work.
- **Your year group or age bracket** — so we show you the right practice content.

### While you use the app

- **Your essays and practice answers** — because we mark them and give you feedback.
- **How you're doing** — things like how many questions you got right. This is so you can see your own progress and so your teacher can help you.
- **What device and browser you're using** — so the site works properly and so we can spot people trying to break in.
- **Your IP address** — this is like a rough clue to where your internet connection is. We keep it very briefly for security, then delete most of it.

### What we do NOT collect

We do not collect:
- Your surname (unless your teacher gives us a list for your class).
- Your home address or phone number.
- Your precise location — we never ask your device for GPS.
- Your face, voice, or fingerprint.
- Your contacts or friends.
- What other websites you visit.

---

## Why we collect it

We only collect data for reasons that help you learn. Here they are, plain:

1. **To run the app** — you can't log in without an email and password.
2. **To mark your work** — we can't give you feedback if we don't have your essays.
3. **To show your progress** — so you can see what you're getting better at.
4. **To let your teacher help you** — if you're on a school account.
5. **To keep you safe** — for example, spotting someone trying to break into your account.
6. **To make the app better** — we look at *which pages get used*, not *what you wrote*.

We **do not** use your data:
- For adverts (there are no adverts in the app, ever).
- To sell anything to you or your parents.
- To train AI models that are then sold to someone else.
- To guess your mood or feelings from your essay.

## Who can see your data

**Short answer:** you, your teacher (if you're on a school account), your parent (if they set the account up for you), and a very small number of trusted companies that help us run the service.

### You
You can always see your own data inside the app. There is a "Your data" button in the menu on every page.

### Your teacher
If you joined through your school, your teacher can see:
- Your essays and practice answers
- Your grades and progress
- Which topics you find hard

A banner at the top of any page they can see says "Your teacher can see this work". If you don't want this, tell your teacher — they may be able to move you to a non-school account.

### Your parent
If your parent set up your account (usually because you're under 13), they can see:
- A summary of how you're doing (not the exact words of your essays unless you share them)
- Your privacy settings
- Time-of-use settings

A banner at the top of every page says "A parent is linked to this account". You can see the full list of what a parent can and can't see in the "Your data" menu.

### Companies that help us run the service
These are called "processors". They only get what they need and they're not allowed to use your data for anything else. They are listed in our full privacy policy. The main ones are:
- Our hosting company (where our website lives)
- Our email provider (for important messages like password reset)
- The company that runs our AI marking model — they don't keep your essays and don't train models on them

### Nobody else
We **never** share your data with advertisers, marketing companies, data brokers, or social media platforms.

### The law
Very rarely, the law might say we have to share data — for example, if the police get a court order. This is extremely unlikely to ever affect a student. If it happened, we'd tell you unless the law said we couldn't.

## Your rights

You have a lot of rights over your data. You can exercise all of them inside the app — no emails, no forms — by going to **menu → Your data**.

- **See it** — "Show me everything you have about me". We'll give you a download.
- **Fix it** — "This bit is wrong, change it".
- **Delete it** — "Delete my account and everything in it". We'll confirm, then do it.
- **Take it with you** — "Give me a copy I can take to another site". You get a file you can use somewhere else.
- **Stop using it** — "Stop using my data for this thing". For some things we have to keep using it to run the account, and we'll explain if so.
- **Say no to profiling** — "Don't use an AI to mark my essays". You can turn the AI marker off in your settings.
- **Report a problem** — "Something doesn't feel right". We read every report and come back to you within 3 working days.

If you think we've handled your data badly and we haven't fixed it, you can complain to the UK's data regulator, the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), at **ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint**. They're friendly and free to contact.

## How long we keep things

- **Your account and practice history:** while you have an account. If you don't log in for 24 months, we'll email you a warning and then delete it.
- **Essays you submit:** while you're using the service. You can delete any essay individually from the "Your essays" page.
- **Security logs (IP, login info):** a few months at most.
- **Backups:** backups are kept for 30 days and then overwritten.

When you delete your account, everything goes. The only exception is if we legally have to keep a record — for example, a refund for a payment made by your parent.

## Settings you control

Go to **menu → Settings → Privacy**. Here's what you can change. Everything starts in the safest setting.

| Setting | Default for students | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| Who can see my profile | Nobody | Only you can see your profile page |
| Leaderboard | Off | If on: just your class, first names only |
| Push notifications | Off | If on: max 1 a day, quiet hours after 9pm |
| Email notifications | Weekly digest | A short weekly email |
| Personalised practice order | Off | If on: the app picks what to practise next |
| AI marking on essays | On | You can turn this off and get self-marking instead |
| Share my score on social media | Off | This button doesn't appear in student accounts |

## A word about "nudging"

Some apps try to trick you into giving up your privacy. They put the "accept everything" button in big green letters and hide the "no thanks" button in small grey. We don't do that.

If you see something in the app that feels like a trick, tell us: **privacy@englishhub.example**. We take that seriously and will fix it.

## If something goes wrong

If there's a data breach — someone gets at data they shouldn't — we will:
1. Stop the problem.
2. Tell the ICO within 72 hours if the law requires it.
3. Tell you directly if your data was affected and it could hurt you.

Our full breach plan is in our internal compliance pack, but the bit you care about is: **we will tell you**.
