## 1. Data Protection Officer (DPO) — Article 37 Assessment

### Assessment Outcome

We have assessed our processing activities under Article 37 of UK GDPR and determined that a formal DPO appointment is **not currently required**. This assessment is based on the following criteria:

- **Article 37(1)(a):** We are not a public authority or body.
- **Article 37(1)(b):** Our core activities do not consist of processing operations which, by virtue of their nature, scope, and/or purposes, require regular and systematic monitoring of data subjects on a large scale.
- **Article 37(1)(c):** Our core activities do not consist of processing on a large scale of special categories of data or data relating to criminal convictions and offences.

### Mitigating Factors Considered

While a formal DPO is not required, we recognise that:

- Our platform processes children's personal data (ages 13-16)
- We are subject to the ICO's Age Appropriate Design Code (Children's Code)
- We use AI processing (essay feedback) which involves children's educational data
- We process data of a vulnerable group (minors)

### Responsibilities

The named data protection contact is responsible for:

2. Advising on Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs), particularly for features accessed by children
3. Acting as the point of contact for the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)
4. Handling data subject access requests and other rights requests
5. Overseeing compliance with the ICO's Age Appropriate Design Code
6. Ensuring staff receive appropriate data protection training
7. Maintaining records of processing activities (Article 30)
8. Reporting data breaches in accordance with Articles 33 and 34

### Statutory Basis

The DSL appointment is made in accordance with:

- *Keeping Children Safe in Education (2024)* — Part 2
- *Working Together to Safeguard Children (2023)*
- Children Act 2004, Section 11
- ICO Age Appropriate Design Code (Standard 1: Best Interests of the Child)

### Responsibilities

The DSL is responsible for:

1. Taking lead responsibility for safeguarding and child protection across the platform
2. Referring cases of suspected abuse or allegations to the relevant local authority children's social care team
3. Liaising with the police and other agencies where appropriate
4. Maintaining accurate and secure safeguarding records
5. Ensuring safeguarding concerns are acknowledged within 24 hours
6. Overseeing safeguarding training for all staff and contractors
7. Conducting annual reviews of the safeguarding policy
8. Managing the online reporting system at /safeguarding/report

### Reporting Flow

1. Concerns are reported via the online form (/safeguarding/report) or email to [DSL_EMAIL] or by calling [DSL_PHONE]
2. The DSL reviews and triages within 24 hours
3. Where there is immediate risk of significant harm, the DSL contacts police (999) and/or local authority children's services without delay
4. External reporting routes for users:
   - **Emergency:** Call 999
   - **CEOP:** ceop.police.uk
   - **Childline:** 0800 1111
   - **NSPCC Helpline:** 0808 800 5000
   - **Local authority:** via GOV.UK

### Training Requirements

- The DSL completes enhanced safeguarding training (Level 3 or equivalent) upon appointment
- Refresher training is undertaken at least every two years
- The DSL undertakes inter-agency working training in line with *Working Together to Safeguard Children*
- Training records are maintained centrally

## 3. Interaction Between DPO and DSL Roles

The data protection contact and DSL work together to ensure:

- Data protection considerations are embedded in safeguarding procedures
- Safeguarding data is processed lawfully under Article 6(1)(d) (vital interests) and Article 6(1)(e) (public task)
- Information sharing for safeguarding purposes complies with UK GDPR while not creating barriers to child protection
- DPIAs are conducted for safeguarding-related processing activities
- The Children's Code requirements are met across both data protection and safeguarding domains
