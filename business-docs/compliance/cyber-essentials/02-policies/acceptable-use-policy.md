## 1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy sets the rules for the responsible use of Company information systems, devices, networks, and data. It protects the Company, its customers (including children), and its staff from avoidable harm.

## 2. Scope

Every user of Company systems — employees, contractors, volunteers, and directors. Applies to all Company-owned devices and to personally-owned devices used to access Company data (see BYOD Policy for additional rules).

## 3. General principles

1. Company systems are provided to perform Company work.
2. Reasonable and limited personal use is permitted provided it does not affect performance of work, security, reputation, or legal compliance.
3. All use of Company systems may be monitored, logged, and audited for security and compliance purposes. Staff consent to this monitoring as a condition of access.
4. Use of Company systems in a way that breaches UK law is prohibited and may be reported to law enforcement.

## 4. Permitted use

Staff may use Company devices and services for:

- All work-related activities in their role
- Occasional personal use (e.g. reading a news site during a break) provided:
  - It does not interfere with work
  - It does not breach any provision of this Policy
  - It does not expose Company data or systems to additional risk
  - Personal accounts are not added to Company devices for long-term use (e.g. don't sign into a personal iCloud on a work Mac)

## 5. Prohibited activities

The following are **strictly prohibited** on any Company system or network:

### 5.1 Illegal activity
- Accessing, storing, or distributing material that is illegal under UK law, including indecent images of children, extreme pornography, terrorist content, or content that incites violence or hatred
- Copyright infringement (including unauthorised downloads of films, music, software, or games)
- Defamation, harassment, or stalking
- Unauthorised access to any system or data (hacking, password cracking, network scanning of third-party systems without explicit permission)
- Fraud, theft, or any other criminal offence

### 5.2 Security compromises
- Disabling or circumventing any security control (firewall, anti-malware, MFA, screen lock, encryption)
- Installing software from untrusted sources
- Connecting unapproved removable media (USB sticks, external drives) to Company devices
- Connecting Company devices to untrusted networks without host firewall and anti-malware active
- Attempting to gain access to information systems the user is not authorised to use
- Sharing credentials with another person
- Bypassing the password manager (e.g. writing passwords down, reusing passwords across sites)
- Using personal email to send or receive Company data

### 5.3 Data handling breaches
- Copying sensitive data (Tier 3 or Tier 4) to an unauthorised location (personal cloud storage, USB, personal email)
- Printing sensitive data without a clean-desk safeguard
- Discussing sensitive data in public places where it may be overheard
- Photographing or recording screens showing sensitive data
- Using AI tools (public ChatGPT, Copilot, etc.) to process sensitive or personal data unless the Company has an approved enterprise account with Zero Data Retention. The only approved AI service for sensitive data is the OpenAI API with ZDR enabled.

### 5.4 Harassment and conduct
- Sending messages that harass, bully, or discriminate against any person or group
- Creating or sharing content that is offensive, obscene, or discriminatory
- Impersonating another person or organisation
- Any conduct that would breach the Company's HR conduct policies

### 5.5 Personal use that becomes unacceptable
- Running a personal business on Company systems
- Gambling
- Accessing adult content
- Crypto mining or other resource-intensive personal computing on Company systems

## 6. Email and messaging

- Corporate email (Google Workspace) is the only approved channel for external business communication.
- Slack is the only approved channel for internal real-time chat.
- Staff must not auto-forward corporate email to any personal account.
- Staff must not send Company sensitive data over personal messaging apps (WhatsApp, personal Gmail, etc.).
- Out-of-office messages must not reveal sensitive details (e.g. exactly where the user is, or who can be contacted at a personal number).
- Phishing emails must be reported to the CTO/DPO immediately and **not** forwarded to colleagues.

## 7. Web browsing

- Default browser is Chrome or Safari on macOS, Firefox or Chrome on Linux. All browsers must be kept at a supported, in-vendor-support version.
- Staff must not visit websites known to be malicious or whose reputation is unclear.
- DNS filtering (Cloudflare for Families) is enforced on all devices to block known malware and adult content domains.
- Downloads of executables, installers, or scripts from the web require justification. Only install software from official vendor sites, Mac App Store / Apple Developer signed, official Linux distribution repositories, or approved package managers.

## 8. Device use

- Devices remain in the physical custody of the assigned user at all times.
- Devices are locked whenever unattended — manual lock is expected even within the home office.
- Screen lock is set to a maximum of 10 minutes of inactivity.
- Devices are not left visible in unattended vehicles.
- Devices taken abroad must be declared to the CTO/DPO and reviewed against the Remote Working Policy.
- Suspected theft, loss, or damage is reported to the CTO/DPO **within 1 hour** of discovery.

## 9. Removable media

- USB drives, SD cards, and external hard disks are **not** approved for general use.
- If removable media must be used for a specific purpose (e.g. transferring a video file), the media is:
  - Provided by the Company, not personally owned
  - Encrypted at rest
  - Scanned for malware before use
  - Wiped securely after the transfer

## 10. Personal devices (BYOD)

Personal devices may be used to access Company data only under the BYOD Policy. The AUP applies in full to all use of a personal device that touches Company data.

## 11. Social media

- Staff are free to use social media in a personal capacity in their personal time.
- Staff must not post Company confidential information, unreleased product information, customer names, or any image that identifies a Company customer's child.
- Staff must not publicly represent Company views without prior approval.
- Corporate social media accounts (@englishhub on any platform) are operated only by authorised persons and are subject to the same password, MFA, and access controls as other corporate accounts.

## 12. Monitoring and privacy

- The Company monitors use of Company systems for security, compliance, and capacity purposes.
- Monitoring includes: audit logs in every cloud service, device management telemetry, network telemetry (where applicable), email metadata retention.
- Monitoring does not extend to covert surveillance of staff personal communications and is conducted in compliance with UK data protection law and the ICO Employment Practices Code.
- Staff are informed of monitoring on joining the Company and by this Policy.

## 13. Reporting

Staff are required to report promptly:

- Any suspected security incident or policy breach (including their own)
- Lost or stolen devices
- Suspected phishing, social engineering, or impersonation attempts
- Any request from any person to bypass this Policy (including requests that appear to come from management)

Reports go to the CTO/DPO via Slack DM, email, or phone. After-hours emergencies use the contact in the Incident Response Policy.

## 14. Disciplinary consequences

Breaches of this Policy may result in:

- Informal warning
- Formal written warning
- Suspension
- Summary dismissal (for serious or repeated breaches, or for any breach involving criminal conduct)
- Civil or criminal legal action

The Company reserves the right to report serious breaches to law enforcement.
