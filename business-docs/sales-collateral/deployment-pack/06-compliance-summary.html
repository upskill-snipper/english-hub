Children — ICO Age Appropriate Design Code

Every under-18 account is treated under the Code's standards. Standard 8 high-privacy defaults are enforced in code and asserted by a strict test suite covering ages 11–17 — personalised recommendations, streak gamification, behavioural nudges, non-essential analytics, marketing, social-share nudges, public leaderboards and geolocation are all off by default.

  • Minimum age 11, with verifiable parental consent for ages 11–15 captured via a parent-link email at sign-up. UK GDPR Article 8 sets the digital age of consent at 13; for users aged 11–12 we rely on verifiable parental consent.
  • Data minimisation: age bracket stored, not full DOB shown anywhere; opaque identifiers at the AI boundary.
  • No behavioural profiling of minors for commercial purposes; no targeted advertising to minors.

What we do NOT claim

  • We are not NCSA NIA-certified (separate cybersecurity scheme; deferred).
  • We do not yet hold an Article 16 permit reference (dossier shelf-ready; submission held).
  • We are not ISO/IEC 27001 certified (roadmap, late 2026) or Cyber Essentials Plus certified.
  • We are not endorsed by AQA, Pearson Edexcel, OCR, WJEC, Eduqas or Cambridge — we cover their specifications; endorsement claims would be false.
  • Counter-signed sub-processor DPAs — most are pending counter-signature; the AI DPA + zero-retention confirmation are being finalised with counsel (Document 03).
