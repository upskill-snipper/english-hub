Agreements & data protection (for leadership, DPO, procurement)

#DocumentWho it's for / what it answers
01Pilot AgreementThe one-page contract. Scope, fee, term, no auto-renew, governing law. Signature page included.
02Data Processing AgreementThe DPA the school's DPO reviews. Controller/processor roles, processing detail, sub-processors, transfers, breach, audit.
03Sub-processor RegisterEvery third party that may touch data, what they do, where, and honest DPA status.
04Information Security StatementTechnical + organisational measures. TLS 1.3, AES-256, Argon2id, RLS, MFA, audit logging, breach response. Honest certification status.
05Data Retention & Deletion ScheduleHow long each data category is kept; deletion on request; the 48-hour exit pack.
06Compliance SummaryUK GDPR + DPA 2018 + Children's Code + PDPPL at a glance, plus what we explicitly do NOT claim.
07Vendor Due-Diligence QuestionnaireThe procurement/IT/security questionnaire — pre-answered. Saves the back-and-forth.
08Safeguarding StatementDSL contact, reporting routes, emergency signposting, frameworks, vetting scope, whistleblowing.

How to use this pack

  • At first contact: send the school-pack shareables (separate folder) — Solutions & Value, Pricing, the one-page Pilot Rollout. This deployment pack is what follows once they're interested.
  • When they say "send me the pilot terms": send 01 (Pilot Agreement) + 02 (DPA) + 06 (Compliance Summary).
  • When their DPO / IT engages: send 02–08 (the full data-protection + security + safeguarding set). 07 (the pre-answered DDQ) usually closes most questions on its own.
  • When they sign: the project owner works through 09–12 to run the pilot.
A note on honesty

These documents state our position accurately, including where things are still being finalised — counter-signed sub-processor DPAs, the AI no-training confirmation, certifications not yet held, and the Qatar Article 16 permit not yet submitted. A school's DPO will trust an honest pack far more than a glossy one. Do not edit these to overstate the position.
