Parent Communication Templates

Ready-to-send wording for schools to inform parents and (where needed) capture consent.

Edit the bracketed fields. Use on school letterhead.

When consent is needed

The platform's own sign-up flow handles verifiable parental consent for students aged 11–15 via an email loop, and captures PDPPL Article 17 cross-border consent for Qatar-resident users. Where a school provisions accounts on behalf of students, the school is responsible for the lawful basis and any parental notice/consent — these templates make that easy.

Template A — Notice to parents (school-provisioned accounts)

Subject: Introducing The English Hub for [year group] English

Dear Parent / Guardian,

This term, our English department is running a pilot of The English Hub, an online platform that gives students extra English Language and Literature practice with fast, criteria-referenced feedback on their writing. It is used to support — not replace — teaching.

A few things you should know:

  • Your child's written work is marked by an AI tool to give them quick feedback; their teacher remains the final authority on any grade.
  • We do not share the text of your child's essays with anyone, including you; you will be able to see a weekly progress summary (modules completed, time spent, areas to focus on).
  • The platform is built to the UK's Children's Code — no advertising to children, no behavioural profiling, high-privacy settings on by default.
  • Your child's data is stored securely in the European Union; full detail is in the platform's privacy notice at theenglishhub.app/privacy-policy (and, for Qatar, /legal/privacy-qatar).

If you have any questions, please contact [school contact]. If you would prefer your child not to take part in the pilot, let us know by [date].

Kind regards,
[Name, role]

Template B — Consent request (where the school seeks explicit opt-in)

Subject: Your consent for [child's name] to use The English Hub

Dear Parent / Guardian,

We would like [child's name] to take part in our English department's pilot of The English Hub this term. Because your child is under 16, we are seeking your consent before activating their account.

By giving consent you confirm that you are happy for your child to:

  • Use the platform for English practice and assessment;
  • Have their written practice work processed by the platform's AI marking tool to generate feedback (no name, email or date of birth is sent to the AI provider, and the work is not used to train AI models);
  • Have a weekly progress summary sent to your email.

You can withdraw consent at any time by contacting [school contact] or using the privacy controls in the platform. Withdrawing consent stops the relevant processing.
