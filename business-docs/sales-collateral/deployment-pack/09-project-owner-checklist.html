Project Owner Checklist

Everything the school-side pilot owner does, in order. Total time across the term: ~6–8 hours.

For the Head of English or Deputy Head Academic running the pilot

Before kickoff (Week 0–1)
  • Confirm pilot scope and year groups with The English Hub
  • Review and countersign the Pilot Agreement (Document 01)
  • Hand the compliance pack (Documents 02–08) to your DPO / IT lead
  • Prepare the student roster as a CSV (template + guide in Document 12)
  • Confirm parental-consent position for any under-16 students (Document 11 has the templates)
  • Book the 30-minute academic kickoff and the 30-minute DPO call
Kickoff (Week 1)
  • Join the 30-minute academic kickoff
  • Upload the student roster
  • Create class structures by exam board, teacher and year group
  • Agree the written success criteria (Document 10)
  • Brief the English department — share the teacher quick-start
  • Confirm your DPO has completed the Week-1 data-protection call
Weeks 2–3 — bedding in
  • Check that teachers have logged in and created their first assignments
  • Confirm students can submit an essay and receive AI-marked feedback
  • Raise any technical issues to your English Hub contact (24h response)
  • Glance at class-level adoption in the dashboard
Weeks 4–6 — mid-pilot review
  • Pull the adoption + marking-turnaround metrics from the dashboard
  • Join the 30-minute mid-pilot review
  • Flag what's working and what isn't; request any changes
  • Sense-check progress against the agreed success criteria
Weeks 7–10 — steady state
  • Light-touch weekly check on engagement
  • Collect informal teacher feedback for the close-out
  • Note any students flagged for intervention by the platform
Weeks 11–12 — close-out
  • Run the anonymous teacher NPS survey
  • Join the 30-minute close-out review
  • Assess each success criterion: met / partly met / not met
  • Make the go / no-go recommendation on annual deployment to leadership
  • If not proceeding, request the 48-hour exit pack
What you do NOT have to do

No teacher training day to schedule. No IT integration project. No data migration. No MIS sync unless you specifically want one. No long contract negotiation — the agreement is one page.
