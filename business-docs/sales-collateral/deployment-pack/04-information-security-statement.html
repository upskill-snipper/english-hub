Information Security Statement

The technical and organisational measures protecting student and school data.

Last reviewed 2026-05-24

What we hold and what we don't

We are honest about certification status. We are not yet ISO/IEC 27001 certified (on roadmap, targeted late 2026) and we do not hold Cyber Essentials Plus or the NCSA NIA certification. The measures below are real and in force; the formal certifications that independently attest to them are a separate, in-progress workstream. We say so plainly so your security team knows exactly what they are buying.

Data in transit and at rest

  • In transit: TLS 1.3 across all connections.
  • At rest: AES-256 encryption in the primary database and object storage.
  • Passwords: hashed with Argon2id (memory-hard); never stored or logged in plaintext.

Access control

  • Row-level security in the database scopes every record to its owning user / school.
  • Least-privilege staff access via SSO with multi-factor authentication; access reviewed quarterly.
  • Multi-factor authentication available to all users; SSO (Google Workspace / Microsoft 365) supported for annual school deployments.
  • Administrative actions are written to an append-only audit log.

Application security

  • Per-request Content-Security-Policy with a fresh nonce; HSTS; X-Frame-Options DENY; X-Content-Type-Options nosniff; strict Referrer-Policy; Cross-Origin-Opener-Policy and Cross-Origin-Resource-Policy set to same-origin.
  • Rate limiting on authentication and sensitive endpoints (via Upstash Redis in the serverless runtime).
  • Account-enumeration defences on signup and login.
  • Cloudflare WAF + protective network layer at the edge.
  • Session tokens are high-entropy, rotated, and expire.

AI boundary controls

  • Essay text is sent to the AI sub-processor under opaque identifiers — no name, email, date of birth or school crosses the AI boundary.
  • Outbound payloads are minimised to the essay free-text, the question, and the exam board + year.
  • API keys are scoped and rotatable; every AI request is logged for audit.
  • Contractual position: no training on submissions (counter-signed AI DPA + written zero-retention confirmation being finalised with counsel — see Document 03).

Monitoring and resilience

  • Error and performance monitoring via Sentry with PII scrubbed from payloads before send.
  • Managed database with point-in-time recovery; object storage redundancy at the provider level.
  • Infrastructure hosted on Supabase (EU primary database), Microsoft Azure UK South (backend), and Vercel (front end) — all enterprise-grade providers with their own SOC 2 / ISO posture.

Organisational measures

  • Data Protection Officer appointed (Calum Johnson, dpo@theenglishhub.app).
  • Designated Safeguarding Lead appointed separately (Lauren Johnson, safeguarding@theenglishhub.app).
  • Records of Processing Activities maintained (UK GDPR Article 30).
  • Three Data Protection Impact Assessments on file (children's data, AI features, analytics).
  • Documented breach-response runbook with a 72-hour notification target.
  • Sub-processor register reviewed quarterly and on any material change.

Breach response

On becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting a school's data, we notify the school without undue delay and within 72 hours, with the information needed for the school's own notifications to the ICO and (for Qatar-resident data) the NCGAA. The full runbook and a pre-filled NCGAA notification template are held internally and available to your DPO on request.
