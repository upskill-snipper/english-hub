## The 7-day timeline

This is what actually happens between you replying to my email and your first student getting AI-marked feedback on a real practice essay.

### Day 0 — You reply

You email **dpo@theenglishhub.app** with subject **"Pilot interest — [your school name]"**. Tell me: which pilot scope, which year groups, your project owner's name + email, anyone to cc.

### Day 1 — We send the pack

Within 24 hours of your reply:
- **One-page pilot agreement** — drafted, with your name + scope + fee pre-filled
- **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** — our standard, fits UK GDPR + PDPPL
- **Compliance dossier** for your DPO — PDMS index + RoPA + three v1.0 DPIAs + sub-processor list

Your DPO can review in parallel with the academic conversation.

### Day 2–3 — You countersign

When you're ready. If it takes longer, no problem — the clock is yours.

### Day 4 — We schedule two calls in Week 1

- **30-minute academic kickoff** with your project owner — class setup, agree success criteria
- **30-minute DPO call** with your DPO / Director of Learning Technology — walk through the compliance pack, answer questions

### Day 5 — Academic kickoff

Three things we cover in 30 minutes:
1. **Upload your student roster** (CSV, or MIS export — iSAMS, Engage, Bromcom, Arbor all fine)
2. **Create class structures** — by exam board, by teacher, by year group
3. **Agree the success criteria** in writing — typically marking turnaround, adoption, NPS

### Day 6 — DPO call

Your DPO walks through the compliance pack with our DPO (me). 30 minutes. By the end, your school has everything it needs to satisfy a procurement-security audit on us.

### Day 7 — First essay marked

Your students log in. A Year 11 picks up a practice paper, writes 600 words, submits. **Within about a minute**, they have AI-marked feedback referencing the AOs. Your project owner sees this happen in their dashboard.

## What you DON'T need to do

A lot of EdTech onboarding asks for things schools genuinely don't have time to give. Here's what we don't ask for, and why.

### ❌ No teacher training week

The teacher dashboard is designed to be self-service for anyone who can use a web browser. Most of our teachers don't have any onboarding session beyond a 3-minute platform tour video. If a teacher gets stuck, they email or WhatsApp the project owner, who escalates to us. We answer within 24 working hours, direct.

### ❌ No IT integration project

The platform is a web app. Your students access it via a browser; if your school uses Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 SSO, we support that for the annual deployment. For the pilot, students sign in with school email + password.

### ❌ No MIS sync (unless you specifically want one)

For the pilot, we upload your student roster as CSV. For annual deployment with > 500 students, we can do a one-way sync from iSAMS / Engage / Bromcom / Arbor — but it is **optional**, and a CSV refresh once a term works fine for most schools.

### ❌ No "data migration" project

There is no legacy data to migrate. Students start fresh on the platform with their first practice essay.

### ❌ No "platform configuration" project

The platform ships with sensible defaults. Standard 8 Children's Code defaults are on. Class structures take 10 minutes to set up. Adopting it doesn't require an internal IT change-management process.

### ❌ No long-form contract negotiation

The pilot agreement is **one page**. The DPA is our standard, available to your DPO before signature. Standard liability caps. No 40-page MSA, no protracted back-and-forth.

## How students get onboarded

The student onboarding flow is intentionally minimal — most students log in, click into a practice paper, write, submit, see feedback, all within their first session.

### What a student sees, step by step

1. **Account activation email** — sent automatically when their email is in the uploaded roster
2. **First login** — they pick their exam board (or one is pre-set by the teacher for the cohort) and year group
3. **Children's Code-compliant defaults** are applied automatically — analytics off, marketing off, streak gamification off, public leaderboards off
4. **For students aged 11–15** — the signup flow asks for a parent/guardian email. The account doesn't fully activate until the parent clicks the consent loop email (UK GDPR Article 8 — verifiable parental consent)
5. **For students with country = Qatar** — they see a **named-destination cross-border consent panel** under PDPPL Article 17 before account creation completes
6. **First practice paper** — they pick a paper, write, submit, get AI-marked feedback within a minute

### What parents see

For any account belonging to a child under 18, the parent/guardian email on the account:
- Receives the consent loop email at signup
- Receives a **weekly progress summary** (modules completed, time on platform, weak areas identified, working-at grade) — **but never the text of the child's essays**
- Can request data export, deletion, or consent withdrawal at any time from the parent dashboard

## How teachers get onboarded

Even more minimal than students.

### What a teacher sees, step by step

1. **Project owner adds them** to a class in the teacher dashboard
2. **First login** — they see their classes, their students' essays in a queue, and the platform's analytics view
3. **One 3-minute platform tour video** (optional) — covers the dashboard, how to surface struggling students, how to download class analytics, how to set practice assignments
4. **First class analytics review** — at end of Week 1 they can already see who is and isn't using the platform, what the AI is flagging as common weak areas, and how the marking turnaround compares to their old process

That's it. No training day. No certification. No "Pro Teacher" badge to earn.

## Frequently asked "is this going to be hard" questions

**"Will our IT team need to be involved?"**
Only for SSO if you want it on annual deployment. For the pilot, no.

**"Will our DPO need to be involved?"**
Yes — one 30-minute call in Week 1. We send the compliance pack 24 hours ahead so the DPO can read in their own time. After that call, no further DPO involvement during the pilot unless something changes.

**"Do we need to give you student personal data?"**
We need student email addresses, names, year group, exam board — that's it. No home addresses, no parent income data, no medical, no SEND flags unless you choose to share them. The Children's Code data-minimisation defaults are on by default.

**"What happens if the pilot doesn't work?"**
You don't renew. No clawback. No "minimum 3-year" lock-in. We send the 48-hour exit pack — full data export, deletion confirmation, audit certificate.

**"What happens at the end of a successful pilot?"**
You convert to annual deployment at the Founding rate (£4,000/year) **for the life of the licence**. The price is locked even after our standard pricing doubles to £8,000 in August.

**"How quickly can we get started?"**
Day 0 = your email. Day 7 = first essay marked. If your countersignature on the pilot agreement takes longer, the timeline shifts accordingly — but we can absorb a fast-moving school in one working week if you're ready.
