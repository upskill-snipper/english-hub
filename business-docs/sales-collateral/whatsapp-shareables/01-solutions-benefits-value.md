## The problem

Every English department in every secondary school in the GCC faces the same four problems at once:

### 1. Marking turnaround is too slow to be useful

A typical department of 80 Year 11 students writing fortnightly practice essays generates **160 essays a month**. Most departments are running **7 to 14 days behind** on marking turnaround. By the time feedback lands, the student has moved on and the learning moment is gone.

### 2. Feedback is necessarily light

No teacher has 90 minutes per essay. Practice-essay feedback is, by necessity, a few comments and a band — not the deep paragraph-by-paragraph analysis that actually moves a student from Grade 6 to Grade 8.

### 3. EAL students are penalised for the wrong reasons

Gulf-Arabic L1 students writing in English make different mistakes from L1 English speakers — different word order, different article use, different cohesion patterns. Conventional marking penalises these as basic errors when the underlying English thinking is often sound.

### 4. AI tools in education are a data-protection minefield

Most AI tutoring platforms train on student work, host data in the wrong jurisdiction, and have no Children's Code posture. A Qatari school DPO asking "where does our students' data live, who trains on it, and what's our Article 17 consent position?" gets a vague answer from most vendors.

## Where we move the needle

We are deliberately not in the business of claiming "students improve their grade by X" without an outcome study to back it up. What we **can** evidence is that we move the four levers educational research consistently identifies as drivers of improvement in writing performance.

### Lever 1 — Practice volume

The thing that most distinguishes a Grade 9 candidate from a Grade 5 candidate is the **number of essays they wrote, marked, and revised** in the year before the exam. Our platform makes that volume affordable for any student: a Year 11 can submit five essays in a week and get specific feedback on all five. No human department in the world can sustain that by hand.

### Lever 2 — Feedback turnaround

Research on memory consolidation and feedback timing is clear: feedback that lands while the work is still mentally fresh is materially more useful than feedback that lands a fortnight later. **From 7–14 days to under one minute.**

### Lever 3 — AO-specific feedback

Generic "good work, try harder" doesn't move a Grade 6 to a Grade 8. **AO-referenced** feedback that names the assessment objective the student missed, gives a specific example of how they missed it, and shows what to do instead — that does. Our feedback maps directly to the mark scheme the student is sitting.

### Lever 4 — Mock exam practice under exam conditions

Timed practice papers under exam conditions are one of the most reliable predictors of exam performance. Our mock exam library covers every paper of every board.

### What we don't yet claim

We are launch-stage and do not yet have a validated outcome study. Once the Founding cohort generates 12 months of data, that study is on the roadmap and the results — whatever they are — will be published openly. **We will never invent a grade-improvement statistic to win a sale.** If a competitor quotes you a specific number, ask to see the methodology.
