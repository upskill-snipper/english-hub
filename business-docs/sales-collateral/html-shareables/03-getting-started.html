The week-by-week timeline

From Day 0 to Day 7

Day 0 — Your reply

Email dpo@theenglishhub.app with subject "Pilot interest — [your school name]". Tell us the pilot scope, year groups, project-owner name and email, and anyone to cc.

Day 1 — The pack arrives

Within 24 hours, three documents land in your inbox:

  • Compliance dossier for your DPO — PDMS index + RoPA + three v1.0 DPIAs + sub-processor list

Your DPO can review the compliance dossier in parallel with the academic conversation.

Day 2–3 — Countersignature

You countersign when ready. If it takes longer, the clock is yours — the timeline shifts but nothing escalates.

Day 4 — Two calls scheduled for Week 1

  • 30-minute academic kickoff with your project owner — class setup, success criteria, dashboard walkthrough
  • 30-minute DPO call with your data-protection lead — walks through the compliance dossier, answers any questions

Day 5 — Academic kickoff

Three things, in 30 minutes:

  1. Upload the student roster (CSV, or MIS export — iSAMS, Engage, Bromcom, Arbor)
  2. Create class structures by exam board, teacher, year group
  3. Agree the success criteria in writing

Day 6 — DPO call

Your DPO walks through the compliance pack with our DPO. 30 minutes. By the end, the school has everything it needs to satisfy a procurement-security audit on us.

Day 7 — First essay marked

Students log in. A Year-11 student picks up a practice paper, writes 600 words, submits. Within about a minute they have AI-marked feedback referencing the assessment objectives. Your project owner sees this happen in their dashboard.

What you don't need to do

The friction we deliberately remove

Most EdTech onboarding asks for things schools don't have time to give. Here is what we don't ask for, and why.

No teacher training week

The teacher dashboard is self-service for anyone who can use a web browser. Most teachers do not have any onboarding session beyond a short platform-tour video. If a teacher gets stuck, they email or WhatsApp the project owner, who escalates to us — answered within 24 working hours, direct.

No IT integration project

The platform is a web application. Students access it via a browser. Google Workspace and Microsoft 365 SSO are supported at annual deployment; the pilot uses school email + password.

No MIS sync (unless you want one)

Pilots use a CSV roster. Annual deployments above 500 students can opt into a one-way sync from iSAMS / Engage / Bromcom / Arbor — but a per-term CSV refresh works for most schools and removes a procurement dependency.

No long-form contract negotiation

The pilot agreement is one page. The DPA is our standard. Standard liability caps. No 40-page MSA, no protracted back-and-forth.

Student and teacher onboarding

What individual users see

Students

  1. Account activation email — sent automatically when their email is in the uploaded roster.
  2. First login — they choose their exam board (or it is pre-set by the teacher for the cohort) and year group.
  3. Children's Code high-privacy defaults are applied automatically — analytics off, marketing off, streak gamification off, public leaderboards off.
  4. For users aged 11–15, the signup flow asks for a parent or guardian email. The account does not fully activate until the parent clicks the consent-loop email (UK GDPR Article 8 — verifiable parental consent).
  5. For users whose declared country is Qatar, a named-destination cross-border consent panel appears under PDPPL Article 17 before account creation completes.
  6. First practice paper — pick a paper, write, submit, see AI-marked feedback within a minute.

Teachers

  1. The project owner adds them to a class in the teacher dashboard.
  2. First login — they see their classes, students' essays in a queue, the platform's analytics view.
  3. Optional short platform-tour video. No certification. No "Pro Teacher" badge to earn.
  4. First class-analytics review — at end of Week 1 they can already see who is and isn't using the platform, what the AI is flagging as common weak areas, and how marking turnaround compares to the old process.
Frequently asked "is this going to be hard" questions

Common concerns answered

Will our IT team need to be involved?

Only for SSO on annual deployment, if you want it. For the pilot, no.

Will our DPO need to be involved?

Yes — one 30-minute call in Week 1. We send the compliance pack 24 hours ahead so the DPO can read in their own time. After that call, no further DPO involvement during the pilot unless something changes.

Do we need to give you student personal data?

We need student email addresses, names, year group, and exam board — that is all. No home addresses, no parent income data, no medical information, no SEND flags unless you choose to share them.

What if the pilot doesn't work?

The school does not renew. No clawback. No "minimum 3-year" lock-in. A 48-hour exit pack is sent on request: full data export, deletion confirmation, audit certificate.
