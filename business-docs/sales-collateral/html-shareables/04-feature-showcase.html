Inside the platform

What students, teachers, parents, and leadership actually see. Every screen below is from the live site at theenglishhub.app.

Captured from the live application — not from a design mockup.

The platform is structured around four audiences: students, teachers, leadership, and parents. Each audience sees only the views that matter to their role, with high-privacy defaults enforced in code for any under-18 account.

Student experience

1. Student dashboard

Student dashboard Place screenshot at ./screenshots/01-student-dashboard.png https://theenglishhub.app/demo/student

The student's home view on first login. Recent essays with AI-marked bands, AO heat-map of weakness areas, recommended practice for today, mock exam history, and a working-at grade banner shown low-key (never as the headline number).

What's deliberately absent

No streak counters. No leaderboards. No "you're falling behind" nudges. These are off by default for any minor account, enforced in code under ICO Children's Code Standards 6 and 8.

2. AI essay marking — student view

AI marking output Place screenshot at ./screenshots/02-ai-marking-output.png https://theenglishhub.app/demo/student/practice

What a student sees within seconds of submitting a 600-word essay: a suggested band labelled clearly as a suggestion, paragraph-level annotations referencing the assessment objectives by name ("AO2 — methods analysis: the metaphor in para 3 is identified but not analysed"), strengths and next steps, and a "request human review" button that escalates any AI-marked essay to a qualified English teacher. Human review is the final word.

Teacher experience

3. Teacher dashboard — class view

Teacher class dashboard Place screenshot at ./screenshots/03-teacher-class-view.png https://theenglishhub.app/demo/teacher

Every class the teacher owns or contributes to, per-class adoption rate, the marking queue (essays awaiting optional teacher review — most go straight through), and a weak-area heatmap so the next lesson can target the actual gap.

4. Teacher essay-marking workflow

Side-by-side teacher review Place screenshot at ./screenshots/04-teacher-essay-marking.png https://theenglishhub.app/demo/teacher/essays

The screen splits three ways: student essay with AI annotations inline, AI-suggested AO breakdown, and a teacher override panel. The teacher's decision is the final word and is what appears on the student's record. Teacher time per essay in this workflow is typically 1–3 minutes — the teacher is editing the AI's output, not generating fresh marking.

Leadership view

5. School analytics

Department-wide leadership analytics Place screenshot at ./screenshots/05-school-analytics.png https://theenglishhub.app/demo/school/analytics

Department-wide adoption, marking-turnaround trend over time, cohort progress (Y10 / Y11 / Y12 / Y13 grade-band distribution), mock exam comparison across classes with statistical-significance flagging, and an EAL cohort surfaced as its own segment so progress is measured fairly.

6. School engagement and benchmarking

Engagement and cohort benchmarking Place screenshot at ./screenshots/07-school-engagement.png https://theenglishhub.app/demo/school/engagement

Engagement-by-week, year-on-year benchmark (this cohort vs last year's same cohort at the same point in the academic year), cross-class comparison, and an intervention queue surfacing students whose engagement has dropped in the past two weeks — flagged for the form tutor or Head of Year.

Parent view

7. Weekly parent progress email

Parent weekly email Place screenshot at ./screenshots/06-parent-weekly-email.png https://theenglishhub.app/dashboard/parent

The consent-gated weekly email sent to the parent or guardian of any linked under-18 account. Includes the child's name, week date range, essays completed, time on platform, working-at grade (with the "platform's internal estimate" caveat), 1–3 strengths, 1–3 areas to grow, one suggested action.

What the parent does NOT see

The text of any essay the child wrote. Any disclosure the child made in essay content. Anything not directly relevant to the child's English learning progress.

Content surfaces

8. Mock exam library

Mock exam library Place screenshot at ./screenshots/08-mock-exam-library.png https://theenglishhub.app/mock-exams

Every paper of every board — AQA, Pearson Edexcel, OCR, Eduqas, WJEC, Cambridge IGCSE. Timed mode runs the paper under actual exam conditions with on-screen countdown; mark-scheme-aligned AI marking at the end of each paper.

9. KS3 hub — Year 7 to Year 9

KS3 hub Place screenshot at ./screenshots/09-ks3-hub.png https://theenglishhub.app/ks3

Pearson Edexcel iLowerSecondary English coverage end-to-end — weekly lesson plans, half-term assessments, key vocabulary, skill codes mapped to GCSE/IGCSE assessment objectives.

10. EAL track

EAL hub Place screenshot at ./screenshots/10-eal-hub.png https://theenglishhub.app/eal

Ten grammar topics — word order, articles, verb tenses, modal verbs, prepositions, conjunctions, punctuation, cohesion, vocabulary academic and descriptive. A2 and B1 native bands live; B2 and C1 in content authoring with non-A2 routes currently noindexed pending genuine level-differentiated material.

Compliance UI — visible to your users

11. Qatar-specific cross-border consent panel

PDPPL Article 17 consent panel Place screenshot at ./screenshots/11-pdppl-consent-banner.png https://theenglishhub.app/auth/register

What a Qatar-resident user sees in the signup flow before account creation. Named destinations (EU, US, UK, Ireland, global edge), what's at each destination, the safeguards in place, a required checkbox, and the withdrawal route in plain text. Live as of 20 May 2026. Most EdTech platforms have not shipped this.

12. Privacy dashboard

Privacy and consent dashboard Place screenshot at ./screenshots/12-privacy-dashboard.png https://theenglishhub.app/dashboard/consent

Every consent the user has granted or withdrawn, with timestamp and version. Per-consent toggle to withdraw any optional consent immediately. Full data export in one click. Account deletion in one click. Parent-account view for linked parent accounts lets them see their child's consent ledger and act on the child's behalf.
