Four problems we address

What we hear from English departments

Marking turnaround

Departments running fortnightly practice essays for a Year-11 cohort are typically 7–14 days behind on marking. By the time feedback lands, the student has moved on.

Feedback density

Practice-essay feedback is, by necessity, light. No teacher has 90 minutes per essay to give the paragraph-level analysis that actually moves a student from a Grade 6 to a Grade 8.

EAL fairness

L1-Arabic students writing in English produce different mistakes from L1-English students. Conventional marking can penalise structural transfer errors as basic mistakes when the underlying thinking is sound.

AI without data-protection homework

Many AI education tools have not done the UK GDPR or PDPPL work. A procurement-aware school asking "where does our students' data live, who trains on it, what's our cross-border consent position?" gets a vague answer from most vendors.

What the platform does

Five capabilities, end to end

1. AI essay marking against the actual mark scheme

A student submits a practice essay. Within about a minute they receive a suggested band against the assessment objectives the mark scheme uses, paragraph-level feedback referencing those AOs by name, and concrete revision suggestions. Teachers remain the final authority on any grade that goes on a report — the AI feedback is formative practice feedback, never an awarded grade.

2. All six exam boards covered, end to end

AQA, Pearson Edexcel, OCR, Eduqas, WJEC, and Cambridge IGCSE. Set texts, language papers, mock exam library, mark schemes, and sample answers. KS3 coverage via Pearson iLowerSecondary English; A-level on roadmap.

3. EAL track with Gulf-Arabic L1 awareness

A2 and B1 native bands are live; B2 and C1 content is in authoring with non-A2 routes currently noindexed pending genuine level-differentiated material. Structural-transfer errors are handled fairly rather than penalised as basic mistakes.

4. Visibility for teachers, leadership, and parents

A teacher dashboard surfaces class-level adoption, weak-area heatmaps by AO, and mock-exam analytics. A leadership view shows department-wide progress, marking-turnaround trend, and cohort-on-cohort benchmarking. Consent-gated weekly parent emails summarise modules completed, time on platform, weak areas, and working-at grade — without sharing the text of a child's essays.

5. ICO Children's Code by default

Every under-18 account ships with high-privacy defaults enforced in code: personalised recommendations off, streak gamification off, behavioural nudges off, non-essential analytics off, marketing off, public leaderboards off, geolocation off. The defaults are asserted by a strict test suite covering ages 11 through 17.

Where we move the needle

The levers we move — honestly framed

A note on outcome claims

We do not claim a specific grade-improvement statistic. The platform is at launch stage and a validated outcome study has not yet been completed. What we do is move the four levers educational research consistently identifies as drivers of improvement in writing performance.

Lever 1 — Practice volume

The single biggest distinguishing feature of a Grade 9 candidate is the number of essays they wrote, marked, and revised before the exam. The platform makes that volume affordable: a Year 11 student can submit five essays in a week and receive specific feedback on all five.

Lever 2 — Feedback turnaround

Feedback that lands while the work is still mentally fresh is materially more useful than feedback that lands a fortnight later. The platform delivers AO-referenced feedback in about a minute.

Lever 3 — Criteria-referenced feedback

Generic feedback does not move a Grade 6 to a Grade 8. Feedback that names the assessment objective the student missed, gives a specific example of how they missed it, and shows what to do instead — that does. Every feedback line maps directly to the mark scheme the student is sitting.

Lever 4 — Mock exam practice under exam conditions

Timed practice papers under exam conditions are one of the most reliable predictors of exam performance. The mock exam library covers every paper of every board.
