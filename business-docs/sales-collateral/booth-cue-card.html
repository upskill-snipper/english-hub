Working the stand — opener & appetite read

Say this as they slow down (4 seconds — anchor + qualifier)
"We do AI marking for GCSE and IGCSE English — against the actual mark schemes. Are you here for a school, or in another role?"
It tells them what you do AND hands you the qualifier. Their answer decides which question you ask next ↓
Then branch the appetite question by who they are
Head of English / HoD
Your best buyer — lead with the pain they feel daily
"What's marking turnaround actually like in your department — from a student handing in a practice essay to getting it back?"
The number + the sigh tells you everything. 10+ days & a grimace = hot.
Principal / Deputy Head
Lead with visibility — the data/analytics door
"How much visibility do you actually have into how each English cohort is progressing between data drops?"
Leadership feels the visibility gap; HoDs feel the workload. Same product, different door.
Education consultant
Sell the multiplier — they advise many schools
"Which of the schools you work with are furthest along on AI in the English department — and what's holding the others back?"
One good consultant relationship = several schools.

After the appetite question — SHUT UP.

The 90 seconds of silence after "what's marking turnaround like?" is where they tell you if they're a buyer. Don't fill it.

Exit line for every conversation

"Grab the one-pager — and easiest way to talk further is dpo@theenglishhub.app, straight to me. Thanks for stopping."

Don't say

No exam-board endorsement. No "trusted by X schools". No grade guarantees. No "AI never gets it wrong" — teachers stay the final word.

Numbers you can quote

6 exam boards · pilot from £2,500 / £4,000 / custom · marking < 1 min · PDPPL Art. 17 live in signup · EU-region data.

Don't burn the day on students/parents — the floor is wall-to-wall universities recruiting them. Spend your hours on HoDs, deputies, principals, consultants.
