# IELTS Writing Examiner — Paid Marking Opportunity

**The English Hub** · Remote · Flexible · Paid per script

---

## In short

We're building an AI-assisted IELTS Writing tool that helps learners improve, and we need **qualified IELTS examiners** to mark a set of practice essays. Your expert marks become the **gold-standard reference** that we measure our AI against — your judgement is what makes the tool trustworthy. It's flexible, remote, paid per script, and takes about **10–15 hours total** over a couple of weeks.

---

## What you'd be doing

You'll mark **IELTS Writing Task 2 essays** against the four official assessment criteria, on the **0–9 band scale**:

1. **Task Response (TR)**
2. **Coherence & Cohesion (CC)**
3. **Lexical Resource (LR)**
4. **Grammatical Range & Accuracy (GRA)**

For each essay, through a simple online form, you give:
- a **band (0–9) for each of the four criteria**,
- a short note pointing to **evidence in the text** for each band (a sentence or two — what justifies it),
- the **overall band**.

That's it — exactly the marking you already do as an examiner, captured in a clean structured form instead of on paper.

---

## What you're helping build (and why your marks matter)

We've built an AI marker for IELTS Writing. Before it's ever shown to a single learner, we have to **prove it marks like a real examiner** — that it lands within half a band of qualified humans, reliably. Your marks are that human reference. We compare the AI to *you*; if it doesn't agree closely enough, it stays switched off. So you're not "training a machine to replace examiners" — you're the **quality benchmark that holds it to an examiner's standard.**

Some of the essays you mark will be **synthetically generated** (written by the system to practise across the full band range). You mark them exactly as you would any real essay — band them on what's on the page.

## Who we're looking for

You'd be a great fit if you have:

- **Current or recent IELTS examiner certification** (Writing), **or** equivalent expertise — e.g. DELTA/CELTA plus substantial IELTS-preparation teaching and marking against the public band descriptors;
- **Fluent command of the IELTS Writing band descriptors** for all four criteria;
- **Native or near-native English**;
- **Consistency and attention to detail** — your marks need to hold up against another examiner's;
- Comfort working **online and independently** to a deadline.

Senior/lead examiner experience is a plus — we also need someone to adjudicate the occasional disagreement and write short "why this is a Band 6" exemplar notes, which is paid at a higher rate.

## The process, end to end

1. **Apply** — reply with your IELTS examiner credentials and availability.
2. **Onboard** — we set you up with a marker account, confirm your rate, and you accept a short consent (that your marks may be used to calibrate the tool).
3. **Get your queue** — a batch of IELTS Writing Task 2 essays appears in your dashboard.
4. **Mark** — work through them at your own pace via the online form (band per criterion + brief evidence note + overall band). Each essay is **independently marked by two examiners** — you won't see the other's marks.
5. **Quality check** — where two markers differ by more than a band, a senior examiner adjudicates. This is normal and expected.
6. **Get paid** — once your marks are approved, payment is calculated automatically per script.

**Total commitment:** about **10–15 hours**, flexible, over roughly **two weeks**.

## What we ask of you

- Mark **independently** — to your own examiner judgement, against the descriptors, not to a target.
- Be **consistent** — apply the same standard across every script.
- **Quote the evidence** — a line or two from the essay for each band, so your mark is auditable.
- **Confidentiality** — treat the essays and the work as confidential.
- **Honesty** — if an essay is off-topic, under-length, or looks memorised, mark it as you would in a real exam and flag it.
