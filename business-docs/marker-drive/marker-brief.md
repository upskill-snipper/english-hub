# Marker & Examiner Brief — Paid, Project-Based Opportunity

**The English Hub** · Remote · Flexible · Project-based pay (agreed up front)

---

## In short

We're building AI-assisted marking and feedback across our whole platform — **GCSE & IGCSE English (Language and Literature), KS3 English, IELTS (Academic & General Training), and EAL** — and we need **qualified markers and examiners** to mark real student work. Your expert marks become the **gold-standard reference** we measure our AI against: your judgement is what makes the tool trustworthy.

It's remote, flexible, and **paid per project** — each piece of work is scoped and agreed with you individually before you start. You apply for the **boards, levels and offerings you're qualified in**; you only mark in your areas of expertise.

---

## What you'd be doing

You'll mark student work against the **relevant mark scheme or band descriptors for your area** — exactly the marking you already do as an examiner or experienced marker, captured in a clean structured form instead of on paper.

For each script, through a simple online form, you give:
- the **marks or band(s)** per the mark scheme / criteria for that qualification,
- a short note pointing to the **evidence in the work** that justifies each mark (a sentence or two),
- the **overall mark or band**.

Where we're calibrating a new area, some scripts will be **double-marked** (two markers mark the same script independently — you won't see the other's marks), and a senior marker adjudicates any significant disagreement. This is normal and expected.

---

## What you're helping build (and why your marks matter)

We've built an AI marker for these qualifications. Before it's ever shown to a single learner, we have to **prove it marks like a real examiner** — that it lands close to qualified humans, reliably, for that board and level. Your marks are that human reference. We compare the AI to *you*; if it doesn't agree closely enough, it stays switched off for that area.

So you're not "training a machine to replace examiners" — you're the **quality benchmark that holds it to an examiner's standard.** Some scripts you mark will be **synthetically generated** (written by the system to practise across the full range, copyright-clean, with no real student's personal details). You mark them exactly as you would any real script — on what's on the page.

## Who we're looking for

You'd be a great fit if you have **current or recent marking/examining expertise in one or more** of our areas:

- **GCSE / IGCSE English** — a current or recent **examiner** for AQA, Edexcel, OCR, Eduqas, Cambridge (0500/0990) or Edexcel IGCSE (4ET1), **or** an experienced English teacher who marks confidently to those boards' mark schemes;
- **KS3 English** — an experienced secondary English teacher fluent in KS3 assessment;
- **IELTS** — current or recent **certified IELTS Writing examiner**, or equivalent (DELTA/CELTA + substantial IELTS-prep marking to the public band descriptors);
- **EAL** — qualified and experienced in assessing English-as-an-Additional-Language learners.

Across all areas we look for: **fluent command of the relevant mark scheme / descriptors**, native or near-native English, **consistency and attention to detail** (your marks need to hold up against another marker's), and comfort working online and independently to a deadline.

Senior / lead examiner experience is a plus — we also need people to **adjudicate** occasional disagreements and write short "why this mark / why this band" exemplar notes, scoped as their own paid pieces of work.

## How payment works — project-based

- **Pay is per project, not a fixed per-script rate.** Before you start, we agree the scope with you individually: a defined **quota of scripts and/or services** (for example, "mark 40 GCSE AQA Paper 1 scripts", or "double-mark a 60-essay IELTS calibration set plus 6 exemplar annotations") for an **agreed fee**.
- The **fee is agreed up front, with you, in writing** — it reflects your experience, the qualification, and the volume and complexity of the work.
- **Once you're approved in the system** (your credentials checked and accepted), you can pick up and mark **as much as you like** — but you are **only paid for the quota and services agreed up front** for each project. Anything beyond an agreed project isn't chargeable unless we've scoped and agreed a further project with you.
- Payment is made promptly once your completed, QA-passed work for that project is approved.

This keeps it simple and fair on both sides: you always know exactly what you'll be paid before you begin, and there are no surprises.

## The process, end to end

1. **Apply** — tell us your credentials and which **boards, levels and offerings** you're qualified for, plus your availability.
2. **Get approved** — we verify your credentials and set you up as an approved marker in the system (with a short confidentiality/consent agreement). Your approved areas determine the work you can see.
3. **Agree a project** — we scope a specific piece of work with you (the quota of scripts and/or services) and **agree the fee up front**.
4. **Get your queue** — the agreed batch appears in your dashboard.
5. **Mark** — work through it at your own pace via the online form (marks/bands per the scheme + brief evidence notes + overall). Calibration sets are independently double-marked.
6. **Quality check** — significant disagreements between two markers are adjudicated by a senior examiner.
7. **Get paid** — once your project's marks are approved, you're paid the agreed project fee.

Approved markers can be invited to **further projects** over time as we expand coverage across boards and offerings.

---

## What we ask of you

- Mark **independently** — to your own examiner judgement, against the mark scheme / descriptors, not to a target.
- Be **consistent** — apply the same standard across every script.
- **Quote the evidence** — a line or two from the work for each mark, so your judgement is auditable.
- **Confidentiality** — treat the work and the materials as confidential.
- **Honesty** — if a script is off-topic, under-length, or looks memorised/copied, mark it as you would in a real exam and flag it.

## Important, in plain terms

- This is **practice-feedback tooling**, clearly distinct from any official exam — learners are told that explicitly.
- Your marks are used to **measure and improve the tool's accuracy** for each qualification, never passed off as an official result.
- We follow UK data-protection law; the scripts you mark contain **no real candidate's personal details**.
