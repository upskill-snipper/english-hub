## 1. Why this document exists

The English Hub's primary users are GCSE students, most of whom are 14–16 years old and many of whom are under the age of digital consent when they first encounter us. Any publisher working in the UK schools space must be able to demonstrate to schools, to exam boards, and to parents that its platform meets the standards of Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSIE), UK GDPR / the Data Protection Act 2018, the Age Appropriate Design Code, and the principles of the Online Safety Act 2023 as they apply to services likely to be accessed by children.

AQA, as an exam board whose brand would attach to our platform under approved status, will want to see clear evidence that we take this seriously.

## 2. Our safeguarding stance in one paragraph

The English Hub is designed to be a safe, low-risk environment for students. We do not offer open chat, direct messaging between users, public forums, or any feature that creates a route for strangers to contact children. We do not collect data we do not need. We do not advertise to children. We have a Designated Safeguarding Lead with clear escalation routes, and we handle any disclosure that reaches us in line with KCSIE expectations.

## 6. Content moderation

The English Hub is primarily a one-way content platform. We do not run open user-generated content. Where users do interact with content (quiz answers, essay submissions):

- All text inputs are filtered for abusive language and PII.
- No user input is ever publicly visible to other users.
- Teachers can see their own students' submissions; nobody else can.
- We never publish or share student writing without explicit written consent.

## 7. Content that touches sensitive themes

GCSE English set texts include sensitive themes: violence, self-harm, mental illness, race and racism, sexual violence in historical texts, war, bereavement. We treat these responsibly:

- **Context framing.** Content that engages with sensitive themes opens with a framing paragraph that acknowledges the difficulty.
- **Safeguarding review.** Any new unit touching a sensitive theme goes through a safeguarding review as part of the editorial workflow.
- **Support signposting.** Where appropriate, we signpost to external support (Childline, Samaritans, Shout) at the end of relevant content.
- **Historical language in set texts.** Where a set text contains racialised language or other difficult historical language, we handle it through contextual commentary rather than by censoring the text. AQA itself expects candidates to be taught to engage with these texts critically.
