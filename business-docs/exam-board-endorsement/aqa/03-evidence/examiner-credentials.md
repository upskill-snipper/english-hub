# Examiner Credentials — Evidence Template and Register

> **Critical document.** The single strongest credibility lever we have with AQA is the presence on our team of contributors who are or have been AQA examiners for GCSE English. This document is the register of those contributors and the template for the evidence we present to AQA.

## 1. Why this matters

AQA's published guidance is explicit: their approval process is run by subject experts, and they select publishing partners "carefully." Peer publishers consistently report that the single biggest factor in whether a publisher is taken seriously is the editorial and examining track record of the team.

If we have contributors who have marked AQA GCSE English papers, trained AQA examiners, written AQA specification commentary, or chaired AQA English panels — that is gold. It should be front and centre.

**But:** this must be handled carefully because of AQA's Authoring Conflict of Interest Policy. An examiner contributing to a third-party resource must declare that work to AQA, and AQA may require the work to be structured to avoid the examiner having access to privileged information. If we list an examiner who hasn't declared their work to AQA, we expose that person to sanction and we damage the relationship.

## 2. The rules

From AQA's authoring conflict of interest policy (version 3.3, publicly published):

- AQA examiners who author for third parties must declare the work to AQA.
- Examiners cannot use non-public specification information in third-party resources.
- There are cooling-off expectations for senior roles (chief examiner, principal examiner) before they can author for third parties on the same specification.
- AQA can require the authoring work to be restructured or stopped if a conflict is identified.

**Action for the team:** every contributor on this register who has AQA examining history must have:

1. Declared their authoring work to AQA in writing (dated, on file)
2. Confirmed in writing that they have not used non-public information
3. Confirmed they understand the cooling-off expectations if applicable
4. Signed our internal conflict-of-interest attestation

Without those four items we do not list the person on AQA-facing material.

## 6. What we avoid claiming

- "Written by AQA examiners" (if we cannot fully evidence that for every page)
- "All our content is written by current AQA examiners" (unless 100% true)
- "AQA-endorsed authors" (no such thing)
- "Ex-AQA chief examiner" (only if fully accurate, and the person is demonstrably out of cooling-off)
- Named AQA roles without the person's consent
