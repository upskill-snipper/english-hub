# Accuracy Commitment — The English Hub

> Public-facing draft. Intended to be published on the site AND attached to the AQA application pack.
> Purpose: bind us (and show AQA) to a specific, verifiable accuracy and error-correction policy.

## Our commitment

The English Hub exists to help students and teachers understand GCSE English better. That is only possible if what we publish is accurate, specification-aligned, and honestly maintained. This document sets out the commitments we make on accuracy — and what we will do when we get something wrong.

## 1. What "accurate" means here

Accuracy has five dimensions for us:

1. **Factual accuracy.** Historical facts, biographical facts, critical terminology, and textual references must be correct.
2. **Textual accuracy.** Quotations from set texts must match the canonical edition we are citing. Page references and line numbers must be correct.
3. **Specification accuracy.** Every claim about AQA's specification must match AQA's current published wording for that specification. If AQA says a paper is 1 hour 45 minutes, we do not round it.
4. **Assessment accuracy.** Every claim about command words, Assessment Objectives, mark schemes, or exam format must reflect current AQA practice.
5. **Pedagogical honesty.** We do not teach shortcuts that contradict AQA examiner reports, and we do not sell grade guarantees.

## 2. How we prevent errors

- Two-person review on substantive content (writer and reviewer).
- Subject reviewers are qualified English teachers, many with AQA examining experience.
- Specification references are verified against AQA's live published spec at sign-off.
- An annual editorial audit of every set-text unit and every language paper sequence.
- An active monitoring process for AQA examiner reports so we can incorporate feedback quickly.

2. **Triage** — is this a typo, a factual error, a specification misalignment, a pedagogical issue, or a safeguarding issue? — within [PLACEHOLDER — target: 5 business days].
3. **Action** — correction, clarification, rewrite, or temporary content removal — within a time window proportional to severity:
   - **Critical** (factually wrong in a way that could cost a student marks, or a safeguarding issue): [PLACEHOLDER — target: 48 hours].
   - **High** (specification misalignment or misleading claim): [PLACEHOLDER — target: 5 business days].
   - **Medium** (non-critical factual error): [PLACEHOLDER — target: 15 business days].

4. **Transparent change log.** Every correction is recorded with: what was wrong, what we changed it to, when, who approved it.
5. **Public errata log** for corrections affecting content users may have relied on.

## 5. Specification updates

AQA updates specifications periodically. When AQA publishes any change to the 8700 or 8702 specifications (including set-text list changes), we commit to:

- **Detecting the change** within [PLACEHOLDER — 5 business days] via our spec-monitoring process.
- **Impact-assessing** the change against all affected content within [PLACEHOLDER — 10 business days].
- **Publishing affected updates** within [PLACEHOLDER — 30/60/90 days] of the original change, depending on scope.
- **Notifying teachers** who subscribe to change alerts.
- **Versioning** affected content so teachers can see what changed.

## 6. What we will never claim

To be explicit:

- **No grade guarantees.** Nothing on The English Hub will ever guarantee a Grade 4, 5, 7, 8, or 9, regardless of how many hours a student spends on the platform.
- **No "hacks" or "secrets."** We will never imply that there is a shortcut to GCSE English that AQA's examiners aren't aware of. There isn't.
- **No claim to be AQA.** We are not AQA, and nothing on the platform will imply that AQA wrote, owns, approves, or endorses our content — unless and until AQA grants us formal approved status, and only within the scope of that approval.
- **No misrepresentation of examiner reports.** If AQA's examiner report says most students struggle with X, we do not pretend most students breeze through X.
- **No reproduction of past-paper questions without proper attribution and within copyright limits.**

## 7. Independent accountability

We invite accountability:

- Users can report any claim they believe is inaccurate.
- Teachers can escalate anything that reads as misaligned with the specification.
- AQA itself can, at any time, tell us something on our platform misrepresents the specification — and we commit to acting on such feedback promptly, whether or not AQA has formally approved our resource.

## 8. What we ask of ourselves

- When in doubt, cite the source.
- When the source is unclear, ask an examiner.
- When a student's grade depends on it, be conservative.
- When we are wrong, say so quickly and fix it cleanly.
- When the specification changes, update the content, not the interpretation of the old content.

## 9. Review date

This commitment is reviewed every 12 months and whenever AQA updates its specification or examiner guidance.
