## 4. How to find the right person

Priority order for the research task:

1. **Start on the AQA contact page.** Look for any publisher-specific routing. [aqa.org.uk/contact-us](https://www.aqa.org.uk/contact-us)
2. **Call the main number.** 0800 197 7162. Ask for "the team that handles publisher approvals for GCSE English resources." Get a name and an email address.
3. **LinkedIn search.** Search for "AQA" with job titles containing: publisher, partnerships, approved resources, approvals, publishing, partner manager, GCSE English lead.
4. **Check approved-publisher websites.** Cambridge University Press, Hodder, Oxford University Press — see if any have a named AQA liaison they credit publicly.
5. **Attend an AQA CPD event or teacher conference.** Physical proximity to AQA staff is the highest-signal way to get introduced.
6. **Ask our examiner contributors.** Anyone on our team who is a current AQA examiner will know the internal structure. Use that network (subject to CoI considerations — don't compromise anyone).
7. **Warm intro via the education sector.** Shared investors, former AQA staff in industry, mutual contacts at MATs, etc.

## 7. Relationship etiquette

A short cultural briefing for everyone who talks to AQA on our behalf:

- **AQA is a charity.** They are driven by educational integrity, not by commercial growth. Pitch like you understand that.
- **They are protective of the badge.** Every conversation should reinforce that we respect the badge and the integrity it represents.
- **They are selective.** They choose 2–3 publishers per subject. Be memorable but not pushy.
- **They work on academic-year cycles.** August–October and February–April are typically the right windows for approaching new topics.
- **They do not like hype.** No hockey-stick charts, no "our AI-powered revolutionary" language. Calm, professional, content-led.
- **They will remember a badly handled contact.** Get it right the first time.
