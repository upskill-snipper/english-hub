## What "approval" actually means at AQA

AQA is careful about terminology. The three tiers to understand:

1. **AQA Approved** — the badge. AQA's subject experts work through a detailed 4-stage process with the publisher and sign off that the resource genuinely supports an AQA specification. This is the gold standard and the one worth pursuing.
2. **AQA Endorsed Resource** — a lighter-touch designation historically used by other boards more than AQA; AQA's current public-facing language centres on "approved" rather than "endorsed."
3. **"Aligned to AQA"** — a self-claim. Anyone can say this. It carries no AQA warranty and must not use the AQA badge or logo.

See `01-research/aqa-endorsement-vs-approved.md` for the full breakdown.

## How AQA's process actually works (summary)

Based on AQA's public guidance ([aqa.org.uk approved textbooks page](https://www.aqa.org.uk/about-us/who-we-are/our-standards/approved-textbooks)):

- **AQA initiates.** When AQA begins work on a specification (or a revision), they contact publishers they believe are a good fit and invite them into the process. It is *not* an open application portal — the normal entry route is being known to the partnerships team before a specification cycle opens.
- **Selection.** AQA judges proposals against internal selection criteria and typically selects **2–3 publishers per subject**.
- **4-stage approval.** Selected publishers then work through a detailed four-stage approval process with AQA's subject experts. AQA is explicit that approval is not automatic and publishers may be asked to repeat a stage.
- **Eligibility.** AQA only approves **print and digital textbooks**. Other formats (apps, pure video, tutoring platforms) may not be eligible under the current policy and may require a bespoke conversation.

> **Strategic implication for The English Hub:** because AQA doesn't run an open application, Step 1 of our plan is not "send the application" — it's "get on AQA's radar as a credible candidate before the next 8700/8702 specification refresh cycle." The pack in this folder is what we take into that conversation.
