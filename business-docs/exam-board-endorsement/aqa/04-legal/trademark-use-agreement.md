# Trademark Use — Draft Internal Agreement and Playbook

> INTERNAL DRAFT, NOT A LEGAL DOCUMENT. This is our preparation for AQA's actual badge-use agreement, not a substitute for it.
> Purpose: make sure we understand the rules AQA imposes on publishers using the "AQA approved" badge and the AQA name, so we know what to expect in negotiation and what we will be bound by if approved.

## 1. AQA's real document

AQA publishes a document titled "Use of the AQA approved badge and the AQA name by publishers" (available on the AQA store). Any publisher who enters the approval process receives a definitive version of this. Our internal notes below are based on public information about how AQA handles the trademark and on standard exam-board practice — they are not a substitute for the real document we will receive from AQA, which must be reviewed by qualified legal counsel.

**Action:** before we rely on anything in this file, obtain the current version of AQA's badge-use document and have counsel read it in full.

## 2. The core principles we expect to be bound by

Based on AQA's public statements and peer publisher experience, we can anticipate the following:

### 2.1 The badge is a trademark
- The "AQA approved" badge and the AQA name are trademarks belonging to AQA.
- Use is permitted only under a licence AQA grants and can revoke.
- AQA retains control of how the badge is displayed.

### 2.2 Scope is strict
- The licence only covers resources that have actually been through the approval process.
- A publisher may not put the badge on a resource just because a related resource was approved.
- A publisher may not put the badge on marketing for a resource that has not yet been approved.
- Bundling an approved resource with an unapproved resource in a way that implies the unapproved one is approved is prohibited.

### 2.3 Display rules
- The badge must be displayed in the form AQA provides.
- It must not be altered in colour, proportion, or added text.
- It must not be placed against backgrounds that obscure it.
- Minimum size rules apply.

### 2.4 Language rules
- The publisher may say the resource is "approved by AQA" or carries "AQA approval."
- The publisher may not say AQA "recommends," "guarantees," "authorises teaching," "ensures pass," or uses any similar language AQA hasn't authorised.
- The publisher may not imply that AQA authored the resource.
- The publisher may not imply that using the resource is required or sufficient to pass the qualification.

### 2.5 Misuse consequences
- AQA reserves the right to revoke the licence if the publisher misuses the badge.
- AQA can require the publisher to recall or destroy any material that misuses the badge.
- Revocation can apply to one title or to the entire publisher relationship.
- Revocation may be published by AQA.

### 2.6 Scope per resource
- Approval is per-resource, not per-publisher. The English Hub would not automatically be "an AQA approved publisher" — individual resources would be AQA approved and they would be the only places the badge can appear.
- If we approve, for example, our Literature content, we cannot use the badge on our Language content unless Language content has separately been approved.

### 2.7 Expiry and re-approval
- Approval is tied to a specification. When the specification is updated, approval may need to be re-earned.
- Expired approvals must have the badge removed from live marketing.

## 3. What this means for how we build our site today

Even before we enter the approval process, the way we talk about AQA on the site affects whether AQA will take us seriously. Therefore, as internal policy, effective immediately:

- [ ] **No use of the AQA logo, badge, or any logo resembling them** on any English Hub property.
- [ ] **No use of the phrase "AQA approved" or "AQA endorsed"** anywhere on the site.
- [ ] **"Aligned to AQA" is acceptable** as factual description.
- [ ] **Use of specification codes (8700, 8702) is acceptable** as factual navigation.
- [ ] **References to papers, AOs, and command words are acceptable** as factual content.
- [ ] **No domain name, product name, or tagline that incorporates "AQA"** in a way that could imply endorsement.
- [ ] **No meta descriptions or schema.org markup that implies endorsement.**
- [ ] **No paid search ads that imply endorsement.**
- [ ] **No influencer or affiliate content that claims endorsement.**

## 4. What will change the moment AQA approves us

If AQA grants approved status to a specific resource, at that moment we can (subject to AQA's actual licence terms):

- Display the AQA approved badge on the cover, landing page, and marketing of the specific approved resource.
- State in copy that the resource is "AQA approved."
- Use the badge in approved printed materials, digital ads, and teacher outreach.
- Be listed in AQA's own published approved-resources catalogue.

Still not allowed:
- Imply AQA wrote the resource.
- Imply AQA is a commercial partner beyond the specific scope of the approval.
- Imply that other, non-approved English Hub resources are also approved.
- Use the badge after approval expires.

## 5. Internal controls we will put in place

If we pursue approval, we will put these controls in before any badge appears anywhere:

- [ ] Single source of truth for where the badge is allowed to appear (a whitelist of URLs and product IDs).
- [ ] Marketing pre-publication review gate: any copy mentioning AQA passes through the endorsement lead.
- [ ] Quarterly audit of our site and marketing materials for badge-use compliance.
- [ ] Named individual responsible for badge-use compliance.
- [ ] Prompt removal process if approval is revoked or expires.
- [ ] Backup of the AQA licence terms on file with version history.

## 6. Dispute process

In the unlikely event AQA raises a concern about badge use:

1. Acknowledge within 24 hours.
2. Escalate internally to the endorsement lead and CEO.
3. Take down disputed material within 48 hours while the dispute is assessed.
4. Engage AQA in good faith to resolve.
5. Document the incident and the resolution.

## 7. Legal review checklist

Before signing anything with AQA:

- [ ] AQA's actual badge-use document received in writing
- [ ] Reviewed by qualified UK trademark counsel
- [ ] Reviewed by UK commercial counsel for liability / indemnity clauses
- [ ] Specific territory scope confirmed (we expect UK-only, possibly with exceptions)
- [ ] Specific product scope confirmed
- [ ] Revocation terms understood
- [ ] Term length and renewal process understood
- [ ] Any fee obligations understood
- [ ] Any audit rights for AQA understood

## 8. What we will push back on

Topics we expect may need negotiation (subject to AQA's starting position):

- Breadth of product scope (one resource versus a family of resources)
- Audit and reporting obligations (keeping them reasonable)
- Clauses that would give AQA editorial control beyond the approval review
- Clauses that would restrict our ability to support other exam boards (e.g. Pearson)
- Indemnity caps
- Dispute resolution jurisdiction

## 9. What we will not push back on

- Trademark protection terms
- Rules against misleading claims
- Rules against reproduction of past papers or mark schemes
- AQA's right to revoke for misuse
- AQA's ultimate authority over the approved badge
- Safeguarding and accuracy obligations
