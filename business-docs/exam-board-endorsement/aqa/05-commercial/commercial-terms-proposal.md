## 1. Purpose

This document sets out The English Hub's opening position on the commercial terms for an approved-resources relationship with AQA, covering the GCSE English Literature (8702) and GCSE English Language (8700) specifications. It is intended as the starting point for a good-faith negotiation and all figures are subject to discussion.

## 2. Structure

We propose a commercial structure with three components:

1. A one-off application and approval fee
2. An annual licence fee per approved resource
3. A royalty on attributable net revenue

## 3. Scope of approval

### 3.1 What would be approved

We propose the following resources for approval:

- **Resource A:** The English Hub — GCSE English Literature (8702) Learning Platform
- **Resource B:** The English Hub — GCSE English Language (8700) Learning Platform

Each resource is a defined subset of The English Hub platform: the set-text units, poetry studies, paper-specific teaching sequences, practice questions, mark schemes, and teacher-facing resources aligned to the relevant specification.

### 3.2 Companion PDF study guides

If AQA's current "print and digital textbooks" policy requires a textbook-format artefact for eligibility, we propose producing:

- **Resource A-PDF:** AQA Approved Companion — GCSE English Literature Study Guide (PDF, per set text)
- **Resource B-PDF:** AQA Approved Companion — GCSE English Language Study Guide (PDF)

These would be downloadable PDFs derived from the same editorial content as the platform, with the AQA approved badge displayed on the cover of each PDF. They would be bundled with platform subscriptions at no additional cost.

### 3.3 Out of scope

Content outside the approved resource scope (e.g. our Key Stage 3 content, any future A-level content, or content for other exam boards) is not covered by the approval or by the royalty.

## 4. Fees and royalties — opening position

### 4.1 Application and approval fee

We propose a **one-off application and approval fee of £[PLACEHOLDER]** per resource, payable in two tranches:
- 50% on entry to Stage 1 of the approval process
- 50% on successful completion of Stage 4 and grant of the badge

If the resource does not achieve approval, we would not expect to be charged the second tranche.

### 4.2 Annual licence fee

We propose an **annual licence fee of £[PLACEHOLDER]** per approved resource, payable on the anniversary of approval, covering the right to display the AQA approved badge and to be listed in AQA's approved resources catalogue.

### 4.3 Royalty

We propose a **royalty of 7.5% on attributable net revenue**.

**Definitions:**

- **"Attributable net revenue"** means the share of the English Hub's net revenue from subscription sales that is attributable to approved content, calculated as:

  `attributable net revenue = net revenue × (approved content share)`

- **"Net revenue"** means gross subscription sales less: VAT and other sales taxes; refunds and chargebacks; payment-processing fees; amounts attributable to complimentary or evaluation accounts.

- **"Approved content share"** is a percentage agreed between the parties at the time of approval and reviewed annually, reflecting the proportion of platform value, student engagement, or content volume attributable to approved content. For the first approval period we propose this is set at 80%.

### 4.4 Reporting

We propose quarterly reporting to AQA within [PLACEHOLDER — 30/45] days of each calendar quarter-end, including:

- Total subscriptions
- Subscriptions by customer type (individual, school)
- Gross revenue
- Deductions to net
- Attributable net revenue
- Royalty payable

### 4.5 Payment

Royalty is payable within [PLACEHOLDER — 30/60] days of the reporting date, in GBP by bank transfer.

### 4.6 Audit rights

We accept AQA's right to audit royalty reporting once per calendar year, on reasonable notice, conducted by AQA's finance team or an agreed independent auditor, under confidentiality. We propose the costs of the audit are borne by AQA unless the audit identifies a material underpayment (defined as greater than 5% of the relevant period's royalty), in which case we bear reasonable costs.

## 5. Term

We propose an initial term running from the date of approval until the next material revision of the relevant specification, with a minimum initial term of three (3) years.

At the end of the initial term, we propose an **expedited renewal process** in which a substantively unchanged approved resource can be re-approved on a light-touch basis without re-running the full 4-stage process from scratch.

## 6. Reciprocal commitments from AQA

In return for the fees and royalty above, we ask that AQA commit to:

- Listing each approved resource in AQA's published approved-resources catalogue with a live link to the resource's landing page.
- Referring the resource where appropriate in AQA CPD materials, teacher newsletters, and partnership communications.
- Providing The English Hub with the same advance access to specification drafts that other approved publishers receive.
- Naming a point of contact at AQA for editorial and commercial questions during the term.
- Giving reasonable notice (minimum [PLACEHOLDER — 90 days]) of any change to badge-use rules or approval requirements.

## 7. What we are not asking for

We are not asking for:
- Exclusivity
- Preferential treatment over other approved publishers
- Any right to use the AQA name beyond the terms of the badge-use licence
- Any discount on AQA's standard processes

## 8. What we are not able to offer

- Print manufacturing and distribution (we are a digital business)
- Physical distribution to schools
- Royalties on gross rather than net revenue
- Royalties on non-approved content
- Exclusivity to AQA (we need the option to pursue relationships with other boards for different content)

## 9. Termination

- **For cause:** either party may terminate for material breach on 30 days' notice to cure. AQA may terminate immediately in case of material badge misuse.
- **For convenience:** we propose that neither party may terminate for convenience during the initial term, except by mutual agreement.
- **Post-termination wind-down:** 90 days to remove badges from all marketing and product surfaces; existing paying subscribers retain access for the remainder of their current term with appropriate notice of the status change.

## 10. Confidentiality

The specific fees and royalty rates in this document are commercially confidential between the parties. The existence of the approved-resource relationship is public once granted.

## 11. Governing law

England and Wales.
