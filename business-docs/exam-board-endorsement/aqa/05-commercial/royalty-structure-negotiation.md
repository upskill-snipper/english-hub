# Royalty Structure — Expected Rates and Negotiation Points

> Internal strategy document. Not a contract. Not legal or financial advice.
> Purpose: give the team a defensible starting position and clear boundaries for a royalty negotiation with AQA.

## 1. Important honesty upfront

AQA does not publish its commercial terms. Anything below is drawn from (a) peer reporting by publishers who have been through exam-board approval processes with AQA and with other UK boards, (b) standard educational publishing industry ranges, and (c) common sense about what AQA needs to cover. The numbers here are for internal planning and must be verified in actual conversation with AQA.

## 2. How exam boards typically get paid

Exam boards that run publisher approval schemes typically monetise the relationship in one or more of the following ways:

1. **Upfront application / review fees.** A flat fee for entering the approval process, often per title.
2. **Annual licence fees.** A recurring fee for the right to display the badge.
3. **Royalties on net sales.** A percentage of the publisher's net revenue from the approved resource.
4. **Per-unit fees.** A flat amount per copy sold (more common for physical textbooks).
5. **Combinations of the above.**

AQA's model is widely believed to include a royalty component for approved textbooks. The exact structure is commercially confidential.

## 3. Indicative ranges

Based on industry norms for educational approved-resource royalties:

| Component | Low estimate | Typical | High estimate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Review / application fee (flat, per title) | £2,000 | £5,000–£15,000 | £25,000+ |
| Annual licence fee per approved title | £0 | £2,000–£10,000 | £25,000 |
| Royalty rate on net revenue | 5% | 7.5%–12% | 15%+ |
| Per-unit fee (if applicable) | £0.50 | £1–£3 | £5+ |

For a digital subscription business like The English Hub, royalty on net revenue is the most likely structure — per-unit fees map poorly to SaaS pricing.

## 4. What "net revenue" probably means

Expect AQA's definition to be narrower than "gross sales." Common carve-outs:

- Gross sales
- Less VAT / sales tax
- Less refunds and chargebacks
- Less bulk/trade discounts beyond a threshold
- Less returns (not applicable for digital)
- Less payment-processing fees (sometimes allowed, sometimes not)

We should push for:
- Payment-processing fees to be deductible
- Refunds and chargebacks to be deductible
- Bulk school licence discounts to be part of the net calculation, not the gross
- Complimentary licences (to teachers, schools for evaluation) excluded

## 5. Scope of the royalty

Crucial issue: on what does the royalty apply?

- **Option A: Only the approved resource.** If only our Literature content is approved, the royalty only applies to revenue attributable to Literature content.
- **Option B: The platform as a whole.** If the platform is approved, royalty applies to all subscription revenue.
- **Option C: A blended calculation.** The approved resource is a defined fraction of the platform, and the royalty applies to that fraction.

**Our preferred position:** A blended calculation that reflects the actual share of student attention or content volume attributable to the approved resource. A 100% platform royalty is not acceptable if only a subset of content is approved.

## 6. What we are willing to offer

A defensible opening position that respects AQA's legitimate interests:

- **Review/application fee:** accept a reasonable flat fee (we budget up to £25k)
- **Annual licence fee:** accept a modest recurring fee tied to scope
- **Royalty:** open at 7.5% of attributable net revenue, with a cap
- **Reporting:** quarterly sales reporting in an agreed format
- **Audit rights:** accept AQA's right to audit sales reporting, subject to reasonable notice and confidentiality

## 7. Hard limits

Lines we do not cross without board approval:

- Royalty rate above 15% of net revenue
- Royalty on gross rather than net
- Royalty on the full platform when only a subset is approved
- Any clause that gives AQA a claim on equity or acquisition proceeds
- Any clause that restricts our ability to raise capital
- Any clause that locks us into AQA exclusively (we need the option to also pursue Pearson/Edexcel and others)
- Any MFN ("most favoured nation") clause that would force us to give AQA any better terms we offer another board
- Unlimited liability or indemnity
- Termination-for-convenience clauses in AQA's favour without reasonable notice

## 8. Reciprocal asks

In exchange for committing to a royalty, we should ask for:

- **Catalogue listing.** Inclusion in AQA's published approved-resources catalogue with live links.
- **Reasonable promotion.** Mention in AQA CPD events, teacher newsletters, and partner materials where appropriate.
- **Early specification access.** Standard for approved publishers — access to specification drafts ahead of public release so we can prepare resources for launch.
- **Examiner report briefings.** Advance or supplementary briefings for approved publishers.
- **Support ticket channel.** A named point of contact at AQA for editorial questions.
- **Reasonable notice of changes.** If AQA updates the specification, we need reasonable notice and time to update content before badges lapse.
- **Right to renew.** Clear path to re-approval at the end of a specification cycle without re-running the full 4-stage process from scratch, assuming the resource is substantively unchanged.

## 9. Modelling financial impact

On the basis of a **7.5% royalty on 80% of net revenue** (assuming 80% of subscription revenue is attributable to approved content), the table below shows the annual cost of the royalty at different revenue scales:

| Annual recurring revenue | Attributable (80%) | Royalty (7.5%) |
|---|---|---|
| £500,000 | £400,000 | £30,000 |
| £1,000,000 | £800,000 | £60,000 |
| £2,500,000 | £2,000,000 | £150,000 |
| £5,000,000 | £4,000,000 | £300,000 |
| £10,000,000 | £8,000,000 | £600,000 |

Plus application fees, annual licence fees, and internal costs. This is a meaningful commitment and must be weighed against the valuation uplift and the commercial access the badge unlocks.

## 10. Term and renewal

- **Initial term:** tied to the specification cycle, not a fixed number of years. Typically 3–5 years until the specification is refreshed.
- **Renewal:** should be on substantially similar terms if the resource has been maintained in good standing.
- **Termination for cause:** fair — AQA can terminate for material misuse, we can terminate if AQA does not meet its reciprocal obligations.
- **Termination for convenience:** minimise. If AQA insists, we want at least 12 months' notice and a wind-down period during which the badge remains usable.

## 11. Confidentiality

- Specific royalty rate and terms are confidential between us and AQA.
- We do not disclose AQA's commercial terms to other publishers, to the press, or to competitors.
- We may disclose the existence of the approval (publicly) and the general commercial relationship (to investors under NDA), but not the specific rate.

## 12. Process for negotiation

1. Initial exploratory conversation — no numbers.
2. Formal proposal from us — our opening position (probably attached to the application pack).
3. AQA response.
4. Negotiation through written exchange and one or two meetings.
5. Heads of terms.
6. Full contract drafting (AQA's counsel drafts, our counsel marks up).
7. Sign.

**Internal decision rights:**
- Opening position: CEO and CFO sign off
- Any movement within pre-agreed ranges: endorsement lead can proceed
- Any movement outside pre-agreed ranges: CEO approval
- Final contract: CEO and board sign-off

## 13. What we do not know and need to find out from AQA

- Actual royalty rate AQA asks for
- Actual definition of net revenue in AQA's template
- Scope policy (resource vs. platform)
- Whether a digital-native platform is treated differently from a print textbook
- Whether there is a minimum guarantee floor
- Whether there are cap options
- Audit frequency and process
- Reporting cadence
- Renewal terms

All of these should be explored in early conversations and captured in `06-tracking/application-tracker.md`.
