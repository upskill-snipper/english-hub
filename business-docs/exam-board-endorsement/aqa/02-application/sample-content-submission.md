# Sample Content Submission — What to Send AQA

> Draft plan for the content pack we will submit with the application.
> This is not the content itself — it is the **curation brief** that tells the editorial team which pages, units, and resources to package up.

## 1. Purpose

AQA's Stage 2 subject-expert review is primarily evidence-based. Reviewers want to see the actual content, not a promise. A well-curated sample pack does three things:

1. Demonstrates the range of our coverage across the spec.
2. Demonstrates the depth and quality of any single unit.
3. Demonstrates our pedagogical consistency — that a student who uses one unit will get the same treatment as a student who uses another.

A badly curated pack (too much, too little, unrepresentative, outdated) actively hurts our chances. This document sets out a deliberate, defensible curation.

## 2. Curation principles

- **Breadth over depth** at the top of the pack; **depth over breadth** inside each featured unit.
- **No cherry-picking.** If we only submit our best poem unit and AQA later finds a weaker one live on the platform, trust is destroyed. We submit representative content.
- **Only content currently on the platform.** No "coming soon" placeholders.
- **Every sample fully self-contained.** A reviewer should be able to understand each sample without needing to log into the platform.
- **Every sample carries its source.** Page URL (screenshot OK for content behind auth), last-updated date, author and reviewer names.
- **Every sample is checked for copyright** before submission (no unlicensed images, no unlicensed extracts beyond fair-dealing quotation).

## 3. What to include

### 3.1 Literature — one full set-text unit

Pick a single set text we cover especially well and include the full unit end-to-end. Suggestion: [PLACEHOLDER — e.g. Macbeth].

Package:
- Unit overview page
- Context pages
- Chapter / scene / act walkthroughs
- Character study pages
- Theme study pages
- Language, form, and structure pages
- Critical response pages (AO3)
- Exam technique pages
- Practice question with model answer at Grade 4, 6, 8
- Unit mark scheme
- Spaced retrieval quiz set

### 3.2 Literature — one full poem study

Pick a single poem from the Power and Conflict or Love and Relationships cluster. Suggestion: [PLACEHOLDER — e.g. Ozymandias].

Package:
- Poem page with the poem text (with attribution where needed)
- Context and background
- Line-by-line analysis
- Language, form, and structure
- Themes
- Connections to other anthology poems (for the comparison question)
- Practice question with model answer

Package:

- Two student practice tasks with model responses

### 3.6 Spoken Language Endorsement resource

Package:
- Student guidance on preparing a presentation
- Example presentation briefs
- Guidance for teachers on assessing against AO7–AO9
- Reference to AQA's CI-HI-VI accommodations (signposted, not reproduced)

### 3.7 Quiz and formative assessment samples

Include three quiz sets:
- One set-text comprehension quiz (Literature)
- One language technique identification quiz (Language)
- One mark scheme matching exercise

Each quiz includes the questions, the answer key, and the rationale for each correct answer.

### 3.8 Teacher-facing resources

- One scheme of work for a full Year 10 term
- One lesson plan for a single lesson
- One printable worksheet (PDF)
- One progress tracker export

### 3.9 A single anonymised marked student response

This is the single most persuasive artefact. We take one real student response to a real past-paper-style question, we mark it against our rubric, and we include a marker commentary. It shows AQA that our marking is credible and would survive a real examiner's eyes.

## 4. What NOT to include

- **Past-paper questions reproduced verbatim without attribution.** Use AQA's own past-paper URLs or say "modelled on AQA Paper 1 June 2022 Q2" rather than copy-pasting.
- **AQA mark schemes reproduced verbatim.** Same principle.
- **Third-party copyrighted images, audio, or video** where the licence does not clearly cover redistribution.
- **Any page with an unresolved errata flag.**
- **Any "coming soon" placeholders.**
- **Any marketing copy** — the sample pack is teaching content, not marketing.
- **Grade guarantees of any kind.**

## 5. Format and delivery

- Master pack delivered as a **single PDF** with a table of contents.
- Platform screenshots captured at high resolution, with URLs and timestamps in the footer of each screenshot.
- A parallel **ZIP of source files** (PDFs of units, scheme of work, lesson plan) for any reviewer who prefers documents over screenshots.
- Covering index doc mapping each sample to the specification areas it evidences.

## 6. Volume

Target: 80–150 pages of sample content. Not more.

AQA reviewers are subject experts, not bulk-content processors. Fewer, better-chosen samples with clear commentary beat a 500-page dump.

## 7. Internal review gate before submission

No sample content pack goes to AQA until:

- [ ] Editorial lead has read every sample
- [ ] Examiner-experienced reviewer has read every sample against the live spec
- [ ] Legal has cleared every third-party reference and image
- [ ] Safeguarding lead has cleared any content touching sensitive themes
- [ ] Accessibility has spot-checked at least 20% of samples
- [ ] Each sample carries a version stamp
- [ ] Each sample is reachable on the live platform (or clearly flagged if the sample is a preview of imminent content)

## 8. Post-submission handling

- Keep a sealed copy of exactly what we submitted so that when AQA's feedback arrives we know what they were looking at.
- Track feedback per sample in `06-tracking/application-tracker.md`.
- Do not change the samples on the live platform mid-review without notifying AQA.
