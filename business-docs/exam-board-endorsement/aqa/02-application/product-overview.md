## 1. What The English Hub is

The English Hub is a web-based learning and revision platform for GCSE English students and teachers in the UK, built from the ground up to support the AQA GCSE English Literature (8702) and GCSE English Language (8700) specifications.

It is not a textbook and it is not a video-only revision site. It is a structured, pedagogically sequenced digital resource that combines teaching content, modelled analysis, retrieval practice, assessment practice, and teacher-facing tools in one platform.

## 2. Who it is for

- **Primary users.** GCSE students in Years 10 and 11 preparing for AQA 8700 and 8702.
- **Secondary users.** English teachers using the platform with their classes — as pre-teaching, consolidation, cover lessons, revision, or intervention.
- **Tertiary users.** Parents and tutors who want a reliable source of specification-aligned guidance.

## 4. Pedagogy

### 4.1 Our teaching principles

1. **The text comes first.** Students should read the text as literature before they learn to answer exam questions about it. Our set-text units open with a reading experience, not a question bank.
2. **Specification-accurate, not specification-reducing.** We teach to the specification, but we do not reduce texts to a checklist of AOs and quotations. Our units teach the specification content while preserving the integrity of the text.
3. **Progression over performance.** Content is sequenced so that skills build. A Year 10 student working through our platform develops the same skill set in a different order from a Year 11 student doing final revision.
4. **Model, don't tell.** Exam technique is taught through annotated model answers at multiple grade levels (Grade 4, Grade 6, Grade 8), not through "tips and tricks."
5. **Retrieval and spacing.** Students practise retrieving what they've learned, with spaced revisits at interval-appropriate cadence, not at the end of every unit.
6. **Honest about difficulty.** GCSE English is hard. We do not pretend that passing is easy, and we do not sell guaranteed-grade outcomes.

### 4.2 Unit structure

Each set-text study unit follows a standard shape:

1. **Orientation** — context, author, time period, why this text matters
2. **Read** — guided first reading with comprehension checks
3. **Re-read and annotate** — modelled close-reading of key extracts
4. **Themes and characters** — analytical lenses with textual evidence
5. **Language, form, and structure** — AO2 focus with worked examples
6. **Context and criticism** — AO3 focus with differentiated sources
7. **Exam skills** — modelled AQA-style answers with mark scheme commentary
8. **Practice** — student attempts with mark-scheme-driven feedback
9. **Revisit** — spaced retrieval

### 4.3 Language paper teaching sequences

Language papers are taught as **skill sequences** rather than "topics." Each sequence drills one reading or writing skill at a time, then integrates them into a full-paper simulation.

### 4.4 Assessment practice

- Practice questions in the exact style of AQA command words
- Mark schemes written in the shape of real AQA mark schemes, crediting the same AOs at the same weights
- Model answers at multiple grade levels with marker commentary
- Self-marking rubrics to teach students how examiners actually reward responses

## 8. Eligibility under AQA's "print and digital textbooks" policy

AQA's published policy currently approves **print and digital textbooks**. The English Hub is a web platform, not a textbook in the traditional sense. We intend to address this in two ways:

1. **Honest positioning.** We will describe The English Hub accurately — as a structured digital learning resource built around a textbook-equivalent core of sequenced teaching content — rather than mis-describing it as a "textbook."
2. **Companion print/PDF artefact (if required).** If AQA's policy requires a textbook-format artefact for eligibility, we will produce a downloadable PDF study guide per unit that is a genuine textbook-in-format and can carry the AQA approved badge on its cover, derived from the same editorial content as the platform. This artefact would be available to every English Hub subscriber.

We will seek AQA's view on eligibility in the initial exploratory conversation rather than assume one answer.

## 10. What we are not

To be equally clear:

- **We are not a guarantee of any grade.** Student outcomes depend on many factors; we make no grade-prediction claims.
- **We are not AQA.** All judgements about the specification are ultimately AQA's; we aim to reflect them accurately but we are not the authority.
- **We are not a substitute for teaching.** The platform supports classroom teaching and independent study; it does not replace a qualified teacher.
