## Why we are writing now

We have built The English Hub over the past [PLACEHOLDER] years with the explicit intention of aligning closely to AQA's specifications, assessment objectives, and command-word language. We believe the platform is now mature enough — in scope, pedagogy, and editorial rigour — to be considered for AQA approval.

We recognise that AQA's approval process is initiated by AQA rather than by publishers, and that AQA typically selects two to three publishers per subject when a specification cycle opens. Accordingly, the purpose of this letter is twofold:

1. To place The English Hub on AQA's radar as a credible candidate for the next 8700 / 8702 approval cycle.
2. To request an initial exploratory conversation with your partnerships team, so that we can understand what AQA would need from us in order for our platform to be eligible under your current "print and digital textbooks" policy.

## What we bring to the table

The proposal in the attached document (Appendix B) sets out the full detail. In summary:

- **Rigorous specification alignment.** We have mapped every unit of English Hub content to the relevant sections, assessment objectives, and papers of the 8700 and 8702 specifications. The full alignment document is attached as Appendix C.
- **Editorial credibility.** Our content is written and reviewed by qualified English teachers, including contributors with direct AQA examining experience. Our editorial process (attached as Appendix D) mirrors best-in-class academic publishing practice.
- **Scale and traction.** [PLACEHOLDER — student and school numbers, engagement metrics, outcome signals where available.]
- **Accuracy commitment.** We maintain a published errata and correction policy and commit to updating content within [PLACEHOLDER — timeframe] of any AQA specification amendment.
- **Safeguarding.** Our platform is designed from the ground up for use by minors and meets the standards set out in Keeping Children Safe in Education.
- **Accessibility.** The platform meets WCAG 2.1 AA [to be independently audited before submission].

## What we are asking for

We would welcome the opportunity to:

1. Have an initial 30–45 minute exploratory call with a member of your partnerships team, to understand AQA's current publisher approval priorities and to test whether a digital-native platform like ours is eligible under AQA's current policy.
2. Be added to AQA's publisher contact list for the next 8700 / 8702 specification cycle.
3. Receive any guidance AQA is willing to share on the criteria we should be ready to meet.

## What we are not claiming

We are acutely aware of the distinction between "AQA approved," "AQA endorsed resource" (as used for Pearson / Edexcel materials in wider industry usage), and "aligned to AQA specifications." The English Hub is currently **aligned to AQA specifications** and has never claimed, and will never claim, approved or endorsed status until AQA has granted it in writing. Our marketing, metadata, and product copy do not use the AQA trademark, logo, or badge in any way that implies endorsement; we use the specification codes (8700, 8702) and paper descriptions only in their factual, navigational sense. If AQA ever believes our current positioning crosses that line, we would welcome the feedback and will correct it immediately.
