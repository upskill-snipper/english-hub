# Legal — IP Considerations for Specification References and Set Texts

**Purpose:** Identify the intellectual property issues that sit alongside a Pearson Edexcel endorsement, and set out The English Hub's working position on each. This is the reference document used to brief external IP counsel.
**Status:** Draft v0.1 — internal planning only. Not a legal opinion.

---

## 1. The IP landscape

Three categories of third-party IP are in scope:

1. **Pearson-owned IP.** Specifications, sample assessment materials, past papers, mark schemes, anthologies, figurative logos, Pearson's own published textbook content.
2. **Set text author IP.** Novels, plays, poems and non-fiction texts prescribed or authorised by Pearson Edexcel for GCSE or International GCSE English, owned by the authors, their estates, or their publishers.
3. **Original IP created by The English Hub.** All our own editorial content: explanations, lesson sequences, worked exemplars, practice questions, teacher notes.

Each category has different rules. The endorsement decision only covers our original teaching and learning content. It does not transfer any rights in Pearson-owned IP or in third-party set text IP.

## 2. Pearson-owned IP — working position

### 2.1 Specifications
- Pearson publishes specifications free of charge on qualifications.pearson.com.
- The specification text is Pearson's copyright. We do not reproduce substantial extracts.
- We reference specifications by name, code, issue number and section, and we paraphrase content statements in our own words where we need to discuss them.
- We link to the authoritative PDF on the Pearson website rather than hosting our own copy.

### 2.2 Sample assessment materials and past papers
- We reference past paper questions by paper code, series and question number.
- We do not reproduce past paper stimulus material in the Hub unless it is within fair dealing for the purpose of criticism or review, and even then only where the original source is credited.
- We do not reproduce mark scheme wording verbatim. Where we discuss a mark scheme expectation we paraphrase in our own words.

### 2.3 Anthologies
- The Pearson Edexcel International GCSE English Anthology contains poetry and prose selections that are in copyright to their respective authors or estates. Pearson has cleared rights for use of those texts in the context of the Pearson Edexcel International GCSE specification.
- The English Hub does not reproduce full anthology texts. We work within fair-dealing limits and direct teachers and students to the Pearson-published anthology for the authoritative text.
- Where we teach an anthology poem or extract we quote only as much as is necessary for criticism or review and we credit the author and Pearson.

### 2.4 Pearson-owned figurative logos
- Not for use except under a trademark licence (see `trademark-use.md`).

## 3. Set text author IP — working position

### 3.1 Why this matters especially now
Pearson subject updates confirm that from first assessment in May/June 2026, International GCSE English Literature 4ET1 adds two new texts to the set list: *Klara and the Sun* (Kazuo Ishiguro) and *Western Lane* (Chetna Maroo). These are in-copyright contemporary works. Any Hub content on these texts must be produced under fair dealing for the purpose of criticism, review or quotation under UK copyright law (Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988, ss. 29–30), with clear attribution.

### 3.2 Rules we apply
1. **No full texts.** We never reproduce the full text of a copyright set text or anthology text.
2. **Fair-dealing extracts only.** Quotations are limited to what is necessary for criticism or review, in line with UK fair-dealing practice.
3. **Direct students to authorised editions.** Where Pearson specifies a prescribed edition — as per Appendix 4 in 4ET1 — our content signposts students to that edition, which they or their school must purchase separately.
4. **Attribution.** Every quotation is credited to the author, the work, the edition, the publisher and, where relevant, the year.
5. **Licence check.** Where we are uncertain whether a use falls within fair dealing, we either reduce the scope of the use or seek a licence from the rights holder. We do not press ahead without a view.
6. **Annotations are our own.** Our commentary, annotation, analysis and teaching sequences are original editorial work written by our team.

### 3.3 Decision log
We keep an internal decision log recording, for each prescribed set text:

- Title, author, edition, publisher, ISBN
- Nature of use (annotated extract, short quotation, teaching note)
- Fair-dealing justification OR licence source
- Reviewer who signed off the use
- Date of review

## 4. Original IP owned by The English Hub

- All content written by our editorial team, our contributors and our contracted authors is owned by The English Hub under the author agreements we hold on file.
- Author agreements include a full assignment of copyright in the commissioned work, plus a waiver of moral rights where permissible.
- We retain the right to update, reformat, translate and republish the content.
- We do not assign our original IP to Pearson as part of the endorsement process. Endorsement is a review-and-badging relationship, not an IP transfer.

## 5. What the endorsement process does and does not cover

| Item | Covered by endorsement? |
|---|---|
| Right to display the "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" logo on covered resources | Yes, under separate trademark licence |
| Right to use the Pearson corporate logo | No |
| Right to reproduce specification text verbatim | No |
| Right to reproduce past paper questions | No — governed by Pearson's separate past paper reuse policy |
| Right to reproduce mark scheme text | No |
| Right to reproduce full set texts or anthology texts | No — governed by underlying copyright in the texts |
| Right to reproduce fair-dealing extracts of set texts | Yes, under UK copyright law — not a Pearson matter |
| Right to describe a resource as "endorsed" | Yes, within the scope and phrasing of the endorsement contract |
| Right to describe a resource as "approved" | No — the word is specifically excluded by Pearson |

## 6. Risk areas

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Over-quotation of 2026 4ET1 texts | Copyright claim from publisher / author estate | Editorial rule: quote only as necessary; every quotation reviewed |
| Unintentional verbatim copy of Pearson specification wording | Breach of Pearson IP | Rule: paraphrase, cite, link to authoritative PDF |
| Past paper content reused beyond Pearson's reuse terms | Breach of Pearson IP | Rule: reference, do not reproduce, unless explicit reuse allowed |
| Mis-description of endorsement scope | Breach of trademark licence | Rule: only describe the specifications actually endorsed |
| Contributor claims ownership of commissioned work | Ownership dispute | Author agreement with full assignment on file for every contributor |
| International user licensing (outside UK fair dealing) | Jurisdictional uncertainty | Obtain rights holder licence where content is used outside UK fair-dealing cover |
