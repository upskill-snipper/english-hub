# Legal — Pearson / Edexcel Trademark Use

**Purpose:** Set out The English Hub's working position on use of Pearson Edexcel trademarks, including the "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" logo, and document the open questions for external counsel and for Pearson's legal team.
**Status:** Draft v0.1 — for internal planning only. Requires sign-off from a UK IP / trademark solicitor before any external use.
**Scope:** This is an internal position paper. It is NOT a legal opinion and NOT a contract. Nothing in this document authorises any use of Pearson, Edexcel or BTEC branding.

---

## 1. The problem in one paragraph

Pearson plc and its subsidiaries own a portfolio of trademarks including "Pearson", "Edexcel", "BTEC" and figurative marks associated with each. Pearson offers a specific figurative mark — "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" — that endorsed publishers may use on the cover and marketing material of their resource, subject to a written agreement and to Pearson's brand guidelines. Any use of the Pearson corporate logo or the standalone "Edexcel" mark is a separate question and is not automatically granted to an endorsed publisher. The English Hub must be explicit internally and externally about which marks we may use, how, and from what date.

## 2. Marks in the landscape

| Mark | Owner | Available to endorsed publishers? |
|---|---|---|
| "Pearson" (word mark) | Pearson plc | No — standalone use not granted. Use only in full phrases permitted under a trademark licence. |
| Pearson figurative logo | Pearson plc | No — not available to third parties. |
| "Edexcel" (word mark) | Pearson | Limited — only in the phrase defined by Pearson (e.g., "Pearson Edexcel Qualifications") and only under licence. |
| "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" (figurative) | Pearson | Yes, under the endorsement agreement and Pearson's brand guidelines. |
| "BTEC" | Pearson | Not in scope for this application (English qualifications). |

## 3. Our working position on use

### 3.1 Before endorsement is signed
- We do **not** display the "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" logo anywhere.
- We do **not** claim to be "endorsed", "approved", "certified", or "recognised" by Pearson Edexcel.
- We may refer to the Pearson Edexcel specifications by name (e.g., "our content is mapped to Pearson Edexcel GCSE English Literature (1ET0)") because this is nominative use — identifying the specification our content supports. Even so, we use the full phrase "Pearson Edexcel" rather than "Edexcel" alone, we never imply Pearson has reviewed our content, and we reference the source at `qualifications.pearson.com`.
- We do not use any Pearson or Edexcel figurative logo.

### 3.2 During the application process
- Same rules as before endorsement. Being in the application pipeline does not authorise any additional use.
- We do not describe ourselves as "applying for endorsement" in teacher-facing marketing, because that implies the outcome.

### 3.3 After endorsement is granted and the trademark licence is signed
- We use the "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" logo **only** on the products covered by the endorsement.
- We follow Pearson's brand guidelines on placement, size, clear space, colour variants, background colour and proximity to other logos.
- We do not combine the endorsement logo with any other endorsement badge in a way that suggests joint endorsement.
- We include the endorsement scope disclosure (i.e., which specification is endorsed) wherever Pearson's guidelines require it.
- We mark any publisher-written practice content as not approved by Pearson, per Pearson's public guidance on endorsed resources.
- We cease use within the notice period stated in the trademark licence if endorsement is withdrawn or lapses.

### 3.4 Co-marketing and PR
- We do not issue a press release about endorsement without Pearson's prior approval of the quote we attribute to Pearson (if any) and of the wording about the endorsement scope.
- We do not imply that an endorsement of one specification extends to any other qualification.
- We do not use Pearson or Edexcel branding in paid advertising without Pearson's written consent.

## 4. What we expect in a Pearson trademark licence

This is a brief working view of the clauses we expect to see and the positions we will take. External counsel must confirm and negotiate.

| Clause | Our working position |
|---|---|
| Scope of licence | Per-specification, per-resource; extendable by written agreement |
| Logo format | All Pearson-supplied variants (colour, mono, reversed, small-size, digital) |
| Brand guidelines | Linked from the licence; version-controlled |
| Duration | Tied to endorsement term; automatic suspension on withdrawal |
| Termination | Pearson may terminate on notice for breach or withdrawal of endorsement; we may terminate on notice if we cease to publish the resource |
| Warranties | Mutual: we warrant we will use the logo only as permitted; Pearson warrants title to the mark |
| Indemnity | We indemnify Pearson against claims arising from the content of our resource; Pearson indemnifies us against claims arising from title defects in its mark |
| Governing law | England and Wales |
| Jurisdiction | Exclusive jurisdiction of the English courts |

## 5. Open questions for counsel

1. Under English trademark law, what nominative-use defence applies to our use of "Pearson Edexcel GCSE English Literature (1ET0)" in descriptive copy before endorsement is granted?
2. How do we draft the "endorsement scope" disclosure so that it is accurate even if Pearson later endorses additional specifications?
3. What is the standard Pearson endorsement licence period and what is the minimum notice Pearson typically gives on withdrawal?
4. Does the standard Pearson licence contain any restriction on simultaneous endorsement by another awarding body (e.g., AQA)? If yes, how do we reconcile?
5. Are there any special rules for digital products (where the logo appears on a web UI or within a SaaS subscription) as opposed to print textbooks?
6. What is the right form of disclosure on publisher-written practice content to comply with Pearson's public statement that such content is "not approved"?

## 6. Current external-facing language (pre-endorsement)

The following is the current approved wording. It must be reviewed by counsel before it is used in marketing copy or sales decks.

> "The English Hub produces teaching and learning content mapped to the current Pearson Edexcel GCSE and International GCSE English specifications, including GCSE English Literature (1ET0), GCSE English Language (1EN0 / 1EN2), International GCSE English Literature (4ET1) and International GCSE English Language A (4EA1). We are an independent publisher and are not owned by or affiliated with Pearson. Teachers and centres should consult the current specification on qualifications.pearson.com for the authoritative requirements."

## 7. What we will never say

- "Approved by Pearson"
- "Official Pearson textbook"
- "Edexcel endorsed" (use the full phrase "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications")
- "Pearson Partner" (we are not)
- "Certified by Pearson"
- Any phrase that implies a grade guarantee
