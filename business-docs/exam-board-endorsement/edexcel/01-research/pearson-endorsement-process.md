# Pearson's Endorsement Process — Research Brief

**Purpose:** Internal briefing document so that everyone involved in the application understands how Pearson actually runs its "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" programme.
**Status:** Based on desk research of publicly available Pearson pages as of April 2026. Where Pearson does not publish specifics, assumptions are flagged. **All non-public details must be confirmed by direct contact with Publisher Relations before anything in this document is used in a contract.**

---

## 1. What Pearson calls it

Pearson uses two labels that are easy to confuse:

- **Published resources** — a searchable catalogue on the Pearson Qualifications website that lists textbooks, digital resources and teaching materials from many publishers (including Pearson's own). A listing here is lightweight.
- **Endorsed resources** — a subset of published resources that have been independently reviewed by Pearson and carry the **"Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications"** logo.

The formal badging programme is the **Endorsement of Resources Supporting Pearson Qualifications** policy, run by the **Publisher Relations** team.

## 2. Who runs it

- **Programme owner:** Publisher Relations team, Pearson Qualifications.
- **Contact email:** `publisher.relations@pearson.com` (single public inbox for publishers wishing to apply).
- **Reviewers:** Independent subject reviewers, typically senior examiners and subject experts already engaged by Pearson on specification / assessment work.
- **Internal sign-off:** Publisher Relations acts as programme owner; subject-specialist reviewers have editorial veto.

## 3. Stated principles

From Pearson's public pages:

1. **Open policy.** Any publisher may apply. There are no stated barriers to entry.
2. **Open list.** Edexcel continues to endorse resources from companies other than Pearson's own publishing arm. A third-party endorsement is not zero-sum against Pearson Schools' own titles.
3. **Teaching and learning focus.** Endorsement covers the teaching and learning content of a resource. Publisher-produced *assessment* content (sample questions, practice papers) included in a resource is **not** "approved" — the endorsement does not extend to validating assessment content as mirroring live exam standards.
4. **Not mandatory.** Endorsement does not mean a resource is required or preferred. Teachers and centres are free to use non-endorsed material.
5. **Independent review.** The review is performed by subject reviewers working with, but reporting independently of, Pearson's commercial team.

## 4. Inferred end-to-end process

Pearson does not publish a full step-by-step flow. The following is our best reconstruction, to be validated in the first call with Publisher Relations:

### Step 1 — First contact
Publisher writes to `publisher.relations@pearson.com` introducing the company, the specific Edexcel qualification(s) the resource supports, and the intended use case. A short cover letter + company profile is sufficient at this stage.

### Step 2 — Triage
Publisher Relations acknowledges and assesses basic fit (credible publisher? covers a current spec? no obvious safeguarding or accuracy red flags?). Assumption: 2–4 weeks.

### Step 3 — Scoping call
A call or email exchange agrees (a) which qualification(s) the resource will be endorsed against, (b) what sample material the publisher will submit, (c) which review route applies, and (d) any review fees.

### Step 4 — Sample submission
Publisher submits representative content. Likely expected:

- Cover letter restating the request
- Company profile
- Full product overview
- Curriculum alignment mapping (spec-by-spec)
- A representative sample of the resource itself (not everything, but enough for a reviewer to form a view)
- Editorial process and accuracy policy
- Evidence of subject-specialist authorship (ideally including Edexcel examiners)

### Step 5 — Editorial review
An independent subject reviewer assesses the submission against Pearson's quality criteria. Assumption: 6–10 weeks depending on subject cycle and reviewer availability.

### Step 6 — Reviewer feedback
Reviewer raises queries, corrections and content changes. Publisher revises and resubmits. Usually iterative — one or two rounds.

### Step 7 — Endorsement decision
Publisher Relations confirms endorsement. Terms sheet issued covering logo use, scope (which qualification), duration, royalty or fee arrangement, and termination rights.

### Step 8 — Legal sign-off
Publisher and Pearson sign an endorsement agreement and trademark licence covering the use of "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" wording and the associated logo.

### Step 9 — Launch
Logo is applied to the product. Resource is added to the Pearson published resources catalogue and (if agreed) featured in Pearson co-marketing channels.

## 5. Review criteria (inferred)

Pearson has not published a public checklist. Working inference of reviewer questions:

- **Specification coverage.** Does the resource cover all the content statements of the targeted Edexcel specification (e.g., 1EN0 / 1ET0 / 4EA1 / 4ET1)?
- **Assessment objective alignment.** Does the resource explicitly support the Edexcel AOs as weighted for that spec?
- **Accuracy.** Are quotations, set-text references, exam tips and rubrics factually correct and in line with current Edexcel guidance?
- **Currency.** Is content aligned to the current issue of the specification? E.g., 4ET1 Issue 3 for first assessment in May/June 2026 (new texts *Klara and the Sun* and *Western Lane*), and 4EA1 Issue 7 from September 2025.
- **Teaching quality.** Does the pedagogy support teachers and progress learners?
- **Inclusivity.** Is the content free from bias, appropriate for the target age group, and broadly representative?
- **Safeguarding.** Are texts, images and examples age-appropriate? Is sensitive content handled responsibly?
- **Presentation.** Is the content clear, accessible, and well-structured for classroom or independent use?
- **Distinction of assessment content.** Is any publisher-written practice / sample assessment clearly marked as non-approved (i.e., not produced by Pearson)?

## 6. Known ambiguities (questions to ask in the first call)

1. Is there a review fee? If so, what is it and when is it payable?
2. Is there a per-title endorsement fee in addition to royalty?
3. What is the royalty band Pearson currently expects from endorsed resources in English at GCSE / IGCSE level?
4. How long is an endorsement valid? Does it roll with specification issues or need re-review at each issue?
5. Can a digital subscription product (rather than a printed textbook) be endorsed? Is the review approach the same?
6. Can endorsement be scoped per-specification (e.g., 1ET0 only) or must it cover a whole product family?
7. Who owns reviewer feedback and can it be used to improve product before endorsement is granted?
8. What is the process for updating an endorsed resource when Pearson issues a specification update mid-cycle?
9. What is the minimum notice Pearson gives before withdrawing endorsement?
10. Is the "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" logo available in multiple formats (print, digital, small-size)?
