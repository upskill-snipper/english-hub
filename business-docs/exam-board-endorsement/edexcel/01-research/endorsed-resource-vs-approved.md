# "Endorsed Resource" vs "Published Resources Partner" vs "Pearson Partner"

**Purpose:** Unambiguously define the three status labels that circulate in Pearson publishing conversations so that our marketing copy, sales decks and legal documents use them correctly.
**Status:** Draft reference — legal review required before any of these terms are used externally.

---

## TL;DR

There is no single "Pearson approved publisher" status. Three distinct things exist:

1. **Published resource (listed)** — appears in Pearson's public catalogue. Low bar.
2. **Endorsed resource** — has been independently reviewed by Pearson and may display the "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" logo. Medium-to-high bar.
3. **Official Pearson Partner / Pearson imprint** — first-party, Pearson Schools publishes it. Not available to third parties.

The English Hub is applying for **(2) Endorsed resource** and, as a consequence, **(1) Published resource listing** for the same materials. We are **not** pursuing (3), which is commercially and legally not on the table for a third-party publisher.

---

## 1. Published resource (catalogue listing)

**What it is.** Pearson maintains a searchable catalogue at `qualifications.pearson.com/en/support/published-resources.html` where teachers can filter resources by qualification, subject, publisher and price. Any publisher with a resource that supports an Edexcel or BTEC qualification can be listed.

**What it gives you.**
- A public record that your resource exists and is discoverable by teachers
- A product page with sample content and a "buy" link
- Filter by-publisher and by-subject visibility
- No right to use Pearson logos beyond the catalogue listing

**What it does NOT give you.**
- Any form of quality seal
- Any use of the "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" logo
- Any co-marketing commitment from Pearson
- Any implied recommendation to teachers

**Bar.** Low. Pearson's position is that its catalogue is a service to teachers; listings are essentially a directory.

**Verdict for The English Hub.** A listing is a downstream consequence of endorsement — once endorsed, we expect to appear in the catalogue. It is not a standalone goal.

---

## 2. Endorsed resource

**What it is.** A resource that has been reviewed by Pearson's independent review network (typically senior subject experts and examiners) and confirmed by Publisher Relations as meeting Pearson's quality criteria for supporting teaching and learning of a named Edexcel qualification.

**What it gives you.**
- Right to display the "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" logo on the resource cover / digital equivalent
- Right to describe the resource as "endorsed by Pearson Edexcel" in marketing copy, subject to Pearson's trademark guidelines
- Featured placement in the published resources catalogue
- A credible quality signal for B2B sales into schools and multi-academy trusts
- A potential route into Pearson co-marketing lists and teacher communication channels

**What it does NOT give you.**
- Endorsement does **not** mean Pearson or Edexcel "approves" the resource's own practice questions or sample assessments. Any publisher-written mock exam content in the resource must be clearly marked as not produced by Pearson.
- It does not make the resource mandatory or preferred — teachers are free to use non-endorsed material.
- It does not grant any rights to use the Edexcel / Pearson corporate logo beyond the endorsement logo.
- It does not make the publisher a "Pearson partner" or imply an employment / exclusivity relationship.

**Bar.** Medium-to-high. A specimen content submission must pass independent subject review, usually iterative. Accuracy, curriculum coverage, inclusivity and safeguarding are all assessed. Editorial sign-off by a named reviewer is required.

**Verdict for The English Hub.** This is the real prize. All of the value in this programme — valuation uplift, logo rights, teacher trust, catalogue placement — sits here.

**Language we may use externally (post-endorsement):**
- "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications"
- "An endorsed resource for Pearson Edexcel GCSE English Literature (1ET0)"
- "[Product name] has been reviewed and endorsed by Pearson for use with the Pearson Edexcel GCSE English Literature specification"

**Language we must NOT use:**
- "Approved by Pearson"
- "Pearson-approved practice questions"
- "Official Edexcel textbook"
- "Pearson Partner"
- "Pearson Edexcel certified"
- Any phrasing that implies Pearson wrote the content or validated the assessment content

---

## 3. "Official Pearson Partner" / Pearson Schools imprint

**What it is.** Resources published directly under the Pearson imprint by Pearson Schools (or a commissioned first-party title, e.g., the official Edexcel GCSE English Literature student book). These are Pearson's own products.

**What it gives you.**
- Full Pearson imprint
- Pearson Schools distribution
- First-party marketing
- Route onto the "Pearson UK Schools Shop" as a Pearson title

**Why it is NOT available to The English Hub.**
- Pearson does not open its imprint to third-party publishers on request
- Commissioning is a standing editorial process inside Pearson Schools, not an external programme
- It would require transfer of IP and content editorial control
- It would commercially conflict with our direct-to-teacher subscription model

**Important nuance.** There is no formal, publicly documented "Official Pearson Partner" status for third-party publishers of English GCSE textbooks. The phrase is sometimes used colloquially to refer to Pearson's endorsement relationship, but it is not a Pearson programme name. **Do not use "Pearson Partner" in external copy** — it is either inaccurate (if we mean endorsed) or misleading (if we mean we have an imprint relationship, which we do not).

## 4. Comparison table

| Feature | Published (listed) | Endorsed | Pearson imprint |
|---|---|---|---|
| Open to third parties | Yes | Yes | No |
| Independent review | No | Yes | N/A (internal) |
| Use of "Endorsed" logo | No | Yes | Yes (plus imprint) |
| Use of Pearson corporate logo | No | No | Yes |
| Catalogue listing | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Co-marketing | No | Sometimes | Yes |
| Royalty to Pearson | No | Yes (typical) | N/A (Pearson owns revenue) |
| Duration | Indefinite while published | Typically aligned with spec cycle | Indefinite while in print |
| Quality bar | None | Medium-to-high | Highest |

## 5. What we are applying for

**Decision:** Apply for **endorsed resource** status, scoped initially to:

1. Edexcel GCSE English Literature (1ET0)
2. Edexcel GCSE English Language 2.0 (1EN2, first teaching from 2022) — or 1EN0 if the reviewer prefers the legacy spec
3. Edexcel International GCSE English Literature (4ET1, Issue 3)
4. Edexcel International GCSE English Language A (4EA1, Issue 7)

The published-resources catalogue listing follows automatically.

**Out of scope:** Pearson imprint. Any first-party co-publishing discussion is a separate conversation that only opens after an endorsed resource has been in market for a material period.

## 6. Open questions for Publisher Relations

- Can endorsement be applied at the per-specification level, or must it cover the whole English subject cluster?
- Can a subscription-based digital resource be endorsed in the same way as a printed student book?
- What is the review fee (if any) per specification?
- Are endorsement contracts exclusive in any sense, or can the same resource be endorsed by multiple boards (e.g., AQA) simultaneously?
