# Evidence — Editorial Process

**Purpose:** Document The English Hub's editorial process so that a Pearson subject reviewer can see that every piece of content on the platform has been through an explicit, evidenced workflow. This is the single biggest driver of reviewer confidence in an endorsed resource application.
**Status:** Draft v0.1. Reflects the intended process. Any step currently partially implemented is flagged. The founder must sign off before this document goes to Pearson.

---

## 1. Principles

1. **No content goes live without a second reader.** Even single-paragraph amendments are reviewed by someone other than the author.
2. **No set text reference goes live without a fact check.** Every quotation, character name, chapter number, stanza reference and historical detail is verified.
3. **No AO claim goes live without a spec link.** If content claims to target a specific Pearson Edexcel AO, the source specification clause is cited in the content management system.
4. **No tone or safeguarding issue is resolved informally.** Content that flags for safeguarding, sensitivity or accessibility goes to a named reviewer with an explicit decision and record.
5. **Every change is logged.** The CMS keeps a full version history. Reverts are possible.

## 2. Workflow stages

### Stage 0 — Commissioning
- Editorial lead identifies a gap or an update need (e.g., a Pearson subject update has changed the text list).
- A brief is written that states the specification clause, the target AO(s), the intended audience, the expected length, the accuracy constraints and the due date.
- Brief is assigned to a qualified English teacher or examiner author.

### Stage 1 — Drafting
- Author writes the content inside the CMS.
- Author references the Pearson Edexcel specification at the level of clause and AO.
- Author flags any quotation or fact that needs permission clearance.
- Author declares any potential safeguarding or sensitivity issue.

### Stage 2 — Second-reader review
- A second qualified reader, not the author, reviews the content for:
  - Accuracy (facts, quotations, chapter references, etymology, dates)
  - Specification coverage
  - Pedagogy (is this teachable? Does it progress learners?)
  - AO targeting (does the content actually practise the claimed AO?)
  - Voice, tone and register
- Second reader adds comments in the CMS and either approves or returns the draft.

### Stage 3 — Fact and accuracy pass
- A dedicated accuracy pass checks every quotation against the authorised edition of the text (see `03-evidence/accuracy-commitment.md`).
- Contextual facts (dates, places, biography, historical period) are cross-checked against two independent sources where relevant.

### Stage 4 — Safeguarding and inclusivity review
- Safeguarding-sensitive content goes to the named safeguarding reviewer.
- Any content warning is drafted at this stage.
- Any accessibility concern (e.g., dense text, imagery, colour contrast in figures) is flagged for design.
- See `03-evidence/safeguarding-compliance.md`.

### Stage 5 — Copy edit
- A final copy-editing pass for spelling, punctuation, consistency with the house style guide, and clarity.

### Stage 6 — Editorial sign-off
- Editorial Director approves publication.
- A version of the content, together with its review comments, is snapshotted.

### Stage 7 — Publication
- Content is published to the live product.
- Change note written and, where material, teachers are notified.

### Stage 8 — Ongoing maintenance
- Every resource is tagged with a review-due date.
- Pearson subject updates trigger a content impact assessment within 10 working days.
- User-reported issues are triaged within 5 working days.

## 3. Roles and responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Editorial Director | Sets the editorial standard, chairs sign-off meetings, is accountable for quality |
| Subject Lead (Literature / Language) | Owns curriculum mapping, approves content against the specification |
| Author | Writes the content to the brief |
| Second reader | Independent content review |
| Accuracy checker | Verifies every quotation and factual reference |
| Safeguarding reviewer | Reviews any flagged sensitive content |
| Copy editor | Final language / style pass |
| Designer | Implements visual and accessibility fixes |
| CMS administrator | Maintains tagging, version history and audit trail |

## 4. Evidence we can show the reviewer

On request we can show:

- A walk-through of the CMS showing review comments, version history and approvals for a named piece of content
- An example of a Pearson subject update triggering a content change, with the before / after and the change note
- An example of a safeguarding flag being raised and resolved
- An example of an accuracy correction being pushed as a hotfix
- An example of user-reported errata being triaged and resolved

## 5. Metrics we track

| Metric | What it measures | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Second-reader coverage | % of published items with a recorded second-reader approval | 100% |
| Time to hotfix accuracy issues | Days from report to fix on live | < 5 working days |
| Time to respond to Pearson subject updates | Days from update publication to impact assessment | < 10 working days |
| Safeguarding flags resolved with a recorded decision | % | 100% |
| Editorial sign-off on live content | % | 100% |

All metric targets are the internal standard The English Hub operates to. Actual performance against targets is not quoted in this document — it will be quoted in the live submission, once the editorial team confirms current figures.

## 7. How the editorial process will change once endorsement is granted

- Any content that carries the Pearson Edexcel endorsement logo becomes subject to an additional "endorsement lock" — changes require sign-off by the Editorial Director and a note in the change log explicitly confirming the change does not affect endorsement compliance
- A named endorsement liaison role is created for ongoing communication with Pearson Publisher Relations
- Annual internal audit confirms that endorsed content remains aligned to the current specification issue
