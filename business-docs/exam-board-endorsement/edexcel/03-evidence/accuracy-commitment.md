# Evidence — Accuracy Commitment

**Purpose:** State The English Hub's commitment to factual and textual accuracy clearly enough that a Pearson subject reviewer can rely on it. Accuracy is non-negotiable for any resource that aspires to carry the "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" logo.
**Status:** Draft v0.1. To be signed off by the Editorial Director and the founder before submission.

---

## 1. The commitment

The English Hub commits to the following as an editorial minimum for all published content relevant to Pearson Edexcel specifications:

1. **No incorrect quotations.** Every quoted line from a set text is verified against the prescribed or authorised edition.
2. **No incorrect specification references.** Every claim about what a Pearson Edexcel specification requires is sourced from the current specification PDF on qualifications.pearson.com.
3. **No incorrect assessment objective tagging.** Every AO tag on a content item is traceable to the specification clause that defines that AO.
4. **No unverified biographical or historical context.** Every dated fact is cross-checked against at least one authoritative reference source.
5. **No stale content.** Every resource is reviewed at or before its scheduled review date, and any Pearson subject update triggers a content impact assessment within 10 working days.
6. **No unreported errors.** If a user, teacher, second reader, or Pearson reviewer reports an error, it is logged, triaged, and — where material — fixed within 5 working days of confirmation.

## 2. Standards we apply

- **Set texts.** We use the prescribed edition listed in the current Pearson Edexcel specification (where prescribed editions are specified). Where Pearson names an authorised edition — for example the prescribed editions listed in Appendix 4 of 4ET1 — we quote only from that edition.
- **Anthologies.** We use the current Pearson Edexcel International GCSE English Anthology as the authoritative source for anthology texts in 4ET1 and 4EA1.
- **Specifications.** We work only from the current specification PDF published on qualifications.pearson.com. We do not rely on third-party summaries of the specification for editorial claims.
- **Past papers.** Where we reference a past paper we link to Pearson's published past paper archive and credit Pearson Edexcel.
- **Mark schemes.** Where we paraphrase generic level descriptors we do so in our own words, at the level of concept. We do not copy verbatim.
- **Historical and biographical context.** Cross-checked against at least one peer-reviewed or academic source where possible.

## 3. How accuracy is enforced

| Control | What it does | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Author brief | Specifies the specification clause, the AO(s), the authorised text edition, and the accuracy constraints | CMS record |
| Second-reader review | Independent pass by a qualified reviewer | CMS audit trail |
| Accuracy pass | Dedicated check of every quotation against the authorised edition | Checklist in CMS |
| Pearson subject update watch | Weekly review of the Pearson English subject updates page | Team log |
| Errata channel | Users can report errors via an in-product link or email | Errata ticket queue |
| Hotfix SLA | Confirmed errors are fixed within 5 working days | Ticket status log |

## 4. What we do when Pearson issues a specification change

1. Weekly watch on the Pearson English subject updates page picks up the change.
2. Impact assessment is written within 10 working days:
   - Which resources are affected?
   - Which content is now wrong?
   - Which new content is required?
   - What is the priority?
3. Affected content is tagged as "under update" in the CMS and, where the change is material, flagged to users on the resource page.
4. Updated content is drafted, second-read, fact-checked, safeguarded (if applicable) and published.
5. Change notes are written, and — where a cohort is affected — teachers are notified by email.

## 5. What we do when a user reports an error

1. Any user can report an error via the in-product errata form.
2. The ticket is triaged by the editorial team within 1 working day.
3. If the report is confirmed, the content is fixed within 5 working days.
4. If the report is not confirmed, the reporter receives a written reply explaining why.
5. If the report reveals a pattern — the same error across multiple items — the editorial lead orders a sweep of related content.

## 6. What we do when Pearson's own reviewer flags an error

- Treat it as top priority.
- Fix within 24 hours where feasible; otherwise agree an explicit timetable with Publisher Relations.
- Confirm the fix back to the reviewer with a change note and a link to the updated content.
- Log the error and the fix in the internal audit trail.

## 7. What accuracy does NOT mean

- Accuracy does not mean agreement with every academic interpretation of a text. Where an interpretation is contested, we say so and present alternatives.
- Accuracy does not mean copying official mark scheme wording. We paraphrase in our own words and point students at the specification.
- Accuracy does not mean zero editorial voice. The Hub's writers interpret texts — but interpretation is clearly labelled as such, and grounded in textual evidence.

## 8. Accountability

- The **Editorial Director** is accountable for accuracy across the platform.
- The **Subject Leads** (Literature and Language) are accountable for accuracy within their specification.
- The **Founder / CEO** has ultimate responsibility and signs off on this policy.
