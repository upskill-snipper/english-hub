# Evidence — Safeguarding and Compliance

**Purpose:** Show Pearson Publisher Relations and the subject reviewer that The English Hub takes safeguarding, inclusivity, accessibility and data protection seriously, and has a documented approach appropriate to a digital resource used by students aged 14+.
**Status:** Draft v0.1. To be reviewed by founder and, where relevant, by a safeguarding-qualified advisor before submission.
**Important scope note:** The English Hub is a publisher of teaching and learning content. It is not a school, not a regulated education provider, and does not have a statutory safeguarding duty under KCSIE. The safeguarding commitments in this document are those of a responsible digital publisher, not those of a school. Anything stronger would be overclaiming.

---

## 1. Scope

This document covers:

1. **Content safeguarding** — what we do about sensitive material inside the product
2. **Inclusivity and representation** — how we approach diverse voices and content
3. **Accessibility** — how we support users with access needs
4. **Data protection** — how we handle user data, including children's data
5. **Age-appropriateness** — how we decide what is suitable for a 14+ audience
6. **Reporting routes** — how users and teachers can raise concerns

## 2. Content safeguarding

Set texts and teaching content sometimes include material that touches on themes such as:

- Violence, conflict, war
- Death, bereavement, grief
- Mental health, depression, suicide
- Racism, prejudice, discrimination
- Sexual content, assault, abuse
- Substance misuse
- Extreme political or religious ideology

We do not shy away from these themes where they are part of a prescribed text — that would misrepresent the work and would fail the student. Instead we apply the following principles:

1. **Content warnings.** Where content deals with a topic that may be distressing, we provide a clearly labelled content warning at the top of the resource, with a suggested note to the teacher.
2. **Teacher notes.** Our teacher-facing material includes suggestions on how to introduce sensitive topics responsibly in a classroom.
3. **No gratuitous material.** We do not include sensitive material that is not either (a) in the specification or prescribed text, or (b) necessary for the pedagogy.
4. **Age-appropriate framing.** We frame content in language suitable for 14–16 year olds, not younger children and not university students.
5. **Editorial flag.** Any content that triggers a safeguarding flag in the editorial process goes to a named reviewer for an explicit decision before it goes live.

## 3. Inclusivity and representation

- We actively seek contributions from a range of voices and backgrounds.
- Where a text or a question references groups or identities, we take care that the framing is respectful and does not reinforce stereotype.
- We review content for unconscious bias in language, examples and exemplars.
- We reflect the diversity of the Pearson Edexcel International GCSE text lists in our teaching content.

## 4. Accessibility

**Target standard:** WCAG 2.2 AA for the web product. WCAG 2.1 AA is a minimum.

- Semantic HTML and clear heading structure
- Sufficient colour contrast and no colour-only indicators
- Alt text for informative images; decorative images marked as such
- Keyboard navigation for all core tasks
- Readable font sizes and line lengths
- Captions and transcripts for video content
- Accessible PDFs for downloadable handouts (where technically feasible)
- Testing with screen readers during development

## 6. Age-appropriateness

- Default audience: 14+ (i.e., GCSE and IGCSE students, Years 10 and 11)
- Any content intended for older students (e.g., A Level or university) is tagged accordingly
- No content is intended for under-13s
- Avatars, visuals and illustrations are chosen to be neutral and age-appropriate
- Any user-facing language is drafted at a reading age appropriate to 14+

## 7. Reporting routes

- **Teacher report.** A teacher can report a concern about content from within the product via a clearly signposted "Report an issue" link.
- **Student report.** Students can ask their teacher to raise a concern on their behalf. We do not operate a direct student support channel because we are not a safeguarding body.
- **Public report.** Anyone can email [safeguarding contact — placeholder] to raise a concern.
- **Pearson route.** If Pearson's subject reviewer flags a safeguarding issue, it is treated as a top-priority editorial ticket and the content is suspended from live while it is investigated.

## 9. What we do NOT claim

- We do not act in loco parentis.
- We do not offer counselling or support for students in distress.
- We are not an appropriate first contact for a child at immediate risk. Where concerning content is raised directly with us, we signpost to appropriate UK resources (NSPCC, Childline, Samaritans) and — if the user is a student — we ask their teacher to take it forward.
- We are not a regulator, an inspector or an awarding body.
