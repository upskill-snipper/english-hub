# Evidence — Edexcel Examiner Contributors

**Purpose:** Document the subject-specialist authorship credentials behind The English Hub content, with particular attention to contributors with current or recent examining experience on Pearson Edexcel English specifications. Pearson's endorsement process weighs heavily on whether a resource is genuinely written by people who know the specification and the assessment standard.
**Status:** Scaffold — all named contributors and their roles are placeholders until the editorial team verifies and updates each row. Any public-facing version requires written consent from the named individual.
**Confidentiality:** Named examiners' affiliation with The English Hub requires their consent and, where relevant, compliance with any contractual restriction on board name use imposed by the awarding bodies they work for.

---

## 1. Why this document exists

Pearson Publisher Relations and the subject reviewer want to know that the content in front of them is written by people who understand how Pearson Edexcel English is taught, examined and marked. Claimed examiner involvement must be evidenced on request. This document is the internal register from which those evidence statements are drawn.

## 2. What counts as an "examiner"

For the purposes of this register we count only the following roles as examining experience, and we name the awarding body against each:

- **Senior examiner** (chief examiner, principal examiner, assistant principal examiner)
- **Team leader** (table leader, team leader on live assessment)
- **Examiner / marker** (live marking experience on a named specification)
- **Moderator** (for coursework / NEA / Spoken Language Endorsement)
- **Question writer / specification reviewer** (where engaged as a subject specialist)

A teacher who has only taught the specification, without live assessment experience, is not counted in this register — their contribution is still recorded, but under "Teaching experience" not "Examining experience".

## 4. Evidence file conventions

For each named individual we keep on file:

- Current CV
- Signed consent to be named in the Pearson application
- Evidence of current examining status (where the individual has authority to share it)
- Signed NDA / author agreement with The English Hub
- Confirmation of any restrictions placed on them by their awarding body about acknowledgement of examiner status in external publications

Evidence files are stored in [internal HR / editorial drive — placeholder]. They are not reproduced in this repository.

## 5. How we describe examiner involvement externally

- **We can say:** "The English Hub content is written by a team of English teachers and senior examiners, including contributors with current or recent examining experience on Pearson Edexcel English specifications."
- **We cannot say:** "Written by a Pearson Edexcel chief examiner" unless the named individual has given explicit consent and their awarding body permits that attribution.
- **We never say:** That examiner involvement means the content reflects privileged insider knowledge of live assessments. Examiners contribute subject expertise, not material drawn from live question banks.

## 6. Conflicts of interest register

Any contributor with examining experience must declare:

- Which awarding body they work for
- Which specification(s) they examine
- Whether their contract with that awarding body places any restriction on their participation in this application
- Any financial interest in the outcome of the endorsement decision beyond their contributor fee

## 7. What we will send to Pearson

With the application pack, and with the individual's prior consent for each name and each detail, we can send:

- A redacted summary table (without personal contact details) of contributors with Pearson Edexcel examining experience
- On request, full CVs of the lead subject contributors for each specification in scope
- Written consents confirming each named individual is happy to be identified to Pearson
