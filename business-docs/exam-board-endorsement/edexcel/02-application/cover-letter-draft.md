**Why we are writing**

We have built The English Hub from the start with the Edexcel specifications in view, and a meaningful share of our users teach or are taking Edexcel English qualifications. We believe the platform already meets the substance of what Pearson looks for in an endorsed teaching and learning resource:

- Content is mapped statement-by-statement to the current Edexcel specifications, with explicit reference to assessment objectives and the relevant text lists
- Contributors include [number — placeholder] current or recent senior examiners, at least [number — placeholder] of whom have worked on Pearson Edexcel English specifications
- We operate a documented editorial process with second-reader review, an explicit accuracy policy, and a safeguarding policy appropriate to digital products used by 14–16 year olds
- We do not currently use any Pearson or Edexcel branding and we understand that any use of the "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" logo would be subject to a separate trademark licence

We would welcome the opportunity to have The English Hub independently reviewed. We are happy to start with whichever specification would allow the fastest and most meaningful review — we would suggest GCSE English Literature (1ET0) as the first candidate, and to add 1EN2, 4ET1 and 4EA1 in subsequent submissions.

**What we have prepared**

To make the review as efficient as possible we have prepared the following artefacts, which we can send to the reviewer named by your team on request:

1. Company profile
2. Product overview
3. Spec-by-spec curriculum alignment mapping (1ET0, 1EN2, 4ET1, 4EA1)
4. Assessment objective alignment document
5. Sample content representative of each paper / component
6. Editorial process and accuracy policy
7. Safeguarding policy
8. Our internal view on commercial and trademark terms

We have also reviewed Pearson's publicly available guidance on endorsed resources, including the statement that publisher-produced assessment content within an endorsed resource is not itself "approved" by Pearson. We will ensure that any practice questions, exemplar responses or mock assessments within The English Hub that are endorsed for use with an Edexcel specification are clearly distinguished from official Pearson assessment material in line with that guidance.

**Open questions**

2. What royalty arrangement is usual for endorsed resources in English at GCSE / IGCSE level?
3. Are you able to endorse a subscription-based digital resource on the same basis as a printed textbook, or is the review process different?
4. Do you prefer to scope endorsement per specification or across a wider subject cluster in a single review?
