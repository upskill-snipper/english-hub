## 2. 4ET1 — International GCSE English Literature

### 2.1 Overview (as understood from the public specification)

4ET1 is a linear International GCSE in English Literature. Papers assess responses to set prose, drama and poetry texts and to unseen poetry.

Key structural features we mapped against:

- **Component 1: Poetry and Modern Prose** — set poems from Part 3 of the Pearson Edexcel International GCSE English Anthology (comparison essay) and one modern prose text
- **Component 2: Modern Drama and Literary Heritage Texts** — one modern drama text and one literary heritage text; open book for literary heritage

Assessment objectives: AO1, AO2, AO3, AO4 (weightings as per current specification).

**Note:** Any text or weighting reference in this document must be verified against 4ET1 Issue 3 before submission. The English Hub content must be aligned to Issue 3 and must cover the two new texts coming into assessment in May/June 2026.

## 3. 4EA1 — International GCSE English Language A

### 3.1 Overview

4EA1 is the International GCSE English Language A qualification. Current version: **Issue 7**, effective from 1 September 2025.

Structural features of the qualification the Hub has mapped against:

- **Paper 1: Non-Fiction Texts and Transactional Writing**
- **Paper 2: Poetry and Prose Texts and Imaginative Writing** — references Part 2 of the Pearson Edexcel International GCSE English Anthology
- **Optional Coursework route** (where offered) — separate mapping exists

Assessment objectives cover reading, writing and SPaG. Exact AO codes and weightings must be pulled from the current 4EA1 Issue 7 specification PDF before this document is submitted.

## 6. Editorial integrity notes

- Set text extracts are used only under rights-cleared quotation limits. Full texts are never reproduced. Where students need the full text, the Hub directs them to the authorised edition.
- Level descriptors are paraphrased, not copied verbatim.
- No practice question is labelled "past paper" unless it is a genuine Pearson past paper used in line with Pearson's reuse guidance.
