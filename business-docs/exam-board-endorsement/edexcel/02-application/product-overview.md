## 1. What The English Hub is

The English Hub is a digital teaching and learning resource for secondary English. It provides structured content mapped to the current Pearson Edexcel English specifications at GCSE and International GCSE level. It is designed to be used by:

- Subject teachers in a classroom setting, as a source of lesson material, worked examples, model answers and formative tasks
- Heads of English building or refreshing schemes of work
- Students working independently or with a teacher's supervision
- Tutors supporting GCSE and IGCSE learners
- Home-educating families following a Pearson Edexcel route

## 3. What is inside the product

Below is the content architecture at subject level. All counts are placeholders and must be trued up before the document goes to Pearson.

### 3.1 Lesson sequences

Teacher-ready sequences organised by specification, unit and assessment objective. Each sequence comprises:

- Learning objectives stated in Pearson Edexcel specification wording
- A suggested lesson plan with timings
- Student-facing explanation of the concept or text
- Worked example(s) matched to the AO weighting of the paper
- Guided practice activities
- Independent practice with success criteria
- A short formative check

### 3.2 Set text coverage

For every prescribed Edexcel set text in scope, The English Hub provides:

- Context and author background
- Chapter / scene / act summaries
- Character, theme and motif analysis
- Annotated extracts (extracts only; full text is not reproduced — see permissions below)
- Worked exemplar responses to typical exam question stems
- Revision grids

### 3.3 Assessment objective practice

Structured practice banks mapped to each AO defined in the Edexcel specification (AO1, AO2, AO3, AO4 for Literature; AO1–AO6 for Language; plus the separate Spoken Language Endorsement material for 1EN0/1EN2). Each practice item has:

- A clear AO tag
- Model response
- Marking guidance referencing the generic Edexcel level descriptors without copying verbatim protected language

### 3.4 Revision tools

- Knowledge organisers
- Quote banks for set texts
- Timed question drills
- Self-quizzing
- Printable revision mats

### 3.5 Teacher tools

- Printable worksheets
- Editable lesson slides
- Homework packs
- Progress trackers

## 4. What The English Hub deliberately does NOT do

To keep the scope crisp and to respect both Pearson IP and the limits of Pearson's endorsement remit, the product deliberately does not include:

- Full reproductions of copyright set texts
- Claims that any assessment is "approved" by Pearson
- Any wording that implies Pearson has reviewed or certified practice assessment content
- Claims of being an official Pearson textbook
- Use of the Pearson corporate logo or the Edexcel logo outside the specific use rights granted (if any) under an endorsement agreement
- Practice papers described as "past papers" unless they are genuine past papers in the public domain and used under Pearson's stated reuse guidance

## 6. Editorial standards

Every piece of content in The English Hub goes through the editorial process documented in `03-evidence/editorial-process.md`:

1. Author brief defined against the relevant Pearson Edexcel specification clause
2. Draft by a qualified English teacher or subject specialist
3. Second-reader review by an independent examiner or senior teacher
4. Fact and accuracy check (see `03-evidence/accuracy-commitment.md`)
5. Safeguarding and age-appropriateness check (see `03-evidence/safeguarding-compliance.md`)
6. Copy edit
7. Publication

## 7. How teachers use The English Hub (typical workflow)

- A Year 10 English teacher is about to start teaching the modern drama text for IGCSE English Literature (4ET1). They open The English Hub, filter by "4ET1 > Modern Drama > [text]" and find a 6-lesson sequence, a quote bank, a character analysis pack, and three exemplar extended responses. They use the lesson slides as-is for the first two lessons, then adapt the worksheets.
- A Year 11 teacher is preparing a GCSE English Language 1EN2 class for Paper 1 reading questions. They use The English Hub's AO-tagged practice bank to pull a targeted 20-minute warm-up on AO2 language analysis.
- An independent student uses a knowledge organiser for a set poem from the Pearson Edexcel International GCSE Anthology to revise quotations and poetic devices before a mock.

## 10. Why we are confident The English Hub meets Pearson's criteria

- The content already references Pearson Edexcel specifications directly, and has been rewritten every time Pearson has issued an update
- The editorial team includes current and former examiners with experience in Pearson Edexcel English specifications (see evidence files)
- The product is digital-native and can be updated quickly if a reviewer flags anything. Turnaround on editorial fixes is measured in days, not months
- We are prepared to scope the first endorsement narrowly (e.g., a single specification) to make the review tractable, and to grow scope only after a first endorsement is in place
