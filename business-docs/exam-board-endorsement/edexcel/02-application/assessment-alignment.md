# Assessment Alignment — How The English Hub Supports Edexcel Style and Assessment Objectives

**Purpose:** Show Pearson's subject reviewer how teaching and learning content inside The English Hub supports students in meeting the Edexcel assessment objectives (AOs) for GCSE and International GCSE English. Explicitly separate teaching material from publisher-written practice content so that there is no confusion about what is or is not "approved" by Pearson.
**Status:** Draft v0.1. Placeholders must be verified before submission.
**Key reminder:** Pearson's endorsement covers **teaching and learning** content. Publisher-produced practice assessments in an endorsed resource are **not** approved as mirroring live exam standards. Our language in this document — and in the product — reflects that limit.

---

## 1. The rule we apply internally

Every piece of content in The English Hub falls into one of three categories, and they are clearly labelled in the product and in this submission:

| Category | Definition | Example | Endorsement applies? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Teaching and learning** | Explanations, worked analyses, lesson sequences, annotated extracts, teacher guidance | "Four ways Shakespeare uses imagery in Act 1 Scene 5" | Yes |
| **Practice** | Publisher-written exam-style questions, exemplar responses, marking guidance | "Practice question written by The English Hub editorial team" | **No — not approved as reflecting live exam standards** |
| **Archive / past paper** | Genuine past paper material reused under Pearson's published reuse guidance | A 2022 past paper question | No — source attribution to Pearson only |

## 2. Pearson Edexcel assessment objectives we target

### 2.1 GCSE English Literature (1ET0)

- **AO1** — Read, understand and respond to texts; maintain a critical style and develop an informed personal response; use textual references.
- **AO2** — Analyse the language, form and structure used by a writer to create meanings and effects, using relevant subject terminology where appropriate.
- **AO3** — Show understanding of the relationships between texts and the contexts in which they were written.
- **AO4** — Use a range of vocabulary and sentence structures for clarity, purpose and effect, with accurate spelling and punctuation.

### 2.2 GCSE English Language (1EN0 / 1EN2)

- **AO1** — Identify and interpret explicit and implicit information and ideas; select and synthesise evidence from different texts.
- **AO2** — Explain, comment on and analyse how writers use language and structure to achieve effects and influence readers, using relevant subject terminology.
- **AO3** — Compare writers' ideas and perspectives, as well as how these are conveyed, across two or more texts.
- **AO4** — Evaluate texts critically and support this with appropriate textual references.
- **AO5** — Communicate clearly, effectively and imaginatively, selecting and adapting tone, style and register for different forms, purposes and audiences.
- **AO6** — Use a range of vocabulary and sentence structures for clarity, purpose and effect, with accurate spelling and punctuation.
- **AO7, AO8, AO9** — Spoken Language Endorsement (presenting, responding to questions, Standard English).

All assessment objective descriptors above are paraphrased from the Pearson Edexcel specifications. Students and teachers using The English Hub are always directed to the current specification on qualifications.pearson.com as the authoritative source.

### 2.3 International GCSE

4ET1 and 4EA1 each have their own AO codes and weightings. The Hub maintains a separate internal reference document with the current wording, paraphrased, and updated at every specification issue. See `curriculum-alignment-igcse.md`.

## 3. How The English Hub supports each AO (GCSE Literature example)

The following table shows how teaching and learning content in the Hub supports students in meeting the Literature AOs.

| AO | How we teach it | Example content | Format |
|---|---|---|---|
| AO1 | We model how to read, annotate and structure an argued response with embedded textual evidence | Lesson sequence "Building a Literature paragraph: claim, evidence, analysis" | Lesson sequence + worked examples |
| AO2 | We teach the language and structural terminology expected at GCSE, then show students how to analyse effects | "Close analysis of imagery in [set text extract]" | Annotated extract + lesson |
| AO3 | We teach the social, historical and biographical context of each set text, linked to specific passages | "Context pack: [set text]" | Teacher notes + student summary |
| AO4 | We teach precise vocabulary, sentence-level control and accuracy | "Precision in Literature writing" | Skill module |

Equivalent tables exist for Language AO1–AO6 and for the Spoken Language Endorsement AO7–AO9. They are maintained in the product CMS and can be exported for the reviewer on request.

## 4. Distinguishing teaching content from practice content

Inside the product, every practice item is labelled as such. The following rules apply:

1. **Label.** Every practice question shows a "Practice question — written by The English Hub" tag.
2. **Source.** Where a practice item is modelled on a past question, we say so and credit Pearson Edexcel as the source of the genuine past paper.
3. **Not an exam paper.** No practice item, exemplar or marking guidance is described as "official", "approved", or "endorsed as a past paper".
4. **Marking guidance.** Our marking guidance is written by our editorial team, in our own words, and references the generic Edexcel level descriptors at the level of concept, not verbatim.
5. **Exemplar responses.** Exemplar responses are written by Hub contributors, labelled, and used to illustrate what a response to a given AO weighting might look like. They are not held out as actual candidate responses.

## 5. How we update when Pearson issues a specification change

- Pearson subject updates are monitored weekly by the editorial team.
- When a change is published, an internal ticket is raised to assess impact.
- Impacted content is re-reviewed, updated and second-read.
- Change notes are logged against each updated resource.
- Teachers are notified of material content changes that affect the current cohort.

## 6. Supporting evidence we can send to the reviewer

On request we can supply:

- A list of every AO tag and the content items tagged to it
- Sample marking guidance from at least one exemplar per specification
- Examples of content we withdrew or revised after a Pearson specification update
- Screenshots showing how the "written by The English Hub" label appears in the product

## 7. What we are NOT claiming

- We do not claim that any practice question matches the difficulty, structure or standard of a live Pearson Edexcel exam.
- We do not claim that any exemplar response would be awarded a specific grade or mark in a live assessment.
- We do not claim that the Hub "guarantees" a grade or outcome.
- We do not claim to be an exam board, an awarding body, or a regulator.

## 8. What we are claiming

- Our teaching and learning content is written against the current Pearson Edexcel specifications, by editors and examiners who know those specifications, using only clearly sourced, honestly labelled material.
- Our editorial process is designed so that any endorsed resource is accurate, age-appropriate and classroom-useful.
- We welcome independent review, feedback and correction.
