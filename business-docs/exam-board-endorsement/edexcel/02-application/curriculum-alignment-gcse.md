## 2. How to read the mapping tables

Each table follows the same column structure:

| Spec reference | Content statement (brief, paraphrased) | AO(s) | The English Hub content | Format | Coverage status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

- **Spec reference** — the section number in the specification (e.g., "Paper 1, Section B")
- **Content statement** — short paraphrase of what the specification requires. Not a direct quotation.
- **AO(s)** — Edexcel assessment objectives targeted by that requirement
- **The English Hub content** — module / lesson / resource name inside the product
- **Format** — lesson sequence, worked example, practice bank, set-text pack, etc.
- **Coverage status** — `Full`, `Partial`, `Planned`, `Gap`. Any status other than `Full` needs a note.

## 3. 1ET0 — GCSE English Literature

### 3.1 Overview

GCSE English Literature (1ET0) is assessed by two externally examined papers:

- **Paper 1: Shakespeare and Post-1914 Literature** — one Shakespeare extract and essay, plus one essay on a post-1914 British play or novel
- **Paper 2: 19th-century Novel and Poetry since 1789** — one 19th-century novel extract and essay, plus a comparison of anthology poetry and a response on unseen poetry

Assessment objectives assessed across the qualification: AO1, AO2, AO3, AO4.

## 4. 1EN0 — GCSE English Language (legacy spec)

### 4.1 Overview

GCSE English Language (1EN0) comprises:

- **Paper 1: Fiction and Imaginative Writing** — unseen 19th-century fiction extract, reading questions, plus an imaginative writing task (40% of the GCSE)
- **Paper 2: Non-Fiction and Transactional Writing** — two unseen 20th-/21st-century non-fiction extracts, comparison and analysis questions, plus a transactional writing task (60% of the GCSE)
- **Spoken Language Endorsement** — separately reported, assessing AO7, AO8 and AO9

Assessment objectives assessed across the qualification: AO1, AO2, AO3, AO4, AO5, AO6, plus AO7–AO9 for the Spoken Language Endorsement.
