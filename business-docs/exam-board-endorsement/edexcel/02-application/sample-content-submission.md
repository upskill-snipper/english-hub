# Sample Content Submission — Guidance Note

**Purpose:** Specify exactly what sample content Pearson Publisher Relations and the subject reviewer will receive when they ask for a representative sample of The English Hub. Keep the submission focused: reviewers want a coherent slice, not a content dump.
**Status:** Draft v0.1. Final sample list to be confirmed with the editorial lead and with Publisher Relations once a point of contact is assigned.

---

## 1. Submission principles

1. **Representative, not exhaustive.** A reviewer needs enough content to form a view of editorial quality and curriculum alignment. That is well under 5% of the product.
2. **Self-contained.** Each sample item is readable without needing access to anything else in the platform.
3. **Traceable.** Every sample item links back to a specific clause in a Pearson Edexcel specification and a specific assessment objective.
4. **Honest.** Samples include at least one case where we self-report a weakness or a gap. Reviewers trust honest self-reporting.
5. **Clearly labelled.** Every item shows its author, date of last edit and editorial status. No mystery meat.

## 5. Cross-cutting samples

The reviewer will also receive, alongside the subject samples above:

- A single-page labelling sheet showing how "written by The English Hub" practice content is visually distinguished from teaching content in the product
- One example of a content update ticket that was raised after a Pearson subject update, showing the before / after
- One example of a safeguarding review decision (e.g., where a text was flagged for content warnings)
- A walk-through video (max 5 minutes) of a teacher using the product end-to-end for one lesson

## 6. Format of the submission

- **Bundle format.** PDF exports of each item plus a single master index document.
- **File naming convention.** `EDX_[spec-code]_[item-number]_[short-title].pdf`
- **Master index.** A single spreadsheet listing every sample, its spec reference, its AO focus, the editor who authored it, the second reader, the date of last review, and the coverage status.
- **Video.** Single MP4, hosted on a password-protected page, link in the master index.
- **Login.** Optionally, a reviewer login to the live product with a clearly labelled reviewer account.

## 7. What the submission will NOT contain

- No copyright full texts. Extracts only, within fair-dealing quotation limits.
- No Pearson Edexcel past paper content beyond what Pearson's own reuse guidance permits.
- No use of the Pearson or Edexcel logos.
- No practice content labelled as "approved" or "official".
- No student personal data.
