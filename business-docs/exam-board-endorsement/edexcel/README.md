## 1. Why endorsement matters

Pearson operates a formal programme under which it reviews and badges third-party teaching and learning materials as **"Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications."** Endorsement is not a legal requirement for publishers — Edexcel remains an "open" qualification that any publisher can produce resources for — but endorsed status carries meaningful commercial and strategic weight:

- Right to display the Pearson Edexcel "Endorsed Resource" logo on the product
- Listing in the Pearson "published resources" search directory used by teachers
- Signal of editorial quality to schools, trusts, heads of department and parents
- Defensible moat against unbadged competitors in B2B sales cycles
- Valuation uplift at exit — internal target: **GBP 1.5M–3.0M uplift** based on comparable EdTech transactions
- Unlocks co-marketing routes via Pearson Publisher Relations

## 2. Summary of Pearson's programme

Pearson distinguishes between three overlapping things (detail in `01-research/endorsed-resource-vs-approved.md`):

| Tier | What it is | Who runs it |
|---|---|---|
| Published resources listing | A searchable directory of any publisher's resources tagged to Edexcel qualifications. Lightweight. | Pearson website team |
| Endorsed resource | A resource that has been editorially reviewed and badged by Pearson's independent reviewers. Carries the "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" logo. | Pearson Publisher Relations + subject reviewers |
| "Pearson Publishing" (first-party) | Textbooks published directly by Pearson Schools under the Pearson imprint. Not available to third parties. | Pearson Schools |

The English Hub is pursuing **endorsed resource** status plus a published resources listing. First-party imprint status is not in scope.

**Key fact:** Pearson states it has an open endorsement policy with no barriers to entry. Any publisher may apply. The process is run by the Publisher Relations team (contact: `publisher.relations@pearson.com`).

## 4. Indicative process & timeline

Pearson does not publish a fixed timetable. Based on desk research and comparable textbook endorsement cycles, the realistic end-to-end path is:

| Phase | Weeks | Key deliverables |
|---|---|---|
| 0. Internal readiness | 0–4 | Finalise this pack, legal sign-off, product snapshot fixed |
| 1. First contact | 4–5 | Cover letter + pack sent to `publisher.relations@pearson.com` |
| 2. Publisher Relations triage | 5–9 | Pearson confirms applicability, routes to subject team |
| 3. Sample review | 9–17 | Submit sample content; subject reviewers assess |
| 4. Feedback & revisions | 17–25 | Address reviewer comments; iterate |
| 5. Contract & trademark licence | 25–33 | Sign endorsement agreement, trademark use licence |
| 6. Launch | 33–36 | Logo applied, listing goes live, co-marketing begins |

**Realistic total: 8–9 months from first contact.** Do not promise partners a date before the contract stage.
