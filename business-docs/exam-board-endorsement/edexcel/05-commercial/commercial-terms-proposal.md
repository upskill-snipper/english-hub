# Commercial Terms Proposal — The English Hub <> Pearson Edexcel

**Purpose:** A draft term sheet written from The English Hub's side of the table, to be used as internal preparation for commercial negotiation with Pearson Publisher Relations. This is **not** a sent document. It is the brief from which our negotiator works.
**Status:** Draft v0.1. Requires legal and founder sign-off. Numbers are placeholders pending the internal revenue attribution model (see `royalty-structure.md`).
**Posture:** Reasonable, business-like, ready to walk if Pearson insists on terms that cross any walk-away condition.

---

## 1. Parties

- **Publisher:** The English Hub, registered in England and Wales, company number [placeholder], registered office [placeholder]. ("Publisher")
- **Awarding organisation:** Pearson Education Limited, registered in England and Wales, operator of the Pearson Edexcel suite of qualifications. ("Pearson")

## 2. Subject matter

This agreement will set the terms on which Pearson reviews and, if satisfied, endorses The English Hub's digital teaching and learning resource as an "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" resource for the following specifications (the "Endorsed Specifications"):

- Pearson Edexcel GCSE (9-1) English Literature (1ET0)
- Pearson Edexcel GCSE (9-1) English Language 2.0 (1EN2) **or** Pearson Edexcel GCSE (9-1) English Language (1EN0) — the parties to agree which version is reviewed first
- Pearson Edexcel International GCSE in English Literature (4ET1)
- Pearson Edexcel International GCSE in English Language A (4EA1)

Endorsement may be granted on a specification-by-specification basis and does not need to cover all four Endorsed Specifications in a single review.

## 3. The Publisher's resource

The "Resource" means The English Hub digital teaching and learning platform as it exists at the date of endorsement, together with any content added, updated or removed during the term, in the parts of the platform that relate to the Endorsed Specifications.

The Resource is a digital subscription product. The parties acknowledge that the Resource is not a printed textbook and is updated on a continuous basis.

## 4. Review process

1. The Publisher submits representative sample content to Pearson in a format agreed in advance.
2. Pearson's independent subject reviewers assess the sample against Pearson's published endorsement criteria.
3. Pearson provides written feedback within a reasonable period (target: 8 weeks).
4. The Publisher addresses feedback and resubmits where required.
5. Pearson confirms the endorsement decision in writing.

## 5. Endorsement scope

Endorsement applies only to the teaching and learning content of the Resource that supports the Endorsed Specifications. It does not apply to:

- Content in the Resource that relates to specifications of other awarding bodies
- Publisher-written practice questions, exemplar responses or marking guidance, which remain not approved by Pearson as reflecting live exam standards
- Any third-party content incorporated in the Resource under fair dealing

## 6. Trademark licence

Conditional on endorsement:

- Pearson grants the Publisher a non-exclusive, non-transferable, royalty-free licence to display the "Endorsed for Pearson Edexcel Qualifications" logo on the Resource and in marketing material relating to the Resource, subject to Pearson's brand guidelines.
- The licence is limited to the Endorsed Specifications and to the Resource.
- The licence terminates automatically on termination of the endorsement.
- The Publisher will not use the Pearson corporate logo or the standalone Edexcel mark.
- The parties will execute a trademark licence in Pearson's standard form as an annex to this agreement, subject to reasonable amendment.

## 7. Commercial terms

### 7.1 Royalty

- The Publisher will pay Pearson a royalty of **[placeholder — target 7.5%]** of Net Revenue attributable to the Endorsed Specifications.
- Royalty is calculated quarterly and payable within 30 days of the end of each quarter.
- "Net Revenue" is defined as gross subscription revenue from customers for the Endorsed parts of the Resource, minus sales taxes, refunds, discounts and agent commissions.
- Revenue attribution between endorsed and non-endorsed parts of the Resource is by the method set out in Schedule A (to be drafted — see `royalty-structure.md`).

### 7.2 Review and admin fees

- No upfront review fee payable by the Publisher.
- If Pearson insists on a review fee, the Publisher's fallback position is a single fee, capped at GBP [placeholder], payable on successful endorsement only.

### 7.3 Minimum annual royalty

- The Publisher does not accept a minimum annual royalty in the first year of endorsement.
- From year 2 onwards, the Publisher will consider a minimum calibrated to a realistic share of attributable revenue, not as a protective floor.

### 7.4 Most-favoured-nation

- Pearson confirms that the royalty rate offered to the Publisher is no worse than the rate Pearson offers to comparable digital publishers of equivalent scale at the date of this agreement.

## 8. Reporting and audit

- Quarterly royalty statements issued by the Publisher to Pearson within 30 days of quarter end.
- Annual reconciliation within 60 days of the end of the Publisher's financial year.
- Pearson has the right to audit the Publisher's royalty calculation once per calendar year, on reasonable notice, with scope limited to the royalty and attribution calculations.
- The auditor must sign a confidentiality agreement and must not have access to user-level personal data.

## 9. Editorial undertakings by the Publisher

- The Publisher will maintain the editorial quality represented by the sample content throughout the term.
- The Publisher will update the Resource in response to Pearson subject updates within a reasonable period and will notify Pearson of material changes to Endorsed content.
- The Publisher will distinguish its own practice content from official Pearson assessment material in line with Pearson's public guidance.
- The Publisher will not hold itself out as an exam board, an awarding body, or a regulator.

## 10. Undertakings by Pearson

- Pearson will use reasonable endeavours to complete the review within the target period.
- Pearson will list the Resource in the published resources catalogue during the term.
- Pearson will not unreasonably withhold approval for marketing uses of the endorsement logo that fall within the brand guidelines.
- Pearson will give the Publisher at least 3 months' written notice before withdrawing endorsement, unless withdrawal is required by a material safeguarding, accuracy or IP issue, in which case notice may be shorter.

## 11. Term and termination

- **Term.** Initial term: 3 years from the endorsement date, automatically renewing in 1-year increments unless either party gives 6 months' written notice.
- **Termination for convenience.** Either party may terminate on 6 months' written notice.
- **Termination for breach.** Either party may terminate on 30 days' written notice of an unremedied material breach.
- **Termination on specification change.** If Pearson publishes a new specification issue that materially changes the Endorsed Specifications, the parties will act in good faith to update the Resource. Either party may terminate if the change makes endorsement impracticable.
- **Post-termination.** On termination the Publisher will cease use of the endorsement logo within 90 days, remove it from the live product, and stop describing the Resource as endorsed. Royalty accrued to the date of termination remains payable.

## 12. Warranties, liability and indemnity

- The Publisher warrants it owns or has cleared all rights in the Resource.
- Pearson warrants it owns the endorsement trademark and has the right to grant the licence.
- The Publisher indemnifies Pearson against claims arising from the content of the Resource.
- Pearson indemnifies the Publisher against claims arising from defects in title to its trademark.
- Aggregate liability of each party is capped at the greater of (a) royalties paid or payable in the previous 12 months or (b) GBP [placeholder].
- Neither party excludes liability for death, personal injury, fraud, or anything else that cannot be excluded by law.

## 13. Non-exclusivity

- The Publisher may simultaneously hold endorsement or comparable status with other awarding bodies.
- Pearson may endorse competing resources.

## 14. Dispute resolution, governing law, jurisdiction

- Good-faith negotiation, escalation to senior management, then mediation before litigation.
- Governing law: England and Wales.
- Exclusive jurisdiction: courts of England and Wales.

## 15. Walk-away conditions

The Publisher will not sign an agreement that:

- Charges a royalty on non-attributable revenue
- Imposes a minimum annual royalty the Publisher cannot fund from attributable revenue
- Bars simultaneous endorsement by other awarding bodies
- Requires any assignment of the Publisher's content IP to Pearson
- Requires the Publisher to share user-level personal data
- Imposes uncapped indemnities
- Imposes audit rights that breach UK GDPR
