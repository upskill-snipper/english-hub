## 3. LinkedIn post (~1500 chars)

After two years of marking essays at the kitchen table, English Hub is live on the App Store today.

The pitch is simple. A class of thirty mock papers takes a teacher roughly five hours to mark and annotate to a standard pupils can act on. English Hub does the first pass in around thirty seconds per response, against the actual AQA, Edexcel, OCR or WJEC mark scheme — not a generic rubric. Teachers keep the final judgement; the app does the heavy lifting on AO1 to AO6 banding, evidence-quoting and "what to do next" feedback.

Three things make this different from the generic essay-feedback tools that have appeared over the last eighteen months:

1. Exam-board specificity. The mark schemes are the published ones. A Power and Conflict response is marked as AQA marks Power and Conflict. A Macbeth essay is marked against the relevant board's Literature criteria. The bands are the bands pupils will be assessed against in May.

2. Built by a serving UK English teacher, for UK English departments. The vocabulary, the cadence, the assumed knowledge — all of it is calibrated to the secondary classroom.

3. Children's Code compliance from day one. UK-hosted, ICO-registered (ZC016690), no advertising, no third-party trackers, no data sold. Parents and safeguarding leads can read the policy and find no surprises.

As I told my head of department: "I built the tool I wished I'd had at 8pm on a Sunday." Today it ships to anyone who wants it.

Liverpool, 1 May 2026 — Upskill Energy Limited today released English Hub, an iOS application providing GCSE and IGCSE pupils with structured essay feedback marked against the published AQA, Edexcel, OCR and WJEC mark schemes.
