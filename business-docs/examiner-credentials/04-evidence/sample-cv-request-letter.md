# Sample CV and credentials request letter

**Purpose:** A friendly, professional outreach to a potential examiner contributor asking them to provide the documentation needed to complete the verification file.

**Tone note:** Most contributors respond warmly to this. The tone is respectful, the ask is clear, and the rationale (that we have high editorial standards and diligence in mind) is flattering to the contributor. Do not be defensive about the ask.

---

## Version 1 — First contact (contributor has expressed interest but nothing is in writing yet)

**Subject:** Next steps — credentials and contribution with The English Hub

Dear [First name],

Thank you very much for the conversation on [date / yesterday / last week]. I'm delighted that you're interested in working with us on [topic area, e.g. "our GCSE English Language practice paper programme"].

Before we move to the commercial conversation and the agreement, I'd like to complete a short credentials pack for our internal records. We take editorial quality seriously at The English Hub and the practice of documenting our contributors' backgrounds is important to us — it's also something our investors, partners and (eventually) acquirers will expect to see in good order.

Could I ask you to send me the following, at your convenience:

1. **A current CV**, including your academic qualifications, teaching and professional history, and — most importantly — a clear statement of your examiner appointments. For each appointment, please include:
 - The awarding organisation (e.g. AQA, Pearson Edexcel, Cambridge Assessment)
 - The subject and specification (e.g. GCSE English Language 8700)
 - Your role (examiner, senior examiner, team leader, moderator, assistant principal examiner, etc.)
 - The years in that role.

2. **A copy of a recent appointment letter or contract** from the awarding organisation, confirming your current or most recent examiner role. A redacted copy is completely fine — we don't need to see any commercially sensitive or confidential information. Just enough to evidence the board, subject, role and dates. If this is awkward, a simple email from the board's examiner liaison confirming your appointment is also welcome, but please don't put yourself out to obtain it if it's a hassle.

3. **A LinkedIn profile URL**, if you have one, so we can corroborate the CV internally.

4. **A short professional biography** (around 80–150 words) for potential use on our site, and a **professional photograph** if you have one to hand. Using these is optional — we won't publish anything without your explicit written approval once our contributor agreement is in place.

Two important notes on confidentiality:

- Please do **not** share with us any material you are bound to keep confidential by the awarding organisation. This includes unreleased papers, unpublished mark schemes, candidate scripts or confidential meeting notes. Our commissioning approach always asks you to draw on your own professional judgment and on publicly released materials only. If anything in our briefs ever feels like it might cross that line, please tell me immediately.
- Everything you send me will be stored in our secure contributor file and will not be shared outside The English Hub except under professional obligations of confidence (e.g. with our lawyers or, in due diligence, with a prospective investor under NDA).

If you have any questions about any of this — or about the contributor agreement, which I can share alongside or in parallel — please let me know. I'm very happy to jump on a short call to walk you through it.

Thank you again for your interest. I'm looking forward to working together.

Warm regards,

[Your name]
[Your title]
The English Hub Ltd
[Contact details]

## Version 2 — Historic contributor (work already done, need to formalise)

**Subject:** Formalising our paperwork — retrospective agreement for your past work with The English Hub

Dear [First name],

I hope you're well. I'm writing to ask for a favour — a slightly administrative one, but an important one for us as the business grows up.

You've done some brilliant work for The English Hub in the past, including [specific reference — "the three Paper 1 practice papers in 2023" or "the Literature worked answers you sent over last summer"]. At the time, we didn't have formal contributor agreements in place — it was all done on a handshake basis, and I'm embarrassed to say the paperwork hasn't caught up yet.

As we prepare for [the next growth stage / investment / structured governance], our advisors have quite rightly asked us to formalise the position on everything we publish. That means getting a signed IP assignment on file for every contributor whose work is on our platform, and making sure we have a clean record of each contributor's credentials. This isn't about anything you've done or not done — it's about us closing a paperwork gap that we should have closed at the time.

Could I ask you for the following:

1. **A current CV**, including your examiner appointments with awarding organisations (board, subject, role, years).

2. **If it's easy, a copy of any appointment letter** from the relevant awarding organisation — redacted if necessary to protect anything confidential.

3. **A short signed retrospective IP assignment** covering the work you've already done for us. I'm attaching a draft. It's boilerplate and it simply records that the content you produced belongs to the company now. It does not affect any payment already made and it does not create any new obligations on you going forward.

4. **A signed new contributor agreement** if you're happy to continue working with us, so that any future work is covered from the start. Again, I'm attaching a draft.

5. **Marketing permission** — we'd love to be able to continue describing you as a contributor to the project, using your name, a photograph and a short bio. The permission for this is in both the retrospective assignment and the new agreement. Nothing we say about you will be anything other than truthful and respectful, and we'll run the exact wording past you before publication if you'd like us to.

In exchange, and as a small thank-you for helping us close the paperwork gap, I'd like to offer **[£XXX as a one-off recognition payment / a fresh commission / equity in the EMI scheme — pick your structure]**. This is not a fee for the original work — you've already been paid for that — it's simply a token for your patience with the paperwork.

I know this is a slightly awkward ask and I really appreciate your understanding. If you'd rather we took a different approach, or if you'd prefer to talk it through on a call first, I'm happy to do whatever works for you. Let me know a good time.

With thanks,

[Your name]
[Your title]
The English Hub Ltd
[Contact details]

In that case I'd ask that we remove [your name / your biography / your photograph] from our marketing going forward, which is fine and entirely your right.
