# Examiner Credentials — IP Licensing & Assignment Pack

**Status:** Template pack — to be reviewed by a qualified solicitor before signature

## 1. Why this folder exists

The commercial story The English Hub tells buyers, partners and customers relies on the claim that its learning content is written by — or reviewed by — **practising or former examiners from AQA, Pearson Edexcel and Cambridge Assessment**. That claim is a core differentiator and a significant component of enterprise value.

The problem is that "credentials" are personal. An individual is an AQA examiner — a company cannot be one. If an examiner contributor leaves tomorrow, and there is no contract in place, the company loses:

1. The ability to say *"written by former AQA examiners"* with confidence (because no one on the roster is one any more).
2. The IP in the content that person created, unless there is a valid written assignment.
3. The right to keep using the examiner's name, photograph and biography in marketing.

From an acquirer's perspective, all three of those things must **transfer with the company** on a share sale. If they do not, a buyer will discount their offer — often significantly.

## 2. What "examiner credentials" means in this pack

For the purposes of these documents, "examiner credentials" refers to **four** separable assets:

| Asset | Description | Where it is captured |
|---|---|---|
| **A. The content itself** | Written lesson materials, practice papers, mark schemes, video scripts, marking commentary, rubrics, worked answers | IP assignment clause in the contributor agreement |
| **B. The right to describe the contributor** | Permission to use name, likeness, CV, biography, and the phrase *"written by a former AQA examiner"* in perpetuity | Marketing rights clause |
| **C. Evidence of the credential** | CV, reference letter from the exam board, LinkedIn verification, copy of examiner contract or appointment letter | Examiner register + verification file |
| **D. The ongoing relationship** | Future work, consultations, updates — optional, not essential for transferability | Term & termination clause |

The **first three** survive the relationship ending. The **fourth** does not need to. A buyer cares most about A, B and C.

## 3. How to formalise this

There are four agreements in `02-agreements/`. Pick the right one for each contributor:

- **`examiner-contributor-agreement-template.md`** — Use for examiners who will write or review content. This is the main document. It contains the full IP assignment and the full marketing rights grant.
- **`examiner-advisor-agreement-template.md`** — Use for examiners who only sit on the advisory board, comment on strategy, or lend their name for marketing. Lighter than the contributor agreement.
- **`historic-work-retrospective-assignment.md`** — Use for contributors who have **already done work** that was never contracted. This retrospectively assigns that work to the company. Critical to sign before diligence.
- **`freelance-writer-agreement.md`** — Use for non-examiner content writers (e.g. a qualified teacher who is not an examiner). Still contains full IP assignment, but without the marketing claim rights.

## 5. Minimum viable programme

If you do nothing else, do this (in order):

1. **Build the register.** Fill in `04-evidence/examiner-register.csv` for every contributor who has touched the product, current or past.
2. **Verify credentials.** Follow `04-evidence/cv-verification-procedure.md` for every person on the register. Flag anyone who cannot be verified.
3. **Sign retrospective assignments.** For every historic contributor, execute `historic-work-retrospective-assignment.md`. This closes the biggest gap.
4. **Put the right agreement in place for anyone active now.** Use the contributor or advisor template.
5. **Keep the register up to date.** A single source of truth — not a spreadsheet per project.

## 6. Acquirer perspective in one paragraph

A buyer will ask, on day one of diligence, "show us the agreements with every person whose name appears on a page of your website or in any marketing collateral as an 'examiner' or 'subject expert'." If you cannot produce a signed IP assignment and a verification file for each one, the buyer will either (a) discount the purchase price, (b) escrow part of it pending remediation, or (c) walk. The work in this folder is how you avoid that conversation.

See `05-acquirer-perspective/` for the full buyer's lens.
