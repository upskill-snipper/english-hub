# Anticipated diligence questions and prepared answers

**Audience:** Founders and their deal team.
**Purpose:** A pre-worked list of the questions a buyer's legal, commercial and technical diligence team will ask about examiner credentials, with model answers that we should be able to give in full sentences on the first day of diligence.

> Treat this as a rehearsal script. If you cannot answer any of these today, that is the specific gap the programme needs to close first.

---

## Section A — Legal diligence (IP chain of title)

**Q1. Please provide a list of every person who has contributed content to the company's platform, current or historic, together with the nature of their contribution.**

*Model answer:* See register at `business-docs/examiner-credentials/04-evidence/examiner-register.csv`. The register lists every contributor, with an internal ID, the role they played (writer, reviewer, advisor), the approximate date range of engagement, and a link to their evidence file. The register is maintained by [named owner] and was last refreshed on [date].

---

**Q2. For each contributor, please provide the signed contributor agreement or equivalent IP assignment.**

*Model answer:* Signed agreements are stored in the `examiner-evidence` folder (access controlled) with one folder per contributor, indexed by the register ID. For contributors engaged after [Commencement Date], the agreement is the Examiner Contributor Agreement template (see `02-agreements/`). For contributors engaged before that date, a retrospective IP assignment deed has been signed (see `02-agreements/historic-work-retrospective-assignment.md`). Of [N] historic contributors, [N-1] have signed the retrospective deed; the remaining one is [status, e.g. "unresponsive to two written approaches and has been removed from the site pending resolution"].

---

**Q3. Please confirm that the agreements contain a present-tense assignment of IP to the company, a waiver of moral rights, and a right to use the contributor's name and likeness in marketing.**

*Model answer:* Yes. Clause 4 of the contributor agreement contains a present-tense assignment of all IP in work product. Clause 4.4 contains a waiver of moral rights. Clause 5 grants a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free, sub-licensable and transferable licence to use the contributor's name, photograph, biography and credentials in marketing. Equivalent provisions appear in the retrospective assignment deed.

---

**Q4. Are any of the agreements in the personal name of the founder rather than in the name of the company?**

*Model answer:* No. All agreements are between the contributor and The English Hub Ltd. Any historic correspondence that took place through the founder's personal email or personal accounts prior to incorporation has been reviewed and is not the basis of any IP assignment. *[If this is not true, disclose it here and explain the remediation.]*

---

**Q5. Please provide evidence that the contributor's credentials are accurately described in the company's marketing.**

*Model answer:* For each contributor named in marketing, the evidence file contains a CV received directly from the contributor, at least one additional source of verification (appointment letter, LinkedIn cross-check, publicly visible footprint), and a signed biography and photograph release. The register records the verification method and verification date for each contributor. Marketing claims are reviewed against the register pre-publication using the process in `03-disclosure-restrictions/marketing-claims-review-template.md`, and review records are retained for six years.

---

**Q7. Has the company ever received a complaint or notice from an Awarding Organisation regarding the use of examiner credentials, confidentiality of materials, or the framing of marketing claims?**

*Model answer:* [Yes/No — same rule as Q6.]

## Section C — Editorial and content diligence

**Q12. What process ensures that examiner contributors do not disclose confidential exam board material?**

*Model answer:* The contributor agreement contains an express warranty that the contributor will not include Exam Board Confidential Material in their deliverables (clause 6). The editorial process (see `03-disclosure-restrictions/exam-board-confidentiality-notes.md`) instructs commissioners to brief contributors in a way that asks only for original, publicly-sourced, professionally-judgment material. An incident procedure exists for handling any accidental disclosure. No incidents have been recorded to date. *[Adjust if incidents have occurred.]*

---

**Q13. Is all content created by examiner contributors reviewed for confidentiality-related leakage before publication?**

*Model answer:* Yes. The editorial review checklist explicitly includes a check for any reference to unreleased papers, unpublished mark schemes, candidate scripts or confidential meetings. The review is conducted by a different person from the author.

---

**Q14. Does the company rely on any third-party contracts held between the contributor and an Awarding Organisation?**

*Model answer:* No. The company's rights do not depend on any contract to which it is not a party. The company's rights flow directly from the contributor's own assignment under our agreement, and from the contributor's own professional credentials as evidenced in the verification file.

**Q20. Would any named examiner be materially likely to withdraw from the arrangement on change of control?**

Contributors have been informed, in the standard onboarding conversation, that the company may be sold and that their arrangement will continue under the new ownership. **Q21. Are any contributor agreements being held off-system (paper-only, personal drives, lost files)?**

*Model answer:* No — every agreement is indexed in the central register and stored in the contributor evidence folder. If any are discovered to be held off-system during the diligence period, they will be migrated and the register updated promptly.

## Section F — The "killer" open questions

These are the broad, open-ended questions buyers reserve for the management Q&A session. Pre-agree the answer between founders so the group speaks with one voice.

**Q23. "Walk us through what the 'examiner' claim actually means in your business."**

*Model answer:* Our platform is written and reviewed by a bench of [N] contributors, of whom [N] hold or have held examiner appointments with UK Awarding Organisations. Their role is to contribute their professional expertise as examiners — knowing what makes a strong answer, what common mistakes look like, and how to calibrate practice materials to the published specifications. Their role is not, and never has been, to leak or paraphrase confidential exam board material. Every contributor has a signed agreement that assigns their work to us and grants us the right to describe them fairly in our marketing. The programme is maintained as a living register that is reviewed annually.

---

**Q24. "If we strip the examiner framing out of your marketing tomorrow, what happens to your business?"**

*Model answer:* The examiner framing is a core differentiator, but it is not the whole proposition. Our underlying product — [describe] — would continue to deliver value. The conservative estimate is that removing the examiner framing would compress marketing conversion by [X]% and shift the competitive positioning from a premium to a mid-market product. That said, we believe the framing is both true and defensible, and the programme in this folder is designed to ensure that remains the case.

---

**Q25. "What keeps you up at night about the examiner relationship?"**

*Model answer (honest version, to rehearse internally):* Historically, the biggest risk was that we had great relationships with examiners but thin paperwork. We have spent the last [X months] closing that gap — signing retrospective assignments, verifying credentials, building the register, and formalising our marketing review. The residual risk is (a) the small number of historic contributors we have not yet been able to re-contact for a signed retrospective, and (b) the general market risk of any one high-profile examiner choosing to disengage. We have mitigations for both, described in [reference to mitigation plan].

---

**Tip for the Q&A session:** Short, specific answers beat long, defensive answers. If you don't know the answer, say so, promise to come back with it, and do come back within 24 hours. Buyers forgive a gap identified honestly; they do not forgive a gap spun over.
