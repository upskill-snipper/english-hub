# Academic Advisory Board — Outreach Playbook

**Purpose:** Recruit 3 named Heads of English (or Deputy Heads in charge of English) onto The English Hub's Academic Advisory Board. Named advisors lift the `/about` page out of credibility risk, reduce the key-person / founder-dependency discount on valuation (DD-10 R-LIP-02 adjacent), and provide an ongoing editorial voice on content authenticity.

**Target composition:**

1. **One Head of English** from a respected **UK state secondary** with strong AQA results (for curriculum authenticity)
2. **One Head of English** from a **UK independent school** (for IGCSE / international credibility)
3. **One examiner / senior examiner** for a major board (AQA, Edexcel, OCR, Cambridge, or Eduqas)

**Commitment asked:** 4 hours per quarter (16/year). Quarterly 90-minute content review call + asynchronous review of 2–4 pieces of content per quarter. Annual in-person or Zoom review.

**Offer:**
- **Equity option:** 0.1% non-vested, time-based over 24 months, exercise at current 409a-style valuation — appropriate only once entity restructure completes. Reserve.
- **Cash:** £1,500 annual retainer paid quarterly at £375, OR
- **Honorary:** free family subscription (value ~£180/yr) + named recognition + CPD-logged advisory experience for their own professional record

Most advisors will accept honorary with retainer optional. Budget **£3,000/yr total across all three advisors** as a cash ceiling.

## Sourcing

### UK state-sector HoDs

- **English & Media Centre** (EMC) — follow their editorial board list
- **NATE** (National Association for the Teaching of English) — committee members
- **TES #TeamEnglish** hashtag on X — active HoDs publishing free CPD
- **English in Education** journal editorial board
- **Whole Education** network HoDs
- **Team English** private Facebook group (55k teachers — apply to join, then observe)
- **Ambition Institute English leadership programme** alumni

### UK independent-sector HoDs

- **The Prince's Teaching Institute** (PTI) — runs subject leader networks
- **ISEB** (Independent Schools Examinations Board) — IGCSE examiners in their network
- **HMC / IAPS / GSA** school directories — filter by English department strength
- **Independent Schools Council** directory

### Examiners

- **AQA examiner register** is not public; route via Head of English contacts
- **OCR examiner forum** — engage a recent ex-examiner rather than a serving one (avoids conflict of interest)
- **Cambridge Assessment International Education** publishes principal examiner names in syllabus documents

### Rule-out criteria

- Currently serving as a senior examiner at the board The English Hub claims to be aligned with — conflict of interest
- Already advises a competing platform
- Cannot commit 4 hours per quarter

## Call script — 30-minute introductory call

### Minutes 0–3 · Your story

- One-sentence "who I am" (examiner + founder)
- One-sentence "what The English Hub is" (English specialist + multi-board AI feedback)
- One-sentence "why we're here" (building an advisory board of serving teachers + examiners)

### Minutes 3–13 · Their story

> "Before I tell you more, I'd love to hear where you are right now. What's the hardest part of your English department's week?"

Listen. Take notes. Do not sell.

### Minutes 13–22 · Product reveal

- Show the homepage briefly (<60 sec)
- Show one board-specific AI feedback worked example (e.g. the AQA vs Edexcel vs OCR doc)
- Ask: "If this existed when you were marking Friday's mocks, would it have saved you time?"

### Minutes 22–28 · The ask

> "We're building an Academic Advisory Board — three people, serving HoDs or examiners. The commitment is 4 hours per quarter: a 90-minute content review call and a couple of async reviews. We pay £1,500/yr or the honorary route with a free family subscription. The role is editorial — you get to push back on what we ship. Is that something you'd want to be part of?"

### Minutes 28–30 · Close

- If yes: "Brilliant — I'll send the draft agreement tonight. Quarterly call cadence?"
- If no: "Completely understood. Can I stay in touch as we build? And would you recommend anyone else we should be talking to?"
- If maybe: "Fair enough — let me send a one-off editorial reviewer brief so you can see what this looks like at low commitment."
