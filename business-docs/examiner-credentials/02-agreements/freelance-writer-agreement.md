# FREELANCE WRITER AGREEMENT

> **TEMPLATE — NOT FOR EXECUTION**
> This is a drafting template. It must be reviewed by a qualified English lawyer before signature.

**Use this agreement when:** engaging a **non-examiner** content writer — for example, a qualified teacher, a subject-matter expert, a professional copywriter, or an education consultant who is **not** an appointed examiner of any Awarding Organisation.

**The distinction matters.** The company cannot and must not describe work produced under this agreement as having been *"written by a former AQA examiner"* or similar. Marketing rights under this agreement are limited accordingly. If a contributor is an examiner, use the Examiner Contributor Agreement instead.

## BACKGROUND

(A) The Company operates an online English learning platform for GCSE and A-Level students and commissions freelance writers to produce instructional and practice materials for its platform.

(B) The Writer is a freelance content creator with expertise in [English Language / English Literature / education / related area]. The Writer is not an appointed examiner of any UK Awarding Organisation, and the Parties acknowledge that no statement to the contrary may be made about the Writer in the Company's marketing.

(C) The Writer has agreed to provide writing services to the Company on the terms set out below.

## AGREED TERMS

### 1. Definitions

**"Brief"** means a written specification for a Deliverable issued by the Company to the Writer;

**"Deliverables"** means the items of content the Writer agrees to produce from time to time, as described in a Brief;

**"Intellectual Property Rights"** means patents, copyright and related rights, trade marks, rights in designs, database rights, rights in confidential information and know-how, moral rights and all other intellectual property rights, whether registered or unregistered, anywhere in the world; and

**"Work Product"** means the Deliverables and any drafts, notes, research, outlines, worked answers, rubrics, mark commentary or other material created by the Writer in the course of the Services.

### 2. Services

2.1 During the Term the Writer shall write, edit and deliver original content for the Company in accordance with Briefs agreed from time to time.

2.2 Each Brief shall specify:

 (a) the subject, specification and level targeted (e.g. AQA GCSE English Language 8700, Paper 1);

 (b) the required format and word count;

 (c) the editorial voice and style guide to follow;

 (d) the deadline; and

 (e) the fee payable.

2.3 The Writer shall produce the Work Product with reasonable skill and care and in accordance with the Briefs and the Company's style guide.

2.4 The Writer shall respond reasonably to editorial feedback and shall, where requested, make up to **two (2) rounds** of revisions to each Deliverable at no additional cost.

### 3. Status

3.1 The Writer is engaged as an **independent contractor**. Nothing in this Agreement creates a contract of employment, agency, partnership or joint venture.

3.2 The Writer is responsible for their own income tax, National Insurance and other taxes and shall indemnify the Company against any claim by HMRC in respect of the same, save where such liability arises from the Company's failure to operate PAYE where required.

3.3 The Parties do not intend IR35 to apply to this engagement.

### 4. Intellectual Property — assignment

4.1 **Present assignment.** The Writer hereby assigns to the Company, with full title guarantee and free from all third-party rights, the entire right, title and interest (including all Intellectual Property Rights) in and to all Work Product, to hold the same unto the Company absolutely for the full term of those rights (including any renewals, reversions, extensions and revivals) throughout the world.

4.2 **Retrospective.** To the extent the Writer has produced any work for the Company before the date of this Agreement, clause 4.1 applies to that work as if it had been produced under this Agreement.

4.3 **Trust.** To the extent any Intellectual Property Rights in the Work Product do not vest in the Company under clause 4.1, the Writer holds them on trust for the Company and shall execute further documents as required.

4.4 **Moral rights.** The Writer irrevocably and unconditionally waives all moral rights in the Work Product under the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 in favour of the Company and its successors, licensees and assigns.

4.5 **Background IP.** Background IP owned by the Writer remains the Writer's property. If Background IP is incorporated into the Work Product, the Writer grants the Company a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free, sub-licensable and transferable licence to use it as part of the Work Product.

4.6 **Further assurance.** The Writer shall, at the Company's reasonable request and at the Company's cost, execute documents and do such things as are necessary to perfect the Company's title in the Work Product and to enable enforcement of the Intellectual Property Rights.

### 5. Originality and third-party content

5.1 The Writer warrants that:

 (a) all Work Product is original to the Writer and does not infringe the Intellectual Property Rights of any third party;

 (b) where any third-party material (including quotations from literary works or licensed images) is used, the Writer shall disclose it in the Brief response and shall obtain appropriate permissions or rely on a legally applicable exception, with written confirmation to the Company;

 (c) the Work Product does not contain defamatory, unlawful, obscene or discriminatory material;

 (d) the Work Product has been written by the Writer and not by any third party (human or machine) **save** to the extent disclosed to and agreed with the Company in writing in the Brief, and any AI-generated content has been edited, fact-checked and brought up to professional standard by the Writer; and

 (e) the Writer has taken reasonable steps to check that any quotations, statistics and factual claims in the Work Product are accurate.

5.2 The Writer shall **indemnify** the Company against all losses, damages, costs and expenses arising out of or in connection with any breach of clause 5.1, subject to a cap equal to the fees paid to the Writer under this Agreement in the twelve months preceding the claim, or £10,000, whichever is higher. The cap does not apply to losses arising from fraud or wilful breach.

### 6. Marketing — limited rights and prohibitions

6.1 **Limited credit right.** The Company may credit the Writer by name as the author of specific Work Product (for example, "Written by [Name]") if the Writer so wishes, and the Writer grants the Company permission to do so.

6.2 **No examiner claims.** The Company shall **not**, in any marketing, packaging or description of the Work Product, state or imply that the Writer is or has been an examiner, assessor, moderator, team leader or principal examiner of any Awarding Organisation, unless the Writer subsequently holds such an appointment and the Parties execute an Examiner Contributor Agreement to that effect.

6.3 **Permitted generic descriptions.** The Company may describe the Writer truthfully by reference to their actual qualifications (e.g. "Qualified English teacher", "Subject-matter expert", "Experienced education writer").

6.4 **Biography.** If the Writer provides a biography and photograph, the Writer grants the Company a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free, transferable and sub-licensable licence to use that biography and photograph in marketing, subject to clause 6.2. The Writer may update or withdraw the biography and photograph on reasonable notice; withdrawal does not require the Company to pull existing printed or archived materials but the Company shall remove the biography and photograph from its public-facing marketing within a reasonable time (usually 30 days) of receiving the withdrawal request.

### 7. Fees and payment

7.1 The Company shall pay the Writer the fees specified in each Brief.

7.2 Fees are typically structured as:

 (a) **per Deliverable** (e.g. £[X] per practice paper); or

 (b) **per word** (e.g. £[Y] per 1,000 words); or

 (c) **per hour** (e.g. £[Z] per hour), with a cap agreed in advance.

7.3 The Writer shall invoice the Company on delivery and acceptance of each Deliverable, or monthly in arrears, as agreed.

7.4 The Company shall pay undisputed invoices within **30 days** of receipt.

7.5 Fees are exclusive of VAT, which shall be added to invoices where applicable.

### 8. Confidentiality

8.1 The Writer shall keep confidential any Confidential Information of the Company disclosed in the course of the engagement and shall not disclose it to any third party. This obligation survives termination for **five (5) years**.

### 9. Restrictive covenants

9.1 **Non-solicit of contributors.** During the Term and for **six (6) months** after termination, the Writer shall not solicit or endeavour to entice away any employee or contributor of the Company with whom the Writer had material dealings.

9.2 **Exclusive deliverables.** The Writer shall not re-use or re-purpose any specific Deliverable (or a substantial part of it) for the benefit of any third party or for the Writer's own publication.

9.3 There is **no non-compete** in this Agreement. The Writer is free to write for other education businesses.

### 10. Term and termination

10.1 This Agreement commences on the date above and continues until terminated by either Party on **fourteen (14) days'** written notice, or immediately on written notice for material breach or insolvency.

10.2 On termination, the Company shall pay for Deliverables accepted up to the date of termination. The IP assignment in clause 4 and the confidentiality and restrictive covenant clauses survive termination.

### 11. General

11.1 This Agreement is governed by the laws of **England and Wales**, and the Parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of **England and Wales**.

11.2 No variation is effective unless in writing signed by both Parties.

11.3 The Company may assign this Agreement and the rights granted under it to any successor in business, purchaser of its assets or group company. The Writer may not assign without the Company's prior written consent.

11.4 A person who is not a Party has no rights under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999.

11.5 Electronic signatures are accepted. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts.
