# DEED OF RETROSPECTIVE ASSIGNMENT OF INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND CONFIRMATION OF MARKETING RIGHTS

> **TEMPLATE — NOT FOR EXECUTION**
> This is a drafting template. Because it takes effect as a **deed**, its execution formalities matter: it must be signed in the presence of an independent witness. Take legal advice before use.

**Purpose.** This document is for contributors who have **already done work for the Company** without a written IP assignment in place. It retrospectively assigns that work to the Company and confirms the Company's marketing rights over the contributor's name and credentials.

This is the most important single remedial document in the programme: a buyer in diligence will specifically look for signed retrospective assignments covering every pre-contract contributor whose work still appears on the product or in marketing.

## BACKGROUND

(A) Between approximately **[START DATE]** and the date of this Deed, the Assignor provided services to the Company on an informal or ad hoc basis, including without limitation writing, reviewing, editing and consulting on educational content for the Company's platform (together, the **"Historic Services"**).

(B) In the course of providing the Historic Services, the Assignor created or contributed to a body of materials, an indicative (but not exhaustive) list of which is set out in **Schedule 1** (the **"Historic Work"**).

(C) The Historic Services were provided without a written agreement assigning the Intellectual Property Rights in the Historic Work to the Company.

(D) The Assignor is, or has been, an examiner, assessor, moderator or team leader with one or more UK Awarding Organisations (the **"Credentials"**, as detailed in **Schedule 2**).

(E) In connection with the Historic Services, the Company has, with the Assignor's knowledge, used the Assignor's name, photograph and Credentials in marketing and promotional materials.

(F) The Parties wish to place the ownership of the Historic Work and the Company's marketing rights on a clear written footing by entering into this Deed.

---

## OPERATIVE PROVISIONS

### 1. Definitions

In this Deed:

**"Exam Board Confidential Material"** has the meaning given in clause 5.1;

**"Intellectual Property Rights"** means patents, copyright and related rights, trade marks, rights in designs, database rights, rights in confidential information and know-how, moral rights and all other intellectual property rights, whether registered or unregistered, and including all applications, renewals, extensions and rights to claim priority, subsisting now or in the future anywhere in the world; and

**"Marketing Use"** has the meaning given in clause 3.1.

### 2. Retrospective assignment of Intellectual Property

2.1 **The Assignor hereby assigns to the Company, with full title guarantee and free from all third-party rights, the entire right, title and interest (including all Intellectual Property Rights) in and to the Historic Work**, to hold the same unto the Company absolutely for the full term of those rights (including any renewals, reversions, extensions and revivals) throughout the world.

2.2 For the avoidance of doubt, the assignment in clause 2.1 applies to **all** Historic Work — whether or not specifically listed in Schedule 1 — created by the Assignor for, on behalf of, or in collaboration with the Company before the date of this Deed.

2.3 To the extent that any Intellectual Property Rights in the Historic Work do not vest in the Company by operation of clause 2.1, the Assignor holds those rights on trust for the Company and shall, at the Company's request and cost, do all things and execute all further documents required to give full effect to the assignment.

2.4 **Moral rights.** The Assignor irrevocably and unconditionally waives, in favour of the Company and its successors, licensees and assigns, all moral rights in the Historic Work arising under the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 (including the right to be identified as the author and the right to object to derogatory treatment) and any analogous rights in any other jurisdiction, to the fullest extent permitted by law.

2.5 **No reservation.** The Assignor does not reserve any rights of any kind in the Historic Work. In particular, no royalty, reversionary interest, or further compensation is payable by the Company to the Assignor in respect of the Historic Work save as expressly set out in clause 7.

### 3. Confirmation of marketing rights

3.1 The Assignor confirms, ratifies and (to the extent necessary) grants to the Company a **perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free, sub-licensable and transferable** right and licence to use the Assignor's name, photograph, biographical information and Credentials in connection with the marketing, promotion, sale and operation of the Company's business (the **"Marketing Use"**), including the right to state truthfully:

 (a) that content was written, co-written or reviewed by the Assignor;

 (b) that the Assignor is, or has been, an examiner for the Awarding Organisation(s) identified in Schedule 2;

 (c) descriptive phrases such as *"written by former AQA examiner [Name]"*, *"co-authored by an experienced Cambridge assessor"*, or similar factually accurate characterisations; and

 (d) the Assignor's years of experience and the subjects and levels they have assessed.

3.2 The Company shall use reasonable endeavours to include, where practicable, a disclaimer stating that reference to the Assignor's examiner experience is a description of the Assignor's personal professional background only and **does not imply** that the Company's materials are endorsed, approved or accredited by any Awarding Organisation.

3.3 The Company shall not use the Assignor's name, image or biography in a manner that is derogatory or defamatory, or in a way that falsely attributes to the Assignor content the Assignor did not in fact contribute to.

3.4 The rights granted in this clause 3 **survive the termination of any relationship between the Parties** and continue to bind the Assignor and the Assignor's personal representatives.

### 4. Warranties

4.1 The Assignor warrants to the Company that:

 (a) the Assignor is the sole author of the Historic Work (or, where it was co-authored with the Company's personnel, the Assignor's contribution is the Assignor's own original work);

 (b) the Historic Work does not infringe the Intellectual Property Rights of any third party;

 (c) the Assignor has not assigned, licensed or encumbered the Historic Work in favour of any third party;

 (d) the Assignor was not, in providing the Historic Services, in breach of any contract or other obligation owed to any Awarding Organisation or other third party;

 (e) the Credentials set out in Schedule 2 are accurate and not misleading; and

 (f) the Historic Work does not contain any Exam Board Confidential Material, and if the Assignor becomes aware of any such material included in the Historic Work, the Assignor will promptly notify the Company and co-operate in its removal.

4.2 The Assignor acknowledges that the Company has relied on these warranties in entering into this Deed and in continuing to use the Historic Work and the Marketing Use.

### 5. Exam board confidentiality

5.1 **"Exam Board Confidential Material"** means any material the Assignor is or was contractually or ethically prohibited from disclosing by an Awarding Organisation, including unreleased papers, unpublished mark schemes, candidate scripts, confidential meeting minutes and any material marked "in confidence" or equivalent.

5.2 The Assignor confirms that the Historic Work contains no Exam Board Confidential Material.

5.3 The Assignor shall not at any time disclose to the Company, or incorporate into any further work for the Company, any Exam Board Confidential Material.

### 6. Further assurance

6.1 The Assignor shall, at the Company's reasonable request and at the Company's reasonable cost, do all things and execute all further documents necessary to perfect the Company's title in the Historic Work, to record the assignment with any relevant registry, and to enable the Company to apply for, prosecute, defend and enforce any Intellectual Property Rights in any jurisdiction.

### 7. Consideration

7.1 In consideration for the assignment, the confirmation of marketing rights and the warranties given under this Deed, the Company shall pay the Assignor the sum of **£[AMOUNT]** (the **"Consideration"**) within 30 days of the date of this Deed.

7.2 *(Alternative formulation:)* As this document is executed as a deed, it takes effect without consideration. The Parties nonetheless record that the Assignor has in the past received payment from the Company in respect of the Historic Services on the terms agreed between the Parties at the time, as summarised in Schedule 3.

*(Delete whichever of 7.1 or 7.2 is not applicable. It is strongly recommended, for evidential reasons, that at least a nominal consideration is paid when executing a retrospective assignment, even though the deed format does not technically require it.)*

### 8. Confidentiality of this Deed

8.1 Each Party shall keep the terms of this Deed confidential save where disclosure is required by law, by a regulatory authority, or to professional advisers, investors, prospective purchasers or lenders of the Company under equivalent obligations of confidence.

### 9. General

9.1 **Entire agreement.** This Deed constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties in respect of the Historic Work and the Marketing Use and supersedes any previous understanding, oral or written, relating to its subject matter.

9.2 **Variation.** No variation of this Deed is effective unless in writing and executed as a deed by both Parties.

9.3 **Third-party rights.** A person who is not a Party has no rights under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999.

9.4 **Assignment.** The Company may freely assign the benefit of this Deed (including the assigned rights and the Marketing Use licence) to any successor in business, purchaser of its assets or group company.

9.5 **Counterparts.** This Deed may be executed in counterparts, each of which when executed is an original and all of which together constitute one and the same deed.

### 10. Governing law and jurisdiction

10.1 This Deed and any dispute or claim arising out of or in connection with it (including non-contractual disputes or claims) are governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of **England and Wales**.

10.2 The Parties irrevocably agree that the courts of **England and Wales** have exclusive jurisdiction to settle any such dispute or claim.
