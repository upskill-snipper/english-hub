## BACKGROUND

(A) The Company operates an online learning platform for GCSE and A-Level English students and is building a body of original instructional content, practice materials and assessment commentary of professional quality.

(B) The Contributor is, or has been, an appointed examiner, assessor, team leader or moderator for one or more UK awarding organisations, having such experience and qualifications as are set out in **Schedule 1** (the **"Credentials"**).

(C) The Contributor has agreed to provide certain services to the Company in connection with the creation, review and ongoing improvement of its educational materials, and to grant the Company the rights to use the outputs of those services (including the Contributor's name and professional biography, in the manner permitted by this Agreement) on the terms set out below.

(D) The Parties intend that this Agreement will govern both the services the Contributor has already provided to the Company before the date of this Agreement (as set out in **Schedule 3**) and any services the Contributor may provide to the Company after the date of this Agreement.

## AGREED TERMS

### 1. Definitions and interpretation

1.1 In this Agreement, unless the context otherwise requires:

**"Applicable Law"** means all laws, statutes, regulations and codes from time to time in force in England and Wales;

**"Awarding Organisation"** means any UK exam board, including without limitation AQA, Pearson Edexcel, OCR, WJEC, Eduqas and Cambridge Assessment International Education;

**"Background IP"** means any Intellectual Property Rights owned or controlled by the Contributor before the date of this Agreement, or developed independently of this Agreement, which are not Work Product;

**"Commencement Date"** means the date of this Agreement;

**"Confidential Information"** means information in whatever form disclosed by one Party to the other, or to which a Party has access, which is confidential in nature or identified as confidential, including without limitation business, technical, commercial, financial, customer and product information;

**"Deliverables"** means the items described in the relevant Statement of Work or otherwise agreed between the Parties in writing;

**"Exam Board Confidential Material"** means any material the Contributor is contractually or ethically prohibited from disclosing by an Awarding Organisation, including without limitation unreleased papers, unpublished mark schemes, confidential meeting minutes, live moderation data, candidate scripts and any material marked "in confidence" or equivalent;

**"Fees"** means the sums payable by the Company to the Contributor under clause 7 and **Schedule 2**;

**"Intellectual Property Rights"** or **"IPR"** means patents, utility models, rights to inventions, copyright and related rights, trade marks and service marks, trade names and domain names, rights in get-up and trade dress, goodwill and the right to sue for passing off, rights in designs, database rights, rights to use, and protect the confidentiality of, confidential information (including know-how and trade secrets), moral rights and all other intellectual property rights, in each case whether registered or unregistered and including all applications and rights to apply for and be granted, renewals or extensions of, and rights to claim priority from, such rights and all similar or equivalent rights or forms of protection which subsist or will subsist now or in the future in any part of the world;

**"Marketing Use"** has the meaning set out in clause 5.1;

**"Services"** means the services described in clause 3;

**"Statement of Work"** or **"SoW"** means a statement of work agreed in writing (including by e-mail) between the Parties from time to time;

**"Term"** means the period from the Commencement Date until this Agreement is terminated in accordance with clause 11; and

**"Work Product"** means all deliverables, materials, drafts, notes, documents, commentary, annotations, mark schemes, worked answers, video scripts, rubrics, assessment criteria, illustrations, compilations, data and any other tangible or intangible output created or contributed by the Contributor in the course of performing the Services, whether created alone or jointly with others, and whether delivered or not.

1.2 References to clauses and Schedules are to the clauses of and Schedules to this Agreement. The Schedules form part of this Agreement and have effect as if set out in full in the body of the Agreement.

1.3 Headings are for convenience only and do not affect interpretation.

### 2. Status of the Contributor

2.1 The Contributor is engaged as an **independent contractor** and nothing in this Agreement constitutes or is intended to constitute a contract of employment, a partnership, an agency or a joint venture between the Parties.

2.2 The Contributor:

 (a) shall be responsible for payment of their own income tax, National Insurance contributions and any other applicable taxes arising from the Fees;

 (b) is not entitled to any employee benefits, holiday pay, sick pay, pension contributions or similar;

 (c) is free to provide services to any other person, subject to clause 9; and

 (d) shall indemnify the Company against any liability, assessment or claim by HM Revenue & Customs in respect of income tax, National Insurance contributions or similar arising from or in connection with the Fees, save where such liability arises from the Company's failure to operate PAYE where required by Applicable Law.

2.3 The Parties do not intend IR35 to apply to this engagement and the commercial terms have been agreed on that basis.

### 3. Services

3.1 During the Term the Contributor shall provide such of the following services as are agreed in a Statement of Work from time to time:

 (a) writing original instructional, practice and assessment content for the Company's platform;

 (b) reviewing, editing and commenting on content written by others;

 (c) providing expert consultancy on curriculum, specification mapping and assessment methodology;

 (d) participating in video recordings, webinars and other media outputs; and

 (e) any other related services the Parties agree in writing.

3.2 The Contributor shall provide the Services with reasonable skill and care and in accordance with the standards reasonably expected of a professional with the Credentials.

3.3 The Contributor shall perform the Services at such times and from such locations as the Contributor chooses, subject to reasonable deadlines agreed in the relevant Statement of Work.

### 4. Intellectual Property — assignment

4.1 **Present assignment.** With effect from the creation of each item of Work Product the Contributor hereby assigns to the Company, with full title guarantee and free from all third-party rights, the entire right, title and interest (including all Intellectual Property Rights) in and to that Work Product, to hold the same unto the Company absolutely for the full term of those rights (including any renewals, reversions, extensions and revivals) throughout the world.

4.2 **Retrospective assignment of past work.** The Contributor further assigns to the Company, with full title guarantee and free from all third-party rights, the entire right, title and interest (including all Intellectual Property Rights) in and to each item of work previously created by the Contributor for or on behalf of the Company and listed in **Schedule 3**, together with any such work not listed in Schedule 3 but which was in fact created by the Contributor for or on behalf of the Company before the Commencement Date.

4.3 **Future rights.** To the extent that any Intellectual Property Rights in the Work Product do not vest in the Company by operation of clauses 4.1 or 4.2, the Contributor shall hold those rights on trust for the Company and shall, at the Company's request and cost, do all things and execute all documents required to give effect to the Company's ownership.

4.4 **Moral rights.** The Contributor irrevocably and unconditionally waives, in favour of the Company and its successors, licensees and assigns, all moral rights in the Work Product arising under the CDPA 1988 (including the right to be identified as the author and the right to object to derogatory treatment) and any analogous rights in any other jurisdiction, to the fullest extent permitted by law.

4.5 **No reservation.** Save for the Background IP, the Contributor does not reserve any rights of any kind in the Work Product.

4.6 **Licence of Background IP.** To the extent any Background IP is incorporated into or necessary for the use of the Work Product, the Contributor grants the Company a non-exclusive, irrevocable, perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free, sub-licensable and transferable licence to use, copy, adapt, modify and commercially exploit that Background IP as part of the Work Product.

4.7 **Further assurance.** The Contributor shall, at the Company's reasonable request and at the Company's cost, do all things and execute all documents necessary to perfect the Company's title in the Work Product and to enable the Company to apply for, prosecute, defend and enforce any Intellectual Property Rights in any jurisdiction.

### 5. Use of name, biography and marketing claims

5.1 **Grant.** The Contributor grants the Company a **perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free, sub-licensable and transferable** right and licence to use the Contributor's name, photograph, biographical information, professional credentials and the statements described in clause 5.2 (together, the **"Marketing Use"**) in connection with the marketing, promotion, sale and operation of the Company's business.

5.2 **Permitted statements.** The Marketing Use includes, without limitation, the right to state truthfully:

 (a) that content has been written, co-written or reviewed by the Contributor;

 (b) that the Contributor is, or has been, an examiner for the relevant Awarding Organisation(s) identified in Schedule 1;

 (c) descriptive phrases such as *"written by former AQA examiner [Name]"*, *"reviewed by an experienced Cambridge assessor"*, *"authored by subject experts with examiner experience"*; and

 (d) the Contributor's years of experience and the subjects and levels they have assessed.

5.3 **Disclaimers.** The Company shall, wherever reasonably practicable, include or link to a disclaimer stating that:

 (a) the Contributor's reference to examiner experience is a statement about the Contributor's personal professional background only;

 (b) it is **not** a statement that the Company's materials are endorsed, approved, accredited or recommended by any Awarding Organisation; and

 (c) the Company is not affiliated with any Awarding Organisation except where an express endorsement agreement exists.

5.4 **Contributor's co-operation.** The Contributor shall, on reasonable request, provide the Company with an up-to-date biography, a good-quality photograph, and updated details of qualifications and examiner appointments, for use in Marketing Use.

5.5 **No derogatory use.** The Company shall not use the Contributor's name, image or biography in any manner that is derogatory or defamatory to the Contributor or that falsely implies personal endorsement of content the Contributor did not in fact contribute to.

5.6 **Survival.** The rights granted in this clause 5 **survive termination of this Agreement for any reason** and continue to bind the Contributor and the Contributor's personal representatives.

### 6. Exam board confidentiality and accuracy warranties

6.1 The Contributor **warrants** to the Company that:

 (a) the Contributor is, or has been, the holder of the Credentials described in Schedule 1;

 (b) the Services and Work Product are, and will be, original to the Contributor and do not, and will not, infringe any third-party Intellectual Property Rights;

 (c) the Services and Work Product do not, and will not, contain or disclose any Exam Board Confidential Material;

 (d) the Contributor is not under any contractual, fiduciary or professional duty owed to any Awarding Organisation that prohibits the provision of the Services or the grant of the rights under this Agreement;

 (e) the Contributor will comply with any code of conduct, professional standard or confidentiality obligation applicable to the Contributor as a result of the Credentials; and

 (f) the information set out in Schedule 1 is complete, accurate and not misleading.

6.2 The Contributor shall **not** use, reference, paraphrase or reproduce any Exam Board Confidential Material in the Work Product, in any marketing material, or in any conversation with the Company's staff.

6.3 If the Contributor becomes aware that any Work Product inadvertently contains or discloses Exam Board Confidential Material, the Contributor shall notify the Company immediately and the Parties shall co-operate in good faith to remove or rewrite the relevant material.

6.4 The Contributor shall **indemnify** the Company against all losses, liabilities, damages, costs and expenses (including reasonable legal fees) suffered or incurred by the Company arising from or in connection with a breach of clause 6.1 or 6.2, subject to a financial cap equal to the aggregate Fees paid to the Contributor under this Agreement in the twelve months preceding the relevant claim, or £25,000, whichever is higher. The cap in this clause 6.4 does not apply to losses arising from fraud, fraudulent misrepresentation or wilful breach.

### 7. Fees and expenses

7.1 In consideration for the Services and the rights granted under this Agreement, the Company shall pay the Contributor the Fees set out in **Schedule 2**.

7.2 The Fees may be structured as one or more of the following, as specified in Schedule 2:

 (a) a **fixed fee per deliverable** (e.g. £[X] per practice paper);

 (b) a **time-based fee** (e.g. £[Y] per hour or per day);

 (c) a **revenue share** calculated as a percentage of net revenue attributable to content substantially written by the Contributor, defined and reported in accordance with Schedule 2;

 (d) **equity** in the Company (subject to a separate subscription or option agreement); or

 (e) any combination of the above.

7.3 The Contributor shall invoice the Company monthly in arrears. The Company shall pay undisputed invoices within **30 days** of receipt.

7.4 All Fees are exclusive of VAT, which shall be added to invoices where applicable and payable on receipt of a valid VAT invoice.

7.5 The Contributor shall bear their own expenses unless specifically agreed in a Statement of Work.

### 8. Confidentiality

8.1 Each Party shall keep the Confidential Information of the other Party strictly confidential, not disclose it to any third party without prior written consent, and use it only for the purposes of this Agreement.

8.2 The obligations in clause 8.1 do not apply to information which:

 (a) is in the public domain other than through a breach of this Agreement;

 (b) was lawfully in the receiving Party's possession before disclosure;

 (c) is lawfully received from a third party free of any obligation of confidence; or

 (d) is required to be disclosed by law, court order or regulatory authority, provided the receiving Party gives prompt notice where lawful.

8.3 The obligations in this clause 8 survive termination of this Agreement for a period of **five (5) years**.

### 9. Restrictive covenants

9.1 The Contributor acknowledges that the Company has a legitimate business interest in protecting its goodwill, confidential information and relationships with its contributors and customers, and that the restrictions in this clause 9 are reasonable and necessary to protect those interests.

9.2 **Non-compete.** The Contributor shall not, during the Term and for a period of **six (6) months** after termination, directly or indirectly engage in, work for, or advise any business which:

 (a) produces or sells GCSE or A-Level English learning materials in direct competition with the Company's published product range; and

 (b) markets those materials on the basis of the Contributor's examiner credentials.

 For the avoidance of doubt, this clause 9.2 does not prevent the Contributor from continuing to work as an examiner for any Awarding Organisation, from teaching, from private tutoring, from academic writing, or from any activity that does not both compete with the Company's published products **and** rely on the Contributor's examiner credentials for marketing.

9.3 **Non-solicit of contributors.** The Contributor shall not, during the Term and for a period of **six (6) months** after termination, solicit, entice or endeavour to entice away any other contributor, advisor or employee of the Company with whom the Contributor had material dealings during the Term.

9.4 **Non-solicit of customers.** The Contributor shall not, during the Term and for a period of **six (6) months** after termination, solicit business from any customer or prospective customer of the Company with whom the Contributor had material dealings during the Term, in competition with the Company's business.

9.5 Each restriction in this clause 9 is a separate and severable undertaking. If any restriction is held unenforceable, the remainder continues to apply.

### 10. Data protection

10.1 Each Party shall comply with its respective obligations under the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

10.2 In relation to the Contributor's personal data processed by the Company for the purposes of this Agreement (including for Marketing Use), the Company acts as **data controller**. The Contributor acknowledges having read the Company's privacy notice for contributors.

### 11. Term and termination

11.1 This Agreement commences on the Commencement Date and continues until terminated in accordance with this clause.

11.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time on **thirty (30) days'** written notice to the other Party.

11.3 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately by written notice if the other Party:

 (a) commits a material breach of this Agreement which is either incapable of remedy or, if capable of remedy, is not remedied within 14 days of written notice requiring remedy;

 (b) becomes insolvent, is subject to a bankruptcy order, enters into a voluntary arrangement with creditors, has a receiver or administrator appointed, or ceases to carry on business.

11.4 **Consequences of termination.** On termination of this Agreement for any reason:

 (a) the Company shall pay the Contributor any Fees accrued up to the date of termination in respect of Services properly provided;

 (b) the assignment of IPR in clauses 4.1 to 4.3 and the Marketing Use rights in clause 5 **continue in full force** and are not affected by termination;

 (c) the Contributor shall return, or destroy and certify the destruction of, all Confidential Information in the Contributor's possession or control;

 (d) the restrictive covenants in clause 9 take effect for their stated periods; and

 (e) the provisions of clauses 1, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, 10, 11.4, 12, 13 and 14 survive termination.

### 12. Limitation of liability

12.1 Nothing in this Agreement limits or excludes either Party's liability for:

 (a) death or personal injury caused by negligence;

 (b) fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation; or

 (c) any other liability that cannot be limited or excluded by Applicable Law.

12.2 Subject to clause 12.1, the Company's total aggregate liability to the Contributor arising out of or in connection with this Agreement is limited to the total Fees paid to the Contributor in the twelve months preceding the claim.

12.3 Subject to clause 12.1 and clause 6.4, the Contributor's total aggregate liability to the Company arising out of or in connection with this Agreement is limited as set out in clause 6.4.

### 13. General

13.1 **Entire agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties in relation to its subject matter and supersedes all previous agreements, representations and understandings.

13.2 **Variation.** No variation of this Agreement is effective unless in writing and signed by or on behalf of both Parties.

13.3 **Assignment.** The Contributor may not assign, transfer or sub-contract this Agreement without the prior written consent of the Company. The Company may assign or transfer this Agreement and the rights granted under it to any successor in business, purchaser of its assets, or group company.

13.4 **Third-party rights.** A person who is not a Party to this Agreement has no rights under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999 to enforce any term.

13.5 **Notices.** Any notice under this Agreement must be in writing and delivered by hand or sent by first-class post or by e-mail to the address of the recipient set out at the head of this Agreement, or as otherwise notified in writing.

13.6 **Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which when executed is an original and all of which together constitute one and the same instrument. Electronic signatures are effective.

### 14. Governing law and jurisdiction

14.1 This Agreement and any dispute or claim arising out of or in connection with it (including non-contractual disputes or claims) are governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of **England and Wales**.

14.2 The Parties irrevocably agree that the courts of **England and Wales** have exclusive jurisdiction to settle any dispute or claim arising out of or in connection with this Agreement.
