# Marketing claims review — pre-publication checklist

**Purpose:** Every customer-facing piece of content that mentions examiners, awarding organisations, accreditation, approval or examiner experience must pass this review before it is published.

**Turnaround target:** Within one working day. Do not hold up publication unnecessarily, but do not publish until a reviewer has signed off.

**Reviewer:** The CEO, or a delegated reviewer appointed in writing (Head of Content or General Counsel once appointed).

**How to use:** Fill in one of these for each item of content under review. Save a copy in the content management system against the published item. Keep the review record for at least **six (6) years** (to cover the likely diligence lookback period in an exit process).

## Section D — Prohibited phrase scan

Tick any that appear in the content. **If any box is ticked, the content is rejected pending rewrite.**

- ☐ "AQA-approved" / "AQA-endorsed" / "Approved by AQA"
- ☐ "Edexcel-accredited" / "Cambridge-approved" / similar for any awarding organisation
- ☐ "Official AQA/Edexcel/Cambridge/Eduqas resource"
- ☐ "In partnership with AQA / Pearson / Cambridge Assessment"
- ☐ "Insider knowledge"
- ☐ "Written from inside the exam board"
- ☐ "Based on the real / confidential / unreleased mark scheme"
- ☐ "See next year's paper"
- ☐ "Our examiners know what's coming up"
- ☐ Use of an awarding organisation **logo** without a licence
- ☐ Implied endorsement through layout, visual proximity to logos, or colour scheme that mimics a board's branding

---

## Section E — Precision check

- ☐ Where the claim refers to a specific examiner, is that examiner **named**?
  *(Preferred. Anonymous claims are weaker and harder to defend.)*

- ☐ If named, does the named examiner **exist in the register**?

- ☐ Is the board correctly identified? (e.g. we do not describe an AQA examiner as an "Edexcel examiner")

- ☐ Is the tense correct? ("former" vs. "current"). If the person is currently appointed, we may say so only with their express written consent for that specific use.

- ☐ Is the role correctly described? (e.g. examiner vs. principal examiner vs. team leader vs. moderator)

- ☐ Have we verified the years of experience mentioned?

---

## Section F — Disclaimer presence

- ☐ Does the content (or a reasonably proximate linked page) include the standard disclaimer:

> *"References to examiner experience describe the personal professional background of named contributors. The English Hub is independent and is not endorsed, approved, accredited or otherwise affiliated with AQA, Pearson Edexcel, OCR, Cambridge Assessment, WJEC, Eduqas or any other awarding organisation."*

  If "no", either add the disclaimer or note where a user can reach it within two clicks. A disclaimer buried six clicks deep is not a disclaimer.

> **Record retention:** Keep this review record for at least **six years** from the date the content is taken down, in an indexed location searchable by the content URL. Being able to produce one on demand is the difference between a two-hour answer and a two-week slog.
