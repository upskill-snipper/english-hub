# The English Hub — Campaign Packs v1 (Top-Quality, Per-Channel)

**Purpose:** not a template library — finished, shippable creative for each priority campaign, per channel, with asset briefs, measurement, and exact send windows. Built from the site facts and audience segmentation locked in `OUTREACH_MESSAGES_v2.md`.

**In this file:**

1. Campaign 01 · UK English Teacher Creators (affiliate partnerships)
2. Campaign 02 · UK Secondary School English HoDs (free-trial acquisition)

Campaigns 03–08 (Parent Bloggers, Student Micro-Influencers, Founding Schools / MATs, International British Curriculum Schools, UK Tutoring Companies, UK Education Journalists) scoped at the end of this doc — please confirm priority order and I'll build them next to the same depth.

**Global brand rules that apply to every campaign below:**

- Tone: confident, warm, teacherly. Never chirpy. No "literally", no "no cap", no emoji salad. One emoji max per social post if at all.
- British English throughout. "Programme" not "program". "Maths" not "math". "Year 10", "Year 11", "GCSE", "IGCSE".
- Colour palette (from the site): primary green `#2DD4A8`, secondary apricot `#E8A382`, cream `#FBF7F0` on ink `#0F1411`. Serif "The" / italic "English" / roman "Hub" lockup exactly as the footer renders it — never redraw the wordmark.
- Type: serif display for editorial / social hero graphics, geometric sans for UI captions. A good stand-in pair: **Fraunces** (display) + **Inter** (body). If fonts aren't procured yet, Times New Roman + Helvetica is acceptable but mark the asset as "v0 — pending font procurement".
- Every outbound link carries UTM: `?utm_source=[channel]&utm_medium=[email|dm|social]&utm_campaign=[campaign_code]&utm_content=[variant]`. Never send a bare `theenglishhub.app` URL in a measurable channel.
- Every outbound creative carries a single CTA. If a second CTA is unavoidable, make it a softer "or reply with your question" fallback — never a competing ask.

### 01.9 · Reply playbooks (the bit most campaigns skip)

**If they reply "yes, send access":** within one working hour, send a 4-line email with the access link, a 60-second unlisted Loom tour of the teacher dashboard, and one sentence: "If you'd like the creator side too, the 2-minute form is at [URL]." Do not auto-enroll them — enrollment should be their explicit act.

**If they reply asking for comp / a bigger incentive:** offer the Creator Partner programme as-is (20% / 30 days). If they're a macro-tier (100K+) and push harder, the fallback is: £150 sponsored post fee + standard programme, capped at four such sponsorships across the whole campaign. Anyone in the Mr Bruff / BBC Bitesize / Tutor2u tier is a separate conversation — do not offer cash on a cold reply.

**If they reply "not a fit right now":** one line back — "Totally understood — if you'd like the free teacher account anyway, reply and I'll send it. Otherwise, thank you for reading." Never push. Record "not now" in CRM with a 90-day review flag.

**If they reply negatively / aggressively:** one line — "Thanks for the honest reply, I'll take you off the list. If anything about the product ever changes your mind, the door's open." Remove permanently, flag in CRM, do not re-add.

Only use when we have news worth an exclusive — e.g. the first Founding School signing, or a shipped feature with a principled "what we chose not to build" angle. Send to **one** journalist at a time, not a list.

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> Short note. I'd like to offer you the first look at [SPECIFIC ANGLE / NEWS, e.g. "the decision we've just made to label our AI feedback explicitly with an AO breakdown and an opt-out button rather than a default-on rollout"].
>
> Under embargo until [DATE] if useful. Happy to walk you through the reasoning, the product decision, and the data behind it on a 30-minute call. If it's the wrong shape for your beat, let me know and I'll take it elsewhere.
>
> Calum

### 08.5 · Reply playbooks

**"Happy to quote you on [TOPIC]":** reply same working hour. Answer clearly, on the record, three sentences max. If it needs more thought, say "I'd like 24 hours to give you something considered — by [TIME] tomorrow?" Do not over-deliver a rambling quote.

**"Would love to do the interview":** confirm recording within the working day. Send a one-line summary of what you'll bring. Deliver on it.

**"Not for us":** thank them, ask if there's a colleague who covers this beat, offer to be removed if not. Accept "no" gracefully — journalists remember over-persistent founders more than they remember the pitch.

**"Can you send the data behind [X] claim?":** this is the reply that separates good press outreach from bad. Any quantitative claim we've made must be backed by the underlying data on request. Send it within the day. If we haven't got it, correct the claim publicly and apologise.

**"Send a press release":** we don't currently do press releases — we do founder-signed notes. Say so honestly: "We don't run a press-release workflow — all our media outreach is founder-signed, like this one. If a formal press release would make the piece easier, I can draft one to your spec by end of day."
