### Brand Voice

- **Tone:** Friendly, relatable, slightly witty. The older sibling who got a 9 and actually wants to help.
- **Language:** Gen Z-native without forcing it. Use "lowkey", "no cap", "literally" naturally -- never "fellow kids" energy.
- **Perspective:** Speaks TO students as equals, not AT them from above.
- **Key phrases to own:** "Let's fix this," "Here's the cheat code," "Your examiner wants to see THIS."
- **Avoid:** Condescending language, overly formal academic tone, clickbait that doesn't deliver.

### Content Pillars

All content maps to one of these five pillars:

| # | Pillar | Purpose | Platform Split |
|---|--------|---------|----------------|
| 1 | **Exam Intel** | Grade-boosting tips, examiner secrets, mark scheme decoded | 40% TikTok / 50% YouTube |
| 2 | **Quick Fixes** | Grammar, vocabulary, technique explainers in under 60s | 30% TikTok / 20% YouTube |
| 3 | **Relatable Reality** | Student humor, POV videos, common mistakes roasted | 20% TikTok / 5% YouTube |
| 4 | **Build in Public** | App development updates, behind the scenes, founder journey | 5% TikTok / 10% YouTube |
| 5 | **Deep Dives** | Full walkthroughs, text analysis, essay masterclasses | 5% TikTok / 15% YouTube |

### Posting Frequency

| Platform | Frequency | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|-----------|

| YouTube | **1x per week** (every Sunday) | Longer production cycle; students plan revision at start of week |
| YouTube Shorts | **3x per week** | Repurpose top TikToks; cross-pollinate audiences |

### How to Get Initial Followers Quickly

1. **Comment strategy (Day 1-7):** Spend 30 minutes daily leaving genuinely helpful comments on popular GCSE revision TikToks. Not "check out my page" -- actual advice like "Great tip! I'd also add that examiners love seeing embedded quotes." People will check your profile.

2. **Duet/Stitch popular content:** Find viral GCSE English videos and duet with your expert take. Piggyback on their reach.

3. **Leverage the question sticker:** Post a TikTok with the question sticker: "What GCSE English question do you want me to answer?" Responses boost engagement signals.

4. **Cross-pollinate from the app:** If the app has existing users, send an in-app notification: "We're on TikTok now! Follow for daily tips: @theenglishhubapp."

5. **Share to relevant communities:** Post links in student Facebook groups, Reddit (r/GCSE), The Student Room forums, and Discord study servers. Not spammy drops -- engage in existing conversations and share relevant videos when helpful.

6. **"Follow for Part 2" strategy:** End Videos 15/16 ("Things Your English Teacher Won't Tell You") with "Part 2 drops Thursday -- follow so you don't miss it." Series content drives follows.

7. **Posting consistency above all:** The TikTok algorithm rewards accounts that post daily in their first 2-4 weeks. Do not miss a day in week one.
