# IGCSE-Focused Messaging

Mainland UK GCSE content is the default for most "English resources" on the web. International schools are frequently sold UK content wearing an "international" label. This document captures messaging that addresses the real IGCSE specifications — Cambridge 0500 / 0990 and Edexcel 4ET1 — that international schools actually teach.

## Why "GCSE" marketing alienates international buyers

When an international Head of English sees marketing copy that says:
- "Aligned to the new GCSE spec"
- "AQA, Edexcel, OCR and Eduqas GCSE English"
- "Helping students ace their GCSEs"

...they mentally bin the pitch. Those are all mainland UK exam boards and codes. The international buyer needs to see "Cambridge IGCSE" and "Edexcel IGCSE" within the first 20 words, or they assume the product isn't built for them.

## Core messaging pillars

### 1. Specification-level alignment, not general "English"

We don't say: *"Great resources for IGCSE English."*

We say:

> Every lesson in The English Hub is mapped to specific assessment objectives in Cambridge IGCSE 0500, 0990, 0475, 0992 and Pearson Edexcel IGCSE 4ET1, 4EA1, 4EB1. When you click a resource, you see which paper, which section, which AO, and which mark scheme band it targets.

### 2. The specifications we actually cover

**Cambridge IGCSE:**
- **0500 First Language English** — Paper 1 Reading (summary, directed writing), Paper 2 Directed Writing and Composition, Paper 3 Coursework portfolio.
- **0990 First Language English (9-1 grading)** — sister specification with the same papers using the new grading scale.
- **0475 Literature in English** — Paper 1 Poetry and Prose, Paper 2 Drama, Paper 3 Coursework, Paper 4 Unseen.
- **0992 Literature in English (9-1 grading)** — sister specification.
- **0510 / 0511 / 0991 English as a Second Language** — Reading and Writing, Listening, Speaking.

**Pearson Edexcel IGCSE:**
- **4EA1 English Language Specification A** — Paper 1 Non-fiction texts and transactional writing, Paper 2 Poetry and prose texts and imaginative writing.
- **4EB1 English Language Specification B** — Paper 1 (reading and writing combined).
- **4ET1 English Literature** — Paper 1 Poetry and Modern Prose, Paper 2 Modern Drama and Literary Heritage Texts.
- **4EO1 Oral Endorsement** where used.

**AQA International (growing niche):**
- Oxford AQA International IGCSE English Language 9280
- Oxford AQA International IGCSE English Literature 9275

### 3. The paper-by-paper value

**Cambridge 0500 Paper 1 Reading:**
- Directed writing with a shift of audience or form
- Summary writing
- Reading for ideas and writer's effects

*Our claim:* every practice text is accompanied by a pre-marked model answer with examiner commentary showing exactly why it hits AO1-AO4 and which band it lands in. New teachers see the decision-making live.

**Cambridge 0500 Paper 2 Directed Writing and Composition:**

### 5. Anti-patterns to avoid

In all marketing copy to international schools:

- **Do not** say "aligned to the new English GCSE 9-1 spec" without mentioning IGCSE in the same sentence.
- **Do not** use mainland UK terminology like "KS4", "Year 11 GCSE" alone — use "Year 10-11 IGCSE" or "Key Stage 4 IGCSE".
- **Do not** use only AQA/OCR/Edexcel UK in example mark schemes.
- **Do not** use UK seasonal references ("summer exam season" is fine; "half-term homework" isn't — international schools often don't have half-term breaks).
- **Do not** illustrate with mainland-UK landmarks, place names, or school cultural references that won't land overseas.
- **Do not** price in GBP-only.
