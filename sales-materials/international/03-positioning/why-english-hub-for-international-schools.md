# Why The English Hub for International Schools

Our unique value proposition to British international schools, distilled.

## The Core Problem International Schools Face

British international schools deliver a UK curriculum 5,000 miles from where that curriculum is built. They recruit English teachers from a thin global market — often non-UK-trained, often teaching IGCSE for the first time, often with no line of sight into the subtle expectations of Cambridge and Edexcel markers.

The predictable result:
- Teachers over-rely on UK textbook PDFs that are out of date, mainland-UK-oriented, or tied to the wrong exam board.
- Departments spend 40-60% of prep time converting generic materials to IGCSE spec.
- Students practise from past papers with no marking scheme context.
- English results underperform relative to the school's Maths / Science outcomes, which embarrasses leadership.
- Parents notice — IGCSE English is the single most scrutinised subject at parents' evenings.

## What We Deliver

The English Hub is an online platform of examiner-written IGCSE and GCSE English Language and Literature resources, aligned exactly to exam board specifications, usable by any teacher on any device, anywhere in the world.

## Our Seven Unique Value Propositions for International Schools

### 1. Examiner-written — every lesson, every mark scheme
Our content is written by serving and former Cambridge and Edexcel IGCSE English examiners and senior examiners. These are the people who write the real mark schemes. When your teachers use our materials, they're teaching IGCSE the way IGCSE is actually assessed.

- **Why it matters internationally:** 60-70% of English teachers in international BC schools are not UK-trained. They've never sat through IGCSE moderation or seen a senior examiner mark a script. Our materials bring that expertise directly into the classroom.
- **Proof asset:** author bios, sample mark scheme annotations, alignment to specific Cambridge 0500 and Edexcel 4ET1 assessment objectives.

### 2. Dual exam board coverage — Cambridge and Edexcel
Most content providers pick one board. We cover both:
- Cambridge IGCSE First Language English 0500 and 0990
- Cambridge IGCSE Literature in English 0475 and 0992
- Cambridge IGCSE English as a Second Language 0510 / 0993
- Pearson Edexcel IGCSE English Language A (4EA1) and B (4EB1)
- Pearson Edexcel IGCSE English Literature (4ET1)

A single subscription covers every IGCSE English specification a British international school is likely to use.

- **Why it matters internationally:** single-board resources leave schools needing a second subscription if they switch boards or teach a mixed cohort.

### 3. GCSE + IGCSE — one platform, dual pathway
For expat-heavy schools running UK returners, we also cover the mainland GCSE specs (AQA, Edexcel, OCR, Eduqas). A student who starts in IGCSE 0500 and moves to a UK sixth form on AQA 8700 can continue using the same platform.

### 4. Time zones are irrelevant
The platform is 100% cloud-based, accessible 24/7, with no installation. A teacher in Bangkok at 7am can hand-mark the same evening as a teacher in Buenos Aires doing prep at 9pm. The content is static; the interface is fast; there are no live sessions required.

- **Why it matters internationally:** many UK EdTech products are designed around UK teaching hours; their customer support, webinars, and updates all assume GMT. We designed for global use from day one.

### 5. Per-student, predictable, budgetable
Our pricing is per-student annual — just like a textbook licence. No hidden seat minimums, no consumption billing surprises, no "premium modules". Schools can budget the English Hub line item the same way they budget IGCSE exam entry fees.

Pricing is published in USD, EUR, AED, SGD, GBP — whichever currency a school bursar works in.

### 6. Teacher-time saver — ready-to-teach
Every lesson includes:
- Lesson plan (30/60/90 minute formats)
- Student-facing worksheets (printable and digital)
- Model answers and marking guides written by examiners
- Differentiation materials (higher / lower attainment)
- Plenary and homework options
- CPD notes for the teacher

A new teacher can walk into a Tuesday morning lesson having done 10 minutes of prep and deliver an examiner-quality session.

- **Why it matters internationally:** teacher workload is the number one retention issue at international schools. Hours given back = happier staff = less churn = better exam results.

### 7. Low IT lift, any device
The platform is browser-based. It works on:
- Windows, Mac, ChromeOS, iPad, Android, iPhone.
- Any modern browser.
- Slow connections (optimised asset delivery; offline-friendly for worksheets).
- Inside China (via a CDN endpoint that works without VPN).

There's nothing to install, nothing to integrate (though SSO with Google Workspace and Microsoft 365 is available), and nothing to break.
