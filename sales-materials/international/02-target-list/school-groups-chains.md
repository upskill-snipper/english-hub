# School Groups and Chains — Hit One, Land Many

International school groups are the single biggest leverage point in this market. Winning a framework deal with one group can land 10-100 schools in a single contract. Every sale conversation should start with "does your school belong to a group?" and ladder up.

---

## Nord Anglia Education

- **Size:** 80+ schools; 100,000+ students; 37 countries.
- **HQ:** London, UK.
- **Parent:** Privately held (formerly listed; owned by EQT and Neuberger Berman).
- **CEO / Leadership:** CEO role; Chief Education Officer oversees academic standards.
- **Regional structure:** Europe, Middle East, China, SE Asia, Americas.
- **Curriculum mix:** International British (most common), IB Continuum, US College Prep, bilingual BC.
- **Exam board:** Predominantly Cambridge International IGCSE; some Edexcel.
- **Known tech stack:** Uses a global learning platform; partners with MIT, Juilliard, UNICEF, Cambridge.

**Key schools in this target list:**
- UAE: Nord Anglia International School Dubai
- Qatar: Compass International School Doha, Nord Anglia Al Khor
- Central Asia: The British School of Tashkent
- Europe: British International School of Warsaw, Bratislava, Prague, Budapest
- China: Nord Anglia Shanghai Pudong, The British School of Nanjing, plus 12 more
- SE Asia: British International School Hanoi, HCMC, BVIS (2), St Andrews Bangkok, British International School KL, The King's School Manila
- HK: Nord Anglia International School Hong Kong
- Americas: The British College of Brazil
- Africa: via BVIS brand in some regions

**Route to HQ:**
- Head of Global Teaching and Learning
- Chief Education Officer
- Regional Education Directors (Europe, Middle East, Asia, Americas)
- Procurement / Group Commercial team

**Deal shape:**
- Multi-year master services agreement with per-student pricing.
- Regional pilots first, then global rollout.
- Expect 9-18 month sales cycle.
- Budget scale: USD 300k - USD 2M annual.

**Strategy:** Win 2-3 Nord Anglia schools first as references, then approach the Chief Education Officer or a Regional Education Director with a case study deck and a framework proposal.

## Tactical Playbook for Group Deals

1. **Never sell to HQ first.** Win 1-3 schools as pilots; collect testimonials and data; then approach HQ with a "your schools love us" narrative.
2. **Identify the decision-maker.** For most groups this is the Chief Education Officer or Group Academic Director — not the procurement or commercial team.
3. **Regional first.** Groups often run regional autonomy. Target Europe, Middle East, Asia separately before pushing global.
4. **Co-create a case study.** Offer the group a co-branded report with early pilot data. Everyone wants good PR.
5. **Match their academic brand.** Wellington, Dulwich, Harrow, Brighton all have very particular academic cultures. Our materials must feel aligned.
6. **Pricing:** offer per-student pricing with volume tiers (see `05-commercials/school-group-framework-pricing.md`).
7. **Contract terms:** be willing to negotiate on MSA terms — data residency, IP, termination — groups will push hard.
8. **Multi-year commitment:** 3-year deals with price lock give groups certainty.
9. **Onboarding support:** include teacher CPD for the whole group as part of the deal — high perceived value, low marginal cost.
10. **Executive sponsor:** get our founder/CEO engaged on group-level conversations; these deals are relationship-led.
