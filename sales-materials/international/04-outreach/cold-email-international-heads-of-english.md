## Personalisation Checklist

Before sending ANY of the above:

- [ ] Confirm the recipient's current role (LinkedIn check; Heads of English change every 3-5 years).
- [ ] Confirm the school still teaches IGCSE English (not fully IB).
- [ ] Confirm which exam board (Cambridge or Edexcel) — check public exam results, recent newsletter, department Twitter/X.
- [ ] Reference something specific: recent inspection, new building, exam results press release, personal publication or LinkedIn post.
- [ ] Use the correct school name formally (e.g. "The British School of Brussels", not "BSB" unless they refer to themselves that way).
- [ ] Use their preferred first name, not their formal title.
- [ ] Check spelling — especially of names in non-English-script countries.
- [ ] Run the draft through a read-aloud to check tone isn't too salesy.

## Sending guidelines

- **Volume:** max 10 cold emails per day from one inbox to stay deliverable. Scale across multiple senders if needed.
- **Timing:** send Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9am local time of the school.
- **Reply tracking:** track opens and replies in a CRM (HubSpot, Copper, Pipedrive); don't re-email someone who replied.
- **Follow-up:** use the 5-touch sequence in `follow-up-sequence.md`.
- **Deliverability:** warm up inboxes, use a real email address (first.last@), include unsubscribe link, don't attach files on first contact.
- **Regional adjustment:** rework tone for each region.
  - Middle East and Asia: slightly more formal, use title + surname on first contact.
  - Europe and Americas: first name + direct tone is fine.
  - Africa: mix — lean formal.

## What NOT to do

- Do not send HTML email with images on first contact (flags spam filters).
- Do not send PDF attachments on first contact.
- Do not use "I hope you're well" — it reads as American and generic.
- Do not use "I wanted to reach out" — it's noise.
- Do not promise "the UK's best" — international readers don't respond to UK superlatives.
- Do not name-drop competitors.
- Do not ask for a meeting without offering value first.
