# LinkedIn Outreach Templates — International Schools

LinkedIn is the best channel for international school outreach once you've exhausted or can't find an email. Heads of English, Heads of Secondary and Principals at British international schools are generally active on LinkedIn — especially in the run-up to conference season.

## LinkedIn strategy principles

1. **Connect first, message second.** Don't DM strangers via InMail unless they're on LinkedIn Premium. Send a connection request with a short, warm note; message after they accept.
2. **Warm up the profile.** Before reaching out, spend 3 days: like their posts, comment on an article they shared, react to a school update. They'll recognise your name when the request arrives.
3. **Never pitch in the connection request.** That's how you get ignored and reported.
4. **Keep messages under 100 words.** LinkedIn mobile truncates long messages.
5. **One ask per message.** Don't bundle.

---

## Template 1 — Connection request (all personas)

> Hi {FirstName} — I came across your profile via {COBIS / school website / FOBISIA / a mutual connection} and wanted to say hello. I work with Heads of English at British international schools on examiner-written IGCSE resources. Would love to connect.

**Word count:** 42. Under the LinkedIn 300-character limit. No pitch.

---

## Template 2 — First message after connection (Head of English)

**Subject / Opening:**

> Hi {FirstName}, thanks for connecting.
>
> I run partnerships at The English Hub — examiner-written IGCSE English resources, all spec-aligned to Cambridge 0500 and Edexcel 4ET1. Our authors are serving senior IGCSE examiners.
>
> Would you be open to a 20-minute call or a free sample unit for your {Paper 1 / Paper 2} team? No pressure either way — happy to just send materials if that's more useful.

---

## Template 3 — First message after connection (Head of Secondary)

> Hi {FirstName}, thanks for connecting.

## Template 4 — First message after connection (Principal / Head of School)

> Hi {FirstName}, appreciate the connect.
>
> Briefly — The English Hub provides examiner-written IGCSE English resources. We work with {Region} COBIS/BSME/FOBISIA schools on standardising content across English departments, saving teacher prep time, and nudging mock exam averages up by a grade or two.
>
> If it's relevant, I'd love an introduction to your Head of English or Head of Secondary. Happy to brief them directly.

## Template 5 — Engagement message (after they post)

When a Head of English posts about IGCSE, marking, results, or teacher workload:

> Really interesting post on {specific point}, {FirstName}. We're seeing the same pattern across our school partners — especially on {mark scheme alignment / workload / exemplar responses}. Would love to compare notes sometime. Happy to send over a sample unit if you'd like to see how we approach {specific topic} at The English Hub.

## LinkedIn outreach cadence

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| Day -3 | Like 2-3 of their posts, comment on 1 |
| Day 0 | Connection request (Template 1) |
| Day +2 (if accepted) | First message (Template 2, 3 or 4 depending on role) |
| Day +9 (if no reply) | Soft follow-up: "Just floating this to the top" |
| Day +18 (if no reply) | Final message: "Last note — happy to circle back later" |
| Day +90 | Re-engage with a new hook (new product, new case study) |

## LinkedIn content to complement outreach

Run a parallel LinkedIn content programme so your target audience sees you organically:
- **Weekly:** 1 post tagged {English teaching, IGCSE, COBIS, BSME, FOBISIA}.
- **Monthly:** 1 long-form article on IGCSE English pedagogy, written by our examiner authors.
- **Quarterly:** 1 case study from a real school partner.
- **Event-driven:** posts around COBIS Annual, BSME Annual, FOBISIA Heads' Conference with booth details.

## Do not

- Do not mass-connect with generic requests — LinkedIn throttles and eventually bans.
- Do not use LinkedIn automation tools (Expandi, Dripify, etc.) unless you accept the ban risk.
- Do not send the same message twice in a row to different contacts at the same school on the same day — they talk to each other.
- Do not send sales collateral PDFs as attachments on first contact.
- Do not InMail people who are clearly in a hiring pool or on parental leave (check "Open to Work" or career break indicators).
- Do not DM on weekends or public holidays local to the school's country.
