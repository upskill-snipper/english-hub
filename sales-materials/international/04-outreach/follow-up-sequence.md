# 5-Touch Follow-Up Sequence — International Schools

A disciplined follow-up sequence is worth more than clever openers. 80% of cold outreach responses come after touch 2; 40% come after touch 3 or later. Most salespeople stop at touch 1 — this is how we win.

## Sequence overview

| Touch | Day | Channel | Purpose | Word count |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | Email | Intro and value offer | 120 |
| 2 | Day +4 | Email | Value-add (resource share) | 90 |
| 3 | Day +9 | LinkedIn | Light soft touch | 40 |
| 4 | Day +16 | Email | Different hook (peer reference or paper-specific) | 100 |
| 5 | Day +25 | Email | Close-out / pause | 60 |

Total sequence: 25 days. After that, drop into a 90-day nurture.

---

## Touch 1 — Day 0 — Email

Use the primary cold-email template from `cold-email-international-heads-of-english.md` (Template 1, 2, 3 or 4 depending on research).

**Goal:** Establish credibility, offer one concrete thing, ask for a 20-minute call.

---

## Touch 2 — Day +4 — Email (value-add)

**Subject line:** Reply to Touch 1 (keep the thread)

> Hi {FirstName},
>
> Following up briefly — I thought you might find this useful regardless of whether we chat.
>
> [Attach or link to a free sample unit: e.g. "Cambridge IGCSE 0500 Paper 2 Directed Writing — Examiner Walk-Through (PDF, 8 pages)"]
>
> It's a complete 60-minute lesson with the exemplar answer annotated by our senior examiner. Feel free to share it with your Year 11 team.
>
> If you'd like to see the full Paper 2 unit or the broader library, I'm still happy to book 20 minutes.
>
> Best,
> {Your name}

**Principles:**
- Don't say "just following up" — it's noise.
- Give them something they can use today, even if they never reply.
- Keep it on the same thread so the full context is one click away.

---

## Touch 3 — Day +9 — LinkedIn

Send a connection request (if not already connected) or a light DM if connected.

> Hi {FirstName} — I sent through a Paper 2 sample unit last week by email. No pressure to respond — just wanted to flag it in case it landed in spam. Happy to re-send or jump on a quick call if useful.

**Principles:**
- Cross-channel nudges raise response rates 2-3x.
- Acknowledge the previous touches so you don't seem forgetful.
- One line only — never a mini-pitch.

---

## Touch 4 — Day +16 — Email (new hook)

**Subject line:** {New hook} (fresh subject, not the same thread)

Use a different angle from Touch 1. If Touch 1 was "examiner pedigree", try "peer reference". If Touch 1 was "time saving", try "specific paper hook".

> Hi {FirstName},
>
> I know you're busy — final note from me for now.
>
> One thing I didn't mention in my earlier emails: {Peer School Name} in {City} started using The English Hub last {term / year}. Their Head of English, {Name}, said {short quote or paraphrase about the specific benefit}.
>
> They're a similar size to {Your School} with a similar IGCSE cohort profile. I think a short call with {Peer Name} would be more useful than another email from me — let me know if you'd like an introduction.
>
> Best,
> {Your name}

**Principles:**
- Social proof beats further description.
- Offer a peer intro — it's low effort for them and high credibility.
- Signal that this is not the last email ever, but the last for now.

---

## Touch 5 — Day +25 — Email (close-out)

**Subject line:** Pausing — happy to restart when it's a better time

> Hi {FirstName},
>
> I'll stop emailing for now — I know inboxes get overwhelming.
>
> For whenever the timing is right, here's what we offer in one line: examiner-written IGCSE English for Cambridge 0500 / 0990 and Edexcel 4ET1, per-student pricing, cloud-based, used in 30+ countries.
>
> {Your calendar link} is always open if you'd like to resume. Wishing you and {School Name} a strong finish to the term.
>
> Best,
> {Your name}

**Principles:**
- The "break-up" email has the highest response rate in the sequence.
- Don't say "I assume you're not interested" — it's passive-aggressive.
- Leave the door open without begging.
- This is often the message that triggers a reply.

---

## After the sequence

If no reply by Day +25, drop the contact into a 90-day nurture track:

- Monthly newsletter (if they opted in)
- LinkedIn content follows (organic)
- Re-engage in Day +90 with a new hook: new product feature, new exam series results, new case study, new event invite

## Regional sequencing adjustments

| Region | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| **Middle East** | Add 2-3 days to each gap. Academic year pauses around religious holidays (Ramadan, Eid) and summer break. Don't send during Ramadan afternoons. |
| **Asia** | Add 1 day to each gap for time zones. Avoid Chinese New Year, Songkran (Thailand), Lunar New Year, Diwali. |
| **Europe** | Standard cadence. Avoid July-August entirely. |
| **Africa** | Standard cadence. Email delivery can be unreliable in some regions — use LinkedIn as primary. |
| **Americas** | Standard cadence. Avoid Carnaval (Brazil), Semana Santa (LatAm), summer break (Dec-Feb in Southern Hemisphere). |

## Common mistakes to avoid

1. **Sending the sequence on weekends or public holidays.** Unsubscribes and negative associations spike.
2. **Forwarding the full previous email in Touch 2+.** Keep the thread clean — reply to the original, don't rebuild it.
3. **Over-automating.** If a school responds to Touch 1, pause the rest of the sequence manually. Automated platforms still send Touches 2-5 sometimes.
4. **Same subject line every time.** Subject lines should vary so the email stands out in the inbox.
5. **Forgetting to track.** Every touch should be logged in a CRM with date, channel, outcome, and next action.
6. **Giving up at Touch 3.** Most replies come at Touches 3-5. Push through.
7. **Running the sequence on too-small a list.** If you're running it on 10 contacts, you need 100. Widen the funnel.
8. **Not personalising Touch 1.** Personal Touch 1 is the only reason Touch 2-5 get opened.

## Metrics to track

- **Open rate by touch:** Touch 1 should be 40%+, Touches 2-5 should be 30%+.
- **Reply rate by touch:** Aim for 8-15% cumulative across the sequence.
- **Meeting booking rate:** Aim for 2-5% of the sequence cohort.
- **Deal conversion from meeting:** Aim for 25-40%.
- **Unsubscribe rate:** under 0.5% per send.
- **Spam complaint rate:** under 0.1% per send.

If any of these slip, pause and review before continuing.
