# Cold Email — International School Groups (Group Procurement)

Templates for emailing group-level procurement, academic, or education directors at multi-school operators (Nord Anglia, GEMS, Cognita, Dulwich International, AISL Harrow, Wellington International, Inspired Education, Aldar, Globeducate, ISP, etc.).

The key distinction from school-level outreach: group-level buyers care about **framework pricing, standardisation, risk reduction, and group-wide outcomes**, not the specifics of Paper 2 Section A.

## Template 1 — Post-Pilot Escalation (Ideal Case)

**Subject:** {Group Name} IGCSE English — framework proposal after {Pilot School} pilot

Hi {FirstName},

I'm writing to follow up on our work with {Pilot School Name} — one of your schools in {region}. Their Head of English, {HoD Name}, ran a 12-week pilot of The English Hub last term.

The results from the pilot:
- {X} Year 10-11 teachers using the platform as their primary resource base
- {Y} hours of prep time saved per teacher per week
- {Z}% increase in student practice completion rates
- Improved mock exam averages by {N} grades
- Head of English NPS: {score}/10

Given {Group Name} has {number} schools teaching IGCSE English across {regions}, I'd like to propose a group framework agreement. This would give you:
- Standardised examiner-written IGCSE content across all schools
- One master services agreement with regional billing
- Volume-based per-student pricing (typically 30-50% below list)
- Central CPD and onboarding for new English teachers
- Group-level usage analytics

Would you have 30 minutes to discuss? I can share the pilot case study in advance.

Best,
{Your name}

## Targeting rules for group outreach

| Role | Best angle | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **Chief Education Officer / Group Academic Director** | Quality standardisation, outcomes, CPD | Price-first messaging |
| **Regional Education Director** | Regional efficiency, peer school references | Global framework pitch (they don't own it) |
| **Head of Curriculum / Head of Secondary** | Teacher workload, resource quality | Commercial terms |
| **Group Procurement / Commercial** | Framework pricing, MSA terms, savings vs. aggregated school spend | Pedagogical details |
| **Group CFO** | Per-student predictability, volume discount, FX strategy | Content quality |

## Pre-send research checklist for group outreach

- [ ] Confirm the group structure: who owns the group, who runs each region, who makes vendor decisions at what level.
- [ ] Find the name and role of the Chief Education Officer and Regional Education Directors (LinkedIn, group website, press releases).
- [ ] Check whether the group already has a master framework with a competitor (BBC Bitesize, Kerboodle, Seneca, Twinkl, etc.) — if so, be ready to articulate differentiation.
- [ ] Identify any existing reference schools we have inside the group (even informal relationships).
- [ ] Know the group's curriculum mix — if only 30% of schools teach BC IGCSE, pitch size should reflect that.
- [ ] Know recent group news (acquisitions, new school openings, executive changes).
- [ ] Find a warm-intro path first — LinkedIn mutuals, COBIS supporting associate network, UK examiner connections.

## Sequencing the group conversation

1. **First touch:** Template 2, 3 or 4 depending on role. Offer value; request 30 minutes.
2. **Second touch (1 week):** Forward a relevant artifact (case study, sample unit, white paper on IGCSE English results improvement).
3. **Third touch (2 weeks):** Introduce a reference school contact they can speak to directly.
4. **First call:** Open with discovery — don't pitch. Learn the group's curriculum structure, existing tools, procurement process, academic standards approach.
5. **Second call:** Proposal — framework shape, commercial terms range, pilot design.
6. **Pilot:** 2-3 schools, one term, success criteria agreed up front (usage rate, teacher NPS, student outcomes).
7. **Framework agreement:** scale into master services agreement.

Typical cycle: 6-12 months from first touch to signed framework. Budget for 18 months for the largest groups (Nord Anglia, Cognita).
