# Regional Analysis — British International Schools

## 1. Middle East (Highest-Value Region)

### Market character
- Highest-paying market in the world for international school fees.
- Large expatriate populations (UK, Indian, Filipino, Egyptian) plus aspirational locals.
- Many schools are purpose-built, fee-paying, privately owned by family conglomerates or education groups.
- Dominated by British curriculum — often 50-70% market share in GCC cities.

### Country detail

**United Arab Emirates (UAE)** — 250+ British-curriculum schools.
- Dubai: KHDA-regulated. ~80 British-curriculum schools. Heavy presence of GEMS, Nord Anglia, Taaleem, Aldar, Dulwich, Cranleigh, Repton, Brighton, Kings, Horizon, Arcadia.
- Abu Dhabi: ADEK-regulated. ~60 British schools. Aldar Education is the biggest local operator.
- Sharjah, Ajman, Ras al Khaimah, Fujairah: smaller numbers, often mid-market price points.

**Saudi Arabia** — 60+ British schools, growing fast post-Vision 2030.
- Riyadh, Jeddah, Khobar/Dhahran (ARAMCO), NEOM (planned).
- New entrants: Brighton College Al Ain, Misk Schools, King's Academy.
- Local content sensitivity — textbook-style materials need preview.

**Qatar** — 50+ British schools.
- Doha British School (3 campuses), Compass Nord Anglia, Doha Academy, Park House English School, Sherborne Qatar, Swiss International School.

**Kuwait** — 40+ British schools.
- British School of Kuwait, Kuwait English School, New English School, Al Bayan Bilingual, Fawzia Sultan International.

**Bahrain** — ~20 British schools. St Christopher's School and British School of Bahrain are flagships.

**Oman** — ~20 British schools. British School Muscat, Sultan's School.

**Egypt** — 90+ British schools.
- Cairo, Alexandria, New Administrative Capital.
- British School of Alexandria, British International School Cairo (BISC), Cairo British School, Egypt British International School, Malvern College Egypt.

**Jordan** — ~15 British schools. King's Academy, Amman Baccalaureate, American-British Academy Amman.

**Lebanon** — ~10 British schools, smaller market due to economic conditions.

### Regional priorities
- **Top priority cities:** Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Doha, Riyadh, Kuwait City, Cairo.
- **Key event:** BETT Middle East (Dubai), BSME Annual Conference.
- **Key groups:** GEMS, Nord Anglia, Taaleem, Aldar Education, Bloom Education, Esol Education, Dulwich International.

## 2. Asia-Pacific

### Market character
- Most competitive international school market in the world.
- China has the most premium-branded UK schools (Dulwich, Harrow, Wellington, Wycombe Abbey, Malvern, Shrewsbury).
- South East Asia has the deepest mid-market.
- FOBISIA is the dominant network.

### Country detail

**Singapore** — one of the world's most expensive markets.
- Tanglin Trust School, Dulwich College Singapore, Stamford American, UWCSEA (British-adjacent), Chatsworth, St. Joseph's Institution International, Marlborough College Malaysia (nearby).

**Hong Kong** — 35+ English-medium schools.
- English Schools Foundation (ESF) — largest operator, 22 schools, many teach IGCSE.
- Harrow International Hong Kong, Malvern College Hong Kong, Shrewsbury International, Kellett, Nord Anglia Hong Kong.

**Mainland China** — 900+ international schools, ~400 with British curriculum.
- Dulwich College International (Beijing, Shanghai Pudong, Shanghai Puxi, Suzhou, Zhuhai).
- Harrow International (Beijing, Shanghai, Hong Kong, Shenzhen, Haikou, Hainan).
- Wellington College International (Shanghai, Tianjin, Hangzhou).
- Wycombe Abbey (Changzhou, Nanjing, Hangzhou).
- Malvern College (Qingdao, Chengdu, Hong Kong).
- Nord Anglia (14+ schools).
- NB: for Chinese-national students, regulations now limit foreign curricula at compulsory stages. IGCSE often taught to pass-through expats or in Grades 10+.

**Thailand** — ~200 international schools, ~80 British curriculum.
- Harrow Bangkok, Shrewsbury International, Bangkok Patana School, Regent's International, Brighton College Bangkok, St Andrews International (3 campuses), British International School Phuket, Rugby School Thailand, Wellington College Bangkok.

**Malaysia** — ~150 international schools, ~60 British.
- Marlborough College Malaysia, Epsom College Malaysia, Alice Smith School Kuala Lumpur, British International School Kuala Lumpur, Garden International School, Nexus International.

**Vietnam** — ~70 international schools.
- British International School Hanoi (Nord Anglia), British International School HCMC (Nord Anglia), BVIS Hanoi/HCMC, Wellspring, Renaissance International School Saigon, European International School HCMC.

**Indonesia** — ~50 British schools.
- British School Jakarta, Jakarta Intercultural School (not BC but major), Nord Anglia Jakarta, Sekolah Pelita Harapan.

**Japan** — ~15 British-curriculum schools.
- British School in Tokyo, Yokohama International School, Rugby School Japan.

**South Korea** — ~15.
- Dulwich College Seoul, North London Collegiate School Jeju, British International School Seoul.

**Philippines** — ~10 British schools.
- British School Manila, The King's School Manila (Nord Anglia), Southville International.

**Brunei** — Jerudong International School (one of the largest British schools in Asia).

### Regional priorities
- **Top priority cities:** Bangkok, Hong Kong, Singapore, Shanghai, Kuala Lumpur, Ho Chi Minh City.
- **Key events:** FOBISIA conferences, BETT Asia.
- **Key groups:** Nord Anglia Asia, Dulwich International, AISL Harrow, Wellington International, ESF (HK), Cognita Asia.

## 3. Europe

### Market character
- Historically served UK expats; now majority host-country nationals.
- Longer-established schools (some 50+ years old) tend to be standalone and proudly independent.
- Regulation varies by country — Spain has NABSS, Netherlands has Dutch International Schools Foundation, etc.

### Country detail

**Spain** — 80+ British schools. Largest BC market in Europe.
- Costa del Sol belt: Sotogrande International, British School of Marbella, Swans International, Laude group.
- Madrid: King's College Madrid, British Council School Madrid, ICS Madrid, Runnymede College, Hastings School.
- Barcelona: Benjamin Franklin International, British School of Barcelona (Cognita), Oak House, Kensington School.
- Canary Islands, Mallorca, Valencia, Alicante: numerous smaller schools.
- NABSS is the umbrella.

**Portugal** — 20+ British schools.
- St. Julian's School Lisbon, St. Dominic's International, Oporto British School, Nobel International School Algarve, Prime School International.

**Italy** — 15+ British schools.
- St George's British International School Rome, New School Rome, Sir James Henderson British School Milan, British School of Milan, International School of Bologna.

**Netherlands** — 15+ British / bilingual schools with IGCSE.
- British School in the Netherlands (BSN), Amsterdam International Community School, Optimist International School.

**Germany** — 10+ British schools.
- St George's Cologne, Munich, Duisburg; Bonn International School; Berlin Brandenburg International School; Frankfurt International School.

**Switzerland** — 10+ schools with BC pathway.
- British School Geneva, Aiglon College, Haut-Lac, Institut Le Rosey (bicurricular), TASIS.

**France** — 5+ British schools.
- British School of Paris, Mougins School, British International School of Marseille.

**Belgium** — British School of Brussels, St John's International School, St. Paul's British Primary.

**Cyprus & Malta** — 20+ British schools including English School Nicosia, The Grammar School Nicosia, Verdala International School Malta.

**Austria** — Vienna International School, Danube International, American International (offers IGCSE).

### Regional priorities
- **Top priority cities:** Madrid, Barcelona, Lisbon, Milan, Rome, The Hague, Brussels, Geneva.
- **Key events:** NABSS conference (Spain), COBIS Annual Conference.
- **Key groups:** Globeducate, Cognita Europe, Inspired Education, ISP.

## 4. Africa

### Market character
- Long-established British schools in former colonial networks; increasingly entering pan-African group operators (Dulwich, Braeburn, GEMS Africa).

### Country detail

**Kenya** — 30+ British schools.
- Braeburn Group (9 schools), Hillcrest International, Pembroke House, Brookhouse, Peponi, Banda, St Andrew's Turi, Kenton College.

**Egypt** — (covered under Middle East above).

**South Africa** — 15+ British-curriculum schools.
- Somerset College, Bridge House, Reddam House (Inspired Education), African Leadership Academy, Hilton College (hybrid SA + BC).

**Nigeria** — 30+.
- British International School Lagos, Grange School Lagos, The Regent School Abuja, American International School Lagos, Lekki British School.

**Ghana** — ~15. Lincoln Community School, Ghana International School.

**Tanzania** — St. Constantine's International, International School Moshi.

**Morocco** — British International School of Casablanca, London Academy Casablanca, Rabat American School (offers BC pathway).

**Ethiopia** — International Community School Addis Ababa, Sandford International School.

**Mauritius** — Le Bocage International, Northfields International, Westcoast International.

### Regional priorities
- **Top priority cities:** Nairobi, Lagos, Accra, Cape Town, Johannesburg, Casablanca.
- **Key groups:** Braeburn, GEMS Africa, ADvTECH (SA), Inspired.

## 5. Americas

### Market character
- Historically American-curriculum dominant; British curriculum is a growing premium niche.
- Latin America has surprising depth of British schools (some 100+ years old).
- Caribbean has long-standing British-curriculum schools tied to UK universities.

### Country detail

**Mexico** — 25+ British schools.
- Greengates School Mexico City (Cognita), The Edron Academy, Colegio Inglés.

**Brazil** — 20+ British curriculum.
- St Paul's School São Paulo, British College of Brazil (Nord Anglia), Britannia International School Rio, St Nicholas School São Paulo, Our Lady of Mercy Rio.

**Chile** — 100+ "English-tradition" schools, many with IGCSE option.
- The Grange School Santiago, St George's College, Redland School, Santiago College, Colegio Craighouse, Dunalastair, Nido de Águilas (dual), Saint Gabriel's School.

**Argentina** — 30+.
- St George's College North & Quilmes, St Andrew's Scots School, Belgrano Day School, Northlands School, The Cardinal Newman College, St Catherine's Moorlands.

**Colombia** — 15+.
- The English School Bogotá, Anglo Colombian School, Gimnasio Inglés.

**Peru** — Markham College Lima, San Silvestre, Newton College, Hiram Bingham School.

**Uruguay** — British Schools of Montevideo, St. Patrick's College, St. George's.

**Canada** — limited British-curriculum presence but growing niche.

**Caribbean** — British schools in Bahamas, Barbados, Cayman, Bermuda, BVI.
- Cayman International School, Cayman Prep, St. George's School Vancouver (BC province, dual), St. Andrew's School Nassau.

### Regional priorities
- **Top priority cities:** Mexico City, São Paulo, Santiago, Buenos Aires, Bogotá.
- **Key event:** FLACS and COBIS Latin America workshops.
- **Key groups:** Nord Anglia Americas, Cognita Latin America, Inspired.

## 6. Central Asia, Eastern Europe, Russia CIS

### Market character
- Rapid growth market post-2015. Oil and gas capitals have generous budgets.
- Sanctions have limited Russia, but Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, Azerbaijan, Georgia are expanding.

### Country detail

**Kazakhstan** — 20+ British schools.
- Haileybury Almaty, Haileybury Astana, Miras International School, Nazarbayev Intellectual Schools (partner).

**Uzbekistan** — British School of Tashkent (Nord Anglia), Cambridge International School Tashkent.

**Azerbaijan** — Dunya School Baku, The British School in Baku.

**Georgia** — British International School Tbilisi, QSI Tbilisi.

**Russia** — English International School Moscow, Anglo-American School Moscow, British International School Moscow (subject to sanction reviews).

**Turkey** — British International School Istanbul, MEF International School, Üsküdar American Academy (offers BC).

**Poland, Czech Republic, Hungary** — British International School of Warsaw, British International School of Bratislava, British International School Budapest, Prague British International School, English International School Prague.

**Ukraine** — British International School Kyiv (operating status uncertain).

### Regional priorities
- **Top priority cities:** Almaty, Tashkent, Warsaw, Prague, Istanbul, Baku.
- **Key groups:** Nord Anglia (Czechia, Hungary, Slovakia, Uzbekistan), Orbital Education.
