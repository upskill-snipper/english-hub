# British International Schools Market Overview

## Headline Numbers

- **~13,000 English-medium international schools globally** (ISC Research, 2024).
- **~9,000 deliver British or British-influenced curriculum** at some level (Key Stage 3 upward).
- **~6.5 million students** enrolled in English-medium international schools worldwide.
- **~550,000 staff** employed; growing at 4-6% CAGR.
- **Annual tuition fee revenue:** estimated USD 70+ billion globally.
- **IGCSE entries** (Cambridge and Edexcel combined): approximately 1.2 million candidates per session.

## Growth Drivers

1. **Expatriate demand** — British, American, Indian, Korean, and Chinese expat families following jobs into emerging markets.
2. **Aspirational local demand** — increasingly, the majority of students in British international schools outside the UK are host-country nationals whose parents want a globally-portable, English-medium qualification.
3. **University routing** — IGCSE + A-Level or IB feeds Russell Group, Ivy League, and Group of Eight universities.
4. **Brand licensing** — UK public schools (Harrow, Dulwich, Wellington, Marlborough, Repton, Rugby, Brighton, Malvern) monetise their names by licensing to overseas operators.

## Key Networks

- **COBIS** — Council of British International Schools. 331 member schools across 80+ countries. London-based. Annual conference every May. **Primary target network.**
- **BSME** — British Schools in the Middle East. Founded 1982. Regional focus on GCC, Jordan, Egypt, Lebanon.
- **FOBISIA** — Federation of British International Schools in Asia. 107 member schools across 20 countries (Brunei to Japan).
- **NABSS** — National Association of British Schools in Spain. Strong country-level network.
- **BSA** — Boarding Schools' Association (includes many international boarding schools).
- **AoBSO** — Association of British Schools Overseas (inspection / accreditation body).

## Budget Signals

- **Per-student resource budget:** typically USD 80-250 per student per year across all subjects for premium schools; USD 30-80 for mid-market.
- **English department software budget:** USD 3,000 - USD 25,000 per year for a 2-form-entry secondary.
- **Group-level procurement budgets:** Nord Anglia, GEMS, Cognita each spend millions annually on digital learning platforms.
- Schools refresh resources on **3-year cycles**, typically aligned to summer term budgeting (Jan-Apr window for sign-off).

## Decision-Makers

1. **Head of English** — champion, runs pilot, writes the business case.
2. **Head of Secondary / Deputy Head Academic** — approves pedagogically.
3. **Head of School / Principal** — signs off budget above a threshold.
4. **Bursar / Business Manager** — handles procurement, contracts, tax.
5. **Regional Education Director** (for groups) — sets framework, mandates or recommends central tools.
6. **Group CFO / Head of Procurement** (for groups) — negotiates master agreement.

## What Matters to Them

- **British examiner pedigree** — "written by actual IGCSE examiners" is a huge trust signal overseas.
- **Exam-board alignment** — Cambridge vs. Edexcel specific content, not generic.
- **Teacher CPD** — many staff are not UK-trained; they want materials that also upskill teachers.
- **Off-the-shelf worksheets, lesson plans, marking schemes** — saves teacher prep time.
- **Low IT lift** — must work on any device, any browser, anywhere. No heavy installs.
- **Data residency** — some regions (UAE, China, Saudi) have data-hosting regulations.
- **Per-student pricing** — predictable, budgetable line item.

## Risks and Gotchas

- **Saudi Arabia:** content restrictions; preview all text and images before launch in KSA.
- **UAE:** KHDA (Dubai) and ADEK (Abu Dhabi) curriculum approval can affect third-party content classified as "textbook replacement".
- **China:** data must not cross the firewall for certain user types; be ready with a China-hosted or partner option if expanding seriously.
- **Russia & Belarus:** sanctions-excluded unless the school is operated by a non-sanctioned parent.
- **Currency / FX:** price in local currency bands to avoid sticker shock from USD-denominated invoices.
- **Invoicing tax:** VAT MOSS considerations for B2B digital services into the EU; reverse charge for most B2B exports.
