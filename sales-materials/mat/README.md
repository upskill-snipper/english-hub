# MAT Sales Pack — The English Hub

This pack enables direct outreach to Multi-Academy Trust (MAT) central teams. MAT deals are the highest-leverage sales motion available to The English Hub:

- **Deal size**: One MAT contract covering 8+ secondary schools is worth **£800K–£1.8M in exit uplift** (at 3–5x ARR multiples on typical per-student pricing).
- **Cycle**: 4–9 months from first conversation to framework signature, then rolling per-school order forms.
- **Buyer**: Central MAT team (Director of Education, Head of English, Director of Curriculum, Chief Operating Officer, Procurement Lead).
- **Trigger**: MAT-wide English review, Ofsted-driven improvement, curriculum consolidation, budget squeeze, or new central leadership.

## How to use this pack

1. **Start with `01-target-list/tier-1-top-priority.md`** — the 10 MATs to approach first.
2. Use `01-target-list/contact-research-template.md` to build a named buyer profile for each.
3. Prime the meeting with `03-pitch/executive-summary-one-pager.md` sent in advance.
4. Run the meeting with the `03-pitch/mat-pitch-deck-outline.md` and `mat-speaker-notes.md`.
5. Close with commercials from `05-commercials/` and answer diligence with `06-diligence/`.

## Qualification criteria (MEDDPICC-lite)

Before investing in a pitch, confirm:

- **M**etrics: Trust has a stated reading/English improvement target or Ofsted concern.
- **E**conomic buyer: COO, CFO, or CEO is aware and will sign a £50k+ contract.
- **D**ecision process: Is this a framework that then rolls to schools, or a central mandate?
- **D**ecision criteria: Is per-student pricing, data, Ofsted alignment, or teacher workload the lead driver?
- **P**aper process: Security review, DPIA, procurement route. Assume 6–8 weeks.
- **I**dentify pain: Fragmented current tooling? Budget pressure? Retention issues?
- **C**hampion: Who inside the MAT will advocate for us in central team meetings?
- **C**ompetition: What do they currently use across the trust? Are renewals aligned?

A single Tier-1 MAT win can materially change the company's exit valuation. Treat these opportunities accordingly.

## What not to do

- **Don't** pitch per-school to a MAT. Central teams hate school-by-school sales noise and will block you.
- **Don't** lead with product features. Lead with trust-wide outcomes and cost-per-student.
- **Don't** quote retail school pricing — the whole point of the MAT motion is volume economics.
- **Don't** ignore procurement. Most MATs now have centralised procurement; bypassing them kills deals late.
- **Don't** promise integrations (SIMS, Arbor, Bromcom) without checking with product.
