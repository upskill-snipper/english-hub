# Tier 1 MAT Priority List — Top 10 Detailed Profiles

The 10 MATs to approach first. Each profile is a working document — update as research deepens.

**Prioritisation logic**:
1. Number of secondary schools (English Hub is secondary-first)
2. Central curriculum control (decision maker exists at trust level)
3. Track record of adopting central edtech
4. Our existing access points (any school already a customer?)

---

## 1. United Learning

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Website | unitedlearning.org.uk |
| HQ | Peterborough / London |
| Schools | ~92 total (approx. 45 secondary, 47 primary, including Independent Schools Group) |
| Students | ~80,000+ |
| Group CEO | Sir Jon Coles |
| Secondary education lead | Dame Sally Coates |
| Curriculum approach | Central "United Curriculum" (including English) |
| Procurement | Centralised — large central team |

### Why they're Tier 1
- Largest MAT in England — single win covers 45+ secondaries.
- Track record of making trust-wide adoption decisions.
- Already runs their own curriculum, so they understand the value of centralisation (and the cost of maintaining it internally).

### Why they're hard
- Strong internal curriculum team — will see English Hub as either a supplement or a replacement. Pitch must be very clear.
- Long procurement cycle (6–9 months).
- Expectation of deep integration and bespoke features.

### The wedge
- Teacher workload: even with "The United Curriculum", teachers spend massive time creating assessment materials and tracking data. English Hub reduces the internal curriculum team's maintenance burden.
- Data consistency: the United team wants granular, comparable data across all schools. Our analytics delivers this.
- Supplementary positioning: "We aren't replacing the United Curriculum — we're the assessment, practice, and analytics engine underneath it."

### Research to do before outreach
- [ ] Who in the central English team handles assessment and data?
- [ ] Has United published anything on their current digital English provision?
- [ ] Which of their schools are highest on our existing customer list or pipeline?
- [ ] Any recent Ofsted findings at a United school mentioning reading or writing outcomes?

### Opening line
> "Dame Sally — The United Curriculum is one of the few trust-wide English programmes that genuinely works. The question I wanted to put in front of you is whether we can save your central team's time on assessment and data while giving you the comparable cross-school picture you've been building towards. 30 minutes?"
