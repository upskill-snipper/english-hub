# Top 50 UK Multi-Academy Trusts — Target List

**Purpose**: Primary account list for English Hub MAT outreach. Focus on MATs with 5+ secondary schools since English Hub is secondary-first.

**Sources**: GOV.UK Get Information About Schools, trust websites, Schools Week, Tes MAT Tracker, FFT Education Datalab, Companies House, Wikipedia. Figures are approximate and should be verified before outreach — MAT composition changes via transfers, mergers, and DfE intervention every term.

**Last research**: April 2026.

**Legend**:
- **Sec** = Secondary schools in the trust (primary target for English Hub)
- **Total** = All phases (primary, secondary, special, AP)
- **Students** = Approximate total learners across the trust
- **Priority**: T1 = Tier 1 (highest relevance), T2 = Tier 2, T3 = Tier 3

---

## Tier 1 — Highest Priority (top 10)

### 1. United Learning
- **Website**: unitedlearning.org.uk
- **HQ**: Peterborough / London
- **Schools**: ~92 total (approx. 45 secondary, 47 primary)
- **Students**: ~80,000+
- **Key person**: Sir Jon Coles (Group CEO); Dame Sally Coates (Director of Secondary Education)
- **English curriculum notes**: United Learning runs its own centrally-designed curriculum ("The United Curriculum") including English. This is both a barrier (strong NIH instinct) and an opportunity (they already buy into standardisation at trust level).
- **Partnerships**: Oak National Academy, EEF research partnerships, Teach First.
- **Why relevant**: Largest MAT in England. If we win one school, the trust-level replication model is unmatched.
- **Priority**: **T1**

### 2. Academies Enterprise Trust / Lift Schools
- **Website**: liftschools.org.uk
- **HQ**: London
- **Schools**: ~57 total (mix of secondary and primary)
- **Students**: ~40,000+
- **Key person**: Becks Boomer-Clark (CEO)
- **English curriculum notes**: Rebranded from AET to Lift Schools in 2024. Published "Lift Curriculum" approach. Historically buys centrally.
- **Partnerships**: Regional school improvement networks.
- **Priority**: **T1**

### 3. Delta Academies Trust
- **Website**: deltatrust.org.uk
- **HQ**: Knottingley, West Yorkshire
- **Schools**: ~63 total (heavily secondary-weighted in the North)
- **Students**: ~30,000+
- **Key person**: Paul Tarn (CEO)
- **English curriculum notes**: Strong track record of school improvement in coastal/ex-industrial areas. Outcomes focus.
- **Priority**: **T1** (northern secondary concentration is ideal for English Hub)

### 4. Harris Federation
- **Website**: harrisfederation.org.uk
- **HQ**: Croydon, London
- **Schools**: ~55 primary + secondary
- **Students**: ~44,000
- **Key person**: Sir Daniel Moynihan (CEO); Executive Director of English (research their current incumbent)
- **English curriculum notes**: Harris runs highly centralised curriculum planning. Known for knowledge-rich approach.
- **Partnerships**: Harris Institute of Teaching and Learning (HITL).
- **Priority**: **T1**

### 5. Outwood Grange Academies Trust (OGAT)
- **Website**: outwood.com
- **HQ**: Wakefield, West Yorkshire
- **Schools**: ~40 total (approx. 28 secondary, 12 primary)
- **Students**: ~30,000
- **Key person**: Martyn Oliver was historic CEO (now HMCI at Ofsted); check current CEO on website
- **English curriculum notes**: Outwood is known for its "Praising Stars" data system and highly scripted curriculum. Openness to central edtech at scale.
- **Priority**: **T1** (secondary-heavy = ideal)

### 6. Oasis Community Learning
- **Website**: oasiscommunitylearning.org
- **HQ**: London
- **Schools**: ~56 academies across 5 regions
- **Students**: ~35,000+
- **Key person**: John Barneby (CEO, verify current)
- **English curriculum notes**: Values-led trust, strong cultural capital emphasis (aligns with Ofsted framework).
- **Priority**: **T1**

### 7. Ormiston Academies Trust (OAT)
- **Website**: ormistonacademiestrust.co.uk
- **HQ**: Birmingham
- **Schools**: ~45 (32 secondary, 6 primary, 3 AP, 1 special)
- **Students**: ~35,000
- **Key person**: Tom Rees (CEO, verify)
- **English curriculum notes**: Secondary-dominant. National footprint. One of the longest-established MATs.
- **Priority**: **T1** (highly secondary-weighted)

### 8. Ark Schools
- **Website**: arkonline.org
- **HQ**: London
- **Schools**: ~39
- **Students**: ~30,000
- **Key person**: Lucy Heller (CEO, verify current); Ian Bauckham has historically been involved in curriculum at national level
- **English curriculum notes**: Strongest central curriculum team of any MAT — Ark English Mastery is a published, licensed product. This is either competitive or complementary — pitch carefully.
- **Priority**: **T1** (but watch for NIH — Ark may see us as competitor)

### 9. Star Academies
- **Website**: staracademies.org
- **HQ**: Blackburn, Lancashire
- **Schools**: ~36 (19 secondary, 9 primary, plus free schools)
- **Students**: ~25,000
- **Key person**: Sir Hamid Patel (CEO)
- **English curriculum notes**: Among the highest-performing MATs nationally at Progress 8. Strong central oversight.
- **Priority**: **T1**

### 10. Dixons Academies Trust
- **Website**: dixonsat.com
- **HQ**: Bradford
- **Schools**: ~17 (11 secondary, 3 primary, 2 all-through, 1 sixth form) — expanding post-merger with Great Academies Education Trust
- **Students**: ~20,000
- **Key person**: Luke Sparkes (CEO, verify current)
- **English curriculum notes**: Expanding rapidly. Dixons is a credibility brand — a Dixons win unlocks peer-MAT conversations.
- **Priority**: **T1**

## Action steps for the sales team

1. Verify the CEO / Director of Education contact for each Tier 1 trust using the trust website's "About Us" or "Our Team" page.
2. For each Tier 1, cross-reference against GOV.UK "Get Information About Schools" to confirm current school count.
3. Build LinkedIn personas for each using `contact-research-template.md`.
4. Check Ofsted reports for English-specific strengths/weaknesses — this is the primary wedge.
5. Check each trust's published accounts on Companies House for budget pressure signals.
6. Re-verify all figures every 6 months — MAT composition changes constantly.
