# MAT Framework Agreement — Template

**IMPORTANT**: This is a drafting template for use by The English Hub team and the prospect's legal advisors. It is not legal advice. Any agreement based on this document must be reviewed by qualified legal counsel before signature.

**Purpose**: Provide a clean, MAT-ready framework agreement that sets out overarching terms once, then allows individual schools to activate under order forms without renegotiating the substantive contract each time.

---

## Structure at a glance

```
MASTER FRAMEWORK AGREEMENT
```

## Suggested clauses

### 1. Parties

This Master Framework Agreement ("Agreement") is made between:

**[English Hub Ltd]**, a company registered in [England and Wales] with company number [XXXXXXXX], whose registered office is at [address] ("**Supplier**"); and

**[MAT NAME]**, a multi-academy trust registered in [England and Wales] with company number [XXXXXXXX], whose registered office is at [address] ("**Trust**").

### 2. Definitions

(Illustrative — adjust to final agreement)

- **"Activated School"** — a school within the Trust that has signed an Order Form under this Agreement.
- **"Order Form"** — a document in the form set out in Schedule 4 activating the Services at a specific Activated School.
- **"Pupil"** — an individual learner enrolled at an Activated School and accessing the Services.
- **"Services"** — the English Hub platform, as described in Schedule 1, as updated from time to time.
- **"Trust Data"** — all data belonging to the Trust, including Pupil Data, processed by the Supplier under this Agreement.
- **"Framework Term"** — the initial period defined in clause 3.1 and any renewal period agreed under clause 3.2.

### 3. Framework duration and renewal

3.1 This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for an initial term of [three (3)] years (the "Initial Framework Term").

3.2 The Agreement automatically renews for successive [twelve (12) month] periods unless either party gives written notice of non-renewal at least [ninety (90) days] before the end of the then-current term.

3.3 Termination of this Agreement does not automatically terminate any Order Forms in force; such Order Forms continue until their specified expiry unless terminated in accordance with clause [X].

### 4. Services and activation

4.1 The Supplier will provide the Services described in Schedule 1 to each Activated School from the activation date stated on its Order Form.

4.2 A School becomes an Activated School when a duly authorised representative of the Trust signs an Order Form in the form set out in Schedule 4.

4.3 Activation of new schools under this Agreement does not require renegotiation of the substantive terms of this Agreement; only the commercial and school-specific details in the Order Form are variable.

4.4 Schools newly joining the Trust may be activated under this Agreement at any time, subject to the applicable banded price at the point of activation.

### 5. Commercials

5.1 **Banded per-student pricing.** The Supplier charges the Trust on a per-Pupil basis, banded according to the total number of Pupils across all Activated Schools. The banding and applicable per-Pupil prices are set out in Schedule 1.

5.2 **Price lock.** For the duration of the Initial Framework Term, the per-Pupil price is fixed at the band applicable at the Effective Date, subject to clause 5.3.

5.3 **Favourable band movement.** If the Trust grows into a more favourable band during the Initial Framework Term, the more favourable band applies from the next anniversary.

5.4 **Annual true-up.** On each anniversary of the Effective Date, the Supplier will reconcile the invoiced Pupil numbers against actual enrolled Pupils across Activated Schools. Any adjustment within a 3% tolerance is waived; adjustments above 3% are billed or credited at the anniversary.

5.5 **Invoicing.** Unless otherwise agreed in an Order Form, the Supplier will issue a single annual invoice to the Trust covering all Activated Schools. Payment terms are [thirty (30) days] from invoice date.

5.6 **Term discount.** The Trust may elect in an Order Form to commit to a multi-year term, receiving the discount applicable in Schedule 1.

5.7 **Minimum activation threshold.** The Trust undertakes reasonable endeavours to activate at least [seventy-five percent (75%)] of its secondary schools within [eighteen (18) months] of the Effective Date. Failure to reach this threshold may result in a band adjustment at the next anniversary.

### 6. Data protection

6.1 The Supplier acts as a Data Processor on behalf of the Trust (who acts as Data Controller) in respect of Pupil Data.

6.2 The Data Processing Agreement set out in Schedule 2 forms part of this Agreement and governs the processing of all Personal Data.

6.3 In the event of conflict between the body of this Agreement and the Data Processing Agreement, the Data Processing Agreement prevails in respect of data protection matters.

### 7. Service levels

7.1 The Supplier will meet the service levels set out in Schedule 3, including uptime targets, incident response times, and support availability.

7.2 Service credits for missed service levels are calculated as set out in Schedule 3.

### 8. Governance and change control

8.1 **Joint governance board.** The parties will establish a joint governance board within [thirty (30) days] of the Effective Date, meeting at least [quarterly], comprising at least one representative from each party with decision-making authority.

8.2 **Change control.** Material changes to the scope, pricing, or terms of this Agreement are documented as Change Requests and signed by both parties.

8.3 **Product roadmap transparency.** The Supplier will provide the Trust with a high-level product roadmap at each governance meeting.

### 9. Termination

9.1 Either party may terminate this Agreement for material breach if the breach is not remedied within [thirty (30) days] of written notice.

9.2 **Consequences of termination.**
- All Order Forms continue until their specified end dates unless separately terminated.
- On the final expiry of all Order Forms, the Supplier will provide the Trust with a full export of Trust Data in a machine-readable format.
- The Supplier will delete all Trust Data within [ninety (90) days] of the final expiry date, save where retention is required by law.

9.3 **School leaving the Trust.** If a school ceases to be part of the Trust:
- The Trust notifies the Supplier within [30] days;
- The school's data is made available to the school (or to its new trust at the school's direction);
- The Trust's invoice is adjusted at the next anniversary to reflect the reduced Pupil count.

### 10. Warranties and limitations

10.1 The Supplier warrants that the Services will be provided with reasonable care and skill by appropriately qualified staff.

10.2 **Limitation of liability.** (Leave to legal review. Typical shape: liability capped at 12 months of fees, excluding fraud, death/personal injury, data protection, and IP infringement.)

10.3 **Data protection liability** is dealt with separately in the Data Processing Agreement.

### 11. Intellectual property

11.1 The Supplier retains ownership of all IP in the Services.

11.2 Trust Data (including Pupil Data and any teacher-created content entered into the platform by Trust staff) remains the property of the Trust.

11.3 The Supplier grants the Trust a non-exclusive, non-transferable licence to use the Services during the Framework Term and any Order Form term.

### 12. Confidentiality

12.1 Each party will treat the other's confidential information with reasonable care and will not disclose it except to employees, contractors, or advisors who need to know and are bound by equivalent obligations.

### 13. Governing law and disputes

13.1 This Agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales.

13.2 The parties will attempt to resolve any dispute by senior-level discussion before commencing proceedings.

13.3 The courts of England and Wales have exclusive jurisdiction.

### 14. Notices

Notices must be in writing and sent to the addresses specified on the signature page or to such other address as a party notifies in writing.

- **Support hours**: 07:30–18:00 GMT, Monday to Friday, excluding English bank holidays
- **Support channels**: Email, in-product, telephone (for MATs of 10+ schools)
- **Service credits**: [insert formula — typically a percentage of monthly fee per hour of downtime below target]
- **Escalation path**: Named Customer Success Manager for all MATs 10+ schools

## How to use this template in sales conversations

1. **Send the framework agreement early** — during pilot kick-off, not at the end. Legal review takes 4–8 weeks.
2. **Offer the trust's DPO a call** with our DPO/Head of Legal in the first two weeks.
3. **Keep the framework generic**; put all the commercial specifics in Schedule 1 and the Order Form.
4. **Don't agree to bespoke framework edits per school.** The whole point is to negotiate once and activate many.
5. **Treat procurement as a partner**, not an obstacle. Ask them what their standard framework shape looks like and map ours to it where possible.
