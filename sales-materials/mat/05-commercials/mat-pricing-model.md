# MAT Pricing Model — The English Hub

**Purpose**: Give the sales team a clear, defensible pricing framework for MAT-scale deals. Internal use.

**Core principles**:
1. **Per student, not per school.** Flat per-school pricing punishes the biggest schools and doesn't scale.
2. **Banded by total trust volume.** The more students across the trust, the lower the per-student rate.
3. **Transparent.** Any rep should be able to explain the pricing in 60 seconds.
4. **One framework, multiple order forms.** Central framework, school-level activations.
5. **Co-terminous renewal.** One annual review, one decision.

## Term discounts

Incentivise longer commitments with clean, single-digit discounts. No stacking, no small print.

| Term | Discount | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| 1 year | 0% | List price |
| 2 years | 5% | Modest commitment discount |
| 3 years | 10% | Preferred — matches typical MAT budget cycle |
| 5 years | 15% | Rare; only for flagship trusts with signed governance |

**Price lock**: All multi-year deals lock the per-student price for the full term. Any mid-term band change (e.g., trust grows into a new band) applies the **lower** price automatically at the next annual renewal.

## Volume drivers and minimum commitment

### Minimum commitment

- **Framework agreement**: no minimum student commitment (we want the trust to sign).
- **Order forms**: each activated school must commit to the full cohort (Years 7–11) for the contract term. A school can't partially activate for just Year 8 to test.
- **Trust-wide threshold**: the banded price only applies when the trust commits to activating **at least 75% of its secondary schools** within 18 months of framework signature.

### Phased rollout

Trusts can activate schools in waves without losing their banded price, provided the 75% threshold is met by month 18.

| Phase | When | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 0 | Framework signature | Central MAT console live, no pupil data |
| Phase 1 | Months 1–3 | Pilot schools activate (1–3 schools) |
| Phase 2 | Months 4–9 | First main wave (up to 50% of secondaries) |
| Phase 3 | Months 10–18 | Remainder reaches ≥75% of secondaries |

If the 75% threshold is not hit by month 18, the trust moves up one band. The framework remains in place.

## Payment terms

**Default**: Annual invoice in advance, payable within 30 days of acceptance, after the anniversary date of the framework agreement.

**Alternatives**:

| Option | Terms | Cost impact |
|---|---|---|
| Annual in advance | 30 days net | 0% (default) |
| Quarterly in advance | 30 days net | +2% |
| Monthly in advance | 30 days net | +4% |
| Annual in arrears | 60 days net | +3% |
| Phased — by school as activated | 30 days per order form | 0% if trust meets 75% threshold |

Never go to 90-day terms without deal review. Never commit to a MAT-imposed payment schedule that puts us in the red on cash flow without sign-off from finance.

## True-up and true-down

Pupil numbers change through the year. The framework handles this cleanly.

- **Annual true-up**: At the contract anniversary, the trust's actual enrolled student count across activated schools is reconciled against the invoiced number.
- **Growth buffer**: We allow a free 3% growth buffer during the year — if enrolled students grow by up to 3%, no mid-year invoice change.
- **Above 3%**: A pro-rated additional invoice is issued at the anniversary for the growth above the buffer.
- **Shrinkage**: If pupil numbers fall (school leaving the trust, roll changes), the next year's invoice reflects the new count. No mid-year refunds.

This keeps budgeting predictable and administration light.

## Pilot pricing

**Default pilot terms** (for a 12-week trust-wide pilot before framework signature):

| Pilot type | Cost to trust |
|---|---|
| 2-school pilot, 12 weeks | Free |
| 4-school pilot, 12 weeks | Free if framework signed within 30 days of pilot end |
| Full-trust pilot | List price pro-rated for the term |

**Rule**: Never offer a free pilot longer than 12 weeks. Never offer a free pilot beyond 4 schools. If the trust wants more, it becomes a paid commercial engagement.

## Discretion and discounting rules

Reps have limited discretion to discount. All deviations require approval.

| Discount requested | Authority |
|---|---|
| Up to 5% off list | Sales rep |
| 5–10% off list | Sales lead / Head of Sales |
| 10–15% off list | CRO / CEO |
| Over 15% off list | Board sign-off |
| Free pilot beyond 12 weeks | Head of Sales |
| Extended payment terms (>60 days) | CFO |
| MCST (multi-course, multi-trust) bundles | CEO / CRO |

**Default response to "what's your best price?"**: "Our pricing is banded, so for a trust your size we'd be at £[X] per student. Where I can move is on the term — a 3-year commitment brings that down by 10%."

**Never** drop the per-student rate without first offering a longer term or a phased rollout schedule.

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- **Don't quote school-list price to a MAT.** It makes us look greedy or unprepared.
- **Don't offer indefinite free pilots.** The pilot cost is real — staff time, infra, CS time.
- **Don't layer hidden per-teacher costs.** We price per student, period.
- **Don't commit to custom features during pricing conversations.** Product decisions are separate.
- **Don't agree to "most favoured nation" clauses lightly.** They sound harmless and cause lasting damage.
- **Don't agree to "termination for convenience at any time" in year one.** This breaks the unit economics.

## Commercial FAQ — prepared answers

### "Can we do a per-school flat rate instead?"

> "We moved away from per-school pricing because it punishes your biggest schools and undercompensates us for your smallest. Per-student is fairer to the trust. That said, for a 3-year deal we can agree a fixed annual number that doesn't move within the buffer, so budgeting is clean."

### "What happens if we want to add a primary school?"

> "Happy to activate primary schools on the same framework, though the English Hub primary offer is a separate product and has its own pricing. We can bundle both into one trust-wide agreement for simplicity."

### "Can you price-match [competitor]?"

> "We price on value delivered across your trust, not against single-school list prices of other tools. If you share what you're paying today in total across your schools, I can show you the like-for-like economics — usually our trust-wide number is lower than your current fragmented spend."

### "What's the penalty if we cancel?"

> "Multi-year agreements are fixed-term. If the trust needs to exit early, we ask for the remainder of the current annual period plus 50% of the next annual period. If a specific school leaves the trust mid-term, that school's data moves with them and the trust's invoice adjusts at the next anniversary."
