# MAT Pitch Deck Outline — 15 Slides

**Audience**: MAT central team board or SLT — CEO, Director of Education, Director of Curriculum, CFO, COO, Head of English.
**Length**: 30 minutes presentation + 15 minutes Q&A.
**Outcome**: Agreement to move to a trust-wide pilot scoping meeting.

**Design notes**:
- Use the MAT's logo and branding on slide 1 and slide 15.
- Stat-heavy slides are the backbone. Quotes and stories are the texture.
- Every slide has **one idea**. If a slide has two ideas, split it.
- Slide numbers here match the speaker notes doc.

---

## Slide 1 — Title

**Content**:
- "English, at the scale of [MAT name]"
- Subtitle: "A trust-wide curriculum, assessment, and data platform for secondary English"
- Presenter name, date, logos (English Hub + MAT)

**Visual**: Clean title slide. MAT logo top-left, English Hub bottom-right. No clutter.

---

## Slide 2 — The trust-wide English problem

**One-line headline**: "Every MAT has the same English problem — and it doesn't show up until it's too late."

**Content**:
- Fragmented provision: every school does English slightly differently.
- Incomparable data: central team can't answer basic questions across schools.
- Workload crisis: English teachers are leaving the profession.
- Ofsted exposure: reading and cultural capital are under the microscope.

**Visual**: Four-quadrant graphic, one problem per quadrant. Muted colours.

---

## Slide 3 — Current fragmentation in [Trust name]

**One-line headline**: "This is probably what English looks like across your trust today"

**Content**:
- School A: Pearson + in-house schemes + Seneca for homework
- School B: Oak National + Bedrock + custom spreadsheets
- School C: Teacher-made curriculum + Educake + printed anthologies
- Central team: manual termly data returns, incomparable numbers

**Visual**: A simple table showing different schools using different combinations of tools. The discomfort of the picture is the point.

**Speaker note**: If you have real research on this specific MAT, replace with specifics. That moment of "how did you know that?" is what wins the room.

---

## Slide 4 — The cost of fragmentation

**One-line headline**: "Fragmentation costs your trust more than you think."

**Content** — three bullet groups:
- **Financial**: 30–45% licence overspend vs. trust-wide deals; duplicated admin.
- **Time**: Central team spends X hours/month collating school data that arrives late, out of date, and inconsistent.
- **Outcomes**: You can't compare schools. So you can't improve them fairly.

**Visual**: Three big numbers across the slide. One dominant.

---

## Slide 5 — What if it was centralised?

**One-line headline**: "One English platform across every secondary school in your trust."

**Content**:
- One curriculum backbone, editable at school level.
- One assessment framework, comparable across schools.
- One analytics dashboard, visible to central and school leaders.
- One procurement framework, one invoice.
- One answer to "how is English going in our trust?".

**Visual**: Diagram — eight school buildings connected to a single platform icon in the middle.

---

## Slide 6 — Introducing The English Hub

**One-line headline**: "Built from the ground up for MATs."

**Content**:
- Secondary English curriculum, Y7–Y11, aligned to the National Curriculum.
- Assessment engine — formative, summative, reading age, writing, vocabulary.
- Trust-wide dashboards for leadership, school improvement, and board reporting.
- MAT framework pricing, per-student bands, one annual invoice.

**Visual**: Product screenshot of the MAT dashboard, with real-looking (but anonymised) data.

---

## Slide 7 — Consistency across schools

**One-line headline**: "Same curriculum. Same assessment. Comparable data."

**Content**:
- Year 7 Unit 1 looks the same, but feels local.
- Teacher freedom within a trust-wide framework.
- Pupil movement between schools becomes frictionless.
- Cross-school moderation becomes trivial.

**Visual**: Two classroom screenshots side by side, clearly the same unit, clearly different schools.

---

## Slide 8 — Data and insights across the trust

**One-line headline**: "One view of English across every school — updated in real time."

**Content** — the headline metrics you can see in one place:
- Reading age progression by school
- PP vs. non-PP gap by school
- GCSE forecast by school
- Implementation fidelity by school
- Teacher workload saved by school

**Visual**: Actual dashboard screenshot (anonymised / demo data). This is the most important visual in the deck.

---

## Slide 9 — Ofsted and cultural capital

**One-line headline**: "Inspection-ready, always."

**Content**:
- Curriculum intent, implementation, impact — all documented and evidenced.
- Cultural capital rationale for every text.
- Reading age as the leading indicator inspectors now expect.
- One-click inspection evidence pack per school.

**Visual**: Example inspection evidence pack cover page.

**Speaker note**: No guarantees on outcomes. What we guarantee is the evidence is ready when the call comes.

---

## Slide 10 — Teacher workload and retention

**One-line headline**: "Give your English teachers their evenings back."

**Content**:
- Ready-made, editable resources.
- Auto-marked objective assessment; structured support for extended writing.
- Estimated 4–6 hours per week per teacher.
- Direct contribution to retention — every teacher retained saves the trust ~£10k.

**Visual**: A single stat — "4–6 hours per teacher, per week" — with a small breakdown underneath.

---

## Slide 11 — How a pilot would work

**One-line headline**: "A 12-week pilot across two schools, evaluated by your central team."

**Content**:
- Week 1–2: Onboarding, training, content import.
- Week 3–10: Live use, data collection, weekly check-ins.
- Week 11: Pupil and teacher feedback, data review.
- Week 12: Go/no-go decision with trust leadership.
- No long-term commitment until the pilot is complete.

**Visual**: Simple timeline bar.

---

## Slide 12 — Commercials at trust scale

**One-line headline**: "One framework agreement. Per-student banded pricing."

**Content**:
- Pricing bands by total pupil numbers across the trust.
- Co-terminous renewal across all schools.
- One invoice, or per-school order forms under a master agreement.
- 3-year term with a 10% discount option.
- First 6 months of pilot included at no cost if the framework is signed before [date].

**Visual**: Simple banded-pricing table.

**Speaker note**: This slide is designed to produce one question: "What would our specific trust pay?" That question is exactly where you want to be.

---

## Slide 13 — Diligence and security

**One-line headline**: "Procurement-ready from day one."

**Content**:
- UK-hosted data, GDPR-compliant.
- Data Processing Agreement template included.
- Cyber Essentials Plus certified (or state accurate current status).
- ISO 27001 aligned (or state accurate current status).
- DfE digital standards compliant.
- Pre-filled answers to typical MAT IT security questionnaires.

**Visual**: A row of security/compliance badge logos.

---

## Slide 14 — Why now

**One-line headline**: "Three reasons to move in this academic year."

**Content**:
- Budget planning for next year begins in [month].
- Ofsted framework emphasis on reading and cultural capital continues to sharpen.
- Teacher recruitment market gets harder every year — retention is the battle.

**Visual**: Three-icon row.

---

## Slide 15 — The ask

**One-line headline**: "Next step: a 60-minute pilot scoping session with your team."

**Content**:
- What we bring: product demo with trust data, pilot design, commercial outline.
- Who we'd like to meet: Director of Education, Head of English (x2 schools), Data Lead, Finance contact.
- Timing: within the next 3 weeks.
- Decision by [specific date].

**Visual**: MAT logo + English Hub logo + contact details (named rep, email, phone).

**Speaker note**: Always ask for the specific next meeting, with specific people, on a specific timeline. Never leave with "we'll be in touch."
