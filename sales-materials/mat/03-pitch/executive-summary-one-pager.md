## The opportunity in one sentence

One centrally-managed English curriculum, assessment, and data platform across every secondary school in your trust — delivering measurable outcomes, lower per-student cost, and dramatic reductions in teacher workload.

## Why MATs come to us

| Problem today | What [MAT name] gets with English Hub |
|---|---|
| Fragmented English provision — different tools and schemes in every school | One curriculum backbone across every secondary, editable at school level |
| Incomparable pupil data between schools | Trust-wide dashboard with real-time reading age, writing, vocabulary, and GCSE forecasting |

## What it is

- **Secondary English, Years 7 to 11**, aligned to the National Curriculum.
- A **curriculum layer** with sequenced units, lesson resources, and cultural capital rationale.
- An **assessment engine** covering formative, summative, reading age, writing, vocabulary, and GCSE prep.
- A **trust-wide analytics dashboard** comparing every school in your trust.
- A **procurement-ready commercial** with one framework agreement and per-student banded pricing.

## What it isn't

- It isn't a textbook digitisation — we were built as software from day one.
- It isn't a student-facing revision tool — it's a teacher- and trust-facing platform.
- It isn't a rip-and-replace demand — we coexist with your existing tools for as long as makes sense.
