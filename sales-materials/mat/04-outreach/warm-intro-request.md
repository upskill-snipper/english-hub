# Warm Intro Request — Asking a School Contact for a MAT Introduction

**Situation**: You have a relationship with someone at a school that belongs to a MAT you want to sell into. The school contact is typically a Head of English, Deputy Head, or sometimes the Headteacher. You want them to introduce you to the MAT's central team.

**Why this matters**: A warm intro from inside the trust is worth approximately 10x a cold email. It also gives you instant credibility when the central team checks with the school contact.

**Important**: Never ask for an intro unless the school contact actually likes the product. A reluctant intro is worse than no intro — it signals doubt to the central team.

---

## Pre-flight checklist — before you ask

Do not send the intro request until all of these are true:

- [ ] The school contact has used The English Hub for at least one full half-term.
- [ ] You've had at least one positive conversation with them about outcomes.
- [ ] You can quote something specific and good they've said about the product.
- [ ] The school contact is senior enough that their opinion carries weight with the MAT central team.
- [ ] You know who specifically at the MAT you want to be introduced to.
- [ ] You've checked that your school contact is not in active conflict with the central team about something unrelated (awkward context for an intro).

---

## The ask — email template

**Subject**: Quick favour — intro to [central team person]?

Hi [school contact first name],

Hope this term is treating you well — [brief specific personal reference, e.g., "how did Year 10 mocks land?"].

I've got a small favour to ask, and I want to be upfront: please feel completely free to say no.

As you know, we've been working with [school name] for [time period], and I know you've had [specific positive outcome — e.g., "good results on the Year 8 reading trial", "saved time on marking in the KS4 cohorts"]. Based on conversations we're having elsewhere, I think there's a real case for [MAT name] to look at English Hub as a trust-wide option — not just at [school name] alone.

Would you be willing to make a short introduction to [name of MAT central contact] — Director of [Education / Curriculum / English] at the trust? Even a two-line email saying you've used us and think it's worth 20 minutes of their time would make a real difference.

If you're comfortable doing this, I can send you a one-paragraph draft you can paste into an email or adapt in your own voice. If not — no problem at all, and it doesn't change anything between us. I'll carry on the cold approach from my end.

Thank you for considering it either way.

Best,
[Rep name]

## The draft intro the school contact can send (forward-ready)

When the school contact says yes, send them this template to copy, edit, and send. **Make it short, credit them, remove obvious rep phrasing.**

**Subject**: Intro — English Hub / trust-wide English

Hi [central team contact first name],

Quick intro.

We've been using The English Hub in the English department at [school name] for [time period]. It's the first tool I've seen at my desk in several years that I'd actually want other schools in the trust to be able to use — the [specific feature: curriculum, assessment, reading age tracking, writing feedback] has genuinely saved my team time and given me better data on Year [X] than I had before.

I'm not sure if trust-wide English tooling is on your agenda at the moment, but if it ever will be, it's probably worth a conversation with them. I suggested they drop you a line directly — they won't push and they won't waste your time.

[Rep name] from English Hub is cc'd. I'll leave it with you both.

Best,
[School contact name]
[Title] — [School name]

## What to do once the intro is sent

1. **Reply promptly** — within two working hours of the intro landing in your inbox.
2. **Thank both sides** in the reply. Never make it feel transactional.
3. **Move the school contact to BCC** on your response — don't clutter their inbox further.
4. **Offer three specific time slots** in the next two weeks — don't say "whenever works for you".
5. **Send a short, relevant attachment** — the executive one-pager, not the full pitch deck.
6. **Follow up with the school contact separately** later that week with a small thank-you (handwritten card, a small gift appropriate to sector norms, or simply a well-chosen piece of research you think they'd find useful). Never a hamper or anything that looks like a conflict-of-interest concern for a public-sector buyer.

---

## If the school contact says no

This is absolutely fine and common. Respond warmly:

> "Completely understood and thanks for being honest — that's genuinely more helpful than a lukewarm intro would have been. I'll carry on the cold route from my end. Let me know if there's ever anything I can do for you in return."

Then don't ask again for at least six months. A "no" now might be a "yes" after another positive term.

---

## Common objections from school contacts — and how to handle

### "I don't want to look like I'm pushing something to the central team."

> "Totally fair. One low-pressure alternative — you could just mention over a coffee that your department is using English Hub and it's been useful, and leave it at that. No formal intro needed. If they want to follow up, they will."

### "I'm not sure the central team is receptive to new tools right now."

> "That's really helpful context — thank you. Can I ask what's going on? Is it a budget thing, a strategic priority shift, or something else? Even knowing the 'why not now' saves me wasted effort at their end."

### "The central team isn't very involved in individual school decisions."

> "Understood. In that case, who else in the MAT makes decisions about trust-wide tools if not central? Sometimes it's a lead school's Headteacher rather than a central role."

### "I don't know the central team person by name."

> "No problem — I'll find the right person through their website. What would help is if you're willing to have me use your name as a reference when I reach out, even without a direct intro. Something like 'I understand [school contact] at [school] has been using us this term.' That alone is a big help."

## Tracking

Log every intro request in CRM with:

- Date of ask
- Name of school contact
- Name of MAT target person
- Yes / no / not yet
- If yes: date intro sent, date central replied, next meeting booked
- Thank-you sent: yes/no
- Follow-up scheduled

One good warm intro can be worth a six-figure contract. Treat every request with appropriate care.
