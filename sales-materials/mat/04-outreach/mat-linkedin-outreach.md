# LinkedIn Outreach Templates — MAT Central Leaders

**Purpose**: Start conversations with MAT Directors of Education, curriculum leads, and CEOs on LinkedIn, where they're often more responsive than email.

**Rules**:
1. Never send a cold connection request with a pitch. Warm the relationship first.
2. Comment meaningfully on 1–2 of their posts before connecting.
3. Connection note is 300 characters — make them count.
4. First DM after accept is **not** a pitch. It's a thank-you with a specific reason you wanted to connect.
5. Wait a full week between the first DM and anything with "20 minutes" in it.
6. Never use LinkedIn automation tools. MAT leaders can spot them immediately and the damage is lasting.

---

## Stage 1 — Engage with their content (before connecting)

Spend 10 minutes finding their 3–5 most recent public posts. Pick one or two that are genuinely interesting and leave a substantive comment.

**Good comment examples**:

> "The point about writing assessment needing to be sequenced with curriculum, not bolted on — that's exactly the trap most platforms fall into. Do you find it easier to solve at the school level or at the trust level?"

> "Your framing of cultural capital as 'substantive' rather than 'representative' is useful. We see a lot of trusts struggle with that distinction when they audit their text lists."

**Bad comment examples**:

> "Great post!"
> "So true."
> "Thanks for sharing."

If you can only write "great post", don't comment.

## Stage 2 — Connection request (300 chars max)

### Template 1 — After a thoughtful comment exchange

> Hi [first name], enjoyed your post on [topic] — your point about [specific idea] is exactly the problem we're working on at English Hub (trust-wide English curriculum and data for MATs). No pitch — would just be useful to be connected as I follow your work.

### Template 2 — Common connection intro

> Hi [first name], [mutual connection's name] suggested I follow your work on [topic]. We're building trust-wide English tools for MATs and I'd value being in your network. No pitch attached.

### Template 3 — Event-driven

> Hi [first name], saw you were speaking at [event]. Your session on [topic] hits the exact problem we're trying to solve for MATs — trust-wide English data and curriculum consistency. Happy to be connected.

### Template 4 — New-role congratulations

> Hi [first name], congratulations on the new [role] at [MAT name]. Happy to be in your network — we work with MAT central teams on trust-wide English and would value following your work.

---

## Stage 3 — First DM after connection accepted (24–48 hours later)

Keep it short. Reference **why** you wanted to connect. Do **not** pitch.

### Template A — Gratitude-led

> Hi [first name], thanks for the connect.
>
> Following up on your comment/post about [topic] — the bit about [specific idea] really stuck with me. We've been wrestling with the same question on our side for MAT English provision, and I'd genuinely value your perspective at some point.
>
> For now — no pitch, just wanted to say thanks for sharing your work publicly. It's useful.
>
> [Rep name]

### Template B — Specific curiosity

> Hi [first name], thanks for connecting.
>
> Quick honest question, no sales angle: across [MAT name]'s secondary schools, how unified is your current English curriculum and assessment picture? It's the topic I spend most of my week on and I'm always curious how different MATs approach it.
>
> Happy to share what we're seeing across other trusts if it's useful.
>
> [Rep name]

### Template C — Share without asking

> Hi [first name], thanks for the connect.
>
> Rather than asking for time, here's something that might be useful either way: I pulled a short benchmark recently on how MATs currently measure English provision across their schools. Happy to share if you'd find it useful.
>
> No pitch — just thought it might land well given your recent post on [topic].
>
> [Rep name]

## Stage 4 — Second DM, one week later (soft ask)

### Template X — 15 minutes

> Hi [first name],
>
> Quick one — after reading [specific recent post or article of theirs], it feels like there's enough overlap between what we're building and what [MAT name] is wrestling with to be worth a real conversation.
>
> Would you be open to 15 minutes in the next couple of weeks? I'll come with anonymised data from trusts of similar size to [MAT name] and a specific view of how we'd think about your trust. If it's not useful, you get 15 minutes back.
>
> Best,
> [Rep name]

### Template Y — Referral to colleague

> Hi [first name],
>
> Last note from me for a while, I promise. If [MAT English / curriculum decisions / central edtech buying] isn't in your direct remit, who at [MAT name] would you suggest I introduce myself to? Would value your steer, even if the answer is "no one right now."
>
> Thanks,
> [Rep name]

## Stage 5 — Stay present without pestering

If they haven't responded after two DMs, **stop DMing**. Instead:

- Like and occasionally comment on their posts (thoughtfully).
- Share a relevant piece of content or research on your own feed and tag them only if it's genuinely relevant to something they posted.
- Every 2–3 months, send a lightweight DM with a specific product update or research finding — no ask attached.

---

## LinkedIn outreach checklist per contact

- [ ] Read their last 5 public posts
- [ ] Left at least 1 substantive comment on a public post
- [ ] Sent a personalised connection request (not default "I'd like to connect")
- [ ] Waited for acceptance
- [ ] Sent first DM — gratitude only, no pitch
- [ ] Waited one week
- [ ] Sent second DM — specific value, soft ask
- [ ] Logged all activity in CRM
- [ ] If no response: added to nurture, commented occasionally, no more DMs for 60 days

## LinkedIn profile hygiene for the rep

Before sending any outreach, the rep's own profile must:

- Feature a clear headshot (not a brand logo)
- Have a headline that explains The English Hub's value ("Helping MAT central teams run secondary English consistently across every school" — not "Sales at The English Hub")
- Link to an About section that sounds like a human, not a boilerplate
- Be followed by The English Hub company page
- Have at least 3 recent posts that aren't pure product marketing — show the rep has a point of view on MAT education
- Ideally: a pinned featured post with genuinely useful sector insight (a chart, a piece of research, a short essay)

MAT leaders will click your profile before accepting. If it looks like a standard BDR, they won't bother.

---

## Sector-appropriate posting cadence for reps

Post 1–2 times per week on LinkedIn, focused on:
- Observations about MAT-scale problems (not pitches)
- Data points from the sector (cite sources)
- Quotes from MAT customers (with permission)
- Reactions to Ofsted publications, DfE announcements, Schools Week pieces
- Teacher workload and retention discussion

Avoid:
- Hustle-culture content
- Motivational quotes
- Anything that feels like a sales pitch
- Leaderboards and "top X lists"

The goal is to be a person worth knowing in the MAT leadership space. Sales flows from that.
