# MAT Value Proposition — The English Hub

**Audience**: MAT central team — CEO, Director of Education, Director of Curriculum, Head of English, COO, CFO.

**The core promise**:
> One centrally-managed English curriculum, assessment, and data engine across every secondary school in your trust — with measurable outcomes, lower per-student cost, and dramatic reductions in teacher workload.

---

## The six value pillars

### 1. Consistency across schools

**The MAT problem**: English provision varies wildly between schools in the same trust. One school uses Pearson, another uses Seneca, a third has homegrown schemes of work. Results are incomparable. Teachers moving between schools have to relearn systems. The trust cannot make evidence-based decisions because the evidence isn't comparable.

**What we do**:
- One curriculum backbone across every secondary in the trust.
- Same assessment design, same question bank, same reading age measures.
- Teacher materials standardised but customisable at the school level.
- Pupil progress reports comparable across all schools on day one.

**What the trust gets**:
- Cross-school moderation becomes trivial.
- Teacher movement inside the trust stops costing weeks of relearning.
- "Is School A really ahead of School B, or is it just different assessments?" becomes answerable.

---

### 2. Centralised purchasing — economic leverage

**The MAT problem**: 12 secondary schools each negotiating their own English edtech licences. Duplicated admin. Price is whatever the school can haggle for. Central finance has no line of sight. Renewal dates are scattered across the calendar.

**What we do**:
- One framework agreement at trust level.
- Per-student pricing with banded discounts as trust size grows.
- Single annual invoice, or per-school order forms under the master agreement.
- Central admin console — one login gives trust leadership visibility across every school.
- Co-terminous renewal so budget planning actually works.

**What the trust gets**:
- Typical saving: **30–45% vs. school-by-school list price** at 10+ schools.
- One line on the trust budget instead of twelve.
- Procurement team loves us — we slot into any standard framework.

---

### 3. Per-student pricing at scale

**The MAT problem**: Flat per-school licences punish the biggest schools and subsidise the smallest. They also don't scale — when a school's roll grows or shrinks, the cost doesn't move.

**What we do**:
- Pure per-student pricing.
- Tiered bands: the more students across the trust, the lower the per-student rate.
- Annual true-up so the MAT only pays for what they actually use.
- No hidden "per-teacher" or "per-module" add-ons.

**What the trust gets**:
- Predictable, honest budgeting.
- A free talking point in board meetings: "We negotiated £X per student across the trust."
- Fair distribution of cost by school size.

**Typical bands** (see `05-commercials/mat-pricing-model.md` for detail):

| Students across trust | Per-student price (annual) |
|---|---|
| Up to 1,000 | Retail |
| 1,000–5,000 | ~15% discount |
| 5,000–10,000 | ~25% discount |
| 10,000–25,000 | ~35% discount |
| 25,000+ | ~45% discount + bespoke terms |

---

### 4. Data insights across the trust

**The MAT problem**: Central teams drown in school-supplied spreadsheets. Data is out of date by the time it reaches central. No one can compare schools apples-to-apples. Board meetings are full of qualitative assertions, not evidence.

**What we do**:
- Real-time trust-wide dashboard: reading age, writing accuracy, vocabulary growth, GCSE mock trajectories, intervention effectiveness.
- School comparison views with year-on-year trend lines.
- Pupil Premium vs. non-PP gap tracking across the trust.
- Automated monthly reports sent to the central team and individual HoDs.
- Ofsted-ready evidence packs on demand.

**What the trust gets**:
- Evidence-led trust board conversations.
- Early warning signals when a school's English cohort is drifting.
- Central school improvement team spends its time on improvement, not data collection.

---

### 5. Ofsted alignment

**The MAT problem**: Ofsted's framework is increasingly specific about reading, cultural capital, curriculum breadth, and sequential knowledge-building. Trust boards feel exposed between inspections.

**What we do** (full detail in `ofsted-alignment.md`):
- Curriculum built explicitly around "intent, implementation, impact".
- Reading age and vocabulary data mapped to Ofsted reading priorities.
- Cultural capital evidenced through literature range and knowledge content.
- Clear audit trail at pupil, class, and school level for every judgement an inspector might probe.

**What the trust gets**:
- Pre-inspection confidence for every school in the trust.
- A consistent answer to "what is your trust doing about reading?" that applies everywhere.
- Executive leaders can speak to English provision in any school at any time with real data.

---

### 6. Teacher retention and workload

**The MAT problem**: English teachers are the hardest subject to recruit and retain (alongside maths, MFL, physics). Workload in English is particularly brutal — marking, lesson planning, scheme-of-work creation. When good English teachers leave, whole cohorts suffer.

**What we do**:
- Ready-made, editable lesson resources aligned to trust curriculum.
- Auto-marking of objective items; structured marking support for extended writing.
- Shared trust-wide resource library — no teacher recreating what another school has already built.
- Onboarding new teachers in under a week instead of a term.
- Reducing average English teacher marking load by **an estimated 4–6 hours per week**.

**What the trust gets**:
- Direct contribution to the trust's teacher retention KPIs.
- Budget impact: every teacher retained saves £5k–£15k in recruitment, onboarding, and supply cover.
- New-to-trust teacher productivity earlier in the year.

## The one-sentence pitch

> "The English Hub is the centralised English curriculum, assessment, and analytics layer for your trust — so your central team gets one view of every school, your teachers get their evenings back, and your pupils get measurably better outcomes for less than you're paying today."

## The three-question qualification script

1. "How consistent is English provision across your secondary schools today — same curriculum, same assessments, or different at every school?"
2. "If I asked your board tomorrow for the reading-age gap between your highest- and lowest-performing school, would you have that number?"
3. "When your next secondary school joins the trust, how long does it take to bring their English department up to trust standard?"

Each question is a wedge. Each answer creates a reason to book the next meeting.
