# Ofsted Alignment — How The English Hub Supports the Ofsted Framework

**Purpose**: Give MAT leadership a defensible, inspector-ready answer to every Ofsted question about English provision. This document is both a positioning asset and a reference for trust executives preparing for inspection.

**Framework**: Based on Ofsted's Education Inspection Framework (EIF), the School Inspection Handbook, and the subject-specific research review for English.

---

## Why MATs care about Ofsted alignment

- Every secondary school in the trust is subject to inspection every 3–5 years.
- Trust-level reputation depends on consistent quality judgements across schools.
- A weak English provision at a single school can trigger a visible Ofsted downgrade that dominates local press.
- Inspectors increasingly ask: "Is this a school improving on its own, or is the trust actively supporting it?"

A single English platform deployed consistently across the trust gives the trust a defensible, repeatable answer.

---

## The four Ofsted judgement areas — and our response

### 1. Quality of Education

Ofsted evaluates: intent, implementation, impact.

#### Intent — "What is the curriculum for?"

**What inspectors want to see**:
- A coherent, well-sequenced English curriculum that builds knowledge cumulatively.
- Explicit alignment with the National Curriculum.
- Cultural capital: diverse, substantive literature; vocabulary range; contextual knowledge.
- Ambition for all pupils including SEND and disadvantaged.

**How English Hub supports this**:
- Published curriculum maps for Years 7–11, explicitly sequenced from foundational knowledge to GCSE-level synthesis.
- Cultural capital framework: the platform surfaces the rationale for every text choice — historical context, thematic connection, literary movement.
- SEND scaffolding and challenge built into every unit.
- Trust-wide curriculum intent documents downloadable in MAT-branded format.

**Inspector-ready answer**: "Here is our trust-wide English intent document. Here is the sequencing evidence for every year group. Here is the map of where every Year 7 pupil should be by the end of the autumn term, in every school."

---

#### Implementation — "Is it being taught as designed?"

**What inspectors want to see**:
- Evidence that teachers are delivering the planned curriculum.
- Assessment aligned to the curriculum intent.
- Teacher subject knowledge sufficient for the content.
- Adaptive teaching that responds to pupil need.

**How English Hub supports this**:
- Teacher dashboards show which units have been taught, which assessments completed, and which intervention groups are active.
- Trust central view shows implementation consistency across schools — a Year 8 lesson in School A maps to the same expected outcomes as in School B.
- Built-in teacher CPD materials — short subject knowledge refreshers embedded in each unit.
- Adaptive assessment that identifies misconceptions at the individual pupil level.

**Inspector-ready answer**: "We can show you, for any school in the trust, what has been taught this half-term, how pupils have responded, and which interventions are in flight."

---

#### Impact — "Are pupils actually learning?"

**What inspectors want to see**:
- Pupils remember more, know more, and can do more.
- Outcomes at the end of key stages.
- Disadvantaged pupils making substantive progress.
- Reading fluency and comprehension improving.

**How English Hub supports this**:
- Reading age data updated each half-term, trackable at pupil, class, year, school, and trust level.
- Retention tracking through spaced retrieval quizzes — "do pupils remember the Year 7 content in Year 9?".
- Pupil Premium vs. non-PP gap tracking with narrowing trendlines.
- GCSE forecasting from KS3 data.

**Inspector-ready answer**: "Here is the year-on-year reading age progression across our trust. Here is the PP gap at each school. Here is the percentage of Year 7 pupils on track for a Grade 5+ at GCSE English."

---

### 2. Behaviour and Attitudes

**What inspectors want to see (for English specifically)**:
- Pupils engaged in reading.
- A reading culture.
- Pupils taking pride in their written work.
- Pupils volunteering to read aloud, discuss, contribute.

**How English Hub supports this**:
- Reading-for-pleasure tracking: minutes read, books completed, review contributions.
- Trust-wide reading challenges and campaigns.
- Writing portfolio showcase — pupils' best writing visible and shareable.
- Student voice features where pupils discuss what they've read.

**Inspector-ready answer**: "Our trust reading-culture dashboard shows participation, pages read, and the books our pupils are choosing. Every school has a live reading leaderboard."

---

### 3. Personal Development

**What inspectors want to see**:
- Cultural capital: exposure to the best of what has been thought and said.
- Character education through literature.
- British values addressed through curriculum content.
- Careers and aspiration in English — journalism, law, communications, creative industries.

**How English Hub supports this**:
- Every text choice is anchored to a cultural capital rationale.
- Diverse literature spanning Shakespeare, postcolonial voices, contemporary British fiction, non-fiction journalism.
- Careers-in-English content woven into KS4.
- Mapped links to British values and SMSC.

**Inspector-ready answer**: "Our English curriculum gives every pupil access to X authors across Y cultures and Z historical periods. Here is the cultural capital rationale for every text in Year 9."

---

### 4. Leadership and Management

**What inspectors want to see**:
- Subject leadership is knowledgeable and proactive.
- CPD is appropriate and impactful.
- Workload is managed sensibly.
- Safeguarding integrated.
- Governance has a clear line of sight.

**How English Hub supports this**:
- Central MAT dashboards for trust leadership — one-click school comparisons.
- Embedded CPD modules for subject leads.
- Workload metrics — estimated teacher hours saved per half-term.
- Safeguarding-compliant content moderation and reporting.
- Board-ready reports for governors at each school and across the trust.

**Inspector-ready answer**: "Our trust board sees termly English data across every school. Our central team runs CPD based on where the data says we need it. Our teachers spend fewer hours marking and more hours teaching."

## The subject-specific English research review

Ofsted published a research review for English in June 2022 (referenced throughout their inspection work since). Key points and how we align:

| Ofsted principle | How English Hub delivers |
|---|---|
| Reading as the foundation of the subject | Reading age, comprehension, fluency tracked at pupil level across the trust |
| Cumulative knowledge-building | Curriculum sequenced from foundational knowledge to synthesis |
| Explicit vocabulary instruction | Tier-2 and tier-3 vocabulary systematically introduced and assessed |
| Writing taught systematically | Writing structure, grammar, and style taught and tracked unit by unit |
| Literature as a source of cultural knowledge | Cultural capital rationale for every text; diversity of voices |
| Oracy as a vehicle for understanding | Speaking and listening tasks built into each unit with teacher assessment |
| Assessment integral to learning | Formative and summative assessment embedded, not bolted on |
| Teacher subject knowledge | Embedded CPD and subject-knowledge refreshers for every unit |

## Important caveats

- English Hub does not guarantee any inspection outcome. No edtech product can. What we do is remove the evidence-gathering burden so that leadership time goes into improvement, not documentation.
- Inspection outcomes depend on many factors including teaching quality, safeguarding, behaviour, attendance, and wider leadership.
- This document is a positioning asset and should be reviewed by a qualified Ofsted inspector or MAT school improvement director before being cited verbatim to a prospect.
