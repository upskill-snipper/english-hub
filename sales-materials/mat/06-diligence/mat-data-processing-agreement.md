# Data Processing Agreement Template — MAT-Wide Processing

**IMPORTANT**: This is a drafting template for use by The English Hub team and the prospect's legal/data protection advisors. It is not legal advice. Any DPA based on this document must be reviewed and adapted by qualified legal counsel before signature.

**Purpose**: Provide a single DPA that governs all processing of personal data across every Activated School in a MAT, so the MAT's DPO reviews once and schools activate without reopening data protection review each time.

**Structure**: Body + Annexes. Annexes capture the specific details that vary by trust (subprocessors, data categories, technical and organisational measures).

---

## DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENT

Made between:

**[MAT NAME]** (the "**Controller**"); and
**The English Hub Limited** (the "**Processor**").

### Background

A. The parties have entered into a Master Framework Agreement dated [DATE] (the "**Main Agreement**") under which the Processor provides the English Hub platform (the "**Services**") to the Controller's Activated Schools.

B. In providing the Services, the Processor processes personal data on behalf of the Controller within the meaning of the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018) (together the "**Data Protection Laws**").

C. This Data Processing Agreement (the "**DPA**") sets out the terms on which the Processor processes personal data under the Main Agreement.

---

### 1. Definitions

Capitalised terms have the meaning given in the Main Agreement, unless defined below:

- **"Controller"**, **"Processor"**, **"Data Subject"**, **"Personal Data"**, **"Process"**, **"Processing"**, **"Supervisory Authority"** — have the meanings in the UK GDPR.
- **"Activated School"** — has the meaning in the Main Agreement.
- **"Data Subjects"** — individuals whose Personal Data is processed under this DPA, including pupils, staff, parents (where applicable), and other individuals as described in Annex A.
- **"Data Protection Laws"** — the UK GDPR, DPA 2018, and any successor or related legislation.
- **"Personal Data Breach"** — has the meaning in UK GDPR Article 4(12).
- **"Standard Contractual Clauses"** or **"SCCs"** — the UK International Data Transfer Addendum to the EU Commission's SCCs, or any successor instrument.
- **"Sub-Processor"** — any third party engaged by the Processor to process Personal Data on the Controller's behalf.

### 2. Roles and scope

2.1 In respect of Personal Data processed under the Main Agreement, the Controller is the Data Controller and the Processor is the Data Processor.

2.2 This DPA applies to all Personal Data processed under the Main Agreement, including Personal Data relating to pupils, staff, and other individuals at all Activated Schools within the Controller's MAT.

2.3 A single Activated School may not enter into a separate or competing DPA with the Processor in respect of the same Services without the Controller's written agreement. The intent is a single, trust-wide data protection regime.

### 3. Processing instructions

3.1 The Processor will process Personal Data only on the documented instructions of the Controller, except where required by law. The Main Agreement, this DPA, and the product documentation constitute the Controller's initial documented instructions.

3.2 The Processor will inform the Controller without delay if it believes an instruction infringes the Data Protection Laws.

3.3 The Processor will not use Personal Data for its own purposes, including training machine learning models, except where:
    (a) explicitly permitted under this DPA; or
    (b) the data has been fully and irreversibly anonymised.

### 4. Subject matter, duration, nature and purpose (Annex A)

The subject matter, duration, nature and purpose of the processing, types of Personal Data, categories of Data Subjects, and obligations and rights of the Controller are set out in **Annex A**.

### 5. Confidentiality and staff

5.1 The Processor will ensure that any person authorised to process the Personal Data is bound by appropriate confidentiality obligations.

5.2 The Processor will ensure that staff with access to Personal Data have appropriate training and are subject to suitable background checks in accordance with the Processor's documented policies.

### 6. Security measures

6.1 The Processor will implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, in accordance with UK GDPR Article 32. Those measures are set out in **Annex B**.

6.2 The Processor will review and update its technical and organisational measures on an ongoing basis.

### 7. Sub-processors

7.1 The Controller grants the Processor general authorisation to engage Sub-Processors for the purpose of providing the Services, subject to this clause 7.

7.2 A list of authorised Sub-Processors is set out in **Annex C**.

7.3 The Processor will give the Controller at least 30 days' prior notice of any proposed change to Sub-Processors. The Controller may object in writing, with reasonable grounds relating to data protection, during that notice period.

7.4 If the Controller objects and the parties cannot agree an acceptable alternative within a further 30 days, the Controller may terminate the affected part of the Main Agreement without penalty.

7.5 The Processor will impose contractual obligations on each Sub-Processor that are materially equivalent to those in this DPA.

7.6 The Processor remains fully liable to the Controller for the acts and omissions of any Sub-Processor.

### 8. Data Subject rights

8.1 The Processor will assist the Controller, at the Controller's cost and by appropriate technical and organisational measures, in fulfilling the Controller's obligations to respond to Data Subject requests.

8.2 The Processor provides the Controller with self-service tools within the Services to locate, export, rectify, and delete Personal Data in response to Data Subject requests.

8.3 If the Processor receives a Data Subject request directly, it will promptly forward the request to the Controller and will not respond to it except at the Controller's written direction.

### 9. Assistance with obligations

9.1 The Processor will assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with its obligations under UK GDPR Articles 32–36, including:
    (a) security of processing;
    (b) notification of Personal Data Breaches;
    (c) data protection impact assessments; and
    (d) prior consultation with the Supervisory Authority.

### 10. Personal Data Breach notification

10.1 The Processor will notify the Controller without undue delay after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach affecting Personal Data processed under this DPA.

10.2 The initial notification will include, to the extent available:
    (a) the nature of the breach and categories and approximate numbers of Data Subjects and records affected;
    (b) the likely consequences of the breach;
    (c) measures taken or proposed to address the breach and to mitigate its effects; and
    (d) a named contact for further information.

10.3 The Processor will provide updates as information becomes available.

10.4 The Controller remains responsible for notifying the Supervisory Authority under UK GDPR Article 33 and notifying affected Data Subjects under Article 34 where required.

### 11. International transfers

11.1 The Processor will not transfer Personal Data outside the UK without the Controller's prior written consent, except where permitted in Annex C.

11.2 Where such transfers are permitted, the Processor will ensure an appropriate transfer mechanism is in place, including SCCs where applicable.

### 12. Audit rights

12.1 The Processor will make available to the Controller all information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA.

12.2 The Controller may request an audit of the Processor's compliance no more than once per calendar year, on at least 30 days' written notice, conducted during working hours, and in a manner that does not unreasonably interfere with the Processor's operations.

12.3 The Processor may, in place of an audit, provide an up-to-date independent third-party audit report (e.g., SOC 2 Type II or ISO 27001 surveillance report) that addresses the Controller's questions.

12.4 Audit costs are borne by the Controller, except where the audit reveals material non-compliance, in which case the Processor bears reasonable audit costs.

### 13. Deletion and return of Personal Data

13.1 On termination of the Main Agreement or an individual Order Form, the Processor will, at the Controller's choice:
    (a) return all Personal Data in a commonly used machine-readable format; and/or
    (b) delete all copies of Personal Data within 90 days of termination.

13.2 The Processor will issue a written confirmation of deletion on completion.

13.3 This clause does not require deletion of backups held in accordance with the Processor's documented backup retention policy, provided those backups are encrypted, segregated, and will be deleted on the backup retention cycle.

### 14. Records of processing

14.1 Each party will maintain records of processing activities as required by UK GDPR Article 30.

14.2 The Processor will make its records of processing related to the Services available to the Controller on written request.

### 15. Liability and indemnity

15.1 Each party's liability under this DPA is governed by the limitation of liability provisions in the Main Agreement, except where Data Protection Laws require a different allocation.

### 16. Conflict

16.1 In the event of conflict between this DPA and the Main Agreement, this DPA prevails in respect of data protection matters.

### 17. Term and termination

17.1 This DPA takes effect on the Effective Date of the Main Agreement and continues until the Main Agreement and all Order Forms have expired or terminated and all Personal Data has been returned or deleted.

### 18. Changes to Data Protection Laws

18.1 The parties will negotiate in good faith amendments to this DPA necessary to remain compliant with changes in Data Protection Laws.

## Annex A — Processing details

### Subject matter of processing

The provision of the English Hub platform — a secondary English curriculum, assessment, and analytics platform — to Activated Schools within the Controller's MAT.

### Duration of processing

For the duration of the Main Agreement, plus a 90-day post-termination retention period for export and deletion.

### Nature of processing

Operations on Personal Data for the purpose of delivering the Services, including: storage, retrieval, display, adaptation, generation of reports and analytics, and transmission between users authorised by the Controller.

### Purpose of processing

To deliver the Services to pupils and staff at Activated Schools, to support the Controller's delivery of education, and to enable the Controller to monitor, assess, and improve English provision across its trust.

### Types of Personal Data

- **Pupil data**: full name, date of birth, year group, class, school, unique pupil number (UPN) or internal pupil ID, assessment responses, reading age, writing samples submitted through the platform, engagement and usage data.
- **Staff data**: full name, school email address, role, school, login credentials (where not using SSO), activity and usage data.
- **(Optional) Parent data**: name and email address, where the Controller chooses to enable parent accounts. [VERIFY whether enabled by default.]

### Categories of Data Subjects

- Pupils enrolled at Activated Schools (typically ages 11–16, Years 7–11).
- Teaching and support staff at Activated Schools.
- Central MAT staff with administrative access to the platform.
- Parents of pupils, where the Controller enables parent access.

### Special categories of Personal Data

None are required by the Services. If the Controller chooses to enter special category data (e.g., SEND notes), such data is processed under the same security measures.

- Role-based access control
- Multi-factor authentication for all staff accounts

- All production access logged
- All administrative actions in the product logged and visible to customers

## Notes for the drafter

- **Send early.** The DPA is the most common deal-slipping document. Get it in front of the trust's DPO during pilot scoping, not during contract signing.
- **Offer a call between DPOs.** Most DPO-to-DPO conversations resolve 80% of written comments in 30 minutes.
- **Keep Annex C current.** Sub-Processor lists change. Stale annexes are a red flag for any serious DPO.
- **Do not change core clauses** (1–17) without legal review. Annex wording can be adapted per deal.
- **Translate to plain English on request.** Some MAT DPOs appreciate a plain-English summary alongside the legal text.
- **Don't over-claim.** If we don't have ISO 27001 certification, say so. DPOs will find out.
