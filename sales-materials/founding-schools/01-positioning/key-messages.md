# Key Messages — Founding Schools Programme

These are the top five talking points for every outbound conversation. Use them in emails, calls, meetings and the pitch deck. They are deliberately ordered: lead with the pain, not the product.

---

## Message 1 — The English department is drowning in marking, and no tool has fixed it

**Core line:** "Every English teacher marks thousands of sentences a year, and until now, the edtech built for English has either been ignored or resented."

**Back it up with:**
- English teachers mark around 6 extended responses per student per half term — roughly 500 pieces of extended writing per teacher per term.
- Generic quiz tools (Seneca, Quizlet) can't assess extended writing. Maths tools (Sparx, Eedi) don't translate to English.
- Teacher workload is the number one cited reason for leaving the profession.

**What to ask:** "How does your department currently mark extended writing, and what would you change about it if you could?"

---

## Message 2 — The English Hub was built by people who actually teach English

**Core line:** "We built this because we were the teachers wasting Sunday afternoons on a red pen, and nothing off the shelf was going to change that."

**Back it up with:**
- Product built by and with serving English teachers.
- Every module starts from a real English classroom brief — not a generic quiz template retrofitted for literacy.
- Teacher advisory group meets monthly. Product decisions go through them before they ship.

**What to ask:** "Would you want a five-minute walkthrough of how a real Year 9 lesson fits into this?"

---

## Message 3 — Teachers keep the pen; the tool does the drudge work

**Core line:** "We don't replace the teacher. We give the teacher back Sunday afternoon."

**Back it up with:**
- AI-assisted first-pass feedback on extended writing, reviewed and edited by the teacher in under 30 seconds per piece.
- Auto-generated reports by pupil, class and year group, aligned to GCSE mark schemes.
- Integrated with schemes of work — not a separate tool teachers have to remember.
- Teacher always has the final word. Nothing is published to students without a teacher click.

**What to ask:** "If you could take 2 hours a week back from marking, what would that mean for your department?"

---

## Message 4 — Measurable outcomes, visible to leadership

**Core line:** "We give Heads of English a one-page dashboard they can walk into SLT with on Monday morning."

**Back it up with:**
- Progress reports that map directly to GCSE grade boundaries and KS3 progression steps.
- Cohort comparison over time, split by class and by teacher.
- Early-warning flags for pupils sliding below target.
- Exportable for SLT reports and parents' evenings.

**What to ask:** "How does your SLT currently see progress in English between mocks?"

---

## Message 5 — Founding Schools get a deal no future school will see

**Core line:** "Our first ten partner schools get 50 percent off Year 1, a direct line to the founder, and their feedback goes to the top of the roadmap — because we're building this with them."

**Back it up with:**
- Strictly 10 schools only. First come, first served.
- Direct Slack or WhatsApp channel with the founding team.
- Priority feature requests — up to three guaranteed per year.
- Co-marketing: case studies, logo, joint webinars, MAT network intros.
- Year 2 renewal capped at 10 percent uplift for the life of the reference relationship.

**What to ask:** "We've got [X] of the ten slots left — would you like to hold one while we run a free 4-week pilot?"

---

## Golden rules when using these messages

1. **Never start with the product.** Start with the pain. Message 1 always comes first.
2. **Never pitch without asking a question.** Each message ends with a question for a reason.
3. **Pace yourself.** In a 30-minute call, you should get through messages 1–3 before the demo and 4–5 after.
4. **Name the slot count.** "We're on 3 of 10 Founding Schools" is more powerful than "there are limited places". Use the real number.
5. **Don't mention the 50 percent discount before you've talked about the value.** Discount first = cheap product. Value first = partnership.
