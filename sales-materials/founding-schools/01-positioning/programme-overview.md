# Founding Schools Programme — Overview

## What it is

The Founding Schools Programme is a strictly limited offer for the first ten UK secondary schools to adopt The English Hub. In exchange for being a visible reference customer during our earliest growth phase, these schools receive a materially better commercial deal, a direct relationship with the founding team, and a formal seat at the product roadmap table.

We are not doing a free pilot, a discount, or a trial in isolation — this is a proper partnership for the first year of use.

## Who it's for

- Heads of English in UK secondary schools who are actively dissatisfied with their current homework, revision or literacy tool.
- Curriculum leads in Multi-Academy Trusts (MATs) looking to standardise English provision across several schools.
- Senior leaders who want measurable, reportable English outcomes without adding teacher workload.
- Schools that are comfortable being an early adopter — not bleeding edge, but willing to give feedback in exchange for influence.

If a school wants a fully polished vendor with thousands of customers, they are not the right Founding School. They should wait twelve months and buy at full price.

## What Founding Schools receive

1. **50 percent off the first year.** A direct discount on the standard school licence fee, applied to the first 12 months. No extra conditions. Invoiced annually.
2. **Direct line to the founder.** A private Slack or WhatsApp channel with the founder. Replies within one working day, usually within hours. Monthly 30-minute product call.
3. **Priority on feature requests.** Founding Schools' feedback goes to the top of the roadmap. Up to three features per year are guaranteed to ship in response to Founding School requests (subject to scope review).
4. **Co-marketing opportunities.** A jointly written case study, a logo on our website, a shared LinkedIn post, an optional founder-led webinar for the school's MAT or network, and (for schools that want it) press and media introductions.
5. **Locked-in renewal pricing.** The Year 2 renewal is guaranteed at no more than a 10 percent uplift on the standard rate, as long as the school is willing to remain a reference customer.
6. **Founding School badge.** A visible badge for the school's website and comms saying "Founding Partner School — The English Hub".
7. **First access to new modules.** When we ship new subject modules or AI features, Founding Schools get them at least four weeks before general release.

## What Founding Schools commit to in return

1. Be a **named reference customer** — logo on our website, and agreement to take up to three reference calls per year from prospective schools.
2. Provide **feedback** — monthly product call with the founder, and at least one quarterly written feedback note.
3. Provide **data for a case study** — results from the first 8–12 weeks of use, so we can publish a joint case study (the school can review and approve all public copy).
4. Be open to a **site visit or photo** at some point during the first year for marketing content.

None of these are contractually punitive. They are good-faith commitments captured in the Reference Commitment Letter.

- **Payment terms:** Annual invoice, 30 days net, purchase order accepted.

## Why only 10?

Three reasons:

1. **Quality of attention.** Ten is the number the founding team can personally support without sacrificing product development or response time. More than ten and the "direct line to the founder" promise becomes false advertising.
2. **Scarcity is honest.** It genuinely is a limited offer. Once ten schools have signed, the programme closes and subsequent schools pay full price.
3. **Focused product feedback.** Ten engaged schools give richer feedback than thirty distracted ones. This is how we build the right product for Years 2 and 3.

## What happens after ten schools sign

- The programme closes. New schools pay the standard rate.
- Founding Schools keep their Year 1 discount and the Year 2 renewal cap as long as they continue to act as a reference.
- Case studies, logos and quotes from Founding Schools become the cornerstone of public marketing.
- The founder transitions from full-time sales to a mix of sales, product and hiring a first commercial team member.

## How to position the programme

When pitching to a school, lead with what they get, not what we get. The framing order should always be:

1. "We only work with ten schools in the first year."
2. "Here's what you receive."
3. "Here's what we ask for in return."
4. "Here's the pilot that lets you try it without committing."

Avoid any language that sounds like a discount or a giveaway. This is a partnership, not a promo.
