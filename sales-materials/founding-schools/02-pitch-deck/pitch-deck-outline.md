# Pitch Deck Outline — 12 Slides

This outline defines the content, visuals, and talking points for the Founding Schools pitch deck. Build this deck in Keynote, Google Slides, or Pitch. Keep each slide readable from the back of a classroom. No slide has more than 25 words of copy.

**Target length when presented:** 20 minutes for the deck, 10 minutes for the demo, 15 minutes for Q&A and next steps. Total meeting: 45 minutes plus 15 minute buffer.

---

## Slide 1 — Title

**Copy on slide:**
- "The English Hub"
- "A partner for English departments that want to mark less and teach more."
- "Founding Schools Programme — [DATE]"
- "For: [SCHOOL NAME]"

**Visual:** Clean title slide. School crest if you have permission (always ask).

**Speaker notes:**
- Name-check the school and the lead attendee. Thank them for their time.
- Set expectation: "I'll take 20 minutes to walk you through what we do and why, 10 minutes to show it, and then I'd love to hear your reality."

---

## Slide 2 — Problem

**Copy on slide:**
- "English teachers mark around 500 pieces of extended writing per term."
- "Nothing in the edtech stack takes that weight off."
- "It's the number one workload pain in UK English departments."

**Visual:** One large stat: "500+ pieces / term / teacher". Small footnote citing internal research or NFER / DfE workload data.

**Speaker notes:**
- Start with empathy, not product. "Every Head of English I speak to says the same thing."
- Quote a real teacher line if you have one on file — anonymised.
- Aim: get the audience nodding. If they're not nodding, stop and ask.

---

## Slide 3 — Why nothing else works

**Copy on slide:**
- "Seneca doesn't mark writing."
- "Sparx Reader does reading, not writing."
- "Educake is built for science."
- "Quizlet is flashcards."

**Visual:** A simple 4-logo grid with red dashes next to each, and a single green tick next to "The English Hub". Keep branding polite — no mocking.

**Speaker notes:**
- Be fair. Each of these is a good tool. They just don't solve the extended writing problem.
- "If your school uses any of these, keep them. We sit alongside, not instead."

---

## Slide 4 — Solution

**Copy on slide:**
- "The English Hub"
- "AI-assisted marking. Teacher-reviewed. English-specific. Mark scheme-aligned."

**Visual:** A single product screenshot of the teacher review screen — pupil response on the left, AI-generated draft feedback on the right, teacher edit bar at the top.

**Speaker notes:**
- Name the four pillars: extended writing, English-specific content, teacher in the loop, HoD dashboard.
- Don't explain how it works yet. That's the demo. Here just plant the flag.

---

## Slide 5 — Product demo (transition slide)

**Copy on slide:**
- "Let me show you."
- "Demo — 10 minutes"

**Visual:** Full-bleed product screenshot or a looping 10-second GIF of the teacher dashboard.

**Speaker notes:**
- This is the transition to the live demo. See `demo-script.md`.
- If screen sharing fails, fall back to a short pre-recorded video (keep one on a USB stick).

---

## Slide 6 — Outcomes and data

**Copy on slide:**
- "Teachers report 2–4 hours per week saved."
- "Pupils receive feedback within 24 hours, every time."
- "Pilot schools see grade uplift in progress points within a half term."

**Visual:** Three stat tiles, big numbers only. Include a disclaimer line in 10pt: "Based on early pilot data, [X] schools, [Y] pupils, [DATE]."

**Speaker notes:**
- Be honest about the sample size. Founding Schools are sophisticated — they spot puffery.
- If you don't have a stat yet, state the outcome framework instead: "Here's how we will measure it in your school."

---

## Slide 7 — What it looks like for the Head of English

**Copy on slide:**
- "One screen. Every class. Every pupil. Every skill."

**Visual:** Screenshot of the HoD dashboard with cohort filters visible.

**Speaker notes:**
- This is the slide that wins over the HoD personally, not just the department.
- Focus on how it makes their Monday morning easier, not on the technology.

---

## Slide 8 — Pricing

**Copy on slide:**
- Standard licence: `[PRICING]` per pupil per year (see pricing sheet for current figure).
- Free 4-week pilot.
- Invoiced annually, 30 days net.
- No setup fees. No per-teacher fees.

**Visual:** Simple, uncluttered table.

**Speaker notes:**
- Pricing early. Don't bury it. Hiding the price breeds mistrust.
- Mention pilot availability before anyone asks.

---

## Slide 9 — Founding Schools Programme

**Copy on slide:**
- "10 schools only."
- "50% off Year 1."
- "Direct line to the founder."
- "Priority on features."
- "Co-marketing."

**Visual:** A single large circle showing "[X] of 10 slots remaining" with the current live number.

**Speaker notes:**
- Slow down here. This is the most important slide.
- Read each bullet out loud.
- Name the exact slot count. Never fudge it.

---

## Slide 10 — Testimonials

**Copy on slide:**
- 2 or 3 short quotes from pilot teachers. Each under 20 words. Named or anonymised depending on consent.
- Logos from any schools that have given permission.

**Visual:** Quote cards with a small headshot and a school crest (only if permission).

**Speaker notes:**
- If you have no quotes yet, replace this slide with a "This is what we're building together" slide and skip the logos.
- Never fake or exaggerate a quote.

---

## Slide 11 — The ask

**Copy on slide:**
- "Would you let us run a free 4-week pilot with one KS3 or KS4 class?"
- "Two dates to decide. One to start."

**Visual:** A simple 3-step illustration: Pilot → Review → Sign.

**Speaker notes:**
- Be explicit. Schools are busy; don't leave the ask ambiguous.
- Propose two specific dates for the pilot kickoff before you leave the room.

---

## Slide 12 — Next steps and contact

**Copy on slide:**
- "[FOUNDER NAME]"
- "Founder, The English Hub"
- "[EMAIL] · [PHONE] · [CALENDAR LINK]"
- "Book a 30-minute follow-up at the calendar link above."

**Visual:** A small photo of the founder, a QR code to the calendar link, and the website URL.

**Speaker notes:**
- End on confidence. "I'd love [SCHOOL NAME] to be one of our ten."
- Thank everyone by name if you can.
- Offer to stay for 10 minutes of informal questions after the formal meeting ends.

---

## Design notes

- **Typography:** Two fonts max. A serif display font and a clean sans-serif for body.
- **Colour palette:** Two brand colours, one accent. White space is your friend.
- **No clip art.** No stock photos of multi-ethnic children laughing at laptops. Real product screenshots or real classroom photos with full permission only.
- **Accessibility:** Minimum 24pt body text, WCAG AA contrast, no colour-only meaning.
- **Save two copies:** one with animations, one static PDF to leave behind.
