# Demo Script — 15 Minutes (target 10 in a pitch, 15 in a standalone demo)

This is the step-by-step script for showing The English Hub to a school. It assumes a live product demo from a clean demo account. If the live connection fails, fall back to a pre-recorded walkthrough video saved locally.

**Guiding principle:** the demo exists to show the job being done, not the product being impressive. Every click should answer the unspoken question: *"What does this save me?"*

---

## Before the meeting

- Log into the demo environment on a clean browser profile.
- Load sample data: 1 Year 9 class, 30 pupils, 2 teachers, 1 scheme of work.
- Pre-load the teacher review screen with 5 extended responses already submitted.
- Have the pupil view open in a second tab.
- Have the HoD dashboard open in a third tab.
- Silence notifications. Close all other tabs.
- Test screen sharing 10 minutes before the call. Check your mic and camera.
- Have a local backup video ready in case the product is down.

---

## Step 1 — Set the scene (30 seconds)

**What you say:** "What I'm going to show you is a fictional Year 9 class using the platform for a half-term. Everything you see on screen is realistic — real tasks, real kinds of pupil responses, real teacher actions. I'll stop whenever you want and we can go deeper on anything."

**What to click:** Open the Teacher Home screen.

**What to show:** The week view showing recent tasks, recent pupil submissions, and an "action needed" counter.

---

## Step 2 — The weekly to-do (60 seconds)

**What you say:** "This is how a teacher starts their Monday. One glance tells you: 24 new pupil submissions waiting for review, 3 pupils who missed the deadline, 2 flagged pupils who are slipping below target. Everything I need to do in my English teaching life this week is on one screen."

**What to click:** Hover over each of the three panels.

**What to show:** The 24-item review queue highlighted in the sidebar.

**Question to ask:** "How does your team currently get this kind of Monday-morning overview?"

---

## Step 3 — The teacher review screen — the killer screen (3 minutes)

**What you say:** "This is the screen that does the heavy lifting. On the left is the pupil's response — here's a Year 9 paragraph on how Shakespeare presents ambition in Macbeth. On the right is the AI-generated draft feedback. And critically, here's the teacher edit bar at the top."

**What to click:**
1. Click through to the first submission in the queue.
2. Read the pupil's paragraph aloud.
3. Point at the AI-generated feedback.
4. Click the edit button and amend one sentence.
5. Click "Approve and release".

**What to show:**
- The mark scheme criteria the AI is assessing against (analysis, evidence, vocabulary, structure).
- The specific phrase in the pupil's response the AI is praising or querying, highlighted in the text.
- The one-click approve button.
- The timestamp: "Released to pupil — 2 seconds ago."

**Key line to say:** "Notice the teacher is still in control. Nothing the AI has said here goes near a pupil until I've clicked approve. And if I want to throw out what it's saying entirely, I can rewrite the whole thing — it's just a starting point, not a finished answer."

**Question to ask:** "If the first 60 percent of the marking was already drafted when you sat down on a Sunday, what would that change for you?"

---

## Step 4 — Burn through a stack (90 seconds)

**What you say:** "Let me show you what this looks like at pace."

**What to click:** Return to the queue and review three more submissions in quick succession. For two of them, click approve. For the third, make a small edit and then approve.

**What to show:** The counter going from 24 to 20.

**Key line:** "I've just marked four pieces of extended writing in under two minutes. Not because I skipped the thinking — because the drudge work of writing the same comment thirty times is already done. My brain is free to make the real pedagogical decisions."

---

## Step 5 — The pupil view (90 seconds)

**What you say:** "Let me show you what the pupil sees. This matters because Founding Schools care about how the feedback lands, not just how fast it goes out."

**What to click:** Switch to the second browser tab. Open the pupil view for the submission you just approved.

**What to show:**
- The pupil sees the feedback within seconds.
- The specific praise and query are anchored in their own writing — not a generic comment.
- The pupil has a "redraft" button that takes them to a new version of the task, with the feedback visible alongside.
- A clean, phone-friendly UI.

**Question to ask:** "What do your pupils currently do with feedback? Do they act on it, or does it get filed?"

---

## Step 6 — Set a task (90 seconds)

**What you say:** "Setting work is where schools usually lose time with new tools. Here's how fast this is."

**What to click:**
1. Click "New task".
2. Choose a topic from the pre-built scheme of work library.
3. Pick a class.
4. Hit "Assign".

**What to show:** The entire flow takes under 30 seconds. A confirmation shows that all 30 pupils have been notified.

**Key line:** "No printing. No handing out worksheets. No chasing the kid who lost the sheet."

---

## Step 7 — The Head of English dashboard (2 minutes)

**What you say:** "Now let me put my Head of Department hat on. This is the view that's meant for you, rather than for a class teacher."

**What to click:** Switch to the third tab.

**What to show:**
- Cohort view across all Year 9 English classes.
- Progress against target grades by pupil.
- Skills breakdown — analysis, inference, vocabulary — showing where the whole year group is strong and weak.
- Teacher-by-teacher comparison (shown discreetly — this is sensitive).
- The "export to PDF" button for SLT reports.

**Key line:** "This is the view you take into your SLT meeting on a Monday morning, or into your learning walks, or into a conversation with a trainee teacher who's struggling."

**Question to ask:** "What would your SLT do differently if they could see this every week?"

---

## Step 8 — Data protection and admin (60 seconds)

**What you say:** "Before questions — two quick things the Head or your data lead will ask about."

**What to show:**
- Admin settings — clear list of who in the school has access.
- Data processing agreement link (downloadable).
- UK data hosting location badge.
- Single sign-on options (Google, Microsoft).

**Key line:** "We are GDPR compliant, we have a DPA ready to sign, our data is hosted in the UK, and we integrate with Google and Microsoft single sign-on. No new passwords for anyone."

---

## Step 9 — Close the demo and invite questions (60 seconds)

**What you say:** "That's the core of it. There's plenty more I could show you — homework reports, parent-facing summaries, the KS3 library — but I'd rather stop here and hear what you want to ask. What's jumped out as the most interesting, or the most uncomfortable?"

**Common question: "How long did that demo take to set up?"**
Answer: "Twenty minutes. That's the onboarding experience we promise every pilot school."

**Common question: "What's the AI model?"**
Answer: "We use a combination of models from two providers, under a contract that means your pupil data is never used to train anything. I'm happy to put that in writing."

**Common question: "What if the AI gets it wrong?"**
Answer: "That's exactly why the teacher review step exists. The AI is a first draft, not a verdict."

---

## Demo anti-patterns — never do these

- **Don't show every feature.** Pick the three screens that matter: teacher review, pupil view, HoD dashboard. Everything else is deflection.
- **Don't show settings and admin first.** Start with the classroom. Save plumbing for the end.
- **Don't show fake data that looks fake.** Use realistic pupil names, realistic writing, realistic mark scheme criteria.
- **Don't apologise for the product.** If something is missing, be matter-of-fact: "Coming in April — want me to flag you when it ships?"
- **Don't pitch during the demo.** The demo shows. The pitch tells. Keep them separate.
