# Cold Email Sequences

Two sequences: one for Heads of English (the classroom decision-maker), one for MAT curriculum leads (the multi-school decision-maker). Each is a five-step sequence over 18 days. Send only one sequence to any given contact.

**Golden rules:**
- Never send more than five emails. No "just bumping this" forever.
- Personalise the first line of every email, even if it means 30 minutes of research per school.
- Use the founder's own email address, not a sales@ alias.
- Sign off as "[FOUNDER NAME], Founder" — never "Sales Team".
- All emails plain text. No banners, no big signatures, no tracking pixels visible to the recipient.

## How to personalise the first line of every email

The subject line and opening line should reference something specific to that school or MAT. Sources:

- The school website — latest news, a recent Ofsted report, a public English department page, an exam results press release.
- LinkedIn — a recent post by the HoD or a senior leader.
- The school's Twitter / X account — a recent English-related tweet.
- Nicola Morgan / Chris Curtis / Jennifer Webb — any reference to a known English teaching thought leader the school is connected to.
- GCSE results — compare to LA or national average; use as a flattering hook (never a negative one).

A good personalised opening is 1–2 sentences, references one specific thing, and does not feel like a mail merge. If you can't find anything specific in 3 minutes of research, move on to another school — that one isn't ready for personalised outbound.

## When to switch sequences

- If an HoD replies and mentions their MAT, offer to bring the MAT curriculum lead into the next conversation.
- If a MAT lead replies and delegates to a specific school, switch to Sequence A for that school's HoD.
- Never run both sequences against the same contact.

## Metrics to track per sequence

Track in `07-tracking/activity-log-template.csv`:

- Opens (if using a tracker that respects privacy)
- Replies (any reply, positive or negative)
- Meetings booked
- Meetings held
- Pilots agreed
- Founding Schools signed

Minimum benchmark: 20 percent reply rate on Email 1, 5 percent meeting rate across the full sequence, 1 Founding School per 30 well-personalised sequences.
