# 60-Second Phone Script — Cold Call

Calling a school from cold still works, particularly for secondary school English departments where inboxes are clogged and LinkedIn is used less. The school office is the gatekeeper. This script assumes you are calling the main school phone number and trying to get through to the Head of English — or, failing that, leaving a useful voicemail.

**Time target:** 60 seconds for the initial pitch once you have the right person on the line. The gatekeeper conversation is separate.

**Golden rules:**
- Stand up when you call. Your voice sounds different.
- Smile. It travels down the line.
- Never sound like a salesperson. You are a former teacher calling an English department. That's true — use it.
- Do not call during break, lunch, or in the first 15 minutes after end-of-day. Best times: 08:15–09:00, 10:45–11:00 (break handover), 15:30–16:15.
- Never call on a Monday morning. English departments are firefighting.
- Do call on Tuesday, Wednesday and Thursday afternoons. These are the golden hours.

---

## Step 1 — The gatekeeper (school office / reception)

**What the receptionist will say:** "Good morning, [SCHOOL NAME], how can I help you?"

**What you say:**

> "Morning, could I speak to the Head of English please? My name's [FOUNDER NAME], I'm a former English teacher, and I've written to [HEAD OF ENGLISH NAME] this week about a pilot we're running for English departments. Is [HEAD OF ENGLISH NAME] free?"

**Key moves:**
- Name the person you want. Never ask "who's in charge of English".
- Mention you've written already. It makes this feel like a warm call, not a cold one.
- Say "a pilot", not "a sales call" or "a product".
- If asked "what's this about?", answer short: "It's about a marking tool for the English department. Twenty seconds is enough to describe it if you'd like."

**If the receptionist asks you to email instead:**

> "I have already — happy to forward it again if that helps. I'd prefer to catch [HEAD OF ENGLISH NAME] directly because this really only makes sense when they see it, and I don't want another email sitting in their inbox. Any chance of 30 seconds on the line?"

**If the receptionist says the HoD is teaching:**

> "Totally understand. What's the best time to try back? I'm calling from a mobile — if it's easier, I can leave my number and ask [HEAD OF ENGLISH NAME] to call me at their convenience."

**If the receptionist refuses to put you through:**

> "No problem at all. Could I leave my name and number so [HEAD OF ENGLISH NAME] knows I called? [FOUNDER NAME], from The English Hub. The number is [PHONE]. Thanks so much for your help."

Then: send an email within 10 minutes referencing the call.

---

## Step 2 — The 60-second pitch (when you get the HoD on the line)

**What you say:**

> "Hi [FIRST NAME], thanks so much for picking up. My name's [FOUNDER NAME], I'm a former English teacher and I run The English Hub. I'll be straight with you — this is a cold call, but I've done my homework and I genuinely think it might be useful.
>
> Very short pitch. We've built a tool that takes a hard run at the extended writing marking problem. AI drafts feedback against the mark schemes, teachers review and edit before anything goes to the pupil. Heads of English I've shown it to are reporting two to four hours a week back on their marking load.
>
> We're picking ten schools to work with closely this year — they get 50 percent off, a direct line to me, and their feature requests shape the roadmap.
>
> What I'd love is 20 minutes to show it to you. I'm not asking you to buy anything. I'm asking you to tell me whether this solves a problem you actually have. Could I book 20 minutes with you in the next fortnight?"

**That's the pitch. 58 seconds when read aloud at a calm pace.**

---

## Step 3 — Handling the three most common responses

### Response: "Send me an email."

> "Happy to do that — I'll send it today. Before I do, can I just ask one question so the email is tailored? What's the biggest workload pain in your department this term?"

[Listen. Make notes. Then send an email within the hour that references their answer.]

### Response: "We're not looking for anything new right now."

> "Completely understand. Can I ask what you're using currently? I'm trying to build a picture of what English departments are rating highly — and if there's something we can learn from what's working for you, I'd love to hear it."

[This is not a trick. Listen properly and learn. Then: "Fine if I come back in [three months / next term / after mocks] to see if anything's shifted?" Most people say yes.]

### Response: "Isn't this just ChatGPT?"

> "Fair question. The short answer is no — a generic chatbot can't reliably mark against a GCSE English mark scheme, and more importantly it has no teacher-in-the-loop workflow, no department dashboard and no pupil view. The boring answer: we use AI models, but the actual product is the teacher workflow wrapped around them. Much easier to show than describe — could I have 20 minutes to walk you through it?"

---

## Step 4 — Booking the meeting on the call

The moment they say yes to a meeting — even a tentative yes — book it before you hang up.

> "Brilliant. I'm looking at my calendar right now. How does Thursday at 3:45pm or next Tuesday at 8am suit you? I'll send the invite before this call ends."

**Never** say: "I'll send you my calendar link and you can grab a slot." You will lose 40 percent of those conversions. Book the meeting on the call.

---

## Step 5 — If they push you to email

Accept graciously. Send the email within 10 minutes, reference the call specifically, and include two specific proposed meeting times.

**Subject:** Following up on our call — The English Hub

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> Thanks so much for taking my call just now. As promised, a short note.
>
> The English Hub is a platform built for English departments dealing with the extended writing marking workload. AI-drafted feedback, teacher reviewed, aligned to GCSE and KS3 mark schemes, with a HoD dashboard that gives you a cohort view in one screen.
>
> We have 10 Founding Schools slots this year. They get 50 percent off Year 1, a direct line to me, and priority on feature requests.
>
> Could we do 20 minutes next week? Either Thursday [DATE] at 3:45pm or Tuesday [DATE] at 8am work for me. If neither suits, here's my calendar: [CALENDAR LINK].
>
> [FOUNDER NAME]
> Founder, The English Hub
> [PHONE]

## Voicemail script (45 seconds max)

If you reach voicemail:

> "Hi [FIRST NAME], it's [FOUNDER NAME] from The English Hub. I'm a former English teacher and we've built a platform specifically for the extended writing marking problem in English departments.
>
> I'm calling because we have 10 Founding Schools slots open this year at 50 percent off, and [SCHOOL NAME] seemed like a strong candidate based on [ONE SPECIFIC THING FROM YOUR RESEARCH].
>
> I'll send an email straight after this message. If it's easier, call me back any time on [PHONE]. Thanks so much."

Then send the email immediately, within 10 minutes. Reference the voicemail.

Log every call in `07-tracking/activity-log-template.csv`. Include:

- Date and time of call

- Notes from the conversation
- Follow-up action and date

Never rely on memory. Always log the call within 15 minutes of hanging up.

## When to stop calling a school

- After 3 failed gatekeeper attempts on different days, switch to email/LinkedIn only.
- After an explicit "no thanks" from the HoD, wait 3 months before trying again.
- After a "come back next term", honour the date exactly — then call again once.
