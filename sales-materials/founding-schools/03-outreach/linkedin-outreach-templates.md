# LinkedIn Outreach Templates

LinkedIn is the most useful parallel channel to email for UK school sales. Heads of English and MAT curriculum leads are often more responsive on LinkedIn than on email because their work inbox is buried. Messages should be shorter than emails and more personal in tone.

**Golden rules:**
- Always send a personalised connection request. Never blank.
- Never pitch in the connection request itself. Pitch after they accept.
- Keep every message under 300 characters where possible.
- Never use LinkedIn Sales Navigator InMail templates. Always write from scratch.
- Do not mass-export contacts to a CRM scraper. Connect and message one person at a time.

---

## Template 1 — Connection request to a Head of English

Short version (under 300 chars):

> Hi [FIRST NAME] — came across your profile looking for English leads who think deeply about KS3. I'm a former English teacher, now building The English Hub (we help with extended writing marking). Not pitching — would love to connect and learn from your work.

Long version (if you have more context to drop):

> Hi [FIRST NAME] — I used to run an English department too, and I built a tool specifically for the writing feedback problem. Saw your [RECENT POST / SCHOOL PAGE / SPEAKING GIG] and really appreciated the take on [SPECIFIC THING]. Would love to connect — nothing to pitch, just keen to learn from you.

## Template 3 — First message after a connection is accepted (Head of English)

Send this 24–48 hours after the connection is accepted. Not immediately — immediate pitches kill LinkedIn relationships.

> Thanks for connecting, [FIRST NAME].
>
> Very quick context — I used to be an English HoD and I've built The English Hub, which takes a serious run at the extended writing marking problem (AI first-draft feedback, teacher reviewed, mark scheme aligned).
>
> We're working with 10 founding schools in the first year. They get 50% off, a direct line to me, and their feedback shapes the roadmap.
>
> Would it be useful to show you a 20-minute demo? No obligation at all — it's more useful to me if you tell me what's wrong with it than if you buy it.
>
> Here's my calendar if it's easier: [CALENDAR LINK]

## Template 7 — After a meeting, connecting with other stakeholders at the same school

When the HoD mentions a literacy lead, a deputy head, or a head of KS3 during the pitch meeting, connect with that person within 24 hours.

> Hi [FIRST NAME] — [HEAD OF ENGLISH]'s colleague, I believe. We met today and they mentioned you'd probably want to see The English Hub too. Sending this so we're connected for the follow-ups — feel free to ignore until we cross paths properly.

## Template 8 — Asking for a referral inside the same MAT

If a school signs, ask them to refer you to another school in their MAT.

> Hi [FIRST NAME] — congratulations again on the pilot signing today. Completely understand if this is a no — but if there's a Head of English at one of the other [MAT NAME] schools who you think would find this useful, I'd be enormously grateful for an intro. Happy to draft the email for you so it's a 30-second job.

## When to post your own content on LinkedIn

LinkedIn is not just a DM channel. Your feed matters more than any template.

Post once a week about one of these themes:

1. **Teacher workload** — a stat, a story, a frustration. No product.
2. **A specific English teaching insight** — something you learned from a Founding School that week.
3. **A behind-the-scenes product update** — new feature, teacher feedback on it.
4. **A Founding School case study** (when you have one, with permission).
5. **A hiring moment** or a milestone ("we just hit our fifth Founding School").

Do not post:
- Generic inspirational quotes.
- Screenshots of other people's LinkedIn posts.
- Product screenshots without narrative.
- Anything that could be interpreted as negative about another school or teacher.

When posts perform well, look at who reacted — those are your warmest cold contacts.

---

## Response time expectations

- Reply to any message you receive within 4 working hours.
- Meeting requests within 1 working hour.
- Never leave a LinkedIn thread open for more than 48 hours without a reply.
- If you're going to be travelling or off-grid, set an out-of-office.

## What not to do on LinkedIn

- Do not send the same message to two contacts in the same school.
- Do not use automation tools to send connection requests or messages. Ever.
- Do not scrape LinkedIn into a CSV for outbound emails.
- Do not message people outside the UK school sector during the Founding Schools phase. Stay focused.
