# Referral Request Templates

Warm introductions convert at something like 10x the rate of cold outreach. Every existing contact — teachers, former colleagues, pilot schools, Founding Schools, investors, advisors — is a potential source of intros to other schools. This file contains templates for asking for them without being awkward.

**Principles:**
- Make it easy. Draft the intro email for them. Give them a one-sentence response option.
- Be specific. "Do you know any English HoDs?" is bad. "Do you know the Head of English at [SCHOOL]?" is good.
- Never ask for more than three intros in a single request. More feels like a favour bank withdrawal.
- Always thank the referrer, whether or not the intro leads anywhere.

---

## Template 1 — Asking a former teaching colleague

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> Very quick ask. As you know I've been building The English Hub for the last [X] months, and we're now actively looking for our first ten Founding Schools — English departments willing to pilot and be a reference site. They get 50 percent off Year 1 and a direct line to me.
>
> Any chance you could make an intro to the Head of English at:
> 1. [SCHOOL NAME 1]
> 2. [SCHOOL NAME 2]
> 3. [SCHOOL NAME 3]
>
> Or, if those aren't right, just one English HoD from your old network who you think would find this interesting?
>
> I've written a short draft intro email you could forward — pasted below. Literally a two-minute job for you. I'll take it from there and won't mention you were the one who sent it if you'd rather I didn't.
>
> No pressure at all if it's awkward — and thanks in advance either way.
>
> [FOUNDER NAME]
>
> ---
>
> **Draft intro email for you to forward:**
>
> Hi [RECIPIENT NAME],
>
> I wanted to put you in touch with [FOUNDER NAME], who's building something I think is worth a look. [FOUNDER NAME] is a former English teacher and has built a platform called The English Hub specifically for the extended writing marking workload — AI drafts feedback against the mark scheme, teachers review and approve before anything goes to the pupil, and there's a HoD dashboard I'd have killed for.
>
> They're currently picking ten founding schools to work with at a discount in exchange for being a reference. I thought of you because [ONE SENTENCE OF WHY THIS PERSON].
>
> Copying [FOUNDER NAME] in — I'll let you both take it from here.
>
> [YOUR NAME]

## Template 2 — Asking a Founding School to refer another school

This is the single highest-value referral type. Use it within 2 weeks of a school signing — after the enthusiasm has peaked and before the novelty fades.

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> First things first — thanks again for signing on as one of our ten Founding Schools. Week one has already thrown up two really useful pieces of feedback (more on those in the Slack channel).
>
> A quick ask, and please tell me to go away if it's awkward.
>
> Do you know a Head of English at another school — ideally someone whose opinion you respect — who would benefit from what we're doing? I'm still holding [X] of the ten Founding Schools slots open, and a warm intro from you to one of your peers would be more valuable to us than any amount of cold email.
>
> If it helps, here are three schools I'm already aware of in your area / network that I'd love to talk to:
>
> 1. [SCHOOL 1]
> 2. [SCHOOL 2]
> 3. [SCHOOL 3]
>
> If you'd rather suggest someone else, I'll take any lead you're willing to give.
>
> I've drafted a short intro email you could forward — pasted below. Or I'm very happy for you to phrase it however you prefer.
>
> Thanks so much — and whatever the answer, no hard feelings at all.
>
> [FOUNDER NAME]
>
> ---
>
> **Draft intro email:**
>
> Hi [RECIPIENT NAME],
>
> Quick note — we've just started a pilot with The English Hub at [SCHOOL NAME]. It's a tool specifically for English departments — AI-drafted feedback on extended writing, teacher reviewed, mark scheme aligned. Early days, but the Sunday afternoon marking pain is already meaningfully lower.
>
> They're still taking on Founding Schools at a 50% Year 1 discount. Thought of you because [ONE LINE].
>
> Putting you in touch with [FOUNDER NAME] — worth a 20 minute call.
>
> [YOUR NAME]

## Template 3 — Asking a MAT curriculum lead to refer inside the same MAT

If a single school inside a MAT signs, the MAT curriculum lead is the obvious next step.

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> As you know, [SCHOOL NAME] is just starting their pilot with us this week. Before the case study from that pilot lands — which will probably be a more credible referral — I wanted to ask whether it might be worth a short conversation with the MAT curriculum lead at [MAT NAME], or whoever looks after English standards across your schools.
>
> I'm not trying to leapfrog you — your pilot is ring-fenced and nothing changes there. But there's a version of the Founding Schools offer that makes a lot more sense when taken at MAT scale, and I'd love to put that on the table before the other slots fill up.
>
> Happy for you to make the intro, happy to do it yourself if you prefer, or happy to leave it alone if the timing's wrong. Just let me know.
>
> Thanks,
> [FOUNDER NAME]

## Template 4 — Asking an investor, advisor or board member for intros

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> Quarterly ask. As you know, I'm pushing hard on the Founding Schools Programme — ten named reference schools in the first year.
>
> Status: [X] of 10 signed, [Y] in active pilot, [Z] in advanced conversation.
>
> The bottleneck right now is not the product and not the conversion rate once we're in front of a Head of English. The bottleneck is meetings with the right person.
>
> A warm intro from you to any of the following would be hugely valuable:
>
> 1. Any Head of English or MAT curriculum lead in your network.
> 2. Any CEO or COO of a MAT between 3 and 20 schools.
> 3. Any well-known English teaching voice (author, speaker, conference organiser) who might be persuadable to try the product and write about it.
>
> Even one intro a month would materially move the programme forward.
>
> Thanks — and happy to jump on a call if it's easier to talk through it.
>
> [FOUNDER NAME]

## Template 5 — Asking a happy user to write a LinkedIn post or quote

This isn't a referral strictly, but it generates inbound. Use it with Founding Schools 4 weeks into their pilot.

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> A tiny ask that takes 5 minutes.
>
> Four weeks into your pilot, I'd love to capture one or two lines I can use publicly — a short quote on what's landing well for [SCHOOL NAME]'s English team. Happy to write a draft you can approve (or reject) if that's easier than starting from a blank page.
>
> Even more valuable if you're willing to post it yourself on LinkedIn — I know that's a bigger ask, and totally fine to say no. If you are up for it, I can draft a post you can edit, or you can write your own and I'll amplify it from my account.
>
> No pressure. Even the quickest "not this term" reply is fine.
>
> Thanks for being one of the first to take a chance on us.
>
> [FOUNDER NAME]

## Template 6 — Following up after a referral lands

Always thank the referrer, even before the new contact has replied.

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> Brilliant — message to [RECIPIENT NAME] just landed in my inbox. Thank you. I'll update you either way once we've had a conversation.
>
> [FOUNDER NAME]

And when the outcome is known:

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> Quick update on the intro you made to [RECIPIENT NAME] — we had a 30-minute call last Friday and they've agreed to a 4-week pilot starting [DATE]. Really grateful.
>
> Something I can do for you in return? Intro, feedback on something you're working on, a ticket to our next webinar — happy to help in any direction.
>
> [FOUNDER NAME]

## Tracking referrals

Every referral and every resulting outcome goes into the pipeline tracker (`07-tracking/pipeline-template.csv`) in the "Source" column. Sources to use:

- `Referral — [NAME]`
- `LinkedIn cold`
- `Email cold`
- `Phone cold`
- `Event`
- `Inbound`
- `Founding School referral — [SCHOOL NAME]`

This lets you measure which referrers are actually producing results, so you know who to thank harder and who to ask more of.
