# Mutual Action Plan (MAP) Template

A Mutual Action Plan is the single most valuable document in keeping a school deal moving. It is jointly agreed with the prospective Founding School at the end of the demo meeting and is updated weekly until signature.

**Why it matters:** Deals die in the gap between enthusiasm and signature. The MAP removes that gap by listing every step, owner, and date.

**When to introduce it:** At the end of the demo meeting, say:

> "I find deals like this go well when we both have a shared plan for what needs to happen between now and the pilot starting. I'd love to send over a short document we both edit — called a Mutual Action Plan — that lists every step, who owns it, and when it's due. It's usually about 10 items. Can I send a draft to you tomorrow?"

### Known dependencies

List here anything that could block a step from happening on time. For example:

- **Term dates:** Half term is [DATE]. No training or onboarding possible during this week.
- **SLT meeting cadence:** The next SLT meeting is [DATE]. Approval must make this meeting.
- **Data protection:** The DPO is only available Wednesdays. All DPO-related steps must route through Wednesdays.
- **Budget cycle:** The school's new financial year starts [DATE]. Year 1 invoice timing must align with this.

---

### Known risks

List here the things that might go wrong and the mitigations.

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Head teacher unavailable for approval call | Medium | Record a 5-minute video walkthrough that can be sent async |
| Data protection review runs long | Medium | Pre-share DPA before step 3 so review starts early |
| Pilot teacher off sick during pilot | Low | Identify a backup teacher in step 1 |
| Competing edtech purchase decision | Medium | Clarify in step 1 that this is the current priority |

### Agreed communication rhythm

- **Weekly check-in:** [DAY] at [TIME], 15 minutes, by [VIDEO / PHONE]
- **Written update cadence:** [FOUNDER NAME] updates the MAP every Friday by end of day
- **Emergency contact:** Direct line — [PHONE], [EMAIL]
- **Escalation:** If a step is at risk of slipping, the slipping party notifies the other within 24 hours

---

## How to maintain the MAP

1. **Update after every interaction.** Every call, email, or decision should be reflected in the MAP within 24 hours.
2. **Keep statuses current.** Use four statuses only: Not started, In progress, Done, Blocked.
3. **Never hide bad news.** If step 3 is blocked, mark it Blocked and note why. The HoD will respect transparency.
4. **Keep dates alive.** If a date slips, update it — don't leave a red date sitting in the plan.
5. **Add steps as they emerge.** The template starts with 17 steps. A real deal might end up with 25.

---

## When the MAP is complete

Once every step is Done, the school is a signed Founding School. At that point:

1. Move the MAP to a "Completed" folder.
2. Start a new Onboarding Plan in the same format.
3. Schedule the first monthly founder call.
4. Add the logo to the website.
5. Update the pipeline tracker to reflect the new Founding School count (e.g. "6 of 10 filled").
6. Thank everyone involved by name, in writing.

---

## An anti-pattern to watch for

The MAP is only useful if both sides engage with it. If the school refuses to look at it, or if updates stop coming back, that is a signal: the deal is probably drifting. Have an honest conversation:

> "I notice we haven't had an update on the MAP since [DATE]. I'm completely fine if the priority has shifted — I'd just rather know. Is this still a deal you want to run, or should we put it on the back burner until the new term?"

Nine times out of ten the answer is "sorry, been snowed under, let me get back to it". The tenth time is the honest no you needed anyway.
