# Contract Terms — Plain-English Summary

This is the plain-English version of the Founding Schools contract. It is intended for the Head of English, the business manager, or the head teacher. The real contract is a separate legal document (drafted by solicitors). This summary explains what that contract says in language that doesn't need a lawyer to understand.

**Send this before the legal contract, not instead of it.** It should be paired with the full contract document during the signing step.

---

## The two-minute summary

- You get access to The English Hub for 12 months.
- You pay 50 percent of the standard price as a Founding School.
- You agree to be a named reference customer.
- Either side can walk away under specified conditions.
- We look after your data properly.
- You keep ownership of everything your pupils and teachers create.

---

## 1. The thing you're buying

**In one sentence:** A licence to use The English Hub platform for 12 months from the start date, across the year groups listed in your order.

**Specifically included:**
- Every teacher in your English department gets an account at no extra cost.
- Every pupil in the year groups you've licensed gets an account.
- Every feature of the platform, including any features released during your 12-month term.
- UK-based customer support Monday to Friday.

**Specifically not included:**
- Bespoke development of new features (though Founding Schools do get priority on three feature requests per year).
- Custom integrations with systems outside our published SSO list.
- On-site training beyond the 90-minute onboarding session.
- Weekend support.

---

## 2. What it costs and when you pay

- **Price:** The Year 1 Founding Schools price shown in your order (50 percent of the standard rate).
- **Payment cadence:** One annual invoice, issued on the start date.
- **Payment terms:** 30 days net from invoice date.
- **Payment methods:** BACS (preferred) or purchase order. Credit card on request.
- **VAT:** Charged at the prevailing UK rate.
- **Price increases:** The Year 1 price is locked. Year 2 renewal will not exceed the standard rate plus 10 percent, provided the reference relationship continues.

---

## 3. The term and how it ends

- **Length:** 12 months from the start date.
- **Renewal:** Automatically renews for another 12 months unless either side gives 30 days' notice before the end of the current term.
- **Early termination by the school:** You can exit the contract early if we materially fail to deliver — for example, if the platform has been unavailable for more than 72 hours in any 30-day period, or if we fail to respond to a critical support ticket within 2 working days. In that case, you get a pro rata refund for the unused portion of the term.
- **Early termination by us:** We can exit the contract early only if you fail to pay the invoice within 60 days or if the school uses the platform for something outside its intended purpose.

---

## 4. Being a Founding School reference

This is the thing you give us in return for the discount. It matters.

- You agree to be listed as a reference customer on our website, with your school crest or name, for the duration of Year 1.
- You agree to take up to three reference calls per year from prospective schools. Each call is no longer than 30 minutes. You can say no to any specific request.
- You agree that we may publish a jointly-agreed case study about your use of the platform. You approve all public copy before publication.
- You agree to attend one monthly 30-minute product call with the founder.
- You agree to provide at least one quarterly written feedback note (can be a short email).

**What this does not mean:**
- You are not obliged to recommend us to anyone.
- You are not obliged to say anything positive — only honest.
- You can withdraw the reference commitment if you feel the product is not delivering, after which we revert you to the standard price for the remainder of the term.

---

## 5. Your data, our data, pupil data

- **Ownership:** You own all pupil work, teacher work, and department content you enter into the platform.
- **Use of your data:** We use your data solely to provide the service. We do not sell it. We do not use it to train AI models unless you explicitly opt in via a separate written agreement (and we currently do not ask for that).
- **Hosting:** All pupil data is hosted in the UK.
- **GDPR:** We are GDPR compliant. A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is attached to the contract and signed alongside it.
- **Breach notification:** If something goes wrong with data security, we tell you within 24 hours of becoming aware.
- **Data return on exit:** When the contract ends, you get a structured export of your data within 30 days. We then delete our copies within 60 days.
- **Sub-processors:** We use a small, named list of sub-processors (hosting, email, support tools). The list is in the DPA and we tell you 30 days in advance if we add or change one.

---

## 6. AI and how it's used on your pupils' work

Because this is the question schools ask most often, it is called out separately.

- Every piece of AI-generated feedback is surfaced to a teacher for review before reaching a pupil. Nothing goes to a pupil without a teacher's approval click.
- We do not use your pupils' work to train AI models. Your data is processed by third-party AI providers under contracts that forbid their use of the data for training purposes.
- You can request at any time a log of all AI calls associated with your school, for audit purposes.

---

## 7. Availability and support

- **Target uptime:** 99.5 percent measured monthly (excludes pre-notified scheduled maintenance).
- **Critical issue response:** Within 2 working hours during UK school hours (08:00–16:00 Monday to Friday, UK term time).
- **Non-critical issue response:** Within 1 working day.
- **Direct founder channel:** Founding Schools get a private Slack or WhatsApp channel with the founder, in addition to the standard support route.
- **Planned maintenance:** Never during school hours except in emergencies. Normally Sunday evenings 20:00–23:00 UK time.

---

## 8. Intellectual property

- **The platform:** We own the platform and the code behind it. You get a right to use it, not to copy it.
- **Your content:** You own everything you and your pupils upload or write into the platform.
- **Feedback:** If you suggest a feature and we build it, we own the feature. You still get to use it without paying extra — along with every other school that signs.
- **Case studies:** Joint ownership. You approve all text before publication.

---

## 9. Liability

- Our total liability under the contract is capped at the fees paid in the 12 months preceding the claim. This is standard for software contracts at this scale.
- We are not liable for indirect or consequential losses, except in cases of gross negligence, wilful misconduct, or a data breach caused by our failure.
- Nothing in this clause limits liability for personal injury, death, or fraud.

---

## 10. Governing law

- The contract is governed by the laws of England and Wales.
- Disputes are resolved in the courts of England and Wales.
- Before going to court, both sides agree to spend at least 30 days trying to resolve the issue in good faith conversation.

---

## 11. What's negotiable

Honestly — most of this is not negotiable. It is deliberately a light, reasonable template. The things we will discuss in advance of signing are:

- **Price:** Only if you're committing to multi-year or are part of a MAT deal.
- **Term:** A shorter term is possible but the discount will be reduced.
- **Reference terms:** If you genuinely cannot be listed publicly (some schools have policy reasons), we can discuss a private reference arrangement.
- **Data clauses:** We will happily respond to any reasonable DPO amendment request.

Things we will not negotiate:
- The fact that Founding Schools are only 10 in total.
- UK data hosting.
- Teacher-in-the-loop AI workflow.
- Our sub-processor list.

## A final note

If at any point during the first 30 days of the contract you feel the Founding Schools Programme doesn't feel right for your school, call the founder directly. We'd rather have a graceful early exit and a good relationship than keep a school that doesn't want to be here.
