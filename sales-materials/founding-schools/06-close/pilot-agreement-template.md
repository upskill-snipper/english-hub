# Pilot Agreement Template — 4-Week Free Pilot

A deliberately light-touch agreement that lets a prospective Founding School run a 4-week free pilot without procurement friction. Must be readable and signable by a Head of English without legal review in most cases. Should still be run past the data protection officer before signing.

**Format:** Send this as a simple Word doc or editable PDF. The school countersigns by replacing "[SIGNATURE PLACEHOLDER]" with a typed name or by returning a signed scan.

**Length target:** Under 2 pages. If it runs longer, something has gone wrong.

### 1. What this agreement does

This agreement sets out the terms for a free 4-week trial of The English Hub at [SCHOOL NAME]. It is intentionally short. Its purpose is to give both sides enough confidence to run a sensible trial without the friction of a full commercial contract.

This is not a commercial contract. No money changes hands under this agreement. Either side can end the pilot at any time.

### 2. What the pilot includes

- Access to The English Hub platform for one pilot class of up to 30 pupils, selected by the school.
- Accounts for up to 3 teachers (the pilot teacher plus two observers — typically the Head of English and one senior colleague).
- A 90-minute founder-led onboarding session before the pilot starts.
- A 20-minute mid-pilot check-in call.
- A 45-minute end-of-pilot review meeting.
- Email and Slack support throughout the 4 weeks.

Everything in the platform is included for the pilot — there is no feature-restricted "trial version".

### 3. What the pilot does not include

- Any obligation on the school to purchase a full licence after the pilot ends.
- Any payment of any kind.
- Use of the platform beyond the pilot class.
- Use of the platform beyond the 4-week pilot period, unless extended by mutual written agreement.
- Bespoke development work, custom integrations, or on-site training beyond the onboarding session.

### 4. Dates

- **Pilot start date:** [DATE]
- **Pilot end date:** [DATE] (4 weeks from start)
- **Mid-pilot check-in:** [DATE] (2 weeks from start)
- **End-of-pilot review meeting:** [DATE] (in the final week)
- **Pilot class teacher:** [TEACHER NAME]
- **Primary school contact:** [HEAD OF ENGLISH NAME]

### 5. Data protection

- All pupil data processed under this agreement will be handled in line with UK GDPR.
- We will act as the data processor; the school acts as the data controller.
- All pupil data is hosted in the UK.
- A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is attached to this agreement as Appendix A. The school's data protection officer is invited to review it before the pilot begins.
- Pupil data will not be used to train any AI models.
- All pupil data will be permanently deleted within 30 days of the pilot ending, unless the school proceeds to a full licence (in which case the data is retained under the terms of the main contract).
- We will notify the school within 24 hours of any data breach we become aware of.

### 6. Consent

- Teachers using the platform must be authorised by the school.
- Pupils must use the platform within the school's existing AUP (Acceptable Use Policy) framework. The school is responsible for parent notification where required.
- The school warrants that it has the right to provide pupil names and work to us for the purpose of the pilot.

### 7. Ending the pilot

Either side can end the pilot at any time, for any reason, with no notice and no penalty. To end the pilot, email the other party's primary contact.

If the school ends the pilot:
- All accounts are deactivated within 24 hours of notice.
- All pupil data is deleted within 30 days (as above).
- No further action is required from the school.

If we end the pilot (very unlikely, but possible in the case of a serious misuse or security incident):
- We will explain why in writing.
- We will give the school a pro rata extension of the pilot at no cost, if the school wishes to restart.

### 8. Confidentiality

- Both sides agree to keep confidential any non-public information shared during the pilot.
- We may publicly reference that the school is running a pilot only with the school's explicit written consent.
- The school may publicly reference the pilot without further approval.

### 9. No promise of continuation

Nothing in this agreement obliges the school to sign a Founding Schools contract, a standard licence, or any further commercial arrangement. The pilot is a free trial in the truest sense.

Nothing in this agreement obliges us to hold a Founding Schools slot for the school if the pilot review is not completed within 4 weeks of the pilot end date.

### 10. Liability

- Our liability under this pilot agreement is capped at £1,000 in total. This reflects the fact that no money is changing hands.
- Nothing limits liability for personal injury, death, or fraud.
- The platform is provided "as is" during the pilot. We aim for the same uptime and support standards as the full product, but we do not offer formal SLA terms during the pilot.

### 11. Governing law

- This agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales.
- Any disputes will be resolved in the courts of England and Wales.

### Appendix A — Data Processing Agreement

Attached as a separate document. A signature on this pilot agreement is taken as acknowledgement that the DPA has been shared. The DPA itself may be signed separately by the school's DPO.

### Appendix B — Pilot success criteria

The school and The English Hub agree that the pilot will be judged against the following criteria at the end-of-pilot review:

1. **Teacher time saved:** Did the pilot teacher save meaningful time on marking extended writing?
2. **Feedback quality:** Was the feedback reaching pupils of a quality the school is happy with?
3. **Pupil engagement:** Did pupils read and act on feedback?
4. **Workflow fit:** Did the platform fit the school's existing schemes of work and weekly rhythm?
5. **Data and privacy:** Was the school comfortable with the data protection posture?
6. **Onboarding experience:** Was the onboarding session clear and sufficient?

No numerical thresholds are set. The end-of-pilot review is a conversation, not a score card. The intent of these criteria is simply to anchor that conversation.

## How to use this template

1. **Before sending it to the school:** run it past your own lawyer once, at the start of the Founding Schools Programme, and never again unless you materially change the terms. This is a template, not bespoke legal advice.

2. **Customise per school:** replace every bracketed field.

3. **Send it by email, not DocuSign, for the pilot stage.** DocuSign adds friction; a returned PDF scan is fine for a free pilot. Reserve DocuSign for the full contract.

4. **Expect amendments.** Some schools' data protection officers will want small changes. Accept any reasonable changes and keep a log of what was changed so you can roll common amendments into the template over time.

5. **Countersign within 24 hours.** Never sit on a returned signed pilot agreement. Send a countersigned copy and the Slack channel invite on the same day.

6. **File it.** Save all signed pilot agreements in `07-tracking/` or a dedicated pilot agreements folder. Never rely on your inbox.

## What to do if a school pushes back on a term

The most common areas of pushback, and the response:

- **Data retention:** Some schools want all pilot data deleted on day one of the pilot ending. Fine — change "within 30 days" to "within 7 days".
- **Liability cap:** Some schools want a higher cap. Fine up to £10,000 for the pilot. Above that, escalate to the founder.
- **DPA:** Some schools have their own DPA template they prefer to use. Read it, mark up anything that doesn't work, and negotiate. A bespoke DPA review should take no more than 2 hours of the founder's time.
- **Reference consent:** Some schools want to explicitly opt out of being referenced during the pilot. Fine — add a clause: "For the avoidance of doubt, [SCHOOL NAME] has not consented to public reference as a pilot school." No Founding Schools commitments are triggered until a full licence is signed anyway.

## What never to change

- **The 4-week duration** — shorter is not enough data; longer becomes an unpaid trial.
- **The free nature of the pilot** — no conditional pricing or "you pay if you don't continue" language.
- **UK data hosting and the teacher-in-the-loop AI promise.**
- **The right of either side to end the pilot at any time.**
