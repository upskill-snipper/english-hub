# Reference Commitment Letter

A short, warm letter that a Founding School signs at the point of contract signature. Its purpose is to capture, in writing, the specific commitments the school is making as a reference customer. It sits alongside the main commercial contract and is referred to in clause 4 of the contract ("Being a Founding School reference").

**Why a separate letter and not just a contract clause?** Because it is easier for a Head of English to read and sign, easier to enforce socially, and a better tool for future reference requests than a line in a 12-page contract.

**Format:** A single page, in letter format, signed by both the founder and the school contact.

---

## The letter

---

**THE ENGLISH HUB**
**Founding School Reference Commitment Letter**

[DATE]

Dear [HEAD OF ENGLISH NAME],

Thank you for choosing to become one of our first ten Founding Schools. This letter sits alongside your main contract and sets out the specific commitments you are making as a reference customer, in plainer language than the contract uses.

We take the idea of a Founding School seriously. Your agreement to be a visible, named partner in our early story is — honestly — more valuable to us than the money you are paying. The discount on your Year 1 licence exists because of what this letter describes.

Everything below is what we would like [SCHOOL NAME] to agree to as a Founding School.

---

### 1. Be named as a Founding School

[SCHOOL NAME] agrees to be listed as one of our Founding Schools on our website, in our pitch deck, and in our marketing materials. This includes the school's name and, with your permission, the school's crest.

You can ask us to remove the listing at any time, for any reason, and we will do so within 48 hours.

---

### 2. Take up to three reference calls per year

Other Heads of English and other senior school leaders will want to hear from you before deciding whether to become a Founding School themselves. Peer reference is the most valuable thing you can give us — and one of the most valuable things you can give them.

You agree to take up to three reference calls per year from prospective schools. Each call is:

- No longer than 30 minutes.
- Scheduled at your convenience.
- Confined to your experience with The English Hub (i.e. you are not committing to give commercial or legal advice).
- Completely in your own voice. You are free to share criticisms as well as praise. We would rather you were honest than flattering.

You can decline any specific reference request if the timing, the school, or the context doesn't suit you.

---

### 3. Agree to a joint case study

You agree to allow us to publish one case study about your school's use of The English Hub, drawn from data and quotes gathered during the first 8–12 weeks of the licence (or the end of the pilot, if you came in through a pilot).

Before publication, you will receive a full draft of the case study. We will not publish anything that you have not explicitly approved in writing. Any quote, any number, any photograph — all subject to your approval.

Once approved, the case study will be published on our website and used in our sales materials.

You can withdraw consent for the case study at any time, and we will remove it from all public locations within 10 working days.

---

### 4. Give us monthly feedback

You agree to attend a 30-minute product call once a month with the founder. This is the moment we ask "what's working, what's not, what would make this meaningfully better", and you tell us.

If the monthly call doesn't fit your calendar in a given month, a written note of two or three lines in your Founding School Slack channel is an acceptable substitute.

We promise in return that we will share the feedback summary from all ten Founding Schools with you each quarter, so you can see how your voice compares to the rest of the cohort.

---

### 5. Allow a site visit or a filmed moment, once, in the first year

At some point during your first year, we would love one of the following:

- A one-hour site visit to see the platform in use in a real classroom.
- A 15-minute filmed interview with the Head of English or pilot teacher for our website and social channels.
- A photograph of the department or one of the pilot classes using the platform (subject to your safeguarding policies and parental consent).

You choose which of these you're comfortable with. If none of them work, a written quote is a fine substitute.

---

### What you receive in return

In exchange for the commitments above, as a Founding School you receive:

1. **50 percent off Year 1 licence fees.**
2. **A direct Slack or WhatsApp channel with the founder**, with responses within one working day.
3. **Priority on feature requests** — up to three guaranteed feature ships per year in direct response to your feedback.
4. **Year 2 renewal capped at standard rate + 10 percent maximum**, for the life of the reference relationship.
5. **Early access to new modules and AI features** — at least four weeks before general release.
6. **Co-marketing opportunities** — case study, joint webinars, LinkedIn posts, MAT introductions.
7. **A Founding School badge** for your website and comms materials.

These benefits are also listed in the main commercial contract. This letter summarises them for convenience.

---

### What happens if the relationship changes

If at any point [SCHOOL NAME] decides it no longer wishes to be a reference customer — for example, if the Head of English changes, or the school's priorities shift — you should tell us in writing.

At that point, we will:

1. Remove your listing from our website and materials within 48 hours.
2. Pause or withdraw any published case study if you ask us to.
3. Stop including you in reference calls.
4. Revert your renewal pricing to the standard rate from your next renewal date. (This does not affect your Year 1 pricing, which is locked once the contract is signed.)

No hard feelings. No contractual penalty. The Founding School relationship is meant to be a willing partnership, not a commitment you cannot exit.

## How to use this letter

1. **Send it alongside the main contract at the signing step.** Never send it before the school has agreed in principle — it risks looking like an extra ask.

2. **Walk the school through it verbally.** Take 5 minutes on a call to read the headlines of each section aloud. This is a moral contract as much as a practical one, and a verbal walkthrough makes the commitment stick.

3. **Countersign the same day.** Return a signed copy within 24 hours of receiving theirs. Include a short thank-you email.

4. **File both copies.** Save in the same place as the main contract.

5. **Revisit at month 6.** Schedule a 10-minute check-in with the HoD at the 6-month mark to see how the reference commitments are feeling. If something is awkward, fix it.

6. **Acknowledge their contributions publicly.** Whenever a Founding School takes a reference call, posts on LinkedIn, or approves a case study, thank them by name. Publicly and privately.

## A note on tone

This letter is deliberately warmer and less formal than the main contract. That is the point. It is a reminder that this is a partnership between people, not a procurement transaction. If the school redlines it heavily, that may be a sign they are not quite the right fit for a Founding School arrangement — at which point have an honest conversation about whether they want to sign at the standard rate instead.
