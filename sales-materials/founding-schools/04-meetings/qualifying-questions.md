# Qualifying Questions — MEDDPICC for Schools

MEDDPICC is a sales qualification framework used by enterprise software teams. It maps unusually well onto selling into UK schools once the language is adapted. The goal is not to interrogate a Head of English — it is to give the founder a mental checklist so no Founding School opportunity moves forward with a hidden blocker.

Work through these over the course of 2–3 meetings, not all at once. The discovery call covers half. The demo meeting covers the rest.

---

## M — Metrics

*What measurable outcome does this school want, and how would they know if we'd delivered it?*

**Ask:**

- "How do you currently measure the success of your English department — not just results, but things like staff retention, teacher workload, pupil progress between mocks?"
- "If a new tool worked brilliantly, what would be different in your department in six months?"
- "What do you report to your SLT on a weekly or termly basis?"
- "What does a good English department look like, from your head teacher's point of view?"

**Signals this is a qualified lead:**
- They can name specific metrics without thinking.
- They have a clear sense of what's broken and what they want fixed.
- They talk about teacher retention and workload, not just exam results.

---

## E — Economic buyer

*Who actually has authority to sign off the spend?*

**Ask:**

- "If we agreed a pilot went well and it became a full purchase next term, who would sign off the budget?"
- "Does the English department have its own budget, or does it sit inside a wider curriculum or CPD budget?"
- "Is the headteacher or CFO usually involved in software decisions like this, or is it left to heads of department?"
- "For a MAT: is procurement centralised at trust level, or do individual schools buy their own tools?"

**Signals this is a qualified lead:**
- The HoD can name a specific person, not just "SLT".
- That person is accessible for a 15-minute call if needed.
- There is an existing budget line this could plausibly come out of.

**Red flags:**
- "We'd need to get approval from the board / CFO / LA" and the HoD has never dealt with that process before.
- "We don't really buy software as a department."
- Multiple ambiguous decision-makers.

---

## D — Decision criteria

*On what basis will the school choose between us and doing nothing (or choosing a competitor)?*

**Ask:**

- "If you were seriously considering three options — us, a competitor, and doing nothing — what would the decision come down to?"
- "What's the single thing that would make you say yes? What's the single thing that would make you say no?"
- "Are there specific features or integrations that are must-haves rather than nice-to-haves?"
- "Do you have a rubric or checklist you use when evaluating new tools?"

**Signals this is a qualified lead:**
- The HoD has specific, articulable criteria.
- The criteria are realistic (i.e. not "it has to integrate with SIMS, Google, Teams, Arbor, Bromcom and our learning platform on day one").
- They are open about what would make it a no.

---

## D — Decision process

*What is the actual workflow for the school to say yes, and how long does it take?*

**Ask:**

- "If you wanted to trial this next half term, what would need to happen between now and then?"
- "Who would you need to consult — data lead, IT, SLT, MAT?"
- "What does the school's procurement process look like for something in this price range?"
- "Are there any fixed decision windows — e.g. a budget round in spring, a curriculum meeting in June?"

**Signals this is a qualified lead:**
- The process can be described in 3–5 concrete steps.
- The process completes in weeks, not months.
- The HoD is willing to drive the process, not wait for it.

---

## P — Paper process

*What are the administrative steps once a decision is made?*

**Ask:**

- "Do you have a standard vendor onboarding checklist? DPA, procurement forms, PO system?"
- "Who signs contracts at [SCHOOL NAME] — the HoD, the business manager, the head?"
- "Is there a supplier approval step?"
- "Does the tool need to be added to an approved software register anywhere?"

**Signals this is a qualified lead:**
- The HoD can name the business manager or office manager who handles paperwork.
- The process is familiar and has been run for other suppliers.
- There are no surprises (e.g. supplier insurance requirements, specific ICO certifications).

---

## I — Identify pain

*What is the real pain — the one that made them say yes to this meeting?*

**Ask:**

- "What made you agree to take this call?"
- "What's the biggest frustration in your department this term?"
- "When you think about next term, what are you quietly dreading?"
- "If I could solve one thing for you, what would it be?"

**Signals this is a qualified lead:**
- The pain is named, specific, and emotionally loaded.
- The pain is felt daily or weekly, not annually.
- Solving it has a knock-on effect they care about (staff retention, SLT confidence, their own workload).

**Red flags:**
- They can't articulate the pain beyond vague "workload".
- The pain is someone else's problem, not theirs.
- They seem to be taking the meeting out of politeness.

---

## C — Champion

*Who inside the school will actively advocate for us if the deal gets hard?*

**Ask:**

- "Is there anyone on your team who would love to be the first to try something like this?"
- "Who in the department is most frustrated with marking right now?"
- "If this pilot went well and you wanted to roll it out to the whole department, who would you want leading that?"
- "Is there a new or early-career teacher who might benefit most from this, and would they be willing to be involved?"

**Signals this is a qualified lead:**
- A specific name is volunteered.
- The named person is senior enough to influence but junior enough to be hands-on.
- The named person is either in the meeting or on email for future communications.

---

## C — Competition

*What else is the school considering? What do they currently use? What did they try and reject?*

**Ask:**

- "Are you looking at any other tools for this problem right now?"
- "What have you tried in the past that didn't work?"
- "Is there pressure from your MAT or LA to use a specific tool?"
- "What would a 'do nothing' option look like for you?"

**Signals this is a qualified lead:**
- They are honest about what they use and what they don't like.
- They have tried something before and can articulate why it failed.
- "Do nothing" is not an acceptable option to them.

---

## C — Consequences (paper process of risks)

*What happens if the school says no — to them, not to us?*

**Ask:**

- "What's the cost to your department of the status quo continuing for another year?"
- "What happens if marking workload stays where it is — to your team, to retention, to pupil outcomes?"
- "Is there an external deadline — Ofsted, mock results, a MAT review — that makes this more urgent?"

**Signals this is a qualified lead:**
- The HoD can articulate a clear cost of inaction.
- There is a concrete deadline or pressure point.
- They are feeling personal urgency, not just departmental urgency.

---

## How to use this in a live meeting

Do not run through these as a checklist. Embed them in the natural conversation. In a 30-minute discovery call, aim to cover:

- Pain (always) — 5 minutes
- Metrics — 3 minutes
- Economic buyer — 2 minutes
- Decision process — 3 minutes
- Competition — 2 minutes

In the demo meeting, cover:

- Champion — 2 minutes
- Decision criteria — 3 minutes
- Paper process — 2 minutes
- Consequences — 2 minutes

If after two meetings you still cannot answer any one of the MEDDPICC questions for a school, that is a signal. Either the school is not qualified or you have not built enough trust yet. Don't push forward — probe again or back off.

---

## The MEDDPICC scorecard

After each meeting, score the school out of 10 on each letter. 0 = no information. 10 = complete, confident answer.

Any school below 60/100 after two meetings is probably not a Founding School. Any school above 80 is a likely close.

Track the score in the pipeline tracker (`07-tracking/pipeline-template.csv`) in the "MEDDPICC Score" column. Update after every meeting.
