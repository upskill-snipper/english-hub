# Demo Meeting Agenda — 45 Minutes

This is the second meeting — after a successful 30-minute discovery call. It is the moment the school meets the product properly, brings in more people, and decides whether a 4-week pilot is worth running.

**Target length:** 45 minutes (with a 15-minute buffer at the end for informal Q&A with anyone who wants to hang around).
**Target attendees:** Head of English (required), at least one class teacher (strongly requested), and ideally a data lead / assistant head / MAT curriculum lead.
**Location:** Video call (Teams / Google Meet / Zoom — match their preference). In person if the school is within 90 minutes of the founder and the calendar allows.

---

## The agenda

### [00:00 — 00:03] Welcome and introductions (3 minutes)

- Thank everyone for attending.
- Ask each attendee to introduce themselves in one sentence: name, role, what they want to get out of the meeting.
- Restate the agenda:
  - 10 minutes context and positioning
  - 15 minutes live product demo
  - 10 minutes Q&A and commercial outline
  - 7 minutes on next steps and pilot setup

### [00:03 — 00:13] Context and positioning (10 minutes)

- Run slides 1–4 of the pitch deck (title, problem, why nothing else works, solution).
- Keep it tight. This is not a full 20-minute pitch — they've already heard the discovery call context.
- Refer back to the specific pains they mentioned in the discovery call. "When we spoke last week you mentioned [X]. Let me show you how that plays out in the product."

### [00:13 — 00:28] Live product demo (15 minutes)

- Follow `demo-script.md`.
- Prioritise:
  1. Teacher review screen (the killer feature) — 4 minutes
  2. Pupil view — 3 minutes
  3. HoD dashboard — 4 minutes
  4. Set a task flow — 2 minutes
  5. Data protection and admin — 2 minutes
- Stop for questions at any point. Do not power through if someone looks confused.

### [00:28 — 00:38] Q&A, objections, commercial outline (10 minutes)

- Open floor: "What questions has that raised? What's worrying you? What would you want to see that I haven't shown?"
- Use `objection-handling.md` for any standard pushback.
- Introduce commercials at a high level — no prices on screen, just:
  - Annual licence, invoiced yearly
  - Founding Schools: 50 percent off Year 1, direct line to founder, co-marketing, priority feature requests
  - 4-week free pilot available
- Do not get into per-pupil pricing unless directly asked. If asked: answer clearly and without fumbling.

### [00:38 — 00:45] Next steps and pilot setup (7 minutes)

- Propose the pilot: 4 weeks, one class, teacher of their choice, free, no credit card, no integration needed.
- Walk through the pilot agreement at a very high level — it's in `06-close/pilot-agreement-template.md` and is deliberately light-touch.
- Propose two specific pilot start dates.
- Propose the mid-pilot check-in date (2 weeks in) and the end-of-pilot review date.
- Ask: "What would need to be true for you to say yes to a pilot before we end this call?"

## The close

Before the call ends, aim for one of three outcomes. In descending order of desirability:

1. **Pilot agreed with a start date in the calendar.** (Best outcome.)
2. **Pilot agreed in principle, pending one named approval** (e.g. head teacher, data lead, DPO). Named person. Named date by which approval will come back.
3. **Clear next step with a date.** Usually: "Let me take this to [NAMED PERSON] and come back to you by [DATE]."

Do not leave with "we'll think about it and let you know". That is a no dressed up politely. Instead:

> "I really appreciate that you want to think about it. Could we agree a date by which you'll come back to me? I'd rather not chase you, and I'd rather not miss you either. What about [DATE] — and if the answer is no, a one-line email is completely fine."

## The leave-behind

After the meeting, send a follow-up email within 2 hours with:

1. A one-page PDF summary of the Founding Schools Programme (convert `01-positioning/programme-overview.md` to PDF)
2. A recording of the demo if you were allowed to record
3. A draft pilot plan specific to their named class
4. The pilot agreement (`06-close/pilot-agreement-template.md`) as a Word doc, not a PDF (so they can edit it)
5. A calendar invite for the agreed next step
6. A one-sentence recap of the most important thing they said

---

## Who needs to be in the room

This is the agenda, but it's also worth being firm about the attendee list before you agree to the meeting. The meetings that convert best have:

- **1 Head of English** — mandatory, no exceptions
- **1 class teacher** — hugely helpful for the demo; they'll ask the best questions
- **1 data lead or assistant head** — optional but makes the commercial conversation twice as fast
- **0 IT managers at this stage** — IT can come in later to review data protection. Having them in a product demo slows it down.

If the only person attending is the HoD alone, do the meeting anyway — but use the last 5 minutes to ask who else they'd want at a second demo.

---

## Recording

- Always ask permission to record before the call starts. "I'd love to record the demo section so that I can share it with anyone else on your team who couldn't make it — is that OK? I can send you the recording straight after."
- If they say no, do not record. Simple.
- If they say yes, use the recording ethically: share only with the attendees and the named colleagues they identified.
- Delete recordings after 90 days unless the school signs.
