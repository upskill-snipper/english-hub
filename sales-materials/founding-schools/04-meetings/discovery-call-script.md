# Discovery Call Script — 30 Minutes

The discovery call is the first live meeting with a prospective Founding School. It is deliberately not a demo. Its purpose is to earn the right to run a demo and to qualify whether this school is a good fit for the Founding Schools Programme.

**Time:** 30 minutes.
**Attendees:** Head of English (usually), sometimes an assistant head, sometimes a second English teacher.
**Your goal:** Leave the call with either (a) a demo booked or (b) a clear no — with the reason.

---

## Before the call (10 minutes of prep)

- Check the school's website: current HoD name, department size, recent Ofsted, recent exam results, named English tool on "curriculum" or "learning" page.
- Check LinkedIn: the HoD's background, any public posts, tenure.
- Open the pipeline tracker in a second window.
- Have a blank notes doc ready. Write the school name and date at the top.
- Have the Founding Schools programme page open in a tab.

---

## [00:00 — 00:02] Opening

> "Hi [FIRST NAME] — thanks so much for the half hour. I'll keep us to time.
>
> Before I dive in, a quick question: what made you say yes to this call? I want to make sure we use the time well."

**Listen carefully to the answer.** Whatever they say is your compass for the rest of the call.

---

## [00:02 — 00:05] The one-minute context

> "Very brief so we can get to the interesting part. I'm a former English teacher. I've built The English Hub — a tool specifically aimed at the extended writing marking workload. I'm not going to do a demo today — that's a second meeting if we both want one. Today I'd really like to understand how things are working for you, what's frustrating, and whether there's anything we could usefully help with. Does that work?"

**Wait for an acknowledgement. Do not proceed without it.**

---

## [00:05 — 00:20] Qualifying and pain probing — the real meat of the call

This section is your most important 15 minutes of the week. Ask questions. Let the HoD talk. Your job is to stay quiet and take notes.

Use the MEDDPICC framework (detailed in `qualifying-questions.md`) but don't run through it like a checklist. Let it flow as a conversation.

### Question 1 — The state of the department

> "Tell me about your department at the moment. How many teachers, what the spread of experience looks like, how Year 11 and Year 10 are tracking, anything you're quietly worried about."

**Listen for:** team size, new teachers, retention, workload complaints, recent OFSTED, recent results.

### Question 2 — Existing tools

> "What tools does the department currently use for homework, marking, assessment or revision? What's working, what isn't?"

**Listen for:** Seneca, Educake, Sparx, Quizlet, GCSE Pod, internal assessment platform, Google Classroom, Teams. Don't bash anything they mention — ask what they like about it.

### Question 3 — The marking problem specifically

> "How does extended writing marking currently happen in your department? Who marks what, how often, how much feedback the pupil gets back, how quickly."

**Listen for:** total volume, Sunday afternoon references, teachers leaving, inconsistency between teachers, SLT pressure on feedback turnaround, parents' evening pressure.

### Question 4 — What would change if marking wasn't the bottleneck

> "Put the existing tools aside for a second. If marking wasn't the bottleneck — if you got two hours back per teacher per week — what would you actually do with that time? What would change in the department?"

**This is the most important question in the call.** Listen for the emotional answers, not the operational ones. "I'd see my own children more." "I'd actually plan properly." "I'd stop losing new teachers in their first year."

### Question 5 — Decision process

> "If you were going to bring in a new tool next term — not saying you will, just thought experiment — how would that decision get made? Who would need to be involved, how long would it take, and what would have to be true?"

**Listen for:** budget holder, SLT sign-off required, data protection review, procurement process, annual budget cycle, termly decision points.

### Question 6 — Founding Schools fit

> "Last question before I tell you about what we offer. How does your department feel about being an early adopter of something? Would you describe yourselves as bleeding-edge, keen to try things, cautious but open, or 'wait until it's proven'?"

**If they say "wait until it's proven", they are probably not a Founding School. Thank them for their honesty and offer to come back in six months.**

---

## [00:20 — 00:25] Tell them about the Founding Schools Programme — but briefly

> "Let me tell you quickly what we offer, and then I'd love to hear your reaction.
>
> We're picking ten English departments to work with closely in our first year. They're our Founding Schools. They get 50 percent off Year 1, a direct Slack or WhatsApp channel with me, priority on feature requests — up to three guaranteed ships per year — and co-marketing: case studies, joint webinars, logos. Year 2 renewal is capped at a 10 percent uplift for as long as they stay a reference. In exchange, they agree to be a named reference customer, give us feedback each month, and let us write a case study once the first half term of data is in.
>
> We're at [X] of ten right now.
>
> I'm not going to try to close anything today — we haven't even shown you the product. I'd like to invite you to a 45-minute demo where we walk through it end to end with two or three of your team, and you decide from there whether a four-week pilot is worth doing.
>
> How does that sound?"

---

## [00:25 — 00:28] Handle the reaction

Three possible reactions:

### Reaction A — "Yes, let's book the demo."

> "Brilliant. Who else on your team would you want in the room? I usually ask for at least one other English teacher and, if it's available, a data lead or an assistant head. I'm looking at [DATE 1] and [DATE 2] — which works better? I'll send the invite before we end this call."

**Book the demo now. Never "I'll send my calendar link".**

### Reaction B — "I'm interested but I need to think / check with my head."

> "Completely understand. Two things I'd suggest. First — can I send you a one-pager on the Founding Schools Programme plus a 3-minute product video you can share with your head? Second — shall we hold a provisional demo slot for [DATE] while you check? I can move it if you need to."

**Always create a holding action. Never leave without a date.**

### Reaction C — "No thanks / not a priority."

> "Completely fair. One quick question — is it a 'not now' or a 'not ever'? I'd hate to come back and chase you if it's genuinely not for you, but equally I don't want to lose touch if there's a better moment."

**If "not now" — agree a specific date to re-contact. Put it in the pipeline tracker under "Contact again".**
**If "not ever" — thank them, ask for one sentence of feedback on why, close the file.**

---

## [00:28 — 00:30] Close the call

> "Thanks so much for your time today, [FIRST NAME]. I'll send a short recap of what we discussed by end of day and the demo invite for [DATE] so it's in the calendar.
>
> One ask — if you thought of any other Heads of English who you'd point me at for a Founding Schools slot, I'd be hugely grateful. Not right now if it's awkward — but keep it in the back of your mind.
>
> Enjoy the rest of your week."

---

## After the call (15 minutes, do not skip)

1. **Update the pipeline tracker within 30 minutes.** Stage, next step, date, notes.
2. **Send the recap email within 2 hours.** See template below.
3. **Send the calendar invite for the demo.** Include the demo agenda as the invite description.
4. **Prepare the demo.** Tailor it to the specific pains they mentioned.

## Anti-patterns — things not to do in a discovery call

- **Do not pitch in the first ten minutes.** You are here to listen.
- **Do not demo on a discovery call.** Even if they ask. Hold it back for the proper demo meeting.
- **Do not quote a price.** Mention the Founding Schools discount in passing, but do not get into per-pupil numbers on the first call.
- **Do not over-promise on features.** If they ask "do you do X?", answer honestly. "No but it's on the roadmap for [MONTH]" is fine. "Yes" when the answer is "sort of" is not.
- **Do not go over 30 minutes.** A Head of English's time is sacred. Respect the clock. It earns you the next meeting.
