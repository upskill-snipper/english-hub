## 1. Market Analysis

### 1.1 Target Market Size

**UK Market (Primary)**
- ~1,019,000 students sat GCSEs in Summer 2025 (Ofqual data)
- 801,787 entries for GCSE English Language alone in Summer 2025
- English Language + English Literature are compulsory -- virtually every GCSE student is a potential customer
- Year 10 students (pre-exam year) add another ~550,000 who are actively revising
- **Total addressable UK market: ~1.5 million students in Years 10-11 at any given time**
- At 2% penetration = 30,000 subscribers = ~GBP 300K ARR (monthly plan) to ~GBP 2.4M ARR (annual plan)

**International Market (Qatar, UAE, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman)**
- 784 international schools in UAE alone; 52+ British curriculum schools in Qatar
- ISC Research records 14,833 K-12 international schools globally; British/Cambridge curriculum is the most common
- Estimated 200,000+ students taking IGCSE English across GCC countries
- British curriculum schools in the region charge GBP 8,000-25,000/year in fees -- parents have high willingness to pay for supplementary resources
- **Total addressable international market: ~200,000 IGCSE English students in GCC**
- At 3% penetration (higher due to less competition) = 6,000 subscribers = ~GBP 480K ARR

**Combined TAM estimate: ~1.7 million students, SAM: ~GBP 3M ARR at modest penetration**

### 1.2 Competitor Landscape

| Competitor | Pricing | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Seneca Learning** | Free tier + Premium ~GBP 17/mo seasonal, ~GBP 9/mo annual | Huge user base, free tier drives adoption, covers all subjects, memory science approach | Generic -- not English-specialist. AI marking is basic. No dedicated IGCSE content. |
| **Save My Exams** | ~GBP 9-15/mo (7-day trial) | Excellent exam-board-specific notes, past paper solutions, strong SEO | Static content -- notes/PDFs, no AI feedback, no interactive modules, no essay marking |
| **Mr Bruff** | Free YouTube (430K subs, 86M views) + eBooks (GBP 3-8 each) | Massive brand recognition, trusted by students, free content funnel | One teacher's perspective only, no platform/interactivity, no mock exams, no AI, no progress tracking |
| **CGP Books** | One-off purchases GBP 5-12 per book, CGP+ digital subscription | Ubiquitous in UK schools, trusted brand, physical+digital | Traditional publisher -- limited interactivity, no AI, no personalisation, not subscription-focused |
| **York Notes (Pearson)** | GBP 5-7 per text guide | Exam board aligned, available in shops | Static guides only, no technology integration |
| **Tutorful/MyTutor** | GBP 20-50/hr for 1:1 tutoring | Personalised, human interaction | 20-50x more expensive, not scalable, no self-paced option |

### 1.3 Positioning & USP

**Positioning statement:** The English Hub is the only GCSE/IGCSE English platform that combines exam-board-specific courses (AQA, Edexcel, OCR, WJEC, Cambridge IGCSE) with AI-powered essay feedback, giving students a personal English tutor at 1/50th the cost.

**Core differentiators vs. each competitor:**
1. **vs. Seneca:** English-specialist depth (every text, every question type) vs. Seneca's broad-but-shallow coverage
2. **vs. Save My Exams:** Interactive modules + AI essay feedback vs. static PDFs
3. **vs. Mr Bruff:** Structured learning path with progress tracking + mock exams vs. unstructured YouTube videos
4. **vs. CGP:** Digital-first with AI personalisation vs. traditional publishing
5. **vs. Tutors:** GBP 9.99/mo vs. GBP 120+/mo for weekly sessions, available 24/7

**One-line pitch for marketing:** "Your AI English tutor. Exam-board specific. GBP 9.99/month."

## 2. Go-To-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Pre-Launch (Weeks 1-2)

**Objective:** Build a waiting list of 500+ email subscribers and recruit first 20 affiliates.

**Tactics:**

1. **Landing page with waitlist** (if not already live)
   - Hero: "GCSE English revision that actually marks your essays. Join the waitlist for early access + 50% off your first 3 months."
   - Collect: name, email, year group, exam board
   - Use this data to segment email nurture from day one

2. **Affiliate recruitment sprint** (details in Section 3)
   - Personally reach out to 50 micro-influencers and education creators
   - Target: 20 signed up with Rewardful links by end of week 2
   - Provide early access to the platform so they can create authentic content

3. **Social media setup and content banking**
   - Create accounts: TikTok, Instagram, YouTube (Shorts), Twitter/X
   - Bank 20 short-form videos: essay tips, exam technique, "grade 9 vs grade 5" comparisons
   - Schedule first 2 weeks of daily posts

4. **Teacher outreach (warm leads)**
   - Email 100 English HoDs at UK schools (scrape from school websites or use TES Jobs contacts)
   - Offer free school dashboard trial for the rest of the academic year
   - Target: 10 schools trialling by end of pre-launch

5. **SEO foundation**
   - Publish 10 cornerstone blog posts targeting high-volume keywords (see Section 5)
   - Ensure all course pages are indexed with proper schema markup

**Deliverables by end of week 2:**
- 500+ waitlist emails
- 20 affiliates onboarded with Rewardful
- 40+ social media posts scheduled
- 10 blog posts live
- 10 schools in trial pipeline

### Phase 2: Soft Launch (Weeks 3-4)

**Objective:** Convert waitlist to 100 free trials, achieve 30%+ trial-to-paid conversion, gather testimonials.

**Tactics:**

1. **Waitlist launch email sequence**
   - Email 1 (Day 1): "We're live -- claim your 50% off" (scarcity: first 200 sign-ups only)
   - Email 2 (Day 3): Feature walkthrough + 60-second demo video
   - Email 3 (Day 7): Social proof -- "Here's what beta users are saying"
   - Email 4 (Day 14): Trial ending reminder + annual plan pitch

2. **Affiliate activation**
   - Notify all affiliates that the platform is live
   - Provide each with a unique discount code (e.g., BRUFF15 for 15% off) stacked on top of the launch offer
   - Run a "first 10 sales" bonus: GBP 50 extra for affiliates who drive 10 conversions in the first 2 weeks

3. **Content blitz**
   - Affiliates and in-house team post coordinated content during launch week
   - Target: 50+ pieces of content across all channels in 2 weeks
   - Host one live "GCSE English Masterclass" on YouTube/TikTok to drive sign-ups

4. **Feedback loop**
   - In-app survey after first 7 days: NPS score + "What would make this worth paying for?"
   - Personally email the first 50 users asking for video testimonials (offer 1 month free for a 30-second clip)

5. **Reddit and Student Room seeding**
   - Post genuinely helpful English revision content on r/GCSE, The Student Room
   - Not overt promotion -- become a valued contributor, mention the platform when relevant
   - Target subreddits: r/GCSE (45K members), r/6thForm, r/TeachingUK

**Deliverables by end of week 4:**
- 100+ free trial sign-ups
- 30+ paid conversions
- 5+ testimonials (text or video)
- 3+ affiliate-driven sign-ups validated

### Phase 3: Growth (Months 2-3)

**Objective:** Reach 300 paying subscribers. Establish affiliate channel as primary growth driver.

**Tactics:**

1. **Scale affiliate program to 50 active affiliates**
   - Tier-based outreach (see Section 3 for full breakdown)
   - Launch affiliate leaderboard with monthly prizes
   - Host monthly affiliate webinar showing top-performing content/strategies

2. **Paid advertising (test budget)**
   - Google Ads: Target "GCSE English revision" keywords (GBP 2-4 CPC estimated)
   - TikTok Ads: Spark Ads boosting best-performing organic content (GBP 5-10/day test)
   - Facebook/Instagram: Retargeting website visitors + lookalike audiences from converters
   - Total test budget: GBP 300-500/month

3. **School partnerships push**
   - Attend 2-3 education conferences/expos (e.g., Bett Show, Education Show)
   - Offer "class licence" pricing to schools (see Section 4)
   - Target: 5 schools with paid subscriptions

4. **Content scaling**
   - Publish 2 blog posts/week (SEO-driven)
   - Weekly YouTube video (full essay walkthrough or exam technique)
   - Daily TikTok/Shorts (reuse blog content as micro-content)

5. **International market entry**
   - Reach out to 20 British curriculum schools in Qatar and UAE directly
   - Partner with 5 Middle East education influencers/tutors
   - Localise marketing for exam season differences (IGCSE exams May/June and Oct/Nov)

**Deliverables by end of month 3:**
- 300 paying subscribers (~GBP 3K MRR)
- 50 active affiliates
- 5+ school partnerships
- 10,000+ monthly website visitors

### Phase 4: Scale (Months 4-6)

**Objective:** Reach 1,000 paying subscribers. Establish sustainable growth engine.

**Tactics:**

1. **Double down on what works**
   - Analyse months 1-3 data: which channels, affiliates, and content types drive best LTV:CAC
   - Shift 80% of budget to top 2 performing channels

2. **Affiliate program expansion to 100+ affiliates**
   - Open self-serve affiliate sign-up page
   - Launch "Teacher Ambassador" programme (teachers earn commissions + free premium access)
   - Target: 30 teacher ambassadors actively recommending to students

3. **Exam season campaign (January-May)**
   - This is the critical window. Every piece of marketing should reference exam dates
   - "100 days to your GCSE" countdown campaign starting in January
   - Offer "Exam Season Pass" -- 5-month subscription at GBP 29.99 (covers Jan-May)
   - Ramp ad spend 2x during this period

4. **Referral programme (distinct from affiliate)**
   - Students refer friends: both get 1 month free
   - Viral loop: shareable progress badges, mock exam results cards for social media

5. **B2B sales pipeline**
   - Hire part-time sales rep or allocate founder time for school sales
   - Target: 20 schools by end of month 6
   - School pipeline for September intake (new academic year)

**Deliverables by end of month 6:**
- 1,000 paying subscribers (~GBP 10K MRR)
- 100+ affiliates, 30+ teacher ambassadors
- 20+ school partnerships
- 25,000+ monthly website visitors
- Clear path to GBP 20K MRR by month 12

## 3. Affiliate Marketing Campaign (PRIMARY CHANNEL)

### 3.1 Why Affiliates First

For a bootstrapped EdTech platform at GBP 9.99/month, affiliate marketing is the optimal primary channel because:
- **Zero upfront cost** -- you only pay when revenue is generated
- **Trust transfer** -- education purchases are trust-driven; a teacher's recommendation converts 5-10x better than an ad
- **Recurring revenue alignment** -- 20% recurring commission incentivises affiliates to promote retention, not just sign-ups
- **Compound growth** -- each affiliate is a permanent acquisition channel that grows over time

### 3.3 Commission Structure Summary

| Element | Details |
|---|---|
| **Standard commission** | 20% recurring for the lifetime of the customer |
| **Cookie duration** | 30 days |
| **Minimum payout** | GBP 25 |
| **Payment frequency** | Monthly (net 30) |
| **Payment method** | PayPal or bank transfer via Rewardful |
| **First-sale bonus** | GBP 5 bonus on first referred conversion (activation incentive) |
| **Volume bonus** | GBP 50 at 10 conversions/month; GBP 150 at 25 conversions/month |
| **Annual plan bonus** | Additional 5% one-time bonus when a referral chooses the annual plan (GBP 79.99) |

**Commission economics:**
- Monthly subscriber at GBP 9.99: affiliate earns GBP 2.00/month recurring
- Annual subscriber at GBP 79.99: affiliate earns GBP 16.00/year + GBP 4.00 annual bonus = GBP 20.00
- A teacher ambassador referring 50 annual subscribers = GBP 1,000/year passive income
- **Your effective CAC via affiliates: GBP 0 upfront, ~20% of revenue ongoing (vs. 50-100% of first-year revenue for paid ads)**

## 4. Pricing Strategy
