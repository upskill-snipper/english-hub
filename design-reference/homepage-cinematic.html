AQA anthology · Power & Conflict · "Ozymandias"
I met a traveller from an antique land,
Who said — "Two vast and trunkless legs of stone
Stand in the desert. . . . Near them, on the sand,
Half sunk a shattered visage lies, whose frown,
And wrinkled lip, and sneer of cold command,
Tell that its sculptor well those passions read
Narrator frame Imagery of decay Key quote Theme · power
Click to reveal · "sneer of cold command"

Shelley fuses the facial sneer with military command — the despot's contempt is inseparable from his authority. The coldness strips warmth, humanity; the sculptor has caught not a man but an attitude.

Context card · 1818

Published as Napoleon's legacy was collapsing. Shelley, a radical, read the ruin of tyrants as inevitable — a Romantic reply to imperial pride.

Grade 9 hinge

"Shelley's traveller is a mediating voice — we never meet Ozymandias, only his wreckage, retold."

Your submission · An Inspector Calls · 428 words
Priestley presents Sheila as the emotional conscience of the Birling family. At the start of Act I, her silly exclamations show she is still a child of privilege — but by the end of the play she has become the character who most sharply condemns her parents. When she says "these girls aren't cheap labour", Priestley uses her voice to critique the capitalist assumptions her father takes for granted. Her transformation is important because it shows that change is possible, especially in the younger generation — a political argument Priestley wants the 1945 audience to hear.
