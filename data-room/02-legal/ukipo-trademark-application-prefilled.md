**Scheme:** UK Intellectual Property Office (UKIPO) Standard Application
**To submit:** https://www.gov.uk/how-to-register-a-trade-mark
**Fee:** £170 for one class · £50 per additional class

Formal trademark filing is best-practice handled by a CITMA-member attorney (Chartered Institute of Trade Mark Attorneys) if any earlier-mark conflicts are identified during the clearance search. DIY filing is defensible for a clean word-mark with no blocking prior art — which is the expected outcome here.

## 3 · Classes of goods and services

UK trademarks use the **Nice Classification (12th edition)**. The correct classes for an EdTech SaaS are:

### Class 9 — Software and downloadable content · £170 (the first class is included)

**Specification text to paste:**

> Downloadable computer application software for providing online education; downloadable software for learning English Language and English Literature; downloadable mobile applications for GCSE and IGCSE English revision; downloadable electronic publications in the nature of study guides, revision notes, and mock examination papers; downloadable software featuring artificial intelligence for marking student essays and providing feedback.

### Class 41 — Education and training · £50

**Specification text to paste:**

> Provision of education services; provision of online educational courses; provision of online tutoring services; provision of examination preparation services; publication of educational materials; provision of non-downloadable online educational videos; arranging and conducting of mock examinations; provision of educational information.

### Class 42 — Software as a service · £50

**Specification text to paste:**

> Provision of software as a service (SaaS) featuring software for online education, essay marking, exam preparation, and progress tracking; design and development of software for the education sector; hosting of educational platforms on a website; provision of temporary use of non-downloadable software for providing online education and examination preparation.

**Total: £270.** Leave Class 16 (printed matter) and Class 38 (telecoms) for later if ever needed.

## 5 · Priority claim

Not applicable — this is a first UK filing.

If filing the EU mark later (within 6 months of UK filing), you can claim UK priority on the EUIPO application. UK priority is worth claiming only if a competitor is likely to apply in the EU during the 6-month window.

## 6 · After filing — timeline

| Step | Time from filing | Action |
|---|---|---|
| UKIPO examination | 2–4 weeks | UKIPO examiner reviews — absolute grounds check + earlier-marks search |
| Examination report | 2 months | You receive an examination report. Clean report = proceed. Objections = respond within 2 months. |
| Publication | 3 months | Mark published in the *Trade Marks Journal* |
| Opposition window | 3–5 months (2 months after publication) | Third parties may oppose. Expected to pass without opposition. |
| Registration | 4–5 months | Certificate issued |

Track all correspondence in `data-room/02-legal/trademark-register.md`. Set a calendar reminder for **4 months from filing date** to check registration status.

## 7 · Post-registration actions

1. Add the ® symbol to "The English Hub" on the site, in emails, and on marketing materials (only once registered — before registration, use ™).
2. Update site footer: "THE ENGLISH HUB® is a registered trade mark of Upskill Energy Limited."
3. Update `data-room/02-legal/trademark-register.md` with registration number and date.
4. Calendar **10-year renewal**. Fee at renewal: £200 for the first class + £50 per additional = £300.
5. Consider filing:
   - **EU trademark (EUIPO)** — €850 single class, €50 per additional class. Recommended if international (MENA / SE Asia) sales go live.
   - **US trademark (USPTO)** — USD $350/class intent-to-use. Recommended once US users appear in data.
   - **Madrid Protocol international application** — consolidated filing across 130+ jurisdictions via WIPO. Economic if 3+ countries are targeted.
