### IT IS AGREED:

### 1. Definitions

1.1 "**Confidential Information**" means all information disclosed by or on behalf of one Party (the "**Disclosing Party**") to the other Party (the "**Receiving Party**") in connection with the Purpose, whether in writing, orally, electronically, visually or in any other form, whether or not marked as confidential, and includes: business plans, financial information, investor materials, cap tables, product information, technology, source code, pedagogy, curriculum, student data, strategy, customer lists, pricing and any notes, analyses or compilations derived from such information.

1.2 Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) is or becomes publicly available other than as a result of a breach of this Agreement;
(b) was known to the Receiving Party before disclosure, free of any confidentiality obligation;
(c) is received from a third party who is not under a confidentiality obligation in respect of it;
(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information; or
(e) the Receiving Party is required to disclose by law, court order, or by a competent regulator, provided that (where legally permitted) it gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of the required disclosure.

### 2. Obligations

2.1 The Receiving Party shall:

(a) keep the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information strictly confidential;
(b) use it only for the Purpose;
(c) not disclose it to any third party except to its directors, employees, and professional advisers who need to know it for the Purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as strict as those in this Agreement (the "**Permitted Recipients**");
(d) protect it with at least the same standard of care as it uses for its own confidential information, and in any event with a reasonable standard of care;
(e) not copy it except to the extent reasonably necessary for the Purpose.

2.2 The Receiving Party is responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Permitted Recipients.

### 3. Term

3.1 This Agreement starts on the date of signature and continues for REPLACE WITH DURATION (typically 2 years).

3.2 The Receiving Party's confidentiality obligations in clause 2 continue for REPLACE WITH DURATION (typically 3 years) after termination or expiry.

### 4. Return or destruction

4.1 On written request from the Disclosing Party, and in any event on termination, the Receiving Party shall, at the Disclosing Party's option, return or destroy all Confidential Information in its possession (including all copies), and certify in writing that it has done so, subject to its rights to retain copies to comply with legal or regulatory retention obligations or as part of routine back-ups that are not readily accessible.

### 5. No licence, no warranty

5.1 Nothing in this Agreement grants the Receiving Party any right, licence, or interest in the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information or any intellectual property.

5.2 The Disclosing Party makes no warranty as to the accuracy or completeness of any Confidential Information.

### 6. Remedies

6.1 The Parties acknowledge that damages may not be an adequate remedy for breach of this Agreement and that equitable relief (including injunctions) may be appropriate.

### 7. No obligation to proceed

7.1 Nothing in this Agreement obliges either Party to proceed with the Purpose or any transaction.

### 8. Third parties

8.1 A person who is not a party to this Agreement has no rights under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999 to enforce any of its terms.

### 9. Governing law and jurisdiction

9.1 This Agreement is governed by the law of England and Wales, and the Parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.
