## How to use this register

- Log a matter as soon as it becomes reasonably likely that a dispute will arise (e.g. a letter before action, a formal complaint, a regulator's enquiry).
- Keep privileged documents (legal advice, strategy, without-prejudice correspondence) in a separate privileged folder. Do **not** paste them into this file.
- Review at every board meeting.
- Disclose material matters in investor updates, subject to legal advice.

### Status values

- **Threatened** — letter before action or informal complaint received, not yet filed in court.
- **Active** — claim issued at court (or arbitration filed).
- **Mediation** — in formal mediation.
- **Settled** — resolved by agreement; settlement agreement signed.
- **Judgment** — judgment handed down; enforcement pending.
- **Appeal** — on appeal.
- **Closed** — fully concluded, no outstanding action.

## Disclosure letter note

At any funding round, the disclosure letter should cross-reference this register. Buyer/investor diligence will normally ask for a "litigation warranty" — the standard form is something like: "The Company is not engaged in any litigation, arbitration, prosecution or other legal proceedings or in any proceedings or hearings before any statutory or governmental body... and no such proceedings have been threatened by or against the Company."

If the statement at the top of this document is ever changed from "NONE", ensure warranties in any financing document are updated accordingly.
