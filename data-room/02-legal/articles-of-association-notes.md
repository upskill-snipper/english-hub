# Articles of Association — Notes

> **TEMPLATE ONLY — NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** These notes summarise the difference between model and custom articles of association for The English Hub Ltd (England & Wales) and flag the typical customisations required at seed stage. The actual articles must be drafted or reviewed by a qualified corporate solicitor.

---

## What are the articles of association?

The articles of association are the Company's written constitution. They govern the internal management of the company and the rights attaching to its shares. Under section 17 of the Companies Act 2006 they form part of the company's constitutional documents and are publicly filed at Companies House.

They are legally binding on the Company and its shareholders, and any variation of them requires a special resolution (75% shareholder approval) unless a higher threshold is specified in the articles themselves.

---

## Model articles

On incorporation, a private company limited by shares will typically adopt the model articles for private companies prescribed under the Companies (Model Articles) Regulations 2008.

**Pros:**

- Free, standard, well-understood by Companies House, banks and solicitors.
- Sufficient for a single-founder or pure-solo company with no external investment.
- No legal drafting cost.

**Cons / limitations:**

- Only one share class.
- No vesting provisions.
- No drag-along, tag-along, or pre-emption on transfer beyond the basic approval right.
- No provision for investor director appointments or consent rights.
- No leaver provisions.
- No bad leaver concept.
- No anti-dilution.
- Do not contemplate investor consent over key matters.

As soon as there is more than one founder, or any external investment, the model articles usually need to be amended or fully replaced.

## Customisations typically required at seed stage

The following are the common amendments to model articles at the point of a priced seed round:

### 1. Share classes

- Create a new class of **Seed Preferred** shares.
- Describe economic rights: 1× non-participating liquidation preference is the market default at seed in the UK.
- Describe voting rights (usually same as ordinary, 1 vote per share).
- Describe conversion rights on IPO or defined Qualifying Financing.

### 2. Liquidation preference and waterfall

- Set out the priority of payments on a sale, winding up, or asset distribution.
- Typical order: (1) Preferred shareholders receive the greater of (i) 1× their original subscription price, and (ii) their pro rata share on an as-converted basis; (2) remaining proceeds to ordinary shareholders.

### 3. Anti-dilution

- Weighted-average anti-dilution is the market default in UK seed deals. Full-ratchet is rare and usually resisted.
- Apply on a new issuance at a price below the Seed Preferred issue price.

### 4. Drag-along and tag-along

- Drag requires the defined majority (e.g. 75% of issued shares including Investor Majority) to force a sale of the Company by all shareholders.
- Tag allows minority shareholders to participate in a proposed sale of a controlling stake.

### 5. Pre-emption on allotment and transfer

- Pre-emption on allotment is a statutory right for ordinary shareholders under s.561 Companies Act 2006, but it is usually disapplied for specific issuances (employee options, bona fide acquisitions, the current round).
- Pre-emption on transfer (right of first refusal) is added so existing shareholders can buy shares a departing shareholder wishes to sell.

### 6. Founder vesting / leaver provisions

- Reverse-vesting on founder shares (typically 4 years, 1-year cliff, from a back-dated vesting commencement date).
- Compulsory transfer on leaver: bad leaver loses unvested shares at nominal value and may also lose vested shares at nominal or fair value; good leaver keeps vested shares or sells them at fair value.
- "Good leaver" / "Bad leaver" definitions are contentious — negotiate carefully.

### 7. Investor consent / Reserved matters

- List of matters requiring investor consent (often duplicated in the SHA), e.g. variation of share rights, issuance of new classes, dividends, borrowing above a cap, winding up, exit, material change of business.

### 8. Board composition

- Right of Investor to appoint a director or observer.
- Maximum number of directors.
- Chair appointment.

### 9. Permitted transfers

- Permitted transfers to family trusts, holding companies, spouses, and on death — without triggering pre-emption.

### 10. Share buy-back mechanics

- Enable compulsory transfer of shares on leaver events, complying with the Companies Act 2006 restrictions on the Company purchasing its own shares.

## Practical rules

1. **File updated articles at Companies House within 15 days** of the resolution adopting them.
2. **Keep a clean change log** of all amendments. A good practice is to save every adopted version with a filename like `articles-2026-04-10-seed-round.pdf` in this folder.
3. **Ensure the SHA and articles are consistent.** Where they conflict, the articles generally prevail because they are public and constitutional.
4. **Never adopt investor-favourable articles without corporate legal advice.** The drag-along, anti-dilution, and leaver clauses in particular can have outsized consequences.
5. **Do not amend articles without a special resolution** (75% of votes cast), unless the articles themselves specify a higher threshold.
