## 1. Definitions

1.1 In this Deed:

"**Assigned IP**" means all Intellectual Property Rights in any and all Works, whether created before, on, or after the date of this Deed, including (for the avoidance of doubt) the items listed in Schedule 1.

"**Intellectual Property Rights**" or "**IP Rights**" means all patents, rights to inventions, copyright and related rights, moral rights, trade marks, trade names, domain names, rights in get-up, rights in goodwill or to sue for passing off, unfair competition rights, rights in designs, rights in computer software, database rights, topography rights, rights to use, and protect the confidentiality of, confidential information (including know-how and trade secrets), and all other intellectual property rights, in each case whether registered or unregistered and including all applications (or rights to apply) for, and renewals or extensions of, such rights, and all similar or equivalent rights or forms of protection which subsist or will subsist now or in the future in any part of the world.

"**Works**" means all works, materials, designs, inventions, improvements, discoveries, software, code, algorithms, models, datasets, curriculum, lesson plans, content, brand assets, logos, marketing copy, audio, video, and any other subject matter created or developed by the Assignor (alone or with others) in connection with the Company, its business, its products, or its services.

1.2 Clause headings are for convenience only and do not affect interpretation.

## 2. Assignment of Pre-Existing IP

2.1 The Assignor hereby assigns to the Company with full title guarantee all of its right, title and interest in and to the Assigned IP created before the date of this Deed, including all IP Rights in the items listed in Schedule 1, to hold unto the Company absolutely.

2.2 The Assignor confirms that the consideration for this assignment of pre-existing IP is the sum of £1.00 (one pound sterling), receipt of which is acknowledged, and/or the Assignor's engagement with the Company.

## 3. Assignment of Future IP

3.1 The Assignor hereby assigns to the Company with full title guarantee, by way of present assignment of future rights, all of its right, title and interest in and to all Assigned IP that may be created by the Assignor on or after the date of this Deed, in each case to the extent such Assigned IP arises in connection with the Company, its business, its products, or its services, to hold unto the Company absolutely and as and when such rights come into existence.

3.2 If for any reason any future Assigned IP does not vest automatically in the Company under clause 3.1, the Assignor shall hold such Assigned IP on trust for the Company until formally assigned.

## 4. Moral rights

4.1 To the fullest extent permitted by law, the Assignor irrevocably and unconditionally waives all moral rights (including under Chapter IV of the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988) in the Assigned IP in favour of the Company and its successors, assigns, and licensees.

## 5. Warranties

5.1 The Assignor warrants and represents that:

(a) the Assignor is the sole legal and beneficial owner of the Assigned IP existing as at the date of this Deed, free from any third party rights, encumbrances, or licences;

(b) the Assigned IP does not and will not, so far as the Assignor is aware, infringe the IP Rights of any third party;

(c) the Assignor has not granted, and will not grant, any licence or other right in respect of the Assigned IP to any third party;

(d) the Assignor has authority to enter into this Deed and to grant the assignments set out in it.

## 6. Further assurance

6.1 The Assignor shall, at the Company's cost, promptly execute all such documents and do all such acts and things as the Company may reasonably require to give full effect to this Deed and to perfect the Company's title to the Assigned IP, including executing any confirmatory assignments, patent assignments, trade mark assignments, or powers of attorney.

6.2 The Assignor irrevocably appoints the Company as its attorney in its name and on its behalf to execute any document and do any thing required to give effect to clause 6.1, if the Assignor fails to do so within 10 business days of a written request.

## 7. Confidentiality

7.1 The Assignor shall keep confidential, and shall not use for its own benefit or disclose to any third party, any confidential information belonging to the Company, both during and after its engagement with the Company.

## 8. Governing law & jurisdiction

8.1 This Deed and any dispute or claim (including non-contractual disputes or claims) arising out of or in connection with it shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the law of England and Wales.

8.2 The Parties irrevocably agree that the courts of England and Wales shall have exclusive jurisdiction to settle any dispute or claim arising out of or in connection with this Deed.
