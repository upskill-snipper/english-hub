12.2 **Payment in lieu of notice (PILON).** The Company may, in its sole discretion, terminate the Employee's employment with immediate effect by paying a sum equal to the Employee's basic salary for the unexpired part of the notice period (less tax and NIC). 12.4 The Company may terminate employment summarily without notice or payment in lieu if the Employee is guilty of gross misconduct, material breach of this contract, or conduct bringing the Company into disrepute.

## 13. Post-termination restrictions

13.1 In this clause, "**Restricted Period**" means REPLACE WITH PERIOD (typically 6 months for senior hires, 3 months for more junior, enforceability depends on role), "**Restricted Business**" means the business of providing online English-language education and related services, and "**Restricted Customer**" means any customer, student, or prospective customer of the Company with whom the Employee had material dealings in the 12 months preceding termination.

13.2 The Employee shall not, for the Restricted Period after termination:

(a) **non-compete:** be engaged in any Restricted Business in the UK in competition with the Company;
(b) **non-solicitation of customers:** solicit or try to solicit the business or custom of any Restricted Customer;
(c) **non-dealing with customers:** deal with any Restricted Customer in connection with any Restricted Business;
(d) **non-solicitation of staff:** solicit or try to entice away from the Company any senior employee or contractor of the Company with whom the Employee had material dealings in the 12 months before termination.

13.3 Each restriction in clause 13.2 is a separate and independent restriction. If any restriction is found unenforceable but would be enforceable if part of its wording were deleted, it shall apply with such deletions as are necessary to make it enforceable.
