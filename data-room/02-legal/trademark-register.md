---

## Why track trademarks

1. **Protect the brand** — registered trademarks give enforceable rights against copycats.
2. **Clear investor due diligence** — investors expect the core brand to be registered in at least the home jurisdiction.
3. **Enable enforcement** — without a registration it is much harder (and more expensive) to stop infringement.
4. **Plan international expansion** — trademarks are territorial; the UKIPO does not protect you in the EU, US, or any other jurisdiction.

### Status values

- **Cleared** — search completed, no blocking earlier marks found.
- **Filed** — application submitted, awaiting examination.
- **Examined** — under UKIPO examination.
- **Published** — in opposition period (2 months in UK; 3 months in EU).
- **Opposed** — opposition filed; in dispute.
- **Registered** — final certificate issued.
- **Refused** — application rejected, appeal considered.
- **Lapsed** — not renewed on time.
- **Abandoned** — voluntarily dropped.

## Clearance search checklist (before filing)

- [ ] UKIPO search for identical and similar marks in relevant classes.
- [ ] EUIPO search if EU expansion is planned.
- [ ] USPTO TESS search if US expansion is planned.
- [ ] Google / Bing search for unregistered use that could give rise to passing-off rights.
- [ ] Domain availability (see `domain-register.csv`).
- [ ] Companies House name check.
- [ ] Social media handle availability.
- [ ] Apple App Store and Google Play name check (if launching mobile apps).
