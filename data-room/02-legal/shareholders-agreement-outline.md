# Shareholders' Agreement — Outline

> **TEMPLATE ONLY — NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This is a structural outline of the key clauses in a Shareholders' Agreement (SHA) for The English Hub Ltd (England & Wales). A full SHA should be drafted by a corporate solicitor and negotiated alongside the articles of association. Typical triggers for putting an SHA in place are: first external investment, first co-founder, or first employee receiving direct shares (rather than options).

Optional parties: option-holding managers, strategic investors, holding vehicles.

## 2. Definitions

Key defined terms: Shareholder, Founder, Investor Majority, Group, Business, Permitted Transferee, Exit, Qualifying Financing, IPO, Reserved Matters, Bad Leaver, Good Leaver, Employment.

## 3. Business of the Company

- Describe the business (e.g. "the operation of an online English-language learning platform and related services").
- Require shareholder consent to move materially outside the described business.
- Tie the business description to the Reserved Matters list so material pivots are controlled.

## 4. Board composition and governance

- Number of directors; right of Investor(s) to appoint one or more directors or observers.
- Chair appointment and casting vote (avoid giving a casting vote where possible).
- Board meeting cadence, quorum, and notice.
- Director D&O insurance obligation.
- Right of Investor Director to receive information in advance of meetings.
- Alternate directors.

## 5. Information rights

- Monthly management accounts within X days of month end.
- Annual statutory accounts.
- Annual board-approved budget.
- Cap table on request.
- Notification of material events (loss of customer, IP dispute, litigation, breach of covenant).
- Inspection rights (subject to confidentiality) for material Investors.

## 6. Reserved matters

Actions requiring prior written consent of specified shareholders (typically Investor Majority plus, for some items, Founder consent). Typical list:

1. Changing the articles of association.
2. Creating or issuing new shares, options, warrants, or convertible instruments.
3. Varying rights attached to any class of shares.
4. Declaring or paying dividends.
5. Borrowing over a threshold, or granting security.
6. Disposing of material assets or the business.
7. Winding up, liquidation, administration, or entering a CVA.
8. Changing the name, registered office, auditor, or accounting reference date.
9. Entering into material contracts outside the ordinary course (define a £ threshold).
10. Related party transactions.
11. Hiring or dismissing C-suite; materially changing C-suite compensation.
12. Approving annual budget; material deviation from budget.
13. Acquisitions, joint ventures, or incorporating subsidiaries.
14. Starting or settling litigation above a threshold.
15. Granting exclusive licences of material IP.
16. Changing the nature of the business.

## 7. Share transfer restrictions

- **Lock-up.** No transfers by Founders for REPLACE WITH PERIOD (e.g. 3 years from completion), except Permitted Transfers.
- **Permitted Transfers.** To family trusts, holding companies wholly owned by the transferor, and on death.
- **Consent requirement.** Any other transfer requires board / investor consent.

## 8. Pre-emption on issue

- Existing shareholders have the right to participate in new share issuances pro rata to their existing holdings, subject to the Reserved Matters consent.
- Exceptions: employee share scheme, bona fide acquisitions, consideration shares, and a defined "safe harbour" for small issuances.

## 9. Pre-emption on transfer (right of first refusal)

- If a shareholder wishes to sell, they must first offer the shares to existing shareholders pro rata at the offered price before selling to a third party.
- Process: transfer notice, offer period, deemed acceptance, completion, waterfall if not all taken up.

## 10. Tag-along rights

- If a shareholder (or group) proposes to transfer shares representing more than REPLACE WITH % (commonly 50%) of the issued share capital, other shareholders have the right to "tag" their shares into the sale on the same terms and at the same price per share.
- Designed to protect minority holders from being left behind when control is sold.

## 11. Drag-along rights

- If shareholders representing at least REPLACE WITH % (typically 75% or a separately defined "Drag Majority", often including Investor Majority) accept a bona fide third-party offer for 100% of the Company, they can require the remaining shareholders to sell on the same terms.
- Protections for minority drag-along: same price, same class, limited warranties, capped liability, cash consideration (or equivalent).

## 12. Exit / liquidity

- Founders and Investors agree to work in good faith towards an Exit within a target window (e.g. 5–7 years from completion), without creating a binding obligation.
- Appointment of advisers, drag-along interaction, proceeds waterfall from articles.

## 13. Leaver provisions

- Good leaver vs. bad leaver definitions.
- Compulsory transfer of shares on departure; price depends on good/bad leaver status.
- Vesting — Founder reverse vesting schedule (typically 4 years, 1-year cliff) is often set out here or in the articles.

## 14. Non-compete and non-solicitation

- Founder non-compete for the duration of employment and REPLACE WITH PERIOD (often 12–24 months) after, in respect of the Business.
- Founder non-solicitation of employees, customers, and students for the same period.
- Carve-outs for passive investment in listed companies.

## 15. Confidentiality

- Mutual confidentiality undertaking between all parties in respect of the Company's affairs and the terms of the SHA, with standard exceptions (public domain, required disclosure, advisers).

## 16. Founder commitment

- Founders commit to devote substantially all their working time to the Company.
- Outside interests must be disclosed and consented to.

## 17. Warranties

- Founder warranties on signing (title to shares, no existing liabilities, accuracy of disclosed information, IP ownership, compliance with law). Typically capped at a multiple of salary or a £ amount, time-limited.
- Investor warranties are usually limited to authority and capacity.

## 18. Dispute resolution

- Good-faith negotiation first.
- Escalation to CEO / board.
- Mediation.
- Litigation in England and Wales, or arbitration if the cap table is international.

## 19. Anti-embarrassment (optional)

- If a Founder sells shares at a low price and the Company is resold at a materially higher price within a short window, a top-up may be payable to the original buyer.

## 20. Deed of adherence

- New shareholders must sign a deed of adherence to become bound by the SHA before receiving shares.

## 21. General boilerplate

- Entire agreement.
- Variation only in writing signed by all parties (or specified majorities).
- Severance.
- No partnership.
- Assignment (typically not permitted without consent).
- Notices (addresses and email).
- Counterparts.
- Governing law — England and Wales.
- Jurisdiction — exclusive jurisdiction of the English courts.

## 22. Schedules

1. Cap table at completion.
2. Form of deed of adherence.
3. Disclosure letter / warranties.
4. Existing business plan or budget.
5. Form of notices.

---

## Interaction with Articles of Association

Some provisions (e.g. pre-emption, transfer restrictions, share class rights, drag/tag) must appear in the articles as well as the SHA to be enforceable against the Company and future shareholders. The corporate solicitor must ensure the two documents align.
