# 02 — Legal

> **TEMPLATES ONLY — NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** All documents in this folder are starting-point templates for The English Hub Ltd (a UK company). They must be reviewed by a qualified solicitor before use, signature, or disclosure to any third party. Nothing here constitutes legal advice.

## Purpose

This folder is the legal section of The English Hub's investor Data Room. It holds the cap table, standard agreement templates, registers of contracts / IP / domains / trademarks, and litigation tracking.

During due diligence investors typically ask for: a clean cap table, evidence that all IP has been assigned to the company, evidence that founders and contractors are under written agreements with IP assignment clauses, a contract register showing material commitments, and confirmation there is no pending litigation.

## Document status conventions

Every file in this folder should be marked with one of:

- **TEMPLATE** — blank template, not yet populated.
- **DRAFT** — populated with real data but not reviewed / signed.
- **IN REVIEW** — with solicitors or counterparty.
- **EXECUTED** — signed and in force.
- **SUPERSEDED** — replaced by a later version (keep for audit trail).

## Key due-diligence red flags to avoid

1. **IP not assigned.** Every founder, employee, and contractor who has ever touched product, brand, copy, curriculum, or code must have a written IP assignment in favour of the company. No exceptions.
2. **Verbal / handshake founder arrangements.** Always paper the deal.
3. **Missing option grants.** If you have promised options, document them as unapproved or EMI grants with board minutes.
4. **Cap table does not reconcile to Companies House.** Update the confirmation statement and SH01 filings as shares are issued.
5. **Open source with copyleft in the product.** Audit the dependency tree before launch (see `open-source-license-audit.md`).
6. **Domain / trademark owned in personal name.** Transfer to the company.
7. **Unredacted personal data in this folder.** Use role descriptions where possible; redact home addresses and bank details before sharing externally.
