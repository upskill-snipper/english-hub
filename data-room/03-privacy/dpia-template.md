## Step 1 — Is a DPIA required?

Per Article 35(3) and ICO guidance, a DPIA is **mandatory** where processing involves:

- [ ] Systematic and extensive profiling with significant effects
- [ ] Large-scale processing of special category data or criminal conviction data
- [ ] Systematic monitoring of publicly accessible areas on a large scale
- [ ] Use of new technologies
- [ ] Denial of service or contract to individuals
- [ ] Large-scale profiling of individuals
- [ ] Biometric or genetic data
- [ ] Combining, comparing, or matching data from different sources
- [ ] **Processing children's data for profiling, marketing, or offering online services**
- [ ] Data that could cause risk of harm if breached
- [ ] Tracking location or behaviour
- [ ] Innovative technology (AI, IoT, etc.)

### Key mitigations to consider

- Encryption in transit and at rest
- Pseudonymisation / anonymisation
- Access controls and least privilege
- Logging and audit
- Vendor due diligence and DPAs
- Data minimisation at collection
- Privacy-by-default settings
- Age-appropriate language and UX
- Parental controls (where relevant)
- Regular staff training
- Incident response plan
- Contractual (SCCs, IDTA, DPA)
- User controls (opt-out, rights)
- Transparency notices
- DPIA consultation with the ICO under Art 36 (if residual high risk remains)
