**Last reviewed:** 12 April 2026

**Policy owner:** Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)

> The English Hub is used by children aged primarily 13–16. We take our safeguarding responsibility seriously. This policy explains how we identify, respond to, and escalate safeguarding concerns and aligns with **Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSIE)**, **Working Together to Safeguard Children**, the **Children Act 2004**, and the **Online Safety Act 2023** (where applicable).

---

## 1. Our commitment

We are committed to:

- Keeping children safe whenever they use The English Hub
- Promoting their welfare
- Responding quickly and proportionately to any safeguarding concern
- Escalating concerns to appropriate statutory authorities when needed
- Working with schools, parents, and carers to protect children
- Reviewing this policy at least annually
- Treating every concern seriously, regardless of its apparent severity

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All The English Hub employees, contractors, volunteers, interns
- Trustees / directors
- Anyone representing The English Hub in any capacity
- Subprocessors handling children's data (via contractual flow-down)

- Bullying and online harassment
- Radicalisation / extremism (Prevent duty)

- Any other welfare concern

## 4. Safeguarding by design

### 4.1 Product design

- **No open messaging** between users. Students cannot message each other or strangers.
- **No public profiles** — students cannot publicly identify each other.
- **No image upload** to publicly visible surfaces. Profile pictures are limited to a curated avatar library.
- **Content filters** on AI-generated output. The AI provider's safety classifiers are combined with our own custom filters.
- **Safe response policy** — if a student writes about distress, self-harm, or harm to others, the AI provider's safety system must not encourage, roleplay, or gloss over it. Our system provides a calm acknowledgement and surfaces support information (Childline, Samaritans, etc.).
- **Flagging** — any response flagged by the AI safety classifier or by keyword heuristics is queued for human review within the safeguarding team.
- **Teacher escalation** — where a school is the customer, concerning content is also flagged to the student's teacher / DSL (where contract and legal basis permit).
- **No targeted advertising**, behavioural profiling for marketing, or third-party trackers on child-facing pages.

### 4.2 Content moderation

- Content on the platform is curated and produced in-house.
- No user-generated content is shown to other students.
- Written work is private to the student and their authorised teachers / parents.

### 4.3 AI safety guardrails

- Student writing is sent to the AI provider under a strict system prompt that includes child-safety instructions.
- Outputs are filtered before being shown.
- The AI provider's data use is contractually restricted (no training on our data).
- We test the AI with a safeguarding test set quarterly and on any significant model change.

## 5. Recognising concerns

### 5.1 Signals we look for

- Explicit disclosures of abuse or harm in student writing
- Mentions of self-harm, suicidal ideation, or eating disorders
- Content suggesting exploitation, grooming, or trafficking
- Radicalising language or indicators of extremism
- Content indicating domestic abuse
- Signs of serious mental health distress
- Repeated hostile, violent, or threatening content

### 5.2 Sources

- Automated flags from the AI safety classifier and keyword heuristics
- Teacher or school reports
- Parental reports
- Student self-reports via the "Report a concern" flow
- Staff noticing issues during support
- External reports (e.g., from organisations contacting us)

## 6. Reporting and escalation

### 6.1 If you are a staff member with a concern

1. **Act immediately.** Do not wait.
2. **Record what you know** — exactly what you saw, when, the user's ID (not name in an email subject), and any actions already taken.
3. **Email safeguarding@theenglishhub.app** with the subject "Safeguarding concern — [urgent/non-urgent]".
4. **Call the DSL** for any concern that is urgent or where a child may be at immediate risk.
5. **Do not investigate or interview the child yourself.** Do not promise confidentiality you cannot keep.
6. **Do not delete any evidence.** Preserve the record.
7. **Do not discuss** the concern with anyone who does not need to know.

### 6.2 DSL triage (within 2 working hours for non-urgent, immediately for urgent)

The DSL will:
- Review the concern
- Classify severity: Immediate risk / High / Medium / Low
- Decide next actions
- Record the outcome in the safeguarding log

### 6.3 When to escalate externally

| Situation | Contact |
|---|---|
| **Child in immediate danger** | **Call 999** immediately |
| **Concern requires social services intervention** | Contact the **local authority children's social care** where the child lives (via the school if known, or via the national helpline if urgent) |
| **Suspected online sexual abuse / exploitation** | **CEOP** (ceop.police.uk) |
| **Suspected radicalisation** | **Prevent duty**: contact police anti-terrorism hotline 0800 789 321 or local Prevent coordinator |
| **Non-urgent welfare concern where child has a known school** | Contact the school's DSL |
| **Disclosures from child with no known school** | Escalate to the local authority MASH (Multi-Agency Safeguarding Hub) |
| **Ongoing concerns about a pattern of behaviour** | Consider NSPCC helpline 0808 800 5000 for advice |

### 6.4 Timeframes

- **Immediate risk:** act now (999 if life-threatening)
- **High concern:** contact statutory services within 24 hours
- **Medium concern:** review and decide within 48 hours
- **Low concern:** log, monitor, review within 7 days

### 6.5 Informing parents and schools

- Where safe, we inform the child's school DSL and parents of the concern.
- We **do not** inform a suspected abuser or a person who poses a risk to the child.
- Where disclosure might put the child at more risk (e.g. suspected abuse within the home), we escalate directly to social services and take advice before informing the family.

## 7. Recording

Every safeguarding concern is recorded in a secure safeguarding log, including:

- Unique incident ID
- Date and time of report
- Who reported (role, name)
- Nature of the concern
- User(s) involved (by user ID)
- Actions taken by the DSL
- External contacts made (with time and person)
- Outcome
- Review dates

Records are kept in a restricted-access secure store. **Retention: until the child reaches age 25** (per KCSIE guidance).

Access is limited to the DSL and deputy DSL; broader access requires documented need.

---

## 8. Training

- **All new staff** complete safeguarding induction within 7 days of starting.
- **All staff** complete annual refresher training.
- **DSL and deputy** complete enhanced training every 2 years.
- Training covers: KCSIE principles, recognising abuse and neglect, responding to disclosures, recording, escalation, Prevent duty, online safety.
- Training completion is tracked and reported to the board annually.

---

## 9. Confidentiality

Information about concerns is shared only on a "need to know" basis. Confidentiality is **not** absolute — we will share information with statutory agencies when required to keep a child safe, even without consent.

We never promise a child that we will keep a disclosure secret. We tell them, in child-appropriate language, what we will do with the information.

---

## 10. Whistleblowing

Any staff member who believes safeguarding concerns are not being handled properly can raise the issue with:
- The DSL's manager (CEO)
- The board / nominated board-level safeguarding governor
- The NSPCC whistleblowing helpline on 0800 028 0285
- The Charity Commission or ICO (for data/systemic concerns)

No staff member will suffer retaliation for raising a safeguarding concern in good faith.

## Appendix A — Signs and indicators (summary)

This is a short reference; see KCSIE for the full detail.

**Physical abuse:** unexplained bruises or injuries; fear of a specific person; descriptions of physical harm.
**Emotional abuse:** extreme self-criticism; withdrawal; fear of making mistakes; references to harsh treatment.
**Sexual abuse:** sexualised language or knowledge beyond age; disclosures; references to online contact with adults.
**Neglect:** references to hunger, unclean clothing, being left alone; lack of basic needs.
**Bullying:** references to being targeted, excluded, threatened.
**Self-harm / suicide:** explicit mentions, writing about ending things, detailed plans, recent escalation.
**Radicalisation:** extreme views, isolation from family, changes in behaviour, references to recruitment.
**Domestic abuse:** references to violence, fear of home, controlling behaviour by a family member.

Any single signal might not mean abuse — but any signal warrants careful consideration and escalation where the DSL believes there is a concern.

## Appendix B — AI safety response template

When a student's writing contains safeguarding-relevant content, our system:

1. Does **not** gloss over or ignore the content.
2. Does **not** provide graphic, roleplay, or harmful feedback on that content.
3. Responds with a calm, age-appropriate acknowledgement that the content has been noted.
4. Surfaces support resources (Childline, a trusted adult).
5. Does **not** make the student feel shamed or punished for disclosing.
6. Flags the submission for human safeguarding review.
7. Notifies the school's DSL (where the school is the customer and the contract allows).

The student's writing is preserved in the safeguarding log, not deleted, unless doing so is lawful and in the child's best interests.
