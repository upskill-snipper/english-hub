## 4. Why we process your data and our lawful basis

Under UK GDPR, we need a lawful basis for every use of personal data.

| Purpose | Data used | Lawful basis |
|---|---|---|
| Provide the learning service (account, content delivery, progress tracking) | Account, learning, technical | **Contract** (Art 6(1)(b)) — or, where a school is the customer, **contract with the school** |
| Generate AI feedback on student work | Learning data | **Contract** (Art 6(1)(b)); see §6 for safeguards |
| Keep the service secure (fraud, abuse, bot prevention) | Technical, authentication | **Legitimate interests** (Art 6(1)(f)) — see LIA |
| Customer support | Communications, account | **Contract** (Art 6(1)(b)) |
| Billing and payment | Billing | **Contract** and **legal obligation** (tax records) |
| Product analytics (aggregated, pseudonymised) | Usage, technical | **Legitimate interests** — see LIA; children's data is excluded from behavioural analytics by default |
| Marketing emails to adults | Email | **Consent** (Art 6(1)(a)) — opt-in; withdrawable any time |
| Compliance with legal obligations (safeguarding disclosures, court orders) | Any | **Legal obligation** (Art 6(1)(c)) and **vital interests** (Art 6(1)(d)) where urgent |

Under 13s: where consent is required, we rely on **parental consent** (Art 8 UK GDPR).

## 6. AI-assisted feedback

The English Hub uses AI models to help mark student writing and provide feedback.

- Student writing is sent to our AI provider (`[REPLACE WITH model provider — e.g., OpenAI Ireland Ltd]`) under a data processing agreement that **prohibits training on our data**.
- Feedback is reviewed through automated safety filters before being shown to students.
- Teachers can override or correct any AI feedback.
- Students are clearly told when feedback is AI-generated.
- No fully automated decisions with significant effects are made about students (we do not exclude, penalise, or grade students on the basis of AI output alone). Article 22 UK GDPR does not apply.

## 11. Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

| Right | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Access** | Get a copy of the data we hold about you (a "SAR") |
| **Rectification** | Correct inaccurate data |
| **Erasure** | Ask us to delete your data ("right to be forgotten") |
| **Restriction** | Ask us to limit how we use your data |
| **Portability** | Get your data in a machine-readable format |
| **Object** | Object to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing |
| **Withdraw consent** | Where we rely on consent, you can withdraw it |
| **Not be subject to automated decisions** | We don't make solely automated significant decisions |
| **Complain to the ICO** | Contact the UK Information Commissioner's Office at ico.org.uk |

To exercise these rights, email **privacy@englishhub.`[tld]`**. We will respond within one calendar month. We may need to verify your identity before acting on requests.

Children can exercise these rights themselves. Parents can exercise rights on behalf of children where appropriate (e.g., under 13s).

## 13. Children

The English Hub is designed for children aged 13–16. We comply with the **UK Children's Code (Age Appropriate Design Code)** and its 15 standards. See our [Children's Privacy Notice](./childrens-privacy-notice.md) for an age-appropriate explanation.

Key commitments:
- **Privacy by default** — the most private settings are on by default
- **No profiling** of children for marketing or behavioural advertising
- **No nudge techniques** pushing children to share more data
- **Transparent** language suitable for the age group
- **Data minimisation** — we collect only what we need
- **No geolocation** collection beyond coarse IP
- **No parental monitoring tools** beyond what is necessary for the service
