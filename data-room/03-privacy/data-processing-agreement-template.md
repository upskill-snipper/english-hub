## 4. Processor obligations

The Processor will:

### 4.1 Compliance
Comply with UK GDPR, DPA 2018, the Children's Code, and all other Applicable Law when processing Personal Data.

### 4.2 Documented instructions
Process Personal Data only on the Controller's documented instructions. If the Processor believes an instruction violates Applicable Law, it will notify the Controller.

### 4.3 Confidentiality
Ensure that anyone authorised to process Personal Data is bound by written confidentiality obligations.

### 4.4 Security
Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures (Article 32 UK GDPR) — see **Annex 2**.

### 4.5 Subprocessors
Engage Subprocessors only in accordance with section 5 and **Annex 3**.

### 4.6 Assistance with data subject rights
Assist the Controller, using appropriate technical and organisational measures, to respond to requests from Data Subjects exercising rights under Chapter III UK GDPR (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection). The Processor will action a verified request from the Controller within 7 days.

### 4.7 Assistance with compliance duties
Assist the Controller in meeting its obligations under Articles 32–36 UK GDPR (security, breach notification, DPIA, prior consultation), taking into account the nature of the processing and the information available.

### 4.8 Return or deletion
On termination of the Main Agreement, at the Controller's choice, return or delete all Personal Data and delete existing copies unless Applicable Law requires storage. Standard deletion period: **30 days** after termination unless the Controller instructs otherwise in writing.

### 4.9 Audit
Make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with Article 28 and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller. See section 9.

### 4.10 Records of processing
Maintain records of processing activities under Article 30(2).

### 4.11 DPO
Maintain a Data Protection Officer (or documented Article 37 assessment) and make their contact details available.

## 6. International transfers

### 6.1 Priority to UK / EEA
The Processor will, where reasonably possible, process Personal Data in the United Kingdom or EEA.

### 6.2 Safeguards
Where transfers outside the UK/EEA are necessary, the Processor will ensure one of the following applies:
- an **adequacy regulation** under Article 45;
- the **UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA)** or the **UK Addendum to the EU SCCs**;
- another valid transfer mechanism under Chapter V UK GDPR.

### 6.3 Transfer Impact Assessment
The Processor will conduct and maintain a **Transfer Impact Assessment** for each non-adequate transfer and provide a copy to the Controller on request.

### 6.4 Standard contractual clauses
Where the IDTA or UK Addendum is used, it is incorporated into this DPA by reference, and the Processor will procure that any Subprocessor receiving Personal Data under such terms executes the clauses.

## 7. Security incidents

### 7.1 Notification
The Processor will notify the Controller **without undue delay and in any event within 24 hours** of becoming aware of a Security Incident affecting Personal Data.

### 7.2 Content of notification
Notification will include, to the extent available:
- Nature of the incident
- Categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected
- Categories and approximate number of records affected
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken or proposed to address the incident and mitigate possible adverse effects
- Contact point for further information

### 7.3 Ongoing updates
The Processor will provide updates as new information becomes available.

### 7.4 Support
The Processor will assist the Controller, as necessary, to comply with its own obligations under Articles 33 and 34 UK GDPR (including notification to the ICO within 72 hours and, where required, to Data Subjects).

### 7.5 No presumption of liability
Notification of a Security Incident is not an admission of fault or liability by the Processor.

## 8. Safeguarding

Where the Processor becomes aware of content, behaviour, or disclosure that raises a safeguarding concern about a child, it will act in accordance with its [Safeguarding Policy](./safeguarding-policy.md) and notify the Controller's designated safeguarding lead without undue delay, unless doing so would prejudice the safety of the child (in which case it will escalate directly to the appropriate statutory agency).

---

## 9. Audits

### 9.1 Annual independent audit
The Processor will obtain an annual independent audit (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001, or Cyber Essentials Plus) and provide a copy of the report to the Controller on request.

### 9.2 Controller audit rights
Once per year, and at the Controller's cost, the Controller may conduct, or have conducted by an independent auditor, an on-site or remote audit of the Processor's compliance with this DPA, subject to:
- **30 days** written notice
- Reasonable confidentiality undertakings by the auditor
- No material interference with the Processor's business
- No access to other customers' data

### 9.3 Audit on breach
Section 9.2's limits do not apply following a material Security Incident affecting the Controller's data.

## 10. Liability

Liability under this DPA is subject to the liability regime in the Main Agreement, except that:

- Neither party can limit liability for death or personal injury, fraud, or any matter that cannot be limited by law.
- Where Article 82 UK GDPR applies, each party is liable for damages arising from its own acts, and Article 82's allocation of liability applies.

## 11. Term and termination

This DPA remains in force for as long as the Processor processes Personal Data under the Main Agreement. Clauses imposing obligations to return or delete data, maintain confidentiality, and cooperate with audits survive termination.
