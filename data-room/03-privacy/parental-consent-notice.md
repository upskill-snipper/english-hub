## Why we need your consent

The English Hub is designed for GCSE students (typically ages 13–16). Under the **UK Data Protection Act 2018**, children aged **13 or over** can consent to their own data being processed by online services in the UK.

For children **under 13**, UK law (Article 8 UK GDPR and section 9 DPA 2018) requires **verifiable parental consent** before we can process their personal data on the basis of consent.

If your child is under 13 and has not been enrolled through a school, we need your consent before we can let them use The English Hub.

> If your child's account is being provided by their **school**, the school handles the legal basis for processing their data. You don't need to consent via this form — talk to the school if you have questions.

---

## What we're asking you to agree to

By giving consent, you agree that we may:

1. **Create an account** for your child with the information provided (name, email, year group, exam board).
2. **Store the work your child does in the app** — essays, quiz answers, and progress data — for as long as the account is active.
3. **Use AI to give feedback** on your child's writing, as described in our Privacy Policy. Your child's writing is not used to train AI.
4. **Show your child's progress** to you (via your parent account) and to any teacher you add.
5. **Contact you** by email about your child's account, billing, and important safety matters.
6. **Apply appropriate child safety settings** by default — no marketing, no behavioural advertising, no profiling.

---

## What your consent does NOT cover

We will **never**, under this consent or any other:

- Sell your child's data to third parties
- Use your child's data for advertising or marketing
- Share your child's essays with other users
- Allow open messaging between your child and strangers
- Collect precise location data
- Train AI models on your child's writing

If we ever want to do something new that goes beyond this, we will ask for fresh consent.

## Your child's data — quick summary

**What we collect:** Name, email, year group, exam board, date of birth, writing and quiz answers, progress data, device type, IP address (for security).

**Why:** To run the learning service and provide feedback.

**Who sees it:** Your child, you, teachers you authorise, our authorised staff, and trusted subprocessors (listed in `subprocessor-register.csv`).

**How long:** While the account is active plus around 12 months, unless you ask us to delete sooner.

**Where:** Primarily in the UK / EEA.

The full details are in our [Privacy Policy](./privacy-policy.md).

## Your rights (and your child's)

You can, at any time:

- **Withdraw consent.** Email **privacy@englishhub.`[tld]`** and we will pause the account. Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
- **Ask us to delete the account and data.** We'll action this within 30 days.
- **Ask for a copy of your child's data** (a subject access request).
- **Ask us to correct inaccurate information.**
- **Export your child's data** in a portable format.

Your child also has these rights directly.

To exercise any of these, email **privacy@englishhub.`[tld]`**.

## How we verify your consent

Because this is consent from a parent or guardian, not the child, we use a **layered verification** approach appropriate to the risk:

1. **Email verification.** We send a confirmation link to your email address. You click it to confirm.
2. **Payment verification.** For paid accounts, your card is processed via a verified payment method (additional identity signal).
3. **Government-issued ID check** (only where we have specific concerns). We use `[REPLACE WITH ID verification provider or "a regulated ID verification service"]`, and we do **not** store copies of ID documents — only a pass/fail result.

We maintain a consent audit log: parent name, email, timestamp, IP address, consent version.
