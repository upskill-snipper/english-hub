# Standard NDA — Before Sharing the Data Room

**Purpose:** Every prospective buyer signs an NDA before they get a login to the virtual data room. No exceptions. This document is a template and a process guide — **it is not legal advice and must be reviewed by the company's solicitor before first use.**

---

## When to send the NDA

- **Before** sharing any document labelled "confidential" beyond the investor deck
- **Before** revealing named customers or detailed financials
- **Before** granting access to the virtual data room
- **Before** a detailed management meeting

You can share the investor deck and a high-level teaser without an NDA — those are market-facing documents.

---

## Type of NDA to use

| Type | When to use |
|------|-------------|
| **One-way (mutual only recipient is bound)** | Most buyer situations — you're sharing, they're receiving |
| **Mutual (two-way)** | When the buyer will also share sensitive information (e.g., strategy decks, roadmap) — common in strategic discussions |
| **Process NDA** (sell-side with specific term) | When running a structured process via a broker/banker |

**Default for The English Hub's early stage:** one-way NDA, 2–3 year term, UK-governed.

---

## Key terms to include

1. **Definition of confidential information** — broad, covering oral, written, digital, observational
2. **Permitted purpose** — "evaluating a potential commercial or corporate transaction between the parties"; no other use
3. **Standard carve-outs** — publicly available, already known to recipient, lawfully received from a third party, independently developed
4. **Permitted recipients** — the recipient's named advisers (legal, financial, tax) who are themselves bound by confidentiality
5. **No reverse engineering**
6. **No solicitation** of employees or customers for a defined period (12 months typical)
7. **Return / destruction of materials** on request or at end of discussions
8. **Term** — 2 or 3 years from the date of signature; the obligation to protect trade secrets survives indefinitely
9. **Governing law** — England and Wales
10. **Injunctive relief** — breach entitles the disclosing party to equitable remedies without posting a bond

## NDA template (starter text — not legal advice; review before use)

```
MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Agreement is made on [DATE] between:

(1) [THE ENGLISH HUB LTD / THE COMPANY], a company incorporated in
    England and Wales with company number [NUMBER] whose registered
    office is at [ADDRESS] ("Company"); and

(2) [COUNTERPARTY NAME] ("Counterparty").

Each a "Party" and together the "Parties".

BACKGROUND

The Parties wish to explore a potential commercial or corporate
transaction (the "Purpose"). In order to do so they will each share
confidential information with the other. This Agreement sets out the
terms on which such information will be protected.

1. DEFINITIONS
   1.1 "Confidential Information" means all information in any form
       (oral, written, electronic, visual) disclosed by or on behalf of
       one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the
       "Receiving Party") in connection with the Purpose, whether or
       not marked as confidential, including business plans, financial
       information, customer lists, technology, know-how, source code,
       employee information, and the fact and content of discussions
       between the Parties.

2. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECEIVING PARTY
   2.1 The Receiving Party shall:
       (a) keep the Confidential Information strictly confidential;
       (b) use the Confidential Information only for the Purpose;
       (c) apply at least the same level of protection to the
           Confidential Information as it applies to its own
           confidential information of similar importance, and in any
           event no less than a reasonable standard of care;
       (d) not disclose the Confidential Information to any third
           party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing
           Party, except to its directors, employees, and professional
           advisers who need to know for the Purpose and who are bound
           by obligations of confidentiality at least as protective as
           those in this Agreement ("Permitted Recipients");
       (e) be responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its
           Permitted Recipients.

3. EXCLUSIONS
   3.1 The obligations in clause 2 do not apply to information that:
       (a) is or becomes publicly known other than through breach of
           this Agreement;
       (b) was lawfully in the Receiving Party's possession, without
           restriction, before disclosure;
       (c) is received from a third party without restriction and
           without breach of any obligation of confidence;
       (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without
           reference to the Confidential Information; or
       (e) is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or
           regulatory authority, provided that the Receiving Party
           notifies the Disclosing Party promptly in writing (to the
           extent legally permitted) and cooperates with any effort
           to seek a protective order.

4. NO LICENCE, NO WARRANTY
   4.1 Nothing in this Agreement grants any licence or rights in the
       Confidential Information other than as expressly set out.
   4.2 The Confidential Information is provided "as is" with no
       warranty as to accuracy or completeness.

5. NON-SOLICITATION
   5.1 For a period of twelve (12) months from the date of this
       Agreement, the Counterparty shall not directly or indirectly
       solicit for employment any employee or contractor of the Company
       with whom it has come into contact as a result of the Purpose,
       save that general recruitment advertising not targeted at such
       individuals shall not be a breach.

6. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION
   6.1 On written request from the Disclosing Party, the Receiving
       Party shall promptly return or securely destroy all Confidential
       Information in its possession, save for one copy retained for
       audit / legal / regulatory purposes, which remains subject to
       this Agreement.

7. TERM
   7.1 This Agreement takes effect on the date shown above and the
       obligations in clause 2 shall continue for three (3) years from
       that date, except that obligations relating to Confidential
       Information that constitutes a trade secret shall continue for
       so long as such information remains a trade secret.

8. REMEDIES
   8.1 The Parties acknowledge that monetary damages may be an
       inadequate remedy for breach of this Agreement and that the
       Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive and other
       equitable relief without the requirement to post a bond.

9. GENERAL
   9.1 This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the
       Parties in relation to its subject matter.
   9.2 No variation is effective unless in writing and signed by both
       Parties.
   9.3 This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, including
       electronic signature.
   9.4 A person who is not a party to this Agreement has no rights
       under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999.

10. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION
    10.1 This Agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales.
    10.2 The Parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts
         of England and Wales.

SIGNED for and on behalf of [THE ENGLISH HUB LTD]:
Name: ______________________
Title: _____________________
Date: ______________________

SIGNED for and on behalf of [COUNTERPARTY]:
Name: ______________________
Title: _____________________
Date: ______________________
```

**NOTE:** This template is illustrative and must be reviewed by the company's solicitor before use. Adjust terms based on counterparty sensitivity and specific deal circumstances.

- [ ] Buyer identified and registered

- [ ] Signed NDA received, countersigned, stored in `02-legal/ndas/`
- [ ] VDR permissions granted only after the signed PDF is in hand
- [ ] NDA expiry tracked in calendar
- [ ] Return-of-materials notice issued at end of discussions (if no deal)

## Escalation triggers — when to push back on counterparty edits

Counterparties will redline the NDA. Normal. But push back if they ask for:

- **Term shorter than 2 years** (data loses protection too quickly)
- **Removal of non-solicit** (too easy to poach team)
- **Right to use for "future business relationships"** (too broad)
- **Mutual obligations where only one side is disclosing** (adds unnecessary risk)
- **Governing law of a distant jurisdiction** (expensive to enforce)
- **Unlimited "residuals" clauses** ("anything the recipient remembers" is not confidential) — limited residuals are OK, unlimited are not
