# Diligence Document Checklist

**Purpose:** The exhaustive list of documents a typical SaaS buyer will ask for during diligence. Use this as a **pre-flight check** before entering any deal process — if every box is green, you're ready. If boxes are red, either produce the document or be ready to explain why it's absent.

**Status legend:**
- [x] Have it, current, in the data room
- [~] Have it but needs updating
- [ ] Don't have it yet
- [n/a] Not applicable at this stage

Update the status column before every buyer conversation.

## Rules of thumb

1. A deal that starts with a 50% complete checklist will close at roughly half the probability of one with a 90% complete checklist.
2. The most common missing items at sub-scale are: IP assignments from contractors, DPAs with sub-processors, accessibility audit, safeguarding policy, and supplier change-of-control review.
3. "Not applicable" is a valid answer, but only when you can explain *why* in one line.
