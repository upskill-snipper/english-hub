# Due Diligence Q&A Master — The English Hub

**Purpose:** Pre-prepared answers to every question a serious investor or acquirer will ask during diligence. Every `FILL IN:` marker means "insert the current number/fact here before sharing."

**Rules:**
1. Every answer is honest. If the number is small, say so — and explain *why* it's defensible anyway.
2. Every answer has a structure: **(a) the direct answer, (b) the context, (c) the evidence link.**
3. Every answer should be readable in under 60 seconds.

**How to use:** Control-F for the question the buyer asked. Copy the answer. Update the numbers. Paste into your reply. Log the question in `buyer-q-log.csv`.

## 1. Business & Strategy

### B1. What problem does The English Hub solve?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN — one sentence. Example: "We help ESL learners in [segment] achieve [outcome] faster than traditional methods by [differentiator]."
**Context:** Describe the pain the customer felt before The English Hub existed. Quantify it if you can (hours wasted, £ spent, outcomes missed).
**Evidence:** `data-room/00-overview/problem-statement.md`

### B2. Who is your ideal customer profile (ICP)?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN — segment, geography, firmographic profile (or individual learner profile). Be specific: "B2B schools in [region] with [size]" is better than "anyone who teaches English."
**Context:** Explain how you arrived at this ICP (data, interviews, trial-and-error).
**Evidence:** `data-room/00-overview/icp.md`

### B3. How do you differentiate from competitors?
**Direct answer:** List 2–3 concrete differentiators (not "better UX"). E.g., curriculum design, accent coverage, platform integration, price point, content library depth, compliance posture.
**Context:** Name the 3 closest competitors and for each one state the specific thing The English Hub does that they don't.
**Evidence:** `data-room/00-overview/competitive-landscape.md`

### B4. How big is the market (TAM / SAM / SOM)?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN three numbers with sources. TAM = global ESL market. SAM = your addressable geography × segment. SOM = what you can realistically reach in 3 years.
**Context:** Cite sources (Cambridge English, Statista, British Council). Don't inflate.
**Evidence:** `data-room/00-overview/market-sizing.md`

### B5. What's your moat?
**Direct answer:** Be honest. At this stage the moat is usually a combination of: (i) content/curriculum depth; (ii) founder domain expertise; (iii) customer trust/brand in a narrow niche; (iv) data flywheel if applicable. It is *not* "our technology" at early stage.
**Context:** Explain which moat is strongest today and which you're investing in.
**Evidence:** `data-room/00-overview/moat-analysis.md`

### B6. What's on your product roadmap for the next 12 months?
**Direct answer:** Top 3 initiatives with target quarter. Avoid listing 20 things — buyers see that as unfocused.
**Context:** Explain what *won't* be built and why (focus is a signal).
**Evidence:** `data-room/00-overview/roadmap.md`

### B7. What's the long-term vision (5 years)?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN — one sentence that describes the future state. Don't promise a unicorn; promise a specific, believable future.
**Context:** Explain the 2–3 bets embedded in that vision.
**Evidence:** `data-room/00-overview/vision.md`

### B8. Why now? What's changed that makes this opportunity possible?
**Direct answer:** Name 2–3 tailwinds: e.g., AI-assisted content generation, post-pandemic online learning acceptance, UK/EU compliance tightening pushing schools to audited providers, cheaper infrastructure.
**Context:** Explain why this wasn't possible 5 years ago.
**Evidence:** `data-room/00-overview/market-thesis.md`

### B9. Why is the founding team the right one to build this?
**Direct answer:** Founder's specific experience in ESL/edtech/teaching, relevant prior outcomes, domain network.
**Context:** Be honest about gaps (see Team section).
**Evidence:** `data-room/00-overview/founder-bio.md`

### B10. What have you learned in the last 12 months?
**Direct answer:** Top 3 learnings that changed your strategy. Buyers love this question because it tests self-awareness.
**Context:** For each learning, say what you changed in response.
**Evidence:** Founder memo

### B11. What's your biggest strategic risk?
**Direct answer:** Name it honestly — usually one of: slow customer acquisition, regulatory change (children's data), single-founder dependency, platform risk (if reliant on another provider), content commoditization by AI.
**Context:** Explain mitigations.
**Evidence:** `data-room/04-diligence/anticipated-red-flags.md`

### B12. What's your competitive response plan if a big player enters your niche?
**Direct answer:** Three options: (i) go deeper into the niche, (ii) build complementary features they won't copy, (iii) position for partnership/acquisition.
**Context:** Explain which you'd choose and why.

### B13. What's your pricing strategy?
**Direct answer:** Current price points + rationale. Is it value-based, cost-plus, or competitor-matched?
**Context:** When did you last raise prices? What did you learn?
**Evidence:** `data-room/00-overview/pricing.md`

### B14. What are your key business metrics and targets for the next 12 months?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN: ARR target, customer count target, gross margin target. 3–5 numbers, not 20.
**Context:** Explain the sensitivity — what has to be true for you to hit them.
**Evidence:** `data-room/01-financials/plan.xlsx`

### B15. What's your go-to-market strategy?
**Direct answer:** Top 2 channels (inbound SEO, direct outbound, partnerships, paid). Name the one that's working best.
**Context:** Explain what you tried and abandoned.

### B16. What's your brand positioning?
**Direct answer:** One sentence: "For [audience] who [need], The English Hub is the [category] that [differentiator]."
**Context:** Explain the brand voice and why it resonates with ICP.
**Evidence:** `data-room/00-overview/brand.md`

### B17. What are your biggest strategic opportunities in the next 2–3 years?
**Direct answer:** Name 2–3 concrete opportunities with an approximate revenue/impact estimate.
**Context:** Rank by attractiveness × feasibility.

### B18. How do you think about build vs. buy vs. partner?
**Direct answer:** Default is build core, buy commodity, partner for distribution. Give an example of each.
**Context:** Shows strategic thinking maturity.

### B19. What's your view on AI and how does it affect your business?
**Direct answer:** Be specific. Is AI a tailwind (cheaper content creation, personalized tutoring), a threat (commoditization, students using ChatGPT to avoid lessons), or both?
**Context:** Explain 1–2 concrete ways AI is already in the product or roadmap.

### B20. If you had £1m to invest in growth tomorrow, where would it go?
**Direct answer:** Rank-ordered: 1) X, 2) Y, 3) Z. With rough allocation (%).
**Context:** Shows the founder has thought about scale economics, not just bootstrap.

## 3. Financials

### F1. What's your current ARR?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN exact number + as-of date. E.g., "£FILL IN as of FILL IN date, calculated as sum of active subscription MRR × 12."
**Context:** Explain the ARR definition used (GAAP vs. committed vs. recognized). Consistency matters.
**Evidence:** `data-room/08-metrics/arr-dashboard.md`

### F2. What's your current MRR and trend?
**Direct answer:** Current MRR, last 6 months of MRR by month, YoY growth rate. Show the chart.
**Evidence:** `data-room/08-metrics/mrr-chart.md`

### F3. How is revenue recognized?
**Direct answer:** Subscription revenue recognized ratably over term. One-time fees recognized on delivery. Explain any nuances.
**Context:** If you haven't formalized this, say so and explain the intent.

### F4. What's your revenue model?
**Direct answer:** Subscription / usage-based / one-off / hybrid. Break out the mix (e.g., "80% subscription, 20% one-off course sales").
**Evidence:** `data-room/01-financials/revenue-mix.md`

### F5. What's your gross margin?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN — as %. Typical SaaS is 70–85%. If lower, explain why (e.g., content licensing costs, human-in-loop instruction).
**Context:** Define what's in COGS (hosting, payment fees, content licensing, direct instructor cost).
**Evidence:** `data-room/01-financials/gross-margin.md`

### F6. What's your net revenue retention (NRR)?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN — as %. >100% is a strong signal. Honest: at sub-scale it may be too early to measure reliably; say so.
**Context:** Explain churn and expansion breakdown.

### F7. What's your gross retention?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN — as %. How much of last year's revenue is still on the books regardless of expansion.

### F8. What's your monthly logo churn?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN. At small numbers a single customer leaving can spike churn; explain that.

### F9. What's your CAC (customer acquisition cost)?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN — blended, or by channel if measurable. Definition: total S&M spend / new customers acquired in period.
**Context:** If you don't spend money on paid acquisition, say so — "CAC is founder time, not cash."

### F10. What's your LTV (customer lifetime value)?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN. Definition: ARPU × gross margin / churn rate. If churn is unstable, provide a range.
**Context:** Be transparent about assumptions.

### F11. What's your LTV:CAC ratio?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN. Target is 3:1+; be honest if you're not there.

### F12. What's your payback period on new customers?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN — months. Definition: CAC / (ARPU × gross margin).

### F13. What's your current cash burn per month?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN £/month net burn. Define it clearly: operating expenses minus cash revenue.

### F14. What's your runway?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN — months of cash remaining at current burn. Define cash base.

### F15. What's your capital history? How much has been raised to date?
**Direct answer:** Total raised, by round, from whom. Or "bootstrapped, no external capital."
**Evidence:** `data-room/01-financials/cap-table.xlsx`

### F16. What does the cap table look like?
**Direct answer:** Summary — founder %, employee options %, external investors %, any convertibles outstanding.
**Evidence:** `data-room/01-financials/cap-table.xlsx`

### F17. Are there any debt obligations?
**Direct answer:** Yes/no. If yes, list creditor, amount, rate, term. Include any personal guarantees.

### F18. What are your biggest cost categories?
**Direct answer:** Top 5 as % of total spend. Typically: founder/team comp, hosting, content, tools, professional fees.

### F19. Have you ever restated financials or had an audit issue?
**Direct answer:** Honest. If unaudited, say so — "as a sub-scale company we have management accounts only; we can provide an accountant-prepared compilation."

### F20. How are financial accounts prepared? Who does the bookkeeping?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN — founder in Xero / accountant / fractional FD. Frequency of close.

### F21. What's your unit economics by segment?
**Direct answer:** If you serve multiple segments (B2C/B2B/schools), break out ARPU, CAC, churn, margin for each. At sub-scale with one segment, say so.
**Evidence:** `data-room/01-financials/segment-economics.md`

### F22. Do you have any significant deferred revenue?
**Direct answer:** Annual prepaid subscriptions create deferred revenue. Report the balance and how it's amortized.

### F23. Are there any disputes with customers, suppliers, or HMRC?
**Direct answer:** Honest. If none, state that. If any, describe briefly and state expected resolution.

### F24. What's your tax status? VAT, corporation tax, R&D credits claimed?
**Direct answer:** FILL IN. VAT registered? R&D tax credits claimed in past years? Any open HMRC queries?

### F25. What are the biggest levers to improve financial performance?
**Direct answer:** Top 3: e.g., raise prices, reduce churn, shift to higher-margin channel. Be specific about quantified impact.
