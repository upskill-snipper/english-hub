## 2. The five-step model (IFRS 15 / ASC 606)

For each contract:

1. **Identify the contract.** A contract exists when there are enforceable rights and obligations — typically a signed order form, click-through T&Cs accepted at checkout, or a purchase order.
2. **Identify the performance obligations.** A performance obligation is a promise to transfer a distinct good or service. For a standard subscription, this is "access to the platform for the subscription term". Bundled offerings may contain multiple obligations.
3. **Determine the transaction price.** Include discounts, refunds, and variable consideration. Exclude sales tax / VAT collected on behalf of the government.
4. **Allocate the transaction price** to performance obligations based on standalone selling prices.
5. **Recognise revenue** as (or when) performance obligations are satisfied.

## 3. Specific treatments

### 3.1 Monthly subscriptions
Recognised straight-line over the one-month subscription term. A payment received on the first of the month is recognised fully in that month.

### 3.2 Annual subscriptions
Recognised straight-line over 12 months. On receipt of payment, the full amount is recorded as deferred revenue on the balance sheet and released to revenue at 1/12 per month.

Example: a £120 annual subscription invoiced on 2026-04-15 results in:
- £120 deferred revenue on 2026-04-15
- ~£60 recognised across April (pro-rata 15 days) through the balance of the month — confirm whether the company uses daily pro-rata or first-of-month recognition and apply consistently
- The remainder released straight-line over the following 11 months

### 3.3 Free trials
No revenue is recognised during a free trial period. Revenue recognition begins when the trial converts to a paid subscription.

### 3.4 Refunds
Refund liability is estimated at contract inception and netted against revenue. Track historical refund rates and update the estimate quarterly.

### 3.5 Discounts and promotions
Discounts reduce the transaction price. Do not gross up revenue and show discounts as a marketing expense — this would overstate revenue and gross margin.

### 3.6 Multi-year contracts
Recognise straight-line over the contract term. Record the portion due within 12 months as current deferred revenue, the balance as long-term deferred revenue.

### 3.7 Setup fees
If setup is a distinct service with a standalone price, recognise on completion. If setup is not distinct (a precondition to using the subscription), spread the fee over the expected customer life.

### 3.8 Gift cards and credits
Liability until redeemed. Recognise revenue on redemption or on expiry if allowed under consumer law in the jurisdiction.

### 3.9 Chargebacks
Treated as contra-revenue in the period the chargeback is raised.

## 4. Deferred revenue

All cash collected ahead of the service period sits on the balance sheet as deferred revenue until earned. This is a critical diligence line: it shows forward visibility.

- Current deferred revenue = amount to be recognised in the next 12 months
- Long-term deferred revenue = amount to be recognised beyond 12 months

---

## 5. Disclosures for investor diligence

When responding to diligence questions, always disclose:

- Billings vs. bookings vs. recognised revenue for the period
- Deferred revenue balance and maturity
- % of revenue from annual plans vs. monthly
- Average contract length
- Refund rate
- Any material changes to recognition policy in the period
