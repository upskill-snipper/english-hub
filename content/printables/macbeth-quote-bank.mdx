## What's inside the printable

This PDF gathers around **60 carefully chosen quotations** from *Macbeth*, organised so you can revise the way the exam asks you to think. Quotes are grouped two ways:

- **By theme** — ambition, guilt, kingship, the supernatural, gender, fate vs free will, appearance vs reality.
- **By character** — Macbeth, Lady Macbeth, Banquo, the Witches, Macduff, Duncan.

Each quotation sits next to a short **AO2 hook**: a one-line note on a method (imagery, sound, syntax, form) that you can develop into a full analytical sentence. There is also a blank column for your own notes, so the printable doubles as a working revision sheet.

## Three sample quotations

Here is a taste of how each entry is framed.

- **"Out, damned spot! out, I say!"** — Lady Macbeth, Act 5 Scene 1. *Hook:* the imperative verbs and exclamative syntax expose her unravelling control; the "spot" becomes a metonym for guilt that water cannot wash away.
- **"Vaulting ambition"** — Macbeth, Act 1 Scene 7. *Hook:* the equestrian metaphor of "vaulting" presents ambition as a rider that overleaps itself, foreshadowing the fall Macbeth fears even as he chooses to act.
- **"Tomorrow and tomorrow and tomorrow"** — Macbeth, Act 5 Scene 5. *Hook:* the triadic repetition flattens time into monotony; the trochaic pull of the line stages a man for whom meaning has drained out of language.

Every quote in the bank gets the same compact treatment so you can scan, drill, and recall under timed conditions.

## How to use it

The printable is designed for **spaced repetition** and **retrieval practice**, the two techniques with the strongest evidence base for memorising textual detail.

- **Day one:** read the quote, cover the AO2 hook, and try to write your own.
- **Day three:** test yourself on the quote from the theme or character heading alone.
- **Day seven:** write a 60-second response linking the quote to a thesis.
- **Weekly:** shuffle the order so you are not relying on the layout to cue recall.
