## Why this printable exists

AO2 is the assessment objective most students lose marks on, and the reason is rarely lack of effort — it is the gap between how mark schemes are written and how students read them. Phrases like "analysis of the writer's methods" sit on the page meaning nothing concrete. This printable closes that gap on a single side of A4.

## Layer 1: wording to translation

The left column carries AO2 phrasing typically used in Literature mark schemes — wording such as "analysis of language, form and structure", "the writer's methods", and "perceptive understanding of effect". The right column rewrites each phrase as a plain instruction. "Analysis of the writer's methods" becomes "name the technique, explain what it does, and say why the writer chose it". Students can read across the row and convert examiner language into a checklist they can actually act on.

## Layer 2: three analytical layers and grade bands

The middle of the sheet sets out the three layers of analysis examiners read for, mapped against indicative grade bands.

- **Identify** — naming a specific, accurate technique. A paragraph that stops here typically tops out around band 5.
- **Effect** — explaining what the technique does to the reader: jolts, slows, unsettles, doubles back. Add this layer cleanly and the same paragraph reaches around band 7.
- **Purpose** — connecting the technique to the writer's argument or world-view. This is the move that pushes a paragraph into band 9 territory.

The grid presents the three layers as a vertical staircase, so students can see at a glance which step they have skipped when their teacher's feedback says "more analysis".

## Layer 3: tiered terminology

A tight terminology list sits along the bottom of the printable, sorted into three tiers so students can stretch their range gradually.

- **Tier 1 (foundation):** metaphor, simile, personification, alliteration, imperative, repetition, enjambment.
- **Tier 2 (mid):** sibilance, caesura, end-stopped line, volta, anaphora, juxtaposition, dramatic monologue.
- **Tier 3 (stretch):** spondee, asyndeton, polyptoton, anadiplosis, zeugma, syndetic listing.

Tier 1 terms are non-negotiable for a pass; Tier 2 terms unlock the upper bands; Tier 3 terms, used accurately and sparingly, signal the precision examiners associate with top-band responses.

## The 5-point paragraph self-check

Before submitting any essay, students run each analytical paragraph through five quick questions printed down the right margin.

1. Did I identify a specific named technique?
2. Did I explain the effect on the reader?
3. Did I connect the technique to the writer's purpose?
4. Did I use at least one Tier 2 or Tier 3 term accurately?
5. Did I link the paragraph back to my thesis?

Five ticks per paragraph is the target. Anything less marks the paragraph for a rewrite.
