Memorising quotes is one of the few parts of GCSE English Literature where pure repetition pays off, but only if the repetition is spaced. This tracker turns that principle into a single sheet you can fill in week by week.

## How the grid works

The grid lists up to 50 quotes down the left-hand side and four review columns across the top: **Day 1**, **Day 3**, **Day 7**, and **Day 14**. Each cell takes a self-rating from 1 to 5:

- **1** - I can't recall it at all
- **2** - I get the gist but the wording is wrong
- **3** - I get most of it, with one or two errors
- **4** - I get all of it with a small hesitation
- **5** - I can write it cold, accurately, without thinking

A quote is only "learned" once it scores a 5 on the Day 14 review. Anything else goes back into rotation.

## Four-week structure

The point of the four-week shape is that any quote you add on Monday is reviewed Wednesday, the following Monday, and a fortnight later. By week four, the earliest quotes are stable in long-term memory and the latest quotes are still in active recall, so the whole bank stays warm.

You do not need to do all 50 quotes at once. Add a batch each week. The grid lets you stagger entries.

## Reminders to drop quotes you know cold

If a quote scores 5, 5, 5, 5 across all four reviews, draw a line through the row. Stop reviewing it. Your time is better spent on the weak ones. A common mistake is to keep re-reading the quotes you already know because they feel comfortable - that is procrastination, not revision.

Conversely, if a quote scores 1 or 2 twice in a row, mark it with a star. That is a signal to break it down: write it out long-hand, link it to one image, or replace it with a shorter quote that does the same job in your essay.

## Worked schedule across four texts

For students sitting AQA Literature, a realistic 49-quote bank covering all four major texts looks like this:

- **Macbeth:** 12 quotes. Two each for Macbeth, Lady Macbeth, kingship, the supernatural, ambition, and guilt.
- **A Christmas Carol:** 10 quotes. Three for Scrooge's transformation, two each for the ghosts and poverty, three for moral redemption and family.
- **An Inspector Calls:** 12 quotes. Two each for the Inspector, Sheila, Eric, Mr Birling, Mrs Birling, and the play's social message.
- **Power and Conflict cluster:** 15 quotes. Roughly one stand-out quote per anthology poem, focused on power, nature, and identity.

That gives you 49 quotes - close enough to 50 to fit the grid, manageable across roughly six weeks of preparation.

## Pair this tracker with active recall

Print the grid and keep it in the front of your revision folder. When you sit down to revise quotes, cover the right-hand columns, write the quote out from the title alone, and only then mark the rating. The act of failing to retrieve is what builds the memory.
