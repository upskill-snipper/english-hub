## How to use this character map

This printable gives a one-page overview of the six main characters before you start deep-dive revision. Read it once, then sketch the relationship lines on the back of the page from memory. If you can re-draw the map without prompts, your overview is solid.

## House allegiance

- **Montagues:** Romeo, Benvolio
- **Capulets:** Juliet, Tybalt
- **Neutral / outside:** Mercutio (kinsman to the Prince), Friar Lawrence (Franciscan friar)

## Relationship lines

Use these connections when you sketch the map:

- **Lovers:** Romeo and Juliet (across the feud)
- **Cousins:** Benvolio and Romeo (Montague); Tybalt and Juliet (Capulet)
- **Friends:** Mercutio, Romeo and Benvolio (the trio)
- **Rivals:** Tybalt versus Mercutio, then Tybalt versus Romeo
- **Mentor:** Friar Lawrence to Romeo, then to Juliet
- **Feud:** Capulet versus Montague is the dividing line that every other relationship must cross

## The six main characters

### Romeo (Montague)

- **Function:** Tragic protagonist whose impulsive love drives the plot toward catastrophe.
- **Arc:** Lovesick over Rosaline, transformed by Juliet, exiled after killing Tybalt, dies by poison.
- **Anchor quote:** "I am fortune's fool"

### Juliet (Capulet)

- **Function:** Co-protagonist whose intelligence and resolve match Romeo's passion. She matures faster than any other figure.
- **Arc:** Obedient daughter, secret wife, defiant of the Paris match, dies by Romeo's dagger.
- **Anchor quote:** "What's in a name?"

### Mercutio (kinsman to the Prince)

- **Function:** Foil to Romeo's romanticism; the play's wit, energy and cynicism. His death turns comedy into tragedy.
- **Arc:** Mocks love, fights Tybalt for honour, curses both houses as he dies.
- **Anchor quote:** "A plague o' both your houses!"

### Benvolio (Montague)

- **Function:** Peacekeeper and choric voice; he reports events truthfully and tries to defuse conflict.
- **Arc:** Tries to calm the opening brawl, persuades Romeo to attend the Capulet ball, narrates Mercutio's death to the Prince.
- **Anchor quote:** "I do but keep the peace"

### Tybalt (Capulet)

- **Function:** Antagonist embodying the feud's violence and the masculine code of honour.
- **Arc:** Spots Romeo at the ball, hunts him for revenge, kills Mercutio, is killed by Romeo.
- **Anchor quote:** "Peace? I hate the word"

### Friar Lawrence (Franciscan friar)

- **Function:** Adult mentor who tries to convert private love into public reconciliation. His plan unravels with the missed letter.
- **Arc:** Marries the lovers, devises the sleeping potion, arrives too late at the tomb.
- **Anchor quote:** "These violent delights have violent ends"
